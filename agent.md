# Agent Audit Guide — Attorney General Bondi Memo (structured)

Purpose
- Provide a concise, actionable audit guide for an automated or human agent to evaluate
  policies and programs of the Housing Authority of Clackamas County, Clackamas County,
  and the State of Oregon for practices contrary to the Attorney General guidance (Bondi
  memo, July 29, 2025).

Scope
- Entities: Housing Authority of Clackamas County; Clackamas County departments that
  receive federal funds; relevant State of Oregon programs.
- Areas: hiring/promotions, procurement/contracts/subgrants, program eligibility and
  participation, training programs, facilities policies (restrooms/locker rooms), third-party
  agreements, complaint/retaliation procedures.

Summary of the Bondi Memo (Key points)
- Federal law prohibits discrimination based on protected characteristics (race, color,
  national origin, sex, religion, etc.).
- Unlawful practices include: explicit preferential treatment, proxy criteria that
  replicate protected traits ("cultural competence", "lived experience"), segregation by
  protected characteristic, diversity quotas, discriminatory training materials, and
  funding third parties that discriminate.
- Recipients must avoid discriminatory selection criteria, include nondiscrimination
  clauses in contracts, document legitimate nondiscriminatory rationales, and protect
  individuals from retaliation.

Audit Objective
- Detect policies, programs, contracts, trainings, or practices that (a) explicitly rely on
  protected characteristics to advantage/disadvantage individuals, or (b) use neutral-
  appearing proxies or processes that have the practical effect or intent of causing
  discriminatory outcomes.

High-level Audit Process
1. Collect governance artifacts (policies, employee handbooks, procurement rules,
   contract templates, grant agreements, training materials, program eligibility
   criteria, job postings, selection rubrics, meeting minutes).
2. Inventory third-party agreements and monitor subrecipients who receive federal
   funds.
3. Review documents against a focused checklist (below).
4. Sample operational records (applicant pools, hiring outcomes, award decisions,
   enrollment lists) and run targeted analyses for disparate impact.
5. Interview stakeholders and collect complaints/retaliation reports.
6. Score findings, recommend remedial actions, and track remediation until closed.

Audit Checklist (mapped to Bondi memo sections)
- Preferential Treatment
  - Check for race-, sex-, or religion-exclusive scholarships, programs, or lounges.
  - Review hiring/admissions policies for "preferential" language or required
    demographic minimums ("diverse slate", quotas).
  - Evidence: policy documents, scholarship criteria, selection committee minutes.

- Use of Proxies
  - Identify criteria like "cultural competence", "lived experience", "overcoming
    obstacles" narratives, or geographic targeting that correlate with protected traits.
  - Evidence: position descriptions, application questions, scoring rubrics, sample
    application responses.

- Segregation / Exclusion
  - Look for race- or sex-separated training sessions, "BIPOC-only" facilities, or
    program eligibility requiring self-identification by protected characteristic.
  - Evidence: training agendas, sign-in sheets, room assignments, promotional
    materials.

- Selection & Contracting Practices
  - Review procurement and grant scoring criteria for race/sex tiebreakers, MWBE
    set-asides implemented without legal justification, or contract clauses requiring
    specific hours by protected groups.
  - Evidence: RFPs, bid evaluations, scoring matrices, contract award memos.

- Training Content & Hostile Environment
  - Evaluate training curricula for content that stereotypes or degrades groups or that
    penalizes dissent vs. trainings that neutrally prevent unlawful discrimination.
  - Evidence: slide decks, videos, handouts, assessments, trainer contracts.

- Third-Party Funding & Monitoring
  - Confirm nondiscrimination clauses are in grants, contracts, and MOUs; review
    monitoring records and corrective action plans.
  - Evidence: executed contracts, monitoring reports, termination notices.

- Retaliation Protections
  - Verify clear anti-retaliation policies, confidential reporting channels, and logs of
    complaints and their resolutions.
  - Evidence: HR files, complaint intake forms, separation/discipline records.

Evidence Collection: minimal required items per review area
- Policies, employee handbooks, procurement manuals, contract templates.
- Recent RFPs and award decisions (last 3 years).
- Job postings, candidate shortlists, interview notes, and hiring outcomes (sample).
- Training materials and attendance records.
- Grant/subaward agreements and monitoring records.
- Complaint logs, investigations, and HR disciplinary records.

Red Flags / Indicators of Noncompliance (examples)
- Explicit demographic quotas or required representation in slates/panels.
- Application questions that solicit narratives explicitly tied to race/national origin
  as a selection criterion.
- Training curricula that label an entire protected class as "privileged" or
  "oppressive" or that require confessions of inherent bias tied to protected traits.
- Contracts lacking nondiscrimination clauses or monitoring of subcontractors.
- Documented adverse actions against individuals who raised concerns about
  discriminatory practices.

Scoring & Prioritization (simple rubric)
- For each checklist item, assign: 0 = compliant/no evidence, 1 = possible issue (weak
  evidence), 2 = probable issue (multiple indicators), 3 = clear violation (explicit
  policy/practice).
- Aggregate score categories: 0–4 Low risk; 5–12 Medium risk; 13+ High risk.
- Prioritize remediation for items scoring 2–3 and any retaliation indicators.

Analytical Tests (where data available)
- Disparate impact analysis: compare selection/hiring/award rates across protected
  groups; compute selection ratios and adverse impact ratios.
- Statistical tests: chi-square for categorical outcomes; logistic regression to adjust
  for legitimate qualifications where appropriate.

Recommended Remediation Steps
1. Immediately suspend programs or criteria that create clear discriminatory outcomes
   (score 3 items) pending legal review and corrective action.
2. Replace demographic-driven criteria with objective metrics tied to job/program
   performance (skills, credentials, objective need-based criteria).
3. Insert or strengthen nondiscrimination clauses in all contracts and subawards; add
   monitoring and termination language for noncompliance.
4. Revise training materials to focus on unlawful discrimination prevention and remove
   content that stereotypes or coerces ideological conformity tied to protected traits.
5. Establish or reinforce confidential reporting and anti-retaliation practices; review
   past adverse actions for possible retaliation.

Templates & Examples (short)
- Evidence request template: list of policies, RFPs, contracts, training decks, and
  sample operational records (applicant lists, award lists, employee lists) for last 3 years.
- Interview guide: administrators, HR leads, procurement officers, training vendors.

Deliverables
- A one-page summary of high-risk findings with specific supporting evidence and
  recommended corrective actions.
- A remediation plan with owners, deadlines, and monitoring metrics.
- A compliance checklist updated after remediation and quarterly monitoring schedule.

Next steps for the agent
1. Pull or request the evidence items listed above.
2. Run the search queries and flag documents that match red-flag terms.
3. Score each checklist item and assemble the one-page findings summary.
4. Produce remediation recommendations and an implementation timeline.

Search & Collection Strategy (Brave / Common Crawl / Google + PDF ingestion)

Goal
- Find and collect all State of Oregon references to DEI and common proxies/euphemisms
  (e.g., "underrepresented", "underserved communities", "cultural competence",
  "lived experience", "diversity statement", "safe space", "BIPOC", "minority-only")
  that could be binding on the Housing Authority of Clackamas County in hiring,
  contracting, leases, program eligibility, or other business decisions.

Search scope and constraints
- Domains: restrict to Oregon state and local domains first: `oregon.gov`, `state.or.us`,
  `oregonlegislature.gov`, `clackamas.us`, and the Housing Authority domains (e.g., the
  site in the workspace). Expand to vendor and partner sites where state policy
  documents or contract templates are hosted.
- File types: prioritize `filetype:pdf`, then HTML/text. Use `filetype:docx` if present.
- Time window: last 10 years by default (adjustable).

Query terms (combine with site: and filetype: filters)
- Core: "diversity" OR "equity" OR "inclusion" OR "DEI" OR "DEIA" OR "DEIB"
- Proxies: "underrepresented" OR "underserved" OR "cultural competence" OR
  "lived experience" OR "diversity statement" OR "safe space" OR "BIPOC" OR
  "minority-only" OR "first-generation" OR "low-income targeting" OR "equity
  plan" OR "underserved communities"
- Binding indicators: "policy" OR "ordinance" OR "statewide policy" OR "model
  policy" OR "contract" OR "agreement" OR "standard" OR "required" OR
  "must" OR "shall" OR "mandatory" OR "applicable to" OR "applicability"

Brave Search API (strategy)
- Use Brave Search API to discover recent policy pages and PDFs on `oregon.gov` and
  `clackamas.us` with queries like: `site:oregon.gov filetype:pdf "underrepresented"`.
- Crawl results: capture URL, title, snippet, filetype, crawl timestamp.
- Follow `filetype:pdf` URLs to download artifacts.
- Rate limits: use polite rate (throttle requests), store responses for dedupe and
  requerying.

Common Crawl (strategy)
- Use Common Crawl index (CC-MAIN) via the CC Index API to locate historical copies
  of pages and PDFs that match queries and the target domains. Steps:
  1. Query CC index for the domain and keyword(s) to get candidate URLs.
  2. Fetch the raw WARC or the extracted HTML/PDF payload for those URLs.
  3. Download and store files locally for parsing.
- Advantage: find pages that have been removed or changed since publication.

Google Custom Search (strategy)
- Use Google Custom Search JSON API (or site-restricted Google queries) to find
  authoritative PDFs and pages. Example query: `site:oregon.gov "diversity
  statement" filetype:pdf`.
- Use this primarily to complement Brave and Common Crawl results; obey Google
  API quotas and keys.

Download, parse, and ingest PDFs
- Download process: save raw files under `research_results/documents/raw/` with a
  metadata JSON file (URL, source, crawl_date).
- Parsing: prefer `pdftotext -layout` for quick extraction; for better structural
  extraction use `tika` or Python `pdfplumber`/`pdfminer.six` to extract text and
  basic layout (headings, footers).
- OCR fallback: if PDF is scanned images, run `tesseract` (via `ocrmypdf`) before
  extraction.
- Store parsed text under `research_results/documents/parsed/` with metadata and a
  checksum for deduplication. Use filename pattern: `YYYYMMDD_source_shortname.pdf.txt`.

Indexing and analysis
- Create a small JSON index of parsed documents with fields: `id`, `title`, `url`,
  `domain`, `filetype`, `crawl_date`, `keywords_found` (list), `applicability_tags`
  (e.g., "hiring", "procurement", "training", "contracts"), and path to parsed
  text.
- Run keyword matching and simple NLP to tag documents: search for binding
  indicators ("shall", "must", "required", "policy"), and map to applicability
  tags. Flag documents with both proxy terms and binding indicators as high priority.

Automated workflow (implementation notes)
1. Runner script(s): `collect_brave.py`, `collect_cc.py`, `collect_google.py` that
   save raw outputs and schedule downloads.
2. `download_manager.py`: dedupe URL list, download files, record metadata.
3. `parse_pdfs.py`: run pdftotext/tika and save parsed outputs with metadata.
4. `index_and_tag.py`: build JSON index, run keyword/NLP tagging, produce CSV
   summary for quick review.
5. `report_generator.py`: create one-page findings and attach evidence paths.

Permissions, ethics, and rate limits
- Use API keys where required, and respect robots.txt for live site crawling. For
  Common Crawl and Brave/Google API use, follow their acceptable use rules and
  quotas.

Integration with the current project
- Parsed texts and indexes should be committed under `research_results/documents/`
  (or stored outside git if large, with metadata pointers in repo). Update the
  `agent.md` index with the `index.json` file path after the first run.
- Analysis outputs (CSV summary and one-page findings) should live in
  `research_results/summary/` with timestamps.

Quick-start commands (examples)
```bash
# Download a PDF and extract text (simple)
pdftotext -layout "https://example.oregon.gov/policy.pdf" - | sed 's/\r//' > research_results/documents/parsed/example_policy.txt

# OCR scanned PDF then extract
ocrmypdf scanned.pdf scanned_ocr.pdf && pdftotext -layout scanned_ocr.pdf scanned_ocr.txt
```
