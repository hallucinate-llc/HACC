# Joint Preliminary Injunction Strategy Against HACC and Quantum

## Bottom line

If the goal is a preliminary injunction that runs against both `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` and `Quantum Residential`, the motion should not rely on VAWA alone.

The safer structure is:

- `joint injunction core`: Fair Housing Act non-discrimination, reasonable-accommodation, and anti-interference / anti-retaliation duties that bind both HACC and Quantum;
- `HACC-specific overlay`: VAWA emergency-transfer / transfer-delay duties, hearing duties, and voucher / portability administration duties;
- `Quantum-specific overlay`: duty not to refuse or obstruct housing processing because of disability, service animal, protected complaints, or protected-status discrimination.

That is the stronger injunction posture because courts are more likely to grant interim relief when the requested order tracks clear, non-discretionary legal requirements rather than broad demands that the court supervise the whole relocation process.

## Governing injunction standard

The governing federal standard is still `Winter`:

- likely success on the merits;
- likely irreparable harm absent relief;
- balance of equities in plaintiffs' favor;
- injunction in the public interest.

Source:
- [Winter v. NRDC, 555 U.S. 7, 20 (2008)](https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/07pdf/07-1239.pdf)

In the Ninth Circuit, serious questions can sometimes suffice if the balance of hardships tips sharply in plaintiffs' favor, but irreparable harm and public interest still must be shown.

Source:
- [Alliance for the Wild Rockies v. Cottrell, 632 F.3d 1127 (9th Cir. 2011)](https://cdn.ca9.uscourts.gov/datastore/opinions/2011/02/25/09-35756.pdf)

If the requested injunction is `mandatory` rather than merely status-preserving, the burden is higher: the facts and law should not be doubtful, and the relief should be framed narrowly.

Source:
- [Marlyn Nutraceuticals, Inc. v. Mucos Pharma GmbH, 571 F.3d 873, 879 (9th Cir. 2009)](https://cdn.ca9.uscourts.gov/datastore/opinions/2009/07/17/08-55331.pdf)

## Most unequivocal duties that can support relief against both HACC and Quantum

### 1. FHA anti-interference / anti-retaliation

This is one of the cleanest joint hooks because it applies to both housing actors and is framed as a prohibition, not a matter of discretion.

The statute says it is unlawful to `coerce, intimidate, threaten, or interfere with` a person in the exercise of fair-housing rights or because that person aided another in exercising those rights.

Sources:
- [42 U.S.C. § 3617](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?edition=prelim&num=0&req=granuleid%3AUSC-prelim-title42-section3617)
- [24 C.F.R. § 100.400](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/subtitle-B/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-100/subpart-D/section-100.400)

Why this is strong for injunction:

- it supports an order that both defendants must stop interfering with housing access, voucher-backed relocation, service-animal accommodation, and protected complaints;
- it avoids asking the court to decide the final merits of every underlying housing decision immediately;
- it fits the record of repeated complaints followed by continued barriers, delays, and move pressure.

### 2. FHA reasonable-accommodation duty

The Fair Housing Act requires providers to make reasonable accommodations in rules, policies, practices, or services when necessary to afford equal housing opportunity to a person with a disability.

Sources:
- [42 U.S.C. § 3604(f)(3)(B)](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title42-section3604&num=0&edition=prelim)
- [24 C.F.R. § 100.204](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/subtitle-B/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-100/subpart-B/section-100.204)

Why this is strong for injunction:

- it is a direct mandatory duty, not a discretionary preference;
- it can run to both HACC and Quantum if each controlled part of the housing opportunity or processing path;
- it supports relief requiring defendants to stop refusing or burdening a disability-related housing path, including service-animal issues and unit-processing barriers.

### 3. FHA terms / conditions / services discrimination

The FHA prohibits discrimination in the terms, conditions, privileges, or services connected to a dwelling because of disability.

Source:
- [42 U.S.C. § 3604(f)(2)](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title42-section3604&num=0&edition=prelim)

Why this is useful:

- it supports relief against both defendants if they were jointly creating unusable or selectively obstructed access to the housing opportunity;
- it is stronger when tied to concrete acts like nonprocessing, refusal to honor service-animal rights, or channeling the household into unsuitable alternatives.

## Strong HACC-only or HACC-centered duties

These are strong, but mostly not the best basis for a `joint` injunction against Quantum.

### 4. VAWA emergency-transfer duties

HACC, as the covered housing provider in the HCV context, has the cleaner VAWA emergency-transfer obligations.

Sources:
- [24 C.F.R. § 5.2009](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/title-24/subtitle-A/part-5/subpart-L/section-5.2009)
- [HUD Form 5380](https://www.hud.gov/sites/default/files/OCHCO/documents/5380.pdf)
- [HUD HCV Fair Housing Guidebook, Apr. 2025](https://www.hud.gov/sites/dfiles/PIH/documents/HCV_Guidebook-Chapter_Fair-Housing_April-2025.pdf)

This is strong for:

- stopping HACC from letting the Waterleaf / Multnomah County safety-transfer path drift;
- requiring HACC to process the transfer route promptly and keep safety information confidential.

But standing alone, this is weaker against Quantum because the primary VAWA emergency-transfer administration duty is not as clearly Quantum's.

### 5. Federal hearing / notice duties

HACC has clearer federal notice and hearing obligations in voucher and participant disputes.

Sources:
- [42 U.S.C. § 1437d(k)](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?edition=prelim&num=0&req=granuleid%3AUSC-prelim-title42-section1437d)
- [24 C.F.R. § 982.555](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/part-982/section-982.555)

These support HACC-specific interim relief, especially preserving the status quo while hearing defects remain unresolved.

## Best way to bind both defendants in one motion

The safest joint request is not:

- `order both defendants to give plaintiffs a particular apartment immediately`

unless the record becomes much stronger and the unit is clearly still available.

The safer joint request is:

- `HACC may not terminate, evict, lock out, or extinguish voucher / relocation status while the case is pending`;
- `Quantum may not refuse to process, consider, or communicate about plaintiffs' voucher-backed housing application or successor housing opportunity on discriminatory or retaliatory grounds`;
- `both defendants may not interfere with plaintiffs' fair-housing rights, service-animal rights, accommodation requests, or voucher-backed housing access`;
- `HACC must provide a clear written status update and process the transfer / voucher path promptly`;
- `Quantum must preserve the application record and process it, or any successor equivalent housing opportunity, under nondiscriminatory standards`.

That form of relief is better because each command maps to a concrete legal duty rather than a free-floating fairness request.

## Strongest non-discretionary ask against both together

If you want the most defensible joint injunction language, it should be built around these commands:

1. `No interference / retaliation`
   HACC and Quantum are enjoined from coercing, intimidating, threatening, or interfering with plaintiffs' exercise of FHA rights, including complaints about discrimination, service-animal use, accommodation, and voucher-backed housing access.

2. `No discriminatory refusal to process`
   Quantum and HACC are enjoined from refusing, discarding, ignoring, or selectively burdening plaintiffs' housing application, transfer, or voucher-backed housing opportunity on discriminatory or retaliatory grounds.

3. `No adverse housing action pending compliance`
   HACC is enjoined from eviction, lockout, subsidy loss, or comparable adverse housing action until it has lawfully processed the voucher / transfer / hearing path.

4. `Prompt status and processing`
   HACC must give a written statement of the current voucher, port-out, and transfer status and identify the concrete next steps and deadlines.

5. `Accommodation / service-animal processing`
   Both defendants must process disability and service-animal requests under FHA standards and may not deny or obstruct the housing path on that basis without a lawful written basis.

## What is too discretionary for the first motion

These asks are more vulnerable:

- compelling Quantum to lease a specific named unit right now
- compelling issuance of a specific bedroom size without tying it tightly to FHA accommodation evidence
- compelling the court to supervise every detail of portability administration
- framing the motion as if Quantum plainly owes the primary VAWA transfer duty

Those may still be merits theories, but they are less ideal as the main preliminary-injunction hook.

## Recommended structure

For the actual motion, I would organize it like this:

1. `Joint federal core against both defendants`
   FHA interference / retaliation, FHA accommodation, FHA discriminatory terms / services.

2. `HACC-specific mandatory duties`
   VAWA transfer handling, voucher / portability processing, hearing / notice duties.

3. `Tailored relief`
   Preserve status quo, bar interference, require prompt written status and prompt lawful processing, preserve application / voucher path.

## Best one-sentence recommendation

If you want the highest-probability injunction, make the motion primarily an `FHA anti-interference / accommodation / non-discriminatory-processing` motion against both HACC and Quantum, with the `VAWA Waterleaf transfer-delay` theory used as a strong HACC-specific reason why preserving the status quo and requiring prompt processing is necessary.
