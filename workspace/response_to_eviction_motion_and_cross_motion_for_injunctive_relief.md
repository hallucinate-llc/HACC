DEFENDANTS' RESPONSE TO EVICTION MOTION AND CROSS-MOTION FOR PRELIMINARY INJUNCTIVE OR OTHER INTERIM RELIEF

Defendants Benjamin Jay Barber and Jane Kay Cortez respond to Plaintiff's recently filed eviction motion and, to the extent the Court finds additional interim relief necessary to preserve the status quo, cross-move for preliminary injunctive or equivalent interim relief in this same case.

## Requested Relief

Defendants request that the Court:

1. deny Plaintiff's eviction request as premature because the Section 18 relocation process, voucher path, accommodation process, and related replacement-housing path were not complete;
2. in the alternative, stay, abate, or otherwise withhold possession and enforcement relief while the relocation, transfer, voucher, hearing, and accommodation issues are lawfully completed;
3. order that HACC not treat relocation or displacement as complete unless and until lawful comparable replacement housing has actually been provided;
4. order that HACC and Quantum preserve and not frustrate the relocation-processing chain, including intake, forwarding, review, and status tracking of the Blossom and related housing paperwork;
5. order that HACC provide a written status statement within 7 days identifying the current voucher, portability, transfer, and review status and the concrete next steps and deadlines; and
6. grant such further narrow interim relief as is necessary to prevent loss of housing, loss of subsidy, collapse of the transfer path, or destruction of the subject matter of the case while the Court resolves the merits.

## Short Basis

This is not an ordinary simple holdover. HACC triggered redevelopment-related relocation duties grounded in 42 U.S.C. § 1437p(d) and 24 C.F.R. § 970.21, directed defendants into a replacement-housing and voucher path connected to the Hillside Park redevelopment and the Blossom / Hillside Manor intake route, and then pursued eviction while that same relocation process was still incomplete. HACC's own records show that defendants were told to begin and return the intake materials through that replacement-housing path, but that the application and packet were not lawfully processed after defendants followed those directions. HACC's own records further show the Blossom path had not been lawfully processed, the Waterleaf / Multnomah transfer path remained incomplete despite VAWA emergency-transfer duties under 24 C.F.R. § 5.2009, the accommodation process was still unresolved deep into March 2026 despite the FHA accommodation duty under 42 U.S.C. § 3604(f)(3)(B), and the household still lacked a completed comparable replacement-housing outcome. The present record also supports that substantial portions of that delay were caused by HACC itself, including the late TPV start, the broken intake-routing chain, repeated document demands, the March 19 voucher reversal, and continued HACC-side control over the voucher and transfer steps while eviction pressure was being imposed. The same record further supports that time was being wasted while HACC was acting in plain tension with source-linked governing law on VAWA-related safety-transfer issues, Solomon-related restrained-party concerns, Julio-related household-status and process issues, disability / live-in-aide handling, and race-discriminatory housing obstruction. Quantum, meanwhile, is alleged to have received but not forwarded the household's Blossom intake materials, making Quantum part of the same interrupted relocation chain rather than a separate side issue.

The Court should therefore deny possession or at minimum preserve the status quo while the relocation and housing-access process is lawfully completed.
