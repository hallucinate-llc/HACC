DEFENDANTS' RESPONSE TO EVICTION MOTION AND CROSS-MOTION FOR PRELIMINARY INJUNCTIVE OR OTHER INTERIM RELIEF

Defendants Benjamin Jay Barber and Jane Kay Cortez respond to Plaintiff's recently filed eviction motion and, to the extent the Court finds additional interim relief necessary to preserve the status quo, cross-move for preliminary injunctive or equivalent interim relief in this same case.

## Requested Relief

Defendants request that the Court:

1. deny Plaintiff's eviction request as premature because the Section 18 relocation process, voucher path, accommodation process, and related replacement-housing path were not complete;
2. in the alternative, stay, abate, or otherwise withhold possession and enforcement relief while the relocation, transfer, voucher, hearing, and accommodation issues are lawfully completed;
3. order that HACC not treat relocation or displacement as complete unless and until defendants have actually been relocated through a lawful comparable replacement-housing path;
4. order that HACC and Quantum preserve and not frustrate the relocation-processing chain, including intake, forwarding, review, and status tracking of the Blossom and related Hillside Park paperwork, to the extent each defendant received or controlled those parts of the chain;
5. order that HACC provide a written status statement within 7 days identifying the current voucher, portability, transfer, and review status and the concrete next steps and deadlines; and
6. grant such further narrow interim relief as is necessary to prevent loss of housing, loss of subsidy, collapse of the transfer path, or destruction of the subject matter of the case while the Court resolves the merits.

## Short Basis

This is not an ordinary simple holdover. Section 18 and HUD's relocation rule imposed relocation and replacement-housing duties on HACC, including duties to provide comparable housing, pay reasonable relocation expenses, provide necessary counseling, and not complete displacement until residents were actually relocated. HACC's own January 8, 2026 notice then directed defendants into a Blossom-or-TPV replacement-housing path, and HACC's March 26, 2026 options email later stated that there were three relocation paths and that all of them required a move by April 30, 2026. In the encoded form now preserved in the packet, the current record supports `O(HACC, perform the triggered relocation steps)` and `F(HACC, treat displacement as complete before actual relocation)`. See [housing_duties_fact_law_encoding_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/housing_duties_fact_law_encoding_2026-04-09.md). Those same sources show that HACC kept defendants inside administered relocation paths that remained unresolved. The primary-source sequence for that point is now summarized in [housing_primary_source_record_ladder_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/housing_primary_source_record_ladder_2026-04-09.md), and the household participation record is summarized separately in [household_submission_and_participation_chronology_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/household_submission_and_participation_chronology_2026-04-09.md), which reflects repeated submissions, follow-up, and continued pursuit of both the Clackamas and Multnomah paths. Instead, the intake materials or application path submitted through the Blossom / Hillside Manor route were not processed, and the out-of-county tenant protection voucher / portability path still had not been completed because the required voucher and transfer administration remained unfinished and no actual signable lease or contract had yet been produced on either side. See Ex. G (binder pp. 23-26); Ex. V (binder pp. 94-122); Ex. W (binder pp. 123-126); Ex. X (binder pp. 127-155); Ex. AA (binder pp. 164-167).

The record then shows that the required paths remained materially incomplete. HACC later acknowledged in writing that the Blossom intake packet had been submitted to Quantum staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office but had not been provided to HACC. The Waterleaf / Multnomah transfer path remained incomplete into late March 2026. The accommodation process was still unresolved. The post-voucher period also remained incomplete: after the March 19 voucher marker, Benjamin submitted a Parkside Heights application on March 29, 2026, copied Kati Tilton and HACC Relocation, and Parkside Heights replied on March 30 that the two-bedroom units had estimated occupancy at the end of April, which still fit inside HACC's March 26 April 30 move-out framework. On the present record, a substantial portion of the documented delay from January 8, 2026 through early April 2026 appears attributable to HACC-side administration, including the late TPV start, the intake-routing failure, repeated document demands, the March 19 voucher reversal, and continued HACC-side control over voucher and transfer steps while eviction pressure continued. The same present record is also inconsistent with abandonment by defendants, because the consolidated submission chronology reflects repeated household-side participation rather than inactivity. That legal consequence is the key point: once HACC had triggered relocation duties and directed defendants into specific replacement-housing paths, HACC should not treat its own unfinished relocation and processing obligations as if they were an ordinary tenant default. Quantum matters to that same conclusion because HACC's own records place Quantum inside the interrupted intake-and-relocation chain rather than outside it, and the narrower current theory is that if Quantum received or controlled that intake path, Quantum had project-side duties not to lose, block, or discriminatorily mishandle it.

The Court should therefore deny possession or at minimum preserve the status quo while the relocation and housing-access process is lawfully completed, consistent with the law-first and encoded housing-duty notes.
