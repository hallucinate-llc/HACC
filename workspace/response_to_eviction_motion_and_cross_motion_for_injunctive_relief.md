DEFENDANTS' RESPONSE TO EVICTION MOTION AND CROSS-MOTION FOR PRELIMINARY INJUNCTIVE OR OTHER INTERIM RELIEF

Defendants Benjamin Jay Barber and Jane Kay Cortez respond to Plaintiff's recently filed eviction motion and, to the extent the Court finds additional interim relief necessary to preserve the status quo, cross-move for preliminary injunctive or equivalent interim relief in this same case.

## Requested Relief

Defendants request that the Court:

1. deny Plaintiff's eviction request as premature because the Section 18 relocation process, voucher path, accommodation process, and related replacement-housing path were not complete;
2. in the alternative, stay, abate, or otherwise withhold possession and enforcement relief while the relocation, transfer, voucher, hearing, and accommodation issues are lawfully completed;
3. order that HACC not treat relocation or displacement as complete unless and until lawful comparable replacement housing has actually been provided;
4. order that HACC and Quantum preserve and not frustrate the relocation-processing chain, including intake, forwarding, review, and status tracking of the Blossom and related Hillside Park paperwork;
5. order that HACC provide a written status statement within 7 days identifying the current voucher, portability, transfer, and review status and the concrete next steps and deadlines; and
6. grant such further narrow interim relief as is necessary to prevent loss of housing, loss of subsidy, collapse of the transfer path, or destruction of the subject matter of the case while the Court resolves the merits.

## Short Basis

This is not an ordinary simple holdover. Section 18 and HUD's relocation rule imposed relocation and replacement-housing duties on HACC. HACC's own January 8, 2026 notice then required defendants to proceed through a Blossom-or-TPV replacement-housing path, and HACC's March 26, 2026 options email later stated that there were three relocation paths and that all of them required a move by April 30, 2026. Those same sources allowed defendants to proceed on the understanding that HACC would actually administer the offered paths. Instead, Quantum Residential did not process the intake materials or application path submitted through the Blossom / Hillside Manor route, and the out-of-county tenant protection voucher / portability path still had not been completed because the required voucher and transfer administration remained unfinished. See Ex. G (binder pp. 23-26); Ex. V (binder pp. 94-122); Ex. W (binder pp. 123-126); Ex. X (binder pp. 127-155); Ex. AA (binder pp. 164-167).

The record then shows that the required paths remained materially incomplete. HACC later acknowledged in writing that the Blossom intake packet had been submitted to Quantum staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office but had not been provided to HACC. The Waterleaf / Multnomah transfer path remained incomplete into late March 2026. The accommodation process was still unresolved. On the present record, a substantial amount, and likely most, of the documented delay from January 8, 2026 through late March 2026 was caused by HACC's own administration, including the late TPV start, the intake-routing failure, repeated document demands, the March 19 voucher reversal, and continued HACC-side control over voucher and transfer steps while eviction pressure continued. See Ex. G (binder pp. 23-26); Ex. V (binder pp. 94-122); Ex. M (binder pp. 37-44); Ex. O (binder pp. 45-52); Ex. P (binder pp. 53-57); Ex. W (binder pp. 123-126); Ex. X (binder pp. 127-155); Ex. AA (binder pp. 164-167).

That legal consequence is the key point: once HACC had triggered relocation duties and directed defendants into specific replacement-housing paths, HACC could not treat its own unfinished relocation and processing obligations as if they were an ordinary tenant default. Quantum matters to that same conclusion because HACC's own records place Quantum inside the interrupted intake-and-relocation chain rather than outside it.

The Court should therefore deny possession or at minimum preserve the status quo while the relocation and housing-access process is lawfully completed.
