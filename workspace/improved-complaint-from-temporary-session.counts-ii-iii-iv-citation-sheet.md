## Count IV

Race discrimination in contracting against Quantum under 42 U.S.C. section 1981.

Complaint paragraphs:
- Paragraphs 76 through 82

Primary exhibits:
- Exhibit G
- Exhibit J
- Exhibit R

Supporting exhibits:
- Exhibit Q

Policy-context materials:
- Clackamas County SHS materials
- RLRA materials
- H4S and related county housing-policy materials
- County civil-rights and nondiscrimination materials

Direct evidentiary use:
- Paragraphs 76 through 79 plead the concrete housing-contracting opportunity, paper submission, non-processing, and later written race-discrimination complaints.
- Exhibit R directly supports that plaintiffs wrote that Blossom refused to process the application and discriminated on the basis of race.
- Exhibit G and Exhibit J support the transfer or Blossom process and related complaint chronology.

Supporting use:
- Exhibit Q supports notice, motive, and context only.
- County SHS, RLRA, H4S, and related policy materials support the county-linked landlord and voucher pipeline context, including racial-equity program design and landlord participation.

Limit:
- The policy materials do not prove discriminatory intent by themselves and do not show that Oregon or county law required race discrimination.
- The core section 1981 theory remains intentional impairment of plaintiffs' housing-contracting opportunity on unequal racial terms.

## Safe Summary

- Exhibit R is the strongest direct exhibit for dated complaints, protected activity, and notice.
- Exhibit Q is useful but only as context and notice, not as a direct admission.
- County policy materials are best used as motive and structural context for the county-linked housing pipeline.
- Avoid claiming that the current record proves `blossom@quantumres.com` or that DEI policy legally authorized discrimination.
