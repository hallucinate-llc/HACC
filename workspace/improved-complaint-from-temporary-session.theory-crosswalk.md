# Theory Crosswalk for Counts II, III, and IV

This memo separates direct evidence, context evidence, and legal role so the complaint does not overclaim what each source proves.

## Count II

Fair Housing Act retaliation and interference against HACC and Quantum.

Best direct evidence:
- Exhibit R: preserved Gmail sent-mail records for February 26, 2026, March 2, 2026, and March 9, 2026.
- The preserved Gmail records show that Benjamin Jay Barber sent written complaints to Kati Tilton, Ashley Ferron, and `charity@magikcorp.com`.
- The body text says Blossom refused to process the application for two months, refused to house a service animal, and engaged in discrimination on the basis of race.

Legal role:
- Exhibit R is the strongest documentary support for protected fair-housing complaints and notice to HACC plus a non-HACC recipient plaintiffs associate with the Blossom-side process.
- Exhibit R supports the theory that defendants knew of the complaints before later adverse conduct continued.

Limits:
- The focused Gmail search returned no hits for `blossom@quantumres.com` in the February 26, March 2, and March 9, 2026 sent-mail windows.
- The current record proves notice to `charity@magikcorp.com`, not a proved `@quantumres.com` address.

## Count III

First Amendment retaliation against HACC under 42 U.S.C. section 1983.

Best direct evidence:
- Exhibit R again supplies the strongest dated written petitioning activity.
- The emails expressly reference an intent to sue under the Civil Rights Act and complain to county housing officials about discrimination, service-animal issues, and unlawful housing administration.

Legal role:
- The February 26, 2026, March 2, 2026, and March 9, 2026 emails are protected complaints and petitioning activity directed to government officials.
- This is the cleanest documentary basis for the First Amendment theory because HACC is the government actor.

Supporting context:
- The complaint also alleges later adverse actions including continued notice pressure, voucher delay, documentation escalation, and accommodation-related burdens.

Limits:
- Count III is strongest against HACC, not Quantum, because the theory depends on state action.

## Count IV

Race discrimination in contracting against Quantum under 42 U.S.C. section 1981.

Best direct evidence:
- The complaint pleads a concrete contracting opportunity through the Blossom application and placement process.
- Exhibit R supports that plaintiffs complained directly and in writing that Blossom refused to process the application and discriminated on the basis of race.
- The pleading also alleges in-person paper submission, later non-forwarding to HACC, and better treatment of other households.

Context and motive evidence:
- Exhibit Q is third-party former-employee review material mentioning retaliation after fair-housing complaints, "woke" terminology, and DEI-focused culture.
- Plaintiffs can use Exhibit Q to show why they believed race discrimination was occurring and that they communicated those concerns.
- Exhibit Q should not be presented as a direct corporate admission by Quantum.

County policy backdrop:
- The official Clackamas County SHS and related housing materials describe a racial-equity implementation framework, including leading with racial equity, centering BIPOC households, and evaluating racialized outcomes.
- The RLRA materials also show a county-linked landlord partnership and voucher pipeline in which landlords participate with HACC-administered housing processes.
- Separate county civil-rights materials state that county programs must comply with nondiscrimination law.

Legal role:
- The county policy materials are strongest as program-structure, motive, and context evidence.
- They help explain the environment in which Quantum allegedly participated in a county-linked contracting and housing-access pipeline.
- They do not by themselves prove discriminatory intent or lawfully authorize race discrimination.
- The core 42 U.S.C. section 1981 theory remains that Quantum intentionally impaired plaintiffs' housing-contracting opportunity on unequal racial terms.

## Safe Framing

Use:
- Exhibit R as direct proof of protected complaints, notice, and dated race-discrimination allegations.
- Exhibit Q as context, notice, and motive-related material only.
- County SHS, RLRA, H4S, and related policy materials as background showing the structure and racial-equity focus of the county-linked housing pipeline.

Avoid:
- Saying that Oregon law or Clackamas County law required race discrimination.
- Saying that Exhibit Q is a direct admission by Quantum.
- Saying that the current record proves `blossom@quantumres.com`.

## Strongest Short Form

The strongest current integrated theory is:

Benjamin Jay Barber made dated written complaints to HACC officials and a non-HACC Blossom-side recipient that Blossom had refused to process the application, refused to house a service animal, and discriminated on the basis of race; HACC then continued or intensified adverse housing actions, supporting Count II and Count III; and Quantum's alleged refusal to process the housing application, in a county-linked landlord and voucher pipeline shaped by official racial-equity program design, supports Count IV when combined with the contracting facts, unequal-treatment allegations, and the context evidence preserved in Exhibit Q.
