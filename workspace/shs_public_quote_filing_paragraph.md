# Filing-Safe SHS Quote Paragraph

Plaintiffs further rely on official Clackamas County and HACC Supportive Housing Services (`SHS`) materials as policy-context, motive, notice, and discovery-support evidence. Public SHS documents available through Clackamas County's `dochub` repository state that the program aims to "Advance housing equity by providing access to services and housing to Black, Indigenous and people of color at higher rates than their representation among those experiencing homelessness." See `https://dochub.clackamas.us/documents/drupal/ed77538a-1d00-45f2-b95b-9aecca79e5f3`; `https://dochub.clackamas.us/documents/drupal/cb82a322-0394-479e-a4e5-2eca891e9b98`; `https://dochub.clackamas.us/documents/drupal/5f774a98-6539-473a-b098-470323b34f11`. Plaintiffs also preserve a cropped screenshot of that same published language as Exhibit S. Plaintiffs rely on these materials to show that defendants operated inside a documented race-conscious housing-policy environment and that plaintiffs' written race-discrimination complaints were grounded in real published program language. Plaintiffs do not allege that the quoted policy language, standing alone, automatically proves liability; rather, plaintiffs allege that the published SHS language strengthens the inference of motive, notice, and the need for discovery into how race-linked criteria, prioritization, referrals, or housing opportunities were actually implemented in the Blossom and related relocation pipeline.

## Narrower Version

Plaintiffs rely on official Clackamas County and HACC SHS materials, including public `dochub` documents and Exhibit S, as evidence of a documented race-conscious housing-policy environment. Those public SHS materials state that the program seeks to provide services and housing to Black, Indigenous, and people of color at higher rates than their representation among those experiencing homelessness. Plaintiffs offer that policy language as motive, notice, and discovery-context evidence only, not as a standalone substitute for proving that defendants intentionally discriminated against plaintiffs in the specific housing opportunity at issue.
