# Declaration Of Jane Cortez In Support Of Preliminary Injunction

IN THE UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
FOR THE DISTRICT OF OREGON

Benjamin Barber, Jane Cortez, and Julio Regal Florez-Cortez, Plaintiffs,

v.

Housing Authority of Clackamas County and Quantum Residential, Defendants.

I, Jane Cortez, declare as follows:

1. I am a plaintiff in this action. I make this declaration from personal knowledge, and if called as a witness I could and would testify competently to the matters stated here.
2. I am a member of the household directly affected by the housing, relocation, voucher, and accommodation problems described in this case.
3. The events described in this case have affected my housing stability, my safety, and my ability to obtain housing that meets my disability-related needs.
4. Our household was facing displacement while also trying to move through the HACC-directed relocation and voucher process.
5. I understand from the records and from household communications that HACC issued late-2025 and early-2026 notices placing our housing at risk, including the displacement and for-cause notices referenced in the complaint. Exhibits C, D, E, and H.
6. I understand from the records and household experience that the Blossom application path was not processed in a timely way after the household submitted the paperwork.
7. I further understand from the records that HACC later wrote that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum Residential staff but had not been provided to HACC. Exhibit V.
8. I was also part of the household effort to move to Waterleaf in Multnomah County.
9. I understand from the preserved records that Waterleaf wanted the voucher amount and that the application had already been signed and turned in by January 12, 2026, and that HACC later acknowledged the household had chosen to port to Multnomah County. Exhibit X.
10. I understand from the preserved records that the Waterleaf application had been created by December 23, 2025 and was on the waiting list by January 28, 2026. Exhibit W.
11. Even so, the move to Waterleaf was not completed, and by late March 2026 the household was still waiting on the voucher side of the process. Exhibit M.
12. I have disability-related limitations relevant to housing, including limitations involving stairs and the need for an arrangement that works with caregiver support and daily living needs.
13. A one-bedroom arrangement did not meet the household's actual needs because of stairs, privacy, caregiving, and support issues.
14. I understand from the provider verification completed in March 2026 that I met the fair-housing disability definition, that the dog Sarah alleviated disability symptoms, and that the requested accommodation was disability-related and necessary for equal housing access. Exhibit O.
15. I further understand from HACC's March 26, 2026 letter that HACC denied the requested two-bedroom increase while stating that a one-bedroom voucher could be used for a two-bedroom ground-floor unit within the affordability range. Exhibit P.
16. In my experience, the housing path remained unstable and uncertain even after the provider verification had been completed.
17. The ongoing delays and uncertainty have caused stress and instability for me and my household.
18. If the transfer, relocation, voucher, and accommodation paths are not preserved now, I believe my household may lose housing opportunities that cannot be fully restored later.
19. In my experience, money later would not adequately fix the loss of a safe and workable housing path in the meantime.
20. I support the request for a preliminary injunction because it would preserve the status quo, prevent further harm while the case is pending, and require HACC and Quantum to handle the housing path lawfully.

I declare under penalty of perjury under the laws of the United States that the foregoing is true and correct.
