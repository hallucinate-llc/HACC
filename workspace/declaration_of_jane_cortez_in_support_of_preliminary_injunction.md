# Declaration Of Jane Kay Cortez In Support Of Defendants' Response To Eviction Motion And Cross-Motion For Interim Relief

IN THE CLACKAMAS COUNTY JUSTICE COURT
STATE OF OREGON

HOUSING AUTHORITY OF CLACKAMAS COUNTY,
Plaintiff,

v.

BENJAMIN JAY BARBER and JANE KAY CORTEZ,
Defendants.

Case No. 26FE0586

I, Jane Kay Cortez, declare as follows:

1. I am a defendant in this action. I make this declaration from personal knowledge, and if called as a witness I could and would testify competently to the matters stated here.
2. I am a member of the household directly affected by the housing, relocation, voucher, and accommodation problems described in this case.
3. The events described in this case have affected my housing stability, my safety, and my ability to obtain housing that meets my disability-related needs.
4. Our household was facing displacement while also trying to move through the HACC-directed relocation and voucher process.
5. I understand from the records and from household communications that HACC issued late-2025 and early-2026 notices placing our housing at risk, including the displacement and for-cause notices referenced in the complaint. Exhibits C (binder pp. 8-13), D (binder pp. 14-18), E (binder pp. 19-22), and H (binder pp. 27-36).
6. I understand from the records and household experience that the Blossom application path was not processed in a timely way after the household submitted the paperwork.
7. I further understand from the records that HACC later wrote that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum Residential staff but had not been provided to HACC. Exhibit V (binder pp. 94-122).
8. I was also part of the household effort to move to Waterleaf in Multnomah County.
9. I understand from the preserved records that Waterleaf wanted the voucher amount and that the application had already been signed and turned in by January 12, 2026, and that HACC later acknowledged the household had chosen to port to Multnomah County. Exhibit X (binder pp. 127-155).
10. I understand from the preserved records that the Waterleaf application had been created by December 23, 2025 and was on the waiting list by January 28, 2026. Exhibit W (binder pp. 123-126).
11. Even so, the move to Waterleaf was not completed, and by late March 2026 the household was still waiting on the voucher side of the process. Exhibit M (binder pp. 37-44).
12. During the March 23, 2026 inspection at Hillside Park, I heard Charley Skee make statements in substance explaining why we had not yet been moved.
13. In substance, I understood Charley Skee to say that Benjamin had been taken off the lease because Benjamin's brother and stepfather had been emailing documents to HACC, and that the situation continued until staff were able to sort through the documents after Benjamin sent a large batch of materials.
14. I offer paragraphs 12 and 13 as a statement of what I personally heard during that inspection, not as a claim that I possess a separate written memorialization by Charley Skee using those exact words.
15. I have disability-related limitations relevant to housing, including limitations involving stairs and the need for an arrangement that works with caregiver support and daily living needs.
16. A one-bedroom arrangement did not meet the household's actual needs because of stairs, privacy, caregiving, and support issues.
17. I understand from the provider verification completed in March 2026 that I met the fair-housing disability definition, that the dog Sarah alleviated disability symptoms, and that the requested accommodation was disability-related and necessary for equal housing access. Exhibit O (binder pp. 45-52).
18. I further understand from HACC's March 26, 2026 letter that HACC denied the requested two-bedroom increase while stating that a one-bedroom voucher could be used for a two-bedroom ground-floor unit within the affordability range. Exhibit P (binder pp. 53-57).
19. In my experience, the housing path remained unstable and uncertain even after the provider verification had been completed.
20. The ongoing delays and uncertainty, including the explanation Charley Skee gave during the March 23, 2026 inspection, have caused stress and instability for me and my household.
21. If the transfer, relocation, voucher, and accommodation paths are not preserved now, I believe my household may lose housing opportunities that cannot be fully restored later.
22. In my experience, money later would not adequately fix the loss of a safe and workable housing path in the meantime.
23. I support the request for a preliminary injunction because it would preserve the status quo, prevent further harm while the case is pending, and require HACC and Quantum to handle the housing path lawfully.

I declare under penalty of perjury under the laws of the State of Oregon that the foregoing is true and correct.
