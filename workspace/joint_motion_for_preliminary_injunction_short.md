# Plaintiffs' Motion for Preliminary Injunction

Plaintiffs Benjamin Barber and Jane Cortez move for a preliminary injunction against defendants `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` (`HACC`) and `Quantum Residential` (`Quantum`).

As set out more fully in the accompanying memorandum, plaintiffs seek narrow interim relief preserving the status quo and requiring compliance with non-discretionary federal housing and relocation duties, including:

1. no interference, retaliation, or discriminatory obstruction of plaintiffs' fair-housing rights;
2. no refusal to process, preserve, or lawfully handle plaintiffs' housing application, transfer path, accommodation requests, or service-animal-related housing requests on discriminatory or retaliatory grounds;
3. no displacement, eviction, lockout, or comparable adverse housing action by HACC while the transfer, relocation, voucher, and hearing paths at issue remain unlawfully unresolved;
4. no treatment of plaintiffs' Section 18 relocation as complete unless and until lawful comparable replacement housing has actually been provided;
5. preservation and non-frustration of the relocation-processing chain undertaken by HACC and Quantum, including the Blossom intake and forwarding path;
6. prompt written status disclosure and prompt lawful transfer processing by HACC; and
7. preservation and nondiscriminatory processing of the Blossom application or materially equivalent successor housing opportunity by Quantum.

This motion is supported by the accompanying memorandum, the complaint, the exhibits already assembled in the record, and any declarations or supplemental materials plaintiffs submit in support of interim relief.

Plaintiffs request that the Court enter the proposed order lodged with this motion.
