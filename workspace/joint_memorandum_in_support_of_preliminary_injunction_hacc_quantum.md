# Plaintiffs' Memorandum in Support of Motion for Preliminary Injunction

Plaintiffs submit this memorandum in support of their motion for preliminary injunction against defendants `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` (`HACC`) and `Quantum Residential` (`Quantum`).

## Relief Sought

Plaintiffs seek narrow interim relief tied to clear federal housing duties, Section 18 relocation duties, and relocation-processing commitments that are not matters of discretion. If the housing-duty appendix is attached, the compact duty grid is `Exhibit C-1` and the narrative law-first duty explanation is `Exhibit C-3`, as set out in [129_housing_duty_appendix_exhibit_plan_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/drafts/final_filing_set/129_housing_duty_appendix_exhibit_plan_2026-04-09.md). See also [housing_duty_appendix_table_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/drafts/housing_duty_appendix_table_2026-04-09.md); [law_first_hacc_quantum_duty_memo_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/law_first_hacc_quantum_duty_memo_2026-04-09.md); [housing_duties_fact_law_encoding_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/housing_duties_fact_law_encoding_2026-04-09.md):

1. HACC and Quantum shall not coerce, intimidate, threaten, retaliate against, or otherwise interfere with plaintiffs' exercise of rights protected by the Fair Housing Act, including complaints about discrimination, disability accommodation, service-animal use, and voucher-backed housing access.
2. HACC and Quantum shall not refuse, discard, ignore, or selectively burden plaintiffs' housing application, transfer path, voucher-backed housing opportunity, disability accommodation request, or service-animal-related housing request on discriminatory or retaliatory grounds.
3. HACC shall not terminate assistance, seek or effectuate displacement, cause lockout, extinguish tenancy status, or otherwise take materially adverse housing action against plaintiffs while the transfer, voucher, and hearing paths identified in the current record remain unlawfully unresolved.
4. HACC shall provide a written status statement identifying the current status of plaintiffs' voucher, portability request, safety-based transfer path, and any hearing or review path, together with the concrete next steps and deadlines then being applied.
5. HACC shall administer plaintiffs' external transfer or port-out route in a manner consistent with VAWA confidentiality and emergency-transfer requirements, and shall provide clear status, next-step, and review information while that route remains active and unresolved.
6. HACC shall not treat plaintiffs' relocation or displacement as complete unless and until plaintiffs have actually been relocated through a lawful comparable replacement-housing path consistent with Section 18 relocation requirements and the narrower relocation commitments HACC itself triggered.
7. HACC and Quantum shall preserve and not frustrate the relocation-processing chain identified in the current record, including intake, forwarding, review, and status-tracking of plaintiffs' Blossom application materials and related replacement-housing paperwork, to the extent each defendant received or controlled those parts of the chain.
8. Quantum shall preserve plaintiffs' Blossom application materials and, to the extent Quantum received or controlled that intake path, handle plaintiffs' application materials under nondiscriminatory, nonretaliatory, and project-role-consistent standards.

## Legal Standard

A preliminary injunction is warranted where plaintiffs show likely success on the merits, likely irreparable harm absent relief, that the balance of equities tips in their favor, and that an injunction is in the public interest. [Winter v. NRDC, 555 U.S. 7, 20 (2008)](https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/07pdf/07-1239.pdf). In the Ninth Circuit, serious questions going to the merits may support relief where the balance of hardships tips sharply in plaintiffs' favor, so long as irreparable harm and public interest are also shown. [Alliance for the Wild Rockies v. Cottrell, 632 F.3d 1127 (9th Cir. 2011)](https://cdn.ca9.uscourts.gov/datastore/opinions/2011/02/25/09-35756.pdf). Because mandatory interim relief is disfavored, the requested order should be framed narrowly and should track clear legal duties rather than broad programmatic supervision. [Marlyn Nutraceuticals, Inc. v. Mucos Pharma GmbH, 571 F.3d 873, 879 (9th Cir. 2009)](https://cdn.ca9.uscourts.gov/datastore/opinions/2009/07/17/08-55331.pdf).

## Argument

### I. Plaintiffs are likely to succeed, or at minimum raise serious questions, on the joint Fair Housing Act claims against both HACC and Quantum.

The strongest joint merits theory is the Fair Housing Act's non-discretionary prohibition on interference, retaliation, and disability-based obstruction in housing access. Federal law makes it unlawful to coerce, intimidate, threaten, or interfere with a person because that person exercised or aided the exercise of fair-housing rights. [42 U.S.C. section 3617](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?edition=prelim&num=0&req=granuleid%3AUSC-prelim-title42-section3617); [24 C.F.R. section 100.400](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/subtitle-B/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-100/subpart-D/section-100.400). Federal law also requires housing providers to make reasonable accommodations in rules, policies, practices, or services when necessary to afford a person with a disability equal housing opportunity. [42 U.S.C. section 3604(f)(3)(B)](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title42-section3604&num=0&edition=prelim); [24 C.F.R. section 100.204](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/subtitle-B/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-100/subpart-B/section-100.204). And it is unlawful to discriminate in the terms, conditions, privileges, or services connected with a dwelling because of disability. [42 U.S.C. section 3604(f)(2)](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title42-section3604&num=0&edition=prelim).

The present record supports these claims against both defendants together, although not every sub-theory is equally strong. Plaintiffs repeatedly complained in writing that Blossom had refused to process the application, refused to house a service animal, and was engaging in discrimination on the basis of race while HACC and Quantum continued to delay the processes needed to leave HACC-controlled housing. [improved-complaint-from-temporary-session.cited.md](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/improved-complaint-from-temporary-session.cited.md#L61). On February 2, 2026, Benjamin Jay Barber emailed both Ashley Ferron and `blossom@quantumres.com` stating that he had met a Black couple with one child at Balfour Park who had just been granted Section 8 and were interviewing for Blossom, while plaintiffs' own Blossom application had not been sent onward to HACC. [message.eml](/home/barberb/HACC/evidence/email_imports/starworks5-fraud-reimport-20260404/0047-Re-Allegations-of-Fraud---JC-Household-CAMTdTS-00cFTv4dFf8yZ3NpPztCKwr2LB-on4Xxz-BFEv9_5jA-mail.gmail.com/message.eml). Plaintiffs also produced provider-backed disability and service-animal support by March 2026, yet the record still reflects continued barriers, one-bedroom pressure, and nonprocessing rather than a clean accommodation path. [improved-complaint-from-temporary-session.cited.md](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/improved-complaint-from-temporary-session.cited.md#L72); [improved-complaint-from-temporary-session.cited.md](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/improved-complaint-from-temporary-session.cited.md#L95).

This is enough, at minimum, to raise serious questions that both defendants were participating in interference, discriminatory processing, and accommodation failure of the kind plaintiffs contend violates federal housing law. The requested injunction does not ask the Court to decide the final merits of every leasing or voucher dispute immediately. It asks only that both defendants stop conduct that federal housing law forbids while the merits are litigated.

### II. Plaintiffs are also likely to succeed, or at minimum raise serious questions, on the Section 18 relocation and contract-preservation theories.

Section 18 supplies another set of clear duties that strengthen interim relief. Federal law requires a public housing agency carrying out an approved demolition or disposition to give the required displacement notice, offer each displaced resident comparable housing, pay actual and reasonable relocation expenses, provide necessary counseling, and refrain from commencing demolition or completing disposition until residents are relocated. [42 U.S.C. section 1437p](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title42-section1437p&num=0&edition=prelim). HUD's implementing and explanatory materials likewise treat comparable housing, counseling, moving-expense assistance, and completed resident relocation as mandatory features of an approved Section 18 process, and HUD explains that demolition or disposition cannot be completed until residents are relocated. [24 C.F.R. section 970.21](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/subtitle-B/chapter-IX/part-970/section-970.21); [HUD Section 18 Overview](https://www.hud.gov/sites/dfiles/PIH/documents/Section%2018%20Overview%20.pdf); [HUD SAC Library](https://www.hud.gov/helping-americans/public-indian-housing-sac/library). Where comparable housing is being provided through a tenant-based voucher, the statutory requirement is not fulfilled until the family is actually relocated into that housing. In encoded form, the present record supports `O(HACC, perform the triggered relocation steps)` and `F(HACC, treat displacement as complete before actual relocation)` as the core Section 18 rules. See [housing_duties_fact_law_encoding_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/housing_duties_fact_law_encoding_2026-04-09.md).

The present record supports the conclusion that HACC triggered that relocation framework, directed plaintiffs into a replacement-housing path, and then pursued displacement while relocation was still incomplete. On plaintiffs' current evidence, HACC did not complete the comparable-housing, voucher, portability, counseling, and relocation-follow-through duties that its own Section 18 path required, and plaintiffs were left without an actual signable lease or contract even after the repeated household-side submissions and follow-up summarized in [household_submission_and_participation_chronology_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/household_submission_and_participation_chronology_2026-04-09.md). The primary record for that conclusion is the underlying notice-and-processing sequence itself: HACC's notices and referral instructions, the January 8, 2026 Blossom / TPV direction, HACC's later written acknowledgment that the packet had been left with Quantum staff but had not been forwarded to HACC, the March 19 voucher marker, the March 26 options email, and the later Parkside Heights application sequence. The separately assembled contract analysis is offered only as a synthesis of those underlying records, not as a substitute for them. [contract_breach_report.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/outputs/contract_breach_report.md).

These Section 18 and contract theories are especially well suited to status-quo relief. Plaintiffs are not asking the Court at this stage to award contract damages or to decide every final merits question. Plaintiffs ask only that HACC not treat displacement as complete before lawful relocation is actually completed, that HACC preserve and lawfully carry forward the triggered relocation steps, and that HACC and Quantum not frustrate the relocation and intake process reflected in the current record. The present record also supports the household-side participation premise for that request, and the participation chronology now collects those events in one place rather than leaving them scattered across separate exhibits and declarations. See [household_submission_and_participation_chronology_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/household_submission_and_participation_chronology_2026-04-09.md). This request does not depend on proving that every packet was conclusively complete in every downstream respect; it depends on the narrower showing of repeated submissions, follow-up, and unresolved routing and status failures while displacement pressure continued. As to Quantum, the present request is strongest where Quantum acted as the intake or processing arm for that path, not as though Section 18 by itself imposed every relocation duty directly on Quantum. Where a defendant has undertaken an ongoing relocation process and the plaintiff faces imminent housing loss, preserving that process and preventing pre-completion displacement is narrower and more defensible than attempting to compel a specific final unit award.

### III. Interim relief is also needed to preserve the possibility of final equitable relief, including specific performance.

Plaintiffs do not contend that the words `specific performance` alone entitle them to a preliminary injunction. A preliminary injunction still must satisfy the federal interim-relief standards above. But the availability of final equitable relief matters in two important ways here.

First, Oregon specific-performance law supports the view that at least part of the relief sought at final judgment is equitable in nature. Oregon cases treat specific performance as appropriate where the obligation to be enforced is sufficiently clear and definite and where a legal remedy would not be practical, efficient, and complete. [Kazlauskas v. Emmert](https://law.justia.com/cases/oregon/court-of-appeals/2012/a141575.html); [County of Lincoln v. Fischer](https://law.justia.com/cases/oregon/supreme-court/1959/216-or-421-3.html); [Pittenger Equip. Co. v. Timber Struct., Inc.](https://law.justia.com/cases/oregon/supreme-court/1950/189-or-1-2.html). Here, plaintiffs do not ask the Court at this stage to order every promise fully performed in all respects. But the present record supports several concrete obligations definite enough to preserve now: do not treat relocation as complete before actual relocation occurs, preserve the relocation-processing chain and application materials already submitted, and provide clear status information for the voucher / transfer / review path so those materials and opportunities are not lost through unresolved delay or obstruction.

Second, without interim relief, the final equitable remedy may be defeated before the Court can adjudicate the merits. If defendants are permitted to complete displacement, terminate the housing path, lose or disregard the application materials, or let the relocation chain collapse while litigation is pending, then any final order requiring lawful completion of relocation or lawful processing of the undertaken housing path will be substantially undermined. In that sense, the preliminary injunction is not a substitute for final specific performance. It is the means of preserving the subject matter so final equitable relief remains meaningful.

### IV. Plaintiffs are separately likely to succeed on HACC-specific duties that reinforce the need for interim relief.

HACC carries additional non-discretionary duties that strengthen the injunction record. In the voucher context, federal law requires lawful notice and hearing procedures before covered adverse actions. [42 U.S.C. section 1437d(k)](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?edition=prelim&num=0&req=granuleid%3AUSC-prelim-title42-section1437d); [24 C.F.R. section 982.555](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/part-982/section-982.555). HACC also had the clearer VAWA duty to maintain and use an emergency-transfer framework and to handle safety-based transfer requests with confidentiality and genuine administrative follow-through. [24 C.F.R. section 5.2009](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/title-24/subtitle-A/part-5/subpart-L/section-5.2009); [HUD Form 5380](https://www.hud.gov/sites/default/files/OCHCO/documents/5380.pdf); [HUD HCV Fair Housing Guidebook, Apr. 2025](https://www.hud.gov/sites/dfiles/PIH/documents/HCV_Guidebook-Chapter_Fair-Housing_April-2025.pdf). In encoded form, that is `O(HACC, maintain and use an emergency-transfer framework lawfully)` and `O(HACC, protect confidentiality in the transfer context)`, not a claim that federal law guaranteed port completion by a short fixed deadline. See [housing_duties_fact_law_encoding_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/housing_duties_fact_law_encoding_2026-04-09.md).

The Waterleaf chronology supports the inference that HACC did not perform that safety-transfer function in a lawful and coherent way. Plaintiffs were pursuing Waterleaf in Multnomah County by December 23, 2025. [waterleaf_application.png](/home/barberb/HACC/evidence/history/waterleaf_application.png). By January 12, 2026, HACC was told that Waterleaf wanted the voucher amount and that the application had already been signed and turned in. By January 26, 2026, HACC acknowledged that plaintiffs had chosen to port to Multnomah County. Yet by March 25-26, 2026, Waterleaf still had plaintiffs only on a waiting list pending voucher issuance. [message.eml](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/manual-imap-download-2026-03-31/20260202-164227_Re-Allegations-of-Fraud---JC-Household/message.eml); [message.eml](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/temporary-cli-session-migration/prior-research-results/full-evidence-review-run/chronology/emergency_motion_packet/exhibits/Exhibit%20M%20-%20starworks5-ktilton-orientation-import/0008-Re-HCV-Orientation-CAMTdTS_hM81x2YFGZBGX3tGzBJjMjn5WhiFW-NwOC0k23-63LQ-mail.gmail.com/message.eml). Even if VAWA does not impose a short fixed numeric deadline for out-of-county portability, the present authorities and record support more than passive nonadministration while housing instability and move pressure continue. The narrow point is that HACC had to administer the path lawfully and coherently while it remained active.

### V. Plaintiffs face irreparable harm absent immediate relief.

Loss of housing, loss of subsidy, forced displacement before relocation is lawfully completed, collapse of a safety-based transfer route, and denial of disability accommodation are classic irreparable harms. The present record reflects continuing move pressure, stalled transfer processing, service-animal and accommodation disputes, and a housing opportunity that plaintiffs contend was not processed to completion on the current record. Where the injury is ongoing interference with housing rights and the realistic threat of housing loss, money damages are not an adequate substitute.

### VI. The balance of equities and the public interest favor narrow relief.

The requested relief imposes only the burden of obeying existing federal law and preserving the relocation and processing commitments defendants already undertook. It does not require the Court to micromanage every housing decision or to compel an immediate lease of a particular unit. Instead, it preserves the status quo, bars further interference and retaliation, bars displacement before relocation is lawfully complete, requires lawful accommodation handling, requires written status clarity from HACC, and requires both defendants to stop blocking or distorting plaintiffs' housing path on unlawful grounds. The public interest strongly favors interim orders that prevent discriminatory or premature housing loss and require compliance with federal housing protections and federally required relocation obligations.
