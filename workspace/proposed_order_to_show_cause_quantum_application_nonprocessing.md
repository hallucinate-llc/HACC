# Proposed Order To Show Cause Re Quantum Residential's Nonprocessing Of The Barber-Cortez Applications

IN THE CLACKAMAS COUNTY JUSTICE COURT
STATE OF OREGON

HOUSING AUTHORITY OF CLACKAMAS COUNTY,
Plaintiff,

v.

BENJAMIN JAY BARBER and JANE KAY CORTEZ,
Defendants.

Case No. 26FE0586

PROPOSED ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE RE QUANTUM RESIDENTIAL'S NONPROCESSING OF THE BARBER-CORTEZ APPLICATIONS

The Court, having considered Defendants' motion, the supporting memorandum, and the record, ORDERS as follows:

1. Quantum Residential Property Management, L.P., upon joinder or other authorized appearance in this action, shall show cause in writing why the Blossom / Hillside Manor application materials submitted from or on behalf of Benjamin Jay Barber and Jane Kay Cortez were not processed, transmitted, or brought to a clear written result.

2. The written response shall be filed within `7 days` after Quantum's joinder or first appearance, unless the Court orders otherwise.

3. The written response shall address, at minimum:
   a. whether Quantum received any application, intake packet, or related housing materials from or on behalf of Benjamin Jay Barber and Jane Kay Cortez;
   b. if so, when, where, and through whom those materials were received;
   c. what happened to the materials after receipt, including any scanning, copying, logging, review, forwarding, rejection, or loss;
   d. whether any materials were ever sent to HACC, and if so when and by whom;
   e. whether any written deficiency notice, denial, approval, or other result was issued;
   f. whether service-animal concerns, voucher timing, source-of-income issues, race-related concerns, or any other screening factor played any role in the handling or nonprocessing of the materials; and
   g. what records Quantum currently maintains concerning the same.

4. Quantum shall preserve all documents, emails, logs, scans, status notes, intake records, and communications concerning the Barber-Cortez Blossom / Hillside Manor application path pending further order of the Court.

5. Nothing in this Order resolves the ultimate merits of defendants' claims or defenses. This Order is limited to requiring a targeted explanation and preservation of the disputed application-processing record.

IT IS SO ORDERED.
