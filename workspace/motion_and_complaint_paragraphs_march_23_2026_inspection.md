## Amended Complaint Paragraph

During a HUD NSPIRE inspection conducted on March 23, 2026, Benjamin Barber and Jane Cortez heard HACC representative Charley Skee state in substance that Benjamin Barber had been taken off the lease because Benjamin's brother and stepfather had been emailing documents to HACC, and that the matter persisted until staff sorted through the materials after Benjamin Barber submitted a large batch of documents. Plaintiffs allege that this statement reflects HACC's reliance on outside and irregular document flows in making or maintaining lease and relocation decisions affecting the household, rather than using a lawful, transparent, and reviewable process.

## Narrower Alternative Complaint Paragraph

Plaintiffs further allege that on March 23, 2026, during a HUD NSPIRE inspection, Charley Skee stated in substance that Benjamin Barber had been removed from the lease because of document communications coming from Benjamin's brother and stepfather, and that HACC later had to sort through a large batch of materials submitted by Benjamin Barber. Plaintiffs allege that this statement is evidence of irregular and externally influenced household-composition decision-making.
