## Best Use Note

The strongest joinder theory is the narrow one:

- HACC's own January 8, 2026 notice routed the household into the Blossom / Hillside Manor intake path;
- HACC later acknowledged in writing that the packet had been submitted to Quantum staff but had not reached HACC;
- HACC's March 26, 2026 email still treated relocation as an unresolved three-option process; and
- the Court is already being asked to preserve or restore that same chain in the eviction-response packet.

That is a stronger joinder posture than asking the state court, at the joinder stage, to decide every ultimate federal duty question between HACC and Quantum.

The rule structure is:

- `ORCP 29A` for direct joinder if Quantum is needed for complete relief or to avoid inconsistent obligations; and
- `ORCP 22C` as the fallback if the Court prefers third-party practice against a nonparty who may be liable for all or part of the dispute already before the Court.

If defendants use the show-cause motion, the safest posture is to file it with or immediately after the joinder motion, because the requested response is directed to Quantum upon joinder or other appearance in the action.
