# Judge Question Answer Sheet

## If The Court Says: `Why isn't this just a failure to move?`

Because the record shows HACC itself directed the household into two separate relocation paths, and neither one was complete when HACC moved toward eviction.

## If The Court Says: `What exactly was not processed?`

The Hillside Park replacement-housing application path tied to Blossom / Hillside Manor. HACC later wrote that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum staff but had not been provided to HACC.

## If The Court Says: `What do you mean by Hillside Park path?`

The redevelopment replacement-housing path that HACC tied to the Hillside Park displacement process, including the January 8, 2026 Blossom / TPV notice and the Hillside Manor intake route.

## If The Court Says: `What is the best document for that?`

Exhibit G for the January 8 notice, and Exhibit V for HACC's later acknowledgment that Quantum had the packet but did not transmit it.

## If The Court Says: `What about the port to Multnomah County?`

That was the alternate route HACC identified. But it stretched across four calendar months and still was not complete by late March 2026. Waterleaf existed by December 23, 2025, HACC knew of the signed application by January 12, HACC acknowledged the port choice by January 26, and Waterleaf was still waiting on voucher issuance in late March.

## If The Court Says: `How much of that delay was HACC's own doing?`

Not as an exact percentage from the current record, but a substantial amount, and likely most of the documented delay from January 8 through late March 2026. The main documented bottlenecks in that period were HACC-side: the TPV process had not yet started, the intake packet had not reached HACC through the route HACC set up, HACC kept demanding more documents, HACC reversed the voucher on March 19, and Waterleaf was still waiting on HACC-issued voucher steps in late March. And those were not merely discretionary preferences. They were duties tied to identified sources: Section 18 relocation duties under 42 U.S.C. § 1437p(d) and 24 C.F.R. § 970.21, hearing duties under 42 U.S.C. § 1437d(k) and 24 C.F.R. § 982.555, VAWA transfer duties under 24 C.F.R. § 5.2009, and accommodation duties under 42 U.S.C. § 3604(f)(3)(B) and 24 C.F.R. § 100.204.

## If The Court Says: `Do you have a law that says they had to finish it by a certain date?`

The stronger point is not a fixed short numeric deadline. The stronger point is that Section 18 required HACC to complete the relocation process lawfully before treating displacement as finished, 42 U.S.C. § 1437p(d); 24 C.F.R. § 970.21, and VAWA required HACC to actually administer the emergency transfer path it was using, 24 C.F.R. § 5.2009. So HACC should not obtain possession while those paths were still incomplete.

## If The Court Says: `Why does Quantum matter in this eviction case?`

Because HACC itself wrote that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office and had not been forwarded to HACC. That makes Quantum part of the same interrupted relocation chain.

## If The Court Says: `What is your narrowest ask?`

Deny possession as premature. If the Court will not do that today, then stay or abate possession, require HACC to provide a written status update within 7 days, and preserve the application, voucher, transfer, and relocation path.

## If The Court Says: `Are you asking me to order them to give you a specific apartment?`

No. The narrow request is to preserve the housing path and stop possession from being granted before relocation is lawfully completed.

## If The Court Says: `Why isn't money enough later?`

Because loss of housing, loss of the transfer path, collapse of the application path, and loss of the practical housing opportunity cannot be fully repaired after the fact.
