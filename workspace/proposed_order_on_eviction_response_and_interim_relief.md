# [Proposed] Order On Defendants' Response To Eviction Motion And Cross-Motion For Interim Relief

IN THE CLACKAMAS COUNTY JUSTICE COURT
STATE OF OREGON

HOUSING AUTHORITY OF CLACKAMAS COUNTY,
Plaintiff,

v.

BENJAMIN JAY BARBER and JANE KAY CORTEZ,
Defendants.

Case No. 26FE0586

The Court, having considered Plaintiff's eviction motion, Defendants' response, Defendants' cross-motion for interim relief, the supporting memorandum, declarations, and the record, hereby orders:

1. Plaintiff's request for immediate possession is denied without prejudice; or, in the alternative, is stayed or abated pending further order of the Court.
2. Plaintiff `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` shall not cause eviction, lockout, or materially adverse displacement consequences against Defendants on the matters at issue here unless and until the relocation, voucher, transfer, and review paths at issue have been lawfully completed.
3. Plaintiff `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` shall not treat Defendants' Section 18 relocation as complete unless and until Defendants have actually been provided a lawful comparable replacement-housing path consistent with federal relocation requirements and the relocation commitments Plaintiff triggered.
4. Within 7 days of entry of this Order, Plaintiff `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` shall provide Defendants with a written status statement identifying the current voucher status, portability status, transfer status, and any pending review or hearing path, together with the concrete next steps and deadlines.
5. Plaintiff `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` and Quantum Residential, to the extent the Court finds it appropriate to direct preservation relief affecting the housing-processing chain at issue, shall preserve and not frustrate the intake, forwarding, review, status tracking, and communication concerning Defendants' Blossom application materials and related replacement-housing paperwork.
6. Plaintiff `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` shall continue to process the transfer, voucher, relocation, and accommodation path in a manner consistent with applicable law while this matter remains pending.

IT IS SO ORDERED.
