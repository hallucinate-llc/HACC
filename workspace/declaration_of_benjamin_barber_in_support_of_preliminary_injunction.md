# Declaration Of Benjamin Barber In Support Of Preliminary Injunction

IN THE UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
FOR THE DISTRICT OF OREGON

Benjamin Barber, Jane Cortez, and Julio Regal Florez-Cortez, Plaintiffs,

v.

Housing Authority of Clackamas County and Quantum Residential, Defendants.

I, Benjamin Barber, declare as follows:

1. I am a plaintiff in this action. I make this declaration from personal knowledge, and if called as a witness I could and would testify competently to the matters stated here.
2. I reside in the household directly affected by the housing, relocation, voucher, accommodation, and transfer problems described in this case.
3. My household has been dealing with displacement pressure, relocation delays, voucher delays, accommodation problems, service-animal issues, and the breakdown of the Blossom and Waterleaf housing paths.
4. HACC issued notices in late 2025 and early 2026 that put our housing stability at risk, including the December 23, 2025 no-cause notices, the December 31, 2025 displacement notice, and the February 4, 2026 for-cause notice. Exhibits C, D, E, and H.
5. During the same general period, our household was trying to move through the HACC-directed relocation and voucher path rather than remain where we were.
6. On January 8, 2026, HACC sent a Blossom-related notice stating that the Tenant Protection Voucher process had not yet started and identifying Ashley Ferron as the contact to begin that process. Exhibit G.
7. From my experience and review of the related communications, I understood HACC to be directing our household into the Hillside Park redevelopment replacement-housing application path, including the Blossom / Hillside Manor office intake route.
8. In December 2025, I personally delivered a paper Blossom application in person to a woman working on the Blossom-side intake or application process. I do not know her name.
9. On the same day that I turned in that Blossom paperwork, I was told in substance that my dog could not live there. At that time, my dog was my service animal.
10. I later learned that the paper Blossom application had not been turned in to HACC, despite my in-person submission.
11. On February 2, 2026, I emailed Ashley Ferron and `blossom@quantumres.com` stating that I had met a Black couple with one child at Balfour Park who had just been granted Section 8 and were interviewing for Blossom while our own application had not been sent on to HACC. Exhibit T.
12. In that same February 2, 2026 email, I expressly accused Quantum of race-based `lemon dropping`. Exhibit T.
13. In preserved emails dated February 26, March 2, and March 9, 2026, I also wrote that Blossom had refused to process the application, had refused to house a service animal, and had engaged in discrimination, while HACC and Quantum continued to delay the steps needed to leave HACC-controlled housing. Exhibits J and R.
14. HACC later acknowledged in writing that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum Residential staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office but had not been provided to HACC. Exhibit V.
15. In my experience, that failure to process or forward the packet materially delayed our housing path.
16. Our household was also seeking an out-of-county move to Waterleaf in Multnomah County.
17. The Waterleaf application record shows a created date of December 23, 2025 and shows the application on the waiting list by January 28, 2026. Exhibit W.
18. HACC's own correspondence shows that by January 12, 2026 HACC was told Waterleaf wanted the voucher amount and that the application had already been signed and turned in, and that by January 26, 2026 HACC acknowledged that the household had chosen to port to Multnomah County. Exhibit X.
19. By March 17 and March 18, 2026, I wrote that I had been trying to move to Waterleaf for nearly three months. Exhibits J and M.
20. By March 25 and March 26, 2026, Waterleaf still had our household only on the waiting list pending issuance of the voucher. Exhibit M.
21. In my experience, HACC did not process that Waterleaf transfer path with reasonable promptness.
22. As I understand the timeline, that alternate port-out path stretched across four calendar months, beginning with the December 23, 2025 Waterleaf application and still remaining incomplete by late March 2026.
23. Our household also sought a two-bedroom, first-floor accommodation because Jane Cortez could not climb stairs, needed disability-related support, and needed a housing arrangement that fit her caregiver, privacy, mobility, ADL/IADL, and service-animal needs.
24. HACC first sent materials reflecting a two-bedroom voucher, then stated on March 19, 2026 that the two-bedroom issuance had been created and sent in error and that a one-bedroom voucher would instead issue. Exhibit M.
25. I responded that I had a scheduled appointment to obtain a reasonable accommodation for a two-bedroom unit and that the one-bedroom arrangement was not workable. Exhibit M.
26. By March 24, 2026, provider verification had been completed supporting Jane Cortez's disability status, the service-animal need, and the requested accommodation. Exhibit O.
27. Even after that, the accommodation and housing path remained delayed and uncertain, and HACC denied the two-bedroom increase while stating that a one-bedroom voucher could be used for a two-bedroom ground-floor unit within the affordability range. Exhibit P.
28. In my experience, the combined effect of the Hillside Park replacement-housing application nonprocessing, Waterleaf delay, voucher instability, and accommodation problems left our household without a stable and lawful path out of HACC-controlled housing.
29. Without immediate court intervention, I believe our household faces continuing risk of displacement, continued collapse of the transfer and relocation path, and continued loss of housing opportunities that cannot be fully repaired later with money damages.
30. If the Hillside Park replacement-housing application path, Waterleaf path, or voucher path is allowed to collapse while this case is pending, I believe the practical housing opportunity itself will be lost even if we later win on the merits.
31. The requested preliminary injunction would not give us an unfair windfall. It would preserve the status quo, require defendants to stop interfering with the housing path, and require them to process the relocation, transfer, voucher, and application issues lawfully while the Court decides the merits.

I declare under penalty of perjury under the laws of the United States that the foregoing is true and correct.
