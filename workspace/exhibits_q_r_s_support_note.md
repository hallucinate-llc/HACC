# Exhibits Q, R, and S Support Note

## Filing-Safe Support Note

Exhibit R is the strongest direct evidence that plaintiffs made dated written complaints alleging that Blossom refused to process the application, refused to house a service animal, and discriminated on the basis of race. Exhibit Q is narrower and is offered only as third-party notice, motive, and context evidence, including a former employee's statement that reporting fair-housing violations led to retaliation. Exhibit S is narrower still and is offered as public-policy context, because it preserves the SHS outcome-goal language that is independently grounded to public Clackamas County and HACC `dochub` materials stating that services and housing should be provided to Black, Indigenous, and people of color at higher rates than their representation among those experiencing homelessness. Plaintiffs do not rely on Exhibits Q or S as direct admissions by defendants or as standalone substitutes for proving intentional discrimination in plaintiffs' specific housing opportunity.

## Short Version

Exhibit R is direct complaint proof. Exhibit Q is notice and motive context. Exhibit S is public-policy context grounded to public SHS documents. Only Exhibit R directly proves the dated written complaints themselves.
