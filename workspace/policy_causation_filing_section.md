# Filing-Safe Policy Causation Section

Plaintiffs further allege that the harms they experienced did not occur in a policy vacuum. Official county, HACC, SHS, RLRA, AFH, and related housing-program materials show that the relevant housing pipeline operated inside a documented race-conscious policy environment. Public SHS materials available through Clackamas County's `dochub` repository state that supportive housing services seek to "Advance housing equity by providing access to services and housing to Black, Indigenous and people of color at higher rates than their representation among those experiencing homelessness." Clackamas County and HACC's Assessment of Fair Housing further states that internal housing policies and practices are to be reviewed with a `racial equity lens`, that HACC forms and letters are to be `racially equitable`, and that housing outcomes for `Black, Indigenous and People of Color` are to be monitored. Plaintiffs do not allege that those policy statements alone automatically establish liability. Rather, plaintiffs allege that defendants operated inside that county-linked policy and implementation environment; that Quantum and HACC participated in the same intake, referral, voucher, and placement chain; and that plaintiffs experienced nonprocessing, nontransmission, delay, and comparator-skewed treatment inside that same chain.

That causation theory is strengthened by plaintiffs' contemporaneous February 2, 2026 email to Ashley Ferron and `blossom@quantumres.com`, in which Benjamin Jay Barber wrote that he had met a Black couple with one child at Balfour Park who had just been granted Section 8 and were interviewing for Blossom while plaintiffs' own application had not been sent on to HACC, and in which he expressly accused Quantum of race-based `lemon dropping`. Plaintiffs rely on the official policy materials, the county-linked housing pipeline records, and the February 2, 2026 comparator email together, not separately, to support the inference that the unequal treatment plaintiffs experienced was an extension of a race-conscious housing-administration environment rather than an isolated paperwork mistake.

## Narrower Version

Plaintiffs allege that defendants acted inside a county-linked housing pipeline that official SHS, RLRA, AFH, and HACC materials describe in race-conscious terms, including use of a racial-equity lens, BIPOC-focused outcome monitoring, and race-conscious housing and service-access goals. Plaintiffs further allege that their own housing opportunity was obstructed inside that same pipeline and that, in a contemporaneous February 2, 2026 email to Ashley Ferron and `blossom@quantumres.com`, Benjamin Jay Barber described a Black-family comparator and expressly accused Quantum of race-based `lemon dropping`. Plaintiffs offer these materials as motive, notice, and causation-support evidence, not as a standalone substitute for case-specific proof.
