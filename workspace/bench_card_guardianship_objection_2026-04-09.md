# Bench Card: Objection To Solomon's Guardianship Petition

## Core Theme

This probate petition should not be used to obtain emergency or plenary control over Jane Kay Cortez on a one-sided record that:

- omits the same-day restraining-order context for the APS / police allegation;
- overstates Benjamin Barber's role in the housing crisis while understating HACC / Quantum delay and outside document-flow issues;
- seeks coercive writ-based transport and broad placement authority;
- asks to charge Jane's funds for adverse attorney fees; and
- may be serious enough, if the Court agrees it was materially barred or seriously defective, to justify sanctions reservation or claim-limiting relief.

## Best 30-Second Opening

Your Honor, this petition asks for sweeping control on a disputed and incomplete record. The APS allegation omits that, on the very day Solomon says he was trying to involve APS, Jane was at the courthouse seeking a restraining order against him. The housing emergency was not simply caused by Benjamin; the current record shows overlapping HACC and Quantum relocation delay and disputed outside document flows. And the petition goes far beyond simple help by seeking writ-based forced transport, plenary authority, possible placement, and attorney fees from Jane's funds. At minimum, those requests should be denied or sharply narrowed until there is a clean predicate record.

## Five-Step Story

1. Solomon asked for emergency and plenary control on a one-sided narrative.
2. The APS / police allegation omits that Jane was seeking a restraining order against Solomon that same day.
3. The petition falsely frames Benjamin as a recent household arrival even though he had been living with Jane for more than six years.
4. The petition seeks coercive transport, broad placement power, and financial-control authority while trust and authority papers remain unproduced, even though the earlier restraining-order exhibits appear to show Jane describing papers she signed with Sam / Solomon that she did not understand, including a paper she thought related to her dad's will.
5. The Court should deny or sharply narrow relief unless and until the predicate record is cleaned up.

## Best Legal Point

Probate should not drift into an improvised emergency-control forum for disputed collateral housing and family-control claims. The Court can require a clean notice and predicate-order record, deny overbroad relief, and limit any hearing to genuine guardianship questions.

## Narrow Ask

- deny or sharply narrow emergency relief;
- deny any further writ-based forced transport without a clean predicate record;
- deny plenary and placement authority on the present showing;
- deny fees from Jane's funds;
- leave any HACC / Quantum / Parkside / Blossom or Home Forward / Brighthaven / Vera / Waterleaf fee issues to the separate properly framed federal civil-rights / housing track, not Jane's funds in probate;
- reserve or consider sanctions-related or claim-limiting relief if the Court concludes the petition was materially barred or seriously defective;
- limit any hearing to emergency basis, notice, scope, and record sufficiency.

## If The Judge Pushes Back

`What is the main factual omission?`

- The petition leaves out the same-day restraining-order context for the APS allegation and leaves out the HACC / Quantum delay record that shaped the housing crisis.

`Are you saying Jane needs no help at all?`

- No. The point is that disputed need for some assistance does not justify sweeping emergency, transport, placement, and fee relief on this record.

`Why should the Court care about HACC and Quantum here?`

- Only to avoid misattributing the housing emergency to Benjamin. The probate case should not become the forum for deciding the full housing merits.

`Are you asking for malpractice findings today?`

- No. The narrower point is that if the Court concludes the petition was materially barred or seriously defective, it should reserve or consider sanctions-related or other corrective relief directed to the filing or maintenance of the petition.

`If there are fee issues tied to HACC or Quantum, where do they belong?`

- In the separate properly framed federal civil-rights or housing case, not as a probate-side charge against Jane's funds here.
