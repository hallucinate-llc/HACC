# Official Filing Forms Checklist

This note separates the curated complaint binder from the official court forms that may still need to be filed with it.

## District of Oregon - Civil Case Opening

Based on the District of Oregon civil forms page and case-opening guidance, the standard complaint-opening set includes:

1. Complaint
2. Civil Cover Sheet (`JS-44`)
3. Summons for issuance by the Clerk's Office, if appropriate

## Forms To Prepare Separately

### 1. Civil Cover Sheet (`JS-44`)

Purpose:
Statistical case-opening form used with a new civil complaint.

What still needs to be filled:
- county of residence for first-listed plaintiff
- county of residence for first-listed defendant
- basis of jurisdiction
- citizenship boxes if diversity is alleged
- nature of suit
- cause of action
- requested relief / class action / jury demand boxes
- related-case information if any

Recommended handling:
Prepare as a separate official form PDF, not as part of the exhibit binder.

### 2. Summons in a Civil Action (`AO 440`)

Purpose:
Clerk-issued summons for each defendant to be served, unless waiver practice will be used first.

What still needs to be filled:
- exact defendant name as it should appear on summons
- defendant service name and address
- plaintiff / pro se contact information
- civil action number after assignment by the Clerk, unless the court permits pre-assignment preparation

Recommended handling:
Prepare one summons for each defendant or service recipient that needs separate service.

### 3. Waiver-of-Service Packet, if used

Potential federal forms:
- `AO 398` Notice of Lawsuit and Request to Waive Service of a Summons
- `AO 399` Waiver of the Service of Summons

Recommended handling:
Use only if you plan to request waiver instead of immediate personal or other Rule 4 service.

### 4. Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis, if needed

Prepare only if filing-fee waiver status will be requested.
