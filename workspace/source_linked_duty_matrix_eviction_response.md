# Source-Linked Duty Matrix For Eviction Response And Interim Relief

This matrix is limited to duties actually invoked in the eviction-response and interim-relief packet. Each duty below is tied to an identified legal source rather than presented as a free-floating assertion.

## 1. Section 18 Relocation Completion Duty

Duty: HACC may not treat displacement as complete while comparable replacement-housing and relocation steps remain unfinished.

Sources:
- 42 U.S.C. § 1437p(d)
- 24 C.F.R. § 970.21

Why this duty is being invoked:
- HACC triggered redevelopment relocation through the Hillside Park process, then moved toward eviction while the replacement-housing path, voucher path, and move path were still incomplete.

## 2. Hearing And Adverse-Action Notice Duty

Duty: When HACC takes an assistance-side or occupancy-related adverse action, it must provide reasoned notice and a hearing path.

Sources:
- 42 U.S.C. § 1437d(k)
- 24 C.F.R. § 982.555

Why this duty is being invoked:
- The record reflects lease, voucher, bedroom-size, and household-status changes without a cleanly administered process path.

## 3. VAWA Emergency Transfer Administration Duty

Duty: When HACC is handling a qualifying safety-based move, it must maintain and use an emergency transfer plan and actually administer that path.

Sources:
- 24 C.F.R. § 5.2009
- HACC Administrative Plan effective July 1, 2025, emergency-transfer passages
- HUD HCV Fair Housing Guidebook, Apr. 2025, pages 12-13

Why this duty is being invoked:
- The Waterleaf / Multnomah path is being used as evidence that HACC let a safety-linked transfer drift while eviction pressure continued.

## 4. VAWA Confidentiality And Court-Order Compliance Duties

Duty: Once notified of survivor-status, transfer requests, or protection-order issues, HACC must keep that information confidential and comply with court-order limits in access-and-control decisions.

Sources:
- 24 C.F.R. § 5.2007(c)
- 34 U.S.C. § 12491(b)(3)(C)(i)

Why this duty is being invoked:
- The eviction packet relies in part on Solomon-related restrained-party concerns and the proposition that HACC was not free to manage those issues informally.

## 5. VAWA Bifurcation And Household-Protection Duty

Duty: If HACC handles perpetrator-related household change through VAWA, it must use the lawful bifurcation framework rather than strip remaining lawful occupants of protected rights.

Sources:
- 24 C.F.R. § 5.2009(a)-(b)

Why this duty is being invoked:
- The packet references Julio-related household-status and process inconsistency as part of why the relocation and occupancy picture was not lawfully settled.

## 6. Fair Housing Act Accommodation Duty

Duty: A housing provider must make reasonable accommodations in rules, policies, practices, or services when necessary to afford a person with a disability equal opportunity to use and enjoy a dwelling.

Sources:
- 42 U.S.C. § 3604(f)(3)(B)
- 24 C.F.R. § 100.204

Why this duty is being invoked:
- The first-floor / bedroom-size / disability-related housing path remained unresolved while eviction pressure continued.

## 7. HUD Live-In-Aide Treatment Rules

Duty: When true live-in-aide facts are present, the aide is treated differently from an ordinary counted household member for status and income-review purposes.

Sources:
- 24 C.F.R. § 5.403
- 24 C.F.R. § 5.609
- PIH Notice 2013-04

Why this duty is being invoked:
- The packet argues that HACC spent time on broad income-review demands instead of applying the correct caregiver / live-in-aide framework.

## 8. FHA Anti-Interference / Anti-Retaliation Duty

Duty: Neither HACC nor Quantum may coerce, intimidate, threaten, interfere with, or retaliate against the household for pursuing housing rights, accommodations, or fair-housing complaints.

Sources:
- 42 U.S.C. § 3617
- 24 C.F.R. § 100.400

Why this duty is being invoked:
- The packet asks the court to preserve the housing path while discrimination, accommodation, and rights-invocation issues remain unresolved.

## 9. Section 1981 Contracting Equality Duty

Duty: Quantum may not impair the household's housing-related contracting opportunity on unequal racial terms.

Sources:
- 42 U.S.C. § 1981

Why this duty is being invoked:
- The packet includes the February 2, 2026 comparator email and related race-discrimination allegations concerning the Blossom application path.

## 10. Undertaken Intake-Routing And Application-Processing Obligation

Duty: HACC may not direct the household into a specific replacement-housing intake path and then treat the resulting routing failure as the household's default.

Sources:
- HACC's January 8, 2026 Blossom / Tenant Protection Voucher notice
- HACC's later written acknowledgment that the packet had been submitted to Quantum staff at the Hillside Manor office but had not been provided to HACC

Why this duty is being invoked:
- This is an undertaking-based duty grounded in HACC's own written directions and later acknowledgment, not a free-floating assertion about how the process should have worked.
