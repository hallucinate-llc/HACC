# Probate Primary-Source Record Ladder

This note is a compact primary-source ladder for the probate objection branch.

It is designed to keep the probate filings grounded in the underlying petition, writ, declaration, and communication sequence rather than in broad characterization alone.

## Core Record Sequence

1. **March 31, 2026 guardianship petition**
   - The petition seeks temporary and indefinite guardianship, waiver of notice, and broad authority over Jane Kay Cortez's person, residence, and related affairs.
   - Main use:
     - anchors the breadth of the requested authority
     - shows the petition's own framing of emergency and incapacity

2. **Probate-side writ of assistance**
   - The writ directed law enforcement to accompany Solomon Barber, enter Jane Kay Cortez's residence forcibly if necessary, locate her, and transport her to Providence Milwaukie or another nearby hospital.
   - Main use:
     - anchors the coercive-removal / forced-transport concern
     - supports the demand for a clean predicate order and notice basis

3. **Jane Kay Cortez April 8, 2026 declaration**
   - Jane states in substance that police came to pick her up and take her to the doctor, that she was not allowed to take her purse or ID, that Solomon was trying to take her away without her approval, and that housing had kept the household stuck.
   - Main use:
     - provides first-person evidence against a one-sided emergency narrative
     - supports the narrower claim that coercive relief should not proceed on an incomplete record

4. **Benjamin Barber declaration**
   - Benjamin's declaration provides the household-history, protective-order, writ-sequence, and housing-overlap context.
   - Main use:
     - supports the claim that the emergency narrative is materially incomplete
     - supports the request to fence off collateral housing merits from probate

5. **November 17, 2025 Solomon trust text**
   - Solomon's preserved text states in substance that Jane's disability trust was between her and the estate, that Benjamin had no legal claim to details, and that Solomon was trying to get her rep payee and the trust in contact.
   - Main use:
     - supports the concern that trust / authority issues were active but not fully disclosed
     - supports the demand that actual trust and authority papers be produced

6. **Gerald Miller probate / will / trust packet**
   - The current record includes a Gerald Miller estate, will, and revocable-trust structure involving Jane Kay Cortez as an heir or devisee.
   - Main use:
     - confirms that trust / inheritance issues were real, not speculative
     - strengthens the point that vague references are not enough and the actual papers should be produced

7. **Restraining-order exhibit sequence**
   - The preserved earlier exhibit sequence appears to show Jane saying she signed papers with Sam / Solomon that she did not understand, including a paper she thought had something to do with her dad's will.
   - Main use:
     - supports caution and production
     - does not by itself prove final legal invalidity, and should remain proof-gated

## Best Narrow Inferences Supported

- The petition sought unusually broad authority on a materially disputed record.
- The writ-based coercive-relief branch requires a clean predicate order and notice basis before similar relief is considered again.
- Trust / inheritance / authority issues were active enough that the actual papers should be produced rather than vaguely referenced.
- Probate should not become the merits forum for collateral housing disputes except to the extent narrowly necessary.

## Best Current Uses

- `probate objection`: threshold scope objection, overbreadth objection, writ-predicate objection, fee objection
- `hearing use`: quick anchor for what the underlying record actually shows
- `trust-paper point`: production-and-caution branch, not final merits accusation

## Use Caution

- This note does **not** itself prove final trust-paper invalidity.
- This note does **not** itself prove sanctions should already issue.
- This note does **not** itself resolve all disputed historical details surrounding the writ or transport sequence.

It is best used as a primary-source ladder supporting narrower, proof-gated probate objections.
