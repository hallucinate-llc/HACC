# Two-Minute Hearing Script

Your Honor, this should not be treated as a simple holdover case.

Section 18 and HUD's relocation rule imposed relocation duties on HACC once the Hillside Park displacement process was triggered, including comparable housing, relocation support, counseling, and no completion of displacement until actual relocation occurred. HACC's own January 8, 2026 notice then required our household to proceed through the Blossom-or-TPV replacement-housing path. HACC's own Blossom page says applications for Blossom and Community Apartments will be processed through HACC's PBV waitlist. We followed that path and submitted the application packet, but HACC later acknowledged in writing that the packet had been submitted to Quantum staff and had not been provided to HACC.

HACC then confirmed in writing on March 26, 2026 that there were three relocation options and that all of them required completion by April 30, 2026. But those paths were still unresolved. The Waterleaf application existed by December 23, 2025. HACC knew by January 12 that Waterleaf wanted the voucher amount and that the application had been signed and turned in. HACC acknowledged by January 26 that we had chosen to port to Multnomah County. Yet by late March Waterleaf still had us waiting on voucher issuance. And even after the March 19 voucher marker, I submitted a Parkside Heights application on March 29 and Parkside replied on March 30 that the two-bedroom units would not be ready until the end of April. So that alternate path still had not produced a completed move or a signable lease.

A substantial amount, and likely most, of the documented delay from January 8 through late March was caused by HACC's own administration: the TPV process had not yet started, the intake packet had not reached HACC through the route HACC set up, HACC kept demanding more documents, and HACC reversed the voucher on March 19. Those were duties tied to identified sources: Section 18 relocation duties, VAWA transfer duties, hearing duties under 24 C.F.R. Section 982.555, and accommodation duties under 42 U.S.C. Section 3604(f)(3)(B).

At the same time, the accommodation process was still unresolved. The provider verification was completed in March, but HACC still denied the two-bedroom increase and the housing path remained unstable.

So the core problem is simple: HACC is asking for possession while the very housing paths it directed us to use were still unfinished. Under Section 18 and HACC's own written relocation process, HACC had to carry forward the triggered relocation and transfer steps before treating displacement as complete, and it should not treat the noncompletion of those same unresolved paths, without actual relocation and without a signable lease, as an eviction default. That is the narrower encoded point preserved in [housing_duties_fact_law_encoding_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/housing_duties_fact_law_encoding_2026-04-09.md).

Our request is narrow. The Court should deny possession as premature, or at minimum stay or abate it, require a written status update within 7 days, and preserve the application, voucher, transfer, and relocation path while the case is resolved.
