# Two-Minute Hearing Script

Your Honor, this should not be treated as a simple holdover or ordinary noncompliance case.

HACC triggered redevelopment-related relocation duties through the Hillside Park process. Those duties come from Section 18 and HUD's relocation rule, 42 U.S.C. Section 1437p and 24 C.F.R. Section 970.21. HACC's own plan then says Hillside Manor is a RAD PBV project, Quantum is the property manager, and the owner must provide an opportunity for an informal hearing before eviction. HACC's own Blossom page says applications for Blossom and Community Apartments will be processed through HACC's PBV waitlist. Then HACC told our household to use that replacement-housing path, including the Blossom and Hillside Manor intake route. We followed that route and submitted the application packet, but HACC later admitted in writing that the packet had been submitted to Quantum staff and had not been provided to HACC.

HACC also told us, in the alternative, to port the voucher to Multnomah County. But that route was not actually completed either. The Waterleaf application existed by December 23, 2025. HACC knew by January 12 that Waterleaf wanted the voucher amount and that the application had been signed and turned in. HACC acknowledged by January 26 that we had chosen to port to Multnomah County. Yet by late March Waterleaf still had us waiting on voucher issuance. So that alternate path stretched across four calendar months and still was not complete. Once HACC was administering that safety-related transfer path, VAWA required it to use its emergency transfer process under 24 C.F.R. Section 5.2009.

A substantial amount, and likely most, of the documented delay from January 8 through late March was caused by HACC's own administration: the TPV process had not yet started, the intake packet had not reached HACC through the route HACC set up, HACC kept demanding more documents, and HACC reversed the voucher on March 19. Those were not discretionary favors. They were duties tied to specific sources: Section 18 relocation duties, VAWA transfer duties, hearing duties under 24 C.F.R. Section 982.555, and accommodation duties under 42 U.S.C. Section 3604(f)(3)(B).

At the same time, the accommodation process was still unresolved. The provider verification was completed in March, but HACC still denied the two-bedroom increase and the housing path remained unstable.

So the core problem is simple: HACC is asking for possession while the very housing paths it directed us to use were still unfinished. Under Section 18 and the relocation-process commitments HACC itself adopted and undertook, that means HACC was barred from filing eviction while those duties remained materially incomplete.

Our request is narrow. The Court should deny possession as premature, or at minimum stay or abate it, require a written status update within 7 days, and preserve the application, voucher, transfer, and relocation path while the case is resolved.
