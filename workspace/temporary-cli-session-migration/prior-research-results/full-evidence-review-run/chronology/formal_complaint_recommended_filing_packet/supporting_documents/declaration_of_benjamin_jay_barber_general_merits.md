I, Benjamin Jay Barber, declare as follows:

1. I am a plaintiff in this action. I make this declaration from personal knowledge, and if called as a witness I could and would testify competently to the matters stated here.
2. I reside at 10043 SE 32nd Ave, Milwaukie, OR 97222.
3. Jane Kay Cortez and Julio Regal Florez-Cortez are members of the same household affected by the facts described below.
4. Our household has been dealing with displacement, voucher delays, accommodation problems, and hearing-access problems connected to the Housing Authority of Clackamas County and the Blossom relocation process.
5. HACC issued notices in late 2025 and early 2026 that put our housing stability at risk, including the December 23, 2025 no-cause notices, the December 31, 2025 displacement notice, the February 4, 2026 for-cause notice, and the February 13, 2026 notice directed to Julio Regal Florez-Cortez. Exhibits C, D, E, H, and N.
6. Instead of lawfully handling the requested bifurcation process involving Julio Regal Florez-Cortez, HACC effectively removed or excluded him from the tenancy and treated him as evicted. Exhibit N is consistent with that treatment.
7. Julio Regal Florez-Cortez cannot write in any language, to my personal knowledge.
8. About one week after the February 13, 2026 termination notice, Julio Regal Florez-Cortez requested a hearing orally by calling Ashley Ferron, to my personal knowledge.
9. HACC told Julio Regal Florez-Cortez that he could request a hearing in writing.
10. HACC did not provide Julio Regal Florez-Cortez a hearing after his oral request.
11. HACC also did not provide an oral, assisted, interpreter-supported, or otherwise accessible way for Julio Regal Florez-Cortez to invoke his hearing rights.
12. Because HACC required a written request that Julio Regal Florez-Cortez could not make, the hearing process was not meaningfully available to him.
13. On January 8, 2026, HACC sent a Blossom-related notice stating that the Tenant Protection Voucher process had not yet started and identifying Ashley Ferron as the contact to begin that process. Exhibit G.
14. By that time, our household had already been seeking relocation and voucher assistance, but the earliest direct documentary anchor I have identified for the Tenant Protection Voucher timeline is the January 8, 2026 notice. Exhibit G.
15. The preserved March 17 to March 20, 2026 email chain reflects that HACC and related housing staff were still coordinating orientation and voucher-processing steps during that period. Exhibits G and M.
16. During the same period, our household sought transfer or placement at Blossom Apartments.
17. A paper application for Blossom was submitted in December 2025.
18. In preserved Gmail sent-mail records dated February 26, March 2, and March 9, 2026, I complained in writing that Blossom had refused to process the application, had refused to house a service animal, and had engaged in discrimination. Those preserved emails were sent to or copied Kati Tilton, Ashley Ferron, and `charity@magikcorp.com`. Exhibit R, with broader thread corroboration in Exhibit J including the preserved complaint chain reflected in J-12, J-13, and J-16.
19. On February 9, 2026, HACC issued a written additional-information demand requiring extensive tax, bank, asset, and business documentation. Exhibit I.
20. That documentation sequence continued through later emails even after materials had already been provided. Exhibit J, including J-1 and J-5.
21. In the preserved email thread, I also stated that HACC and related parties were demanding records for entities that had not existed for ten years. Exhibit J, including J-13.
22. In my experience, those requests delayed relocation and voucher progress rather than resolving them.
23. On or about March 9, 2026, I wrote that the available unit did not accommodate a mobility scooter or a mobility-impaired person, that a one-bedroom unit was not large enough, that no two-bedrooms were available, and that there was still no Tenant Protection Voucher. Exhibit J, including J-16.
24. Our household sought a two-bedroom accommodation because of mobility, cognitive, caregiving, privacy, work-from-home, scooter, and service-animal needs that made a one-bedroom arrangement inadequate.
25. HACC first sent materials reflecting a two-bedroom voucher, then stated on March 19, 2026 that the two-bedroom issuance had been created and sent in error and that a one-bedroom voucher would instead issue. Exhibit M, including M-8, M-10, M-11, and M-12.
26. I responded that I had a scheduled appointment to obtain a reasonable accommodation for a two-bedroom unit. Exhibit M, including M-10 and M-12.
27. HACC later stated that the voucher could be reissued as a two-bedroom if the accommodation were approved. Exhibit M, including M-11 and M-12.
28. Even then, the accommodation and housing process remained delayed and uncertain.
29. I also complained about discrimination and communicated an intent to sue under the Civil Rights Act. The preserved February 26, 2026 message states that I had previously indicated to Quantum Residential and HACC that I intended to sue under the Civil Rights Act. In the same preserved email chain, I stated that defendants were demanding that I share a one bedroom apartment and were providing unsuitable conditions instead of allowing the household to relocate. Exhibit J, including J-12, J-13, and J-16.
30. Based on what was said to me, and in light of the preserved February 26 through March 9 complaint sequence followed by continued notice and housing escalation, I believe HACC personnel linked threatened lawsuits or complaints to the issuance of a 30-day notice. At present, that narrower retaliation-attribution point is based on my recollection and the intake history rather than a preserved representative message.
31. Without immediate and effective relief, I believe our household faces continuing risk of eviction, displacement, loss of relocation opportunity, loss or impairment of voucher benefits, and continuing denial of an accessible hearing for Julio Regal Florez-Cortez.
32. Loss of housing or relocation opportunity would cause immediate and severe harm to the household.
33. If Julio Regal Florez-Cortez is denied a meaningful hearing because he cannot comply with a writing requirement, that denial cannot be fully repaired after the fact.
34. I have also lost work time addressing these issues, and the housing instability has caused stress and emotional harm within the household. The underlying dispute and housing-access conflict are documented in the exhibits, but the damages details will need to be supported by further declaration or records.
