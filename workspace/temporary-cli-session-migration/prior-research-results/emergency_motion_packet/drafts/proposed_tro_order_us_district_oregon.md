# Proposed Temporary Restraining Order

IN THE UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
FOR THE DISTRICT OF OREGON

Benjamin Barber, Jane Cortez, and Julio Cortez,
Plaintiffs,

v.

Housing Authority of Clackamas County; Quantum Residential; and Does 1-10,
Defendants.

Upon consideration of Plaintiffs' Emergency Motion for Temporary Restraining Order and Preliminary Injunction, the complaint, and the record before the Court, the Court finds that temporary relief is warranted to preserve the status quo and prevent irreparable harm pending further proceedings.

IT IS HEREBY ORDERED that, until further order of the Court:

1. Defendants Housing Authority of Clackamas County, Quantum Residential, and their officers, agents, employees, attorneys, and all persons acting in concert with them are restrained from evicting, removing, locking out, excluding, or otherwise displacing Plaintiffs Benjamin Barber, Jane Cortez, or Julio Cortez from the housing at issue based on the challenged notices, lease-administration sequence, or related conduct described in the complaint.
2. Defendants are restrained from terminating, canceling, impairing, withholding, or rendering unavailable Plaintiffs' Tenant Protection Voucher, Housing Choice Voucher, transfer opportunity, relocation benefits, or requested accommodation status based on the challenged conduct described in the complaint.
3. Defendant Housing Authority of Clackamas County shall, within ___ days of entry of this Order, provide Plaintiff Julio Cortez a meaningful and accessible method to request a hearing that does not require him to submit a written request and that includes an oral, assisted, or otherwise accessible alternative.
4. Defendant Housing Authority of Clackamas County shall, within ___ days of entry of this Order, schedule and provide Julio Cortez a prompt hearing or other process required by federal law concerning the challenged housing, displacement, tenancy, or voucher-related decisions.
5. Defendants are restrained from retaliating against Plaintiffs for requesting accommodations, requesting a hearing, complaining of housing discrimination, contacting counsel or county officials, or filing this action.
6. Defendants shall preserve all records, emails, notes, hearing records, voucher records, application records, and relocation records relevant to the allegations in the complaint.
7. Plaintiffs' security under Rule 65(c) is waived, or alternatively set in the nominal amount of $____.

IT IS FURTHER ORDERED that Defendants shall appear and show cause on _____________, 2026, at ________ _.m., why a preliminary injunction should not issue.
