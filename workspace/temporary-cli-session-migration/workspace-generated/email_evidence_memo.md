## High-Value Excerpts

### EMAIL-01 / EMAIL-02: February 26 protected complaint and escalation thread

The exported message states that Benjamin Barber had already indicated an intent to sue under the Civil Rights Act, had stated an intention to leave Clackamas County, and alleged that Blossom had refused to process applications for two months, had refused to house a service animal, and had engaged in race discrimination. The same message also alleges intentional delay through demands for financial documents tied to companies that had not existed for years and through pressure to accept a one-bedroom arrangement.

The preserved February 25 chain inside the same exported message also records Kati Tilton's request for proof of 2025 tax filings for multiple businesses, crypto statements, all bank statements, winnings information, and other asset verification. That chain materially strengthens the complaint's allegation that documentation demands escalated after protected complaints and impeded relocation.

### EMAIL-03: March 19 voucher reversal

The March 19, 2026 message from Kati Tilton states: "The subsidy worksheet and voucher issuance for a 2bdrm was created and sent in error. The attached are for a one-bedroom voucher that will be issued today." This is the cleanest currently located documentary support for the allegation that the household was shifted from an expected two-bedroom voucher to a one-bedroom voucher.

### EMAIL-04: March 20 accommodation-conditioned reissuance

The March 20, 2026 message from Kati Tilton states: "The voucher can be reissued as a two-bedroom if you are approved for a reasonable accommodation." The same exported message preserves Benjamin Barber's same-day response that he had attached the scheduled appointment to obtain a reasonable accommodation for a two-bedroom unit. Together, those statements support the allegation that the two-bedroom voucher issue was tied to the accommodation process and that the accommodation request had already been actively pursued.
