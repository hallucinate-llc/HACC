# Section 18 Source Review: Statute, Regulation, and Contracts on File

This review separates:

1. what Section 18 of the Housing Act directly requires as a matter of federal law;
2. what HACC and Quantum-related documents in the local record show about the replacement-housing and PBV/RAD process; and
3. what is still missing from the file.

For clarity, the Section 18 issue in this case is the federal demolition/disposition and relocation framework rooted in Section 18 of the United States Housing Act, codified at `42 U.S.C. § 1437p`, together with HUD's implementing regulations and the PBV/RAD documents HACC relied on.

## 1. Federal Source Text Actually Reviewed

### A. `42 U.S.C. § 1437p`

Official text reviewed at:

- https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?edition=prelim&num=0&req=granuleid%3AUSC-prelim-title42-section1437p

The most important direct statutory points for this case are:

- Section 18 is the federal statute governing demolition and disposition of public housing.
- It expressly contemplates `relocation and replacement housing` for units removed from the public housing inventory.
- It authorizes HUD to use tenant-based assistance amounts for `relocation and replacement housing` for demolished or disposed units. See `42 U.S.C. § 1437p(h)`.

The statute, standing alone, does not spell out every operational step in the same detail as the regulations or HUD guidance. But it is the statutory foundation for the relocation and replacement obligations that HACC invoked in the Hillside redevelopment setting.

### B. `24 C.F.R. § 970.21`

Official text reviewed at:

- https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/subtitle-B/chapter-IX/part-970/section-970.21

This regulation is the clearest federal relocation source for the case. In substance, it requires that residents displaced by approved demolition/disposition receive:

- comparable housing;
- actual relocation assistance;
- counseling;
- payment of reasonable moving expenses; and
- relocation in a manner tied to the demolition/disposition approval process.

This is the strongest federal source for the proposition that relocation is not complete merely because the PHA wants possession back. The agency must actually carry out the relocation obligations that attach to the displacement.

### C. HUD Section 18 guidance reviewed

Official HUD guidance reviewed at:

- https://www.hud.gov/sites/dfiles/PIH/documents/Section%2018%20Overview%20.pdf

That HUD guidance is not the statute itself, but it is a primary agency source that helps explain HUD's own understanding of Section 18 relocation, replacement housing, and approval structure.

## 3. What These Sources Establish, Taken Together

Based on the source text actually reviewed, the best-supported conclusions are:

### A. Federal law imposed real relocation duties on HACC

The strongest source chain is:

- `42 U.S.C. § 1437p`
- `24 C.F.R. § 970.21`
- HUD Section 18 guidance

Together, those sources support the conclusion that once the Hillside redevelopment/displacement path was underway, HACC had actual relocation obligations, including comparable housing, counseling, relocation assistance, and moving-expense support. Those are not merely optional kindnesses.

### B. HACC's own documents added operational commitments

The strongest local source chain is:

- HACC Administrative Plan
- Blossom lease-up page
- January 8, 2026 notice
- HACC email admitting the packet-routing failure

Together, those documents support the conclusion that HACC did not merely owe abstract federal duties. It also undertook a specific local process:

- a PBV waitlist-based Blossom path;
- a TPV alternative path;
- project-specific Hillside Manor PBV/RAD administration;
- hearing rights before eviction in the RAD/PBV context; and
- an intake workflow that involved Quantum staff and HACC processing.

### C. Quantum's role is supported by HACC's own records

The current record does not yet contain the standalone signed management agreement, but HACC's own documents identify:

- `Quantum Residential Property Management` as the property management company for Hillside Manor; and
- Quantum staff at the Hillside Manor office as the place where the completed intake packet had been submitted.

That is enough to support a serious theory that Quantum was part of the replacement-housing intake and forwarding chain HACC was using.

## 4. What Is Still Missing

The biggest documentary gaps are:

- the signed `HAP contract`;
- the signed `property management agreement`;
- any signed `ground lease` or `RAD Use Agreement`;
- the complete `RCC` or other HUD conversion-closing documents for Hillside Manor; and
- a cleaner packet trail showing exactly who at Quantum received the application and what happened next.

Those missing documents matter, because they would let us move from:

- `HACC plan and notice evidence of duties and roles`

to:

- `the exact executed contract language binding the parties`.

But the current file is still enough to do a serious legal analysis, because the Administrative Plan and HACC notices already contain admissions about:

- the existence of the HAP contracts;
- the RAD/PBV status of the project;
- the waiting-list and hearing structure;
- the replacement-housing priority structure; and
- Quantum's property-management role.

## 5. Bottom Line

After reviewing the actual statute, regulation, and local file materials, the most defensible position is:

- HACC's obligations were not just policy preferences. They were grounded in Section 18 relocation law and HUD's implementing regulation.
- HACC's own Administrative Plan and notices then converted those federal duties into a concrete local relocation and replacement-housing process.
- Blossom and Hillside Manor were not being presented as ordinary private-market opportunities. They were presented as part of a HACC-administered PBV/RAD replacement-housing path tied to the Hillside redevelopment.
- HACC's own records place Quantum inside that path as the property manager and intake recipient.
- The strongest remaining gap is not whether the process existed, but the absence of the actual signed contracts and full internal routing records.
