# Judge Pushback Answers Sheet

Use this sheet for short answers to likely questions or objections at hearing.

## Housing Pushback

### 1. `Where exactly does Section 18 say I cannot grant possession?`

Short answer:

`I am not asking the Court to treat Section 18 as a freestanding anti-eviction statute. I am asking the Court to withhold possession as premature because HACC triggered relocation duties, kept this household inside an unresolved relocation process, and should not treat displacement as complete while that process remained materially unfinished.`

### 2. `Isn't this just a normal tenant-default case?`

Short answer:

`No. HACC's own papers show it directed the household into specific relocation paths, later acknowledged a packet-routing failure in that same path, and still described three unresolved relocation options as late as March 26. That is not an ordinary holdover record.`

### 3. `How do I know you actually did your side?`

Short answer:

`The current record shows continued participation: packet submissions, the Waterleaf application path, voucher and portability follow-up, and the March 29 Parkside Heights application after the March 19 voucher marker. The stronger inference is not household abandonment, but unresolved HACC-directed administration.`

### 4. `Why is Quantum in this at all?`

Short answer:

`Not because Quantum bears every HACC duty, but because HACC's own records place Quantum inside the intake and routing chain. The narrow present theory is that if Quantum received or controlled the packet path, it had project-side duties not to lose, block, or discriminatorily mishandle it.`

### 5. `Are you saying federal law guaranteed you a completed move to Waterleaf?`

Short answer:

`No. The narrower point is lawful administration. Once the safety-related transfer path was opened, HACC had to administer it coherently, preserve confidentiality, and not let the path remain unresolved while eviction pressure continued.`

### 6. `Why should I rely on the race-discrimination point?`

Short answer:

`That is not the main pillar of the current request. I rely first on HACC's relocation duties, HACC's own written option structure, and the packet-routing failure. The race point is contextual and corroborative, not the necessary core of interim relief.`

## Probate Pushback

### 7. `Aren't you trying to litigate housing merits in probate?`

Short answer:

`No. I am asking the Court to fence off collateral housing merits except to the extent they are necessary to evaluate the emergency narrative, notice posture, scope of requested authority, and whether the petition is overstating Benjamin as the cause of the crisis.`

### 8. `Why should I care about the writ if it already issued?`

Short answer:

`Because coercive transport or removal relief should rest on a clean predicate order and notice basis. The current record includes the writ, but not yet the full signed predicate order and notice record that would justify such extraordinary relief.`

### 9. `Do you actually have proof of alternative protective arrangements?`

Short answer:

`My present point is narrower. On this record, the petition does not justify immediate plenary and placement authority because narrower alternatives, existing court-order history, and unresolved factual disputes were not fairly accounted for before Solomon requested sweeping control.`

### 10. `Are you proving the trust papers are invalid?`

Short answer:

`Not yet. The present use of that issue is to show that trust, inheritance, and authority papers were referenced but not produced, while broad financial-control authority was requested anyway. That supports production and caution, not a final merits ruling on the papers' validity.`

### 11. `Why shouldn't fees come from Jane's funds if the petition was protective?`

Short answer:

`Because on this record the petition seeks disputed coercive relief, writ-based transport, overbroad placement and plenary authority, and other contested measures whose benefit to Jane is not clearly established. The present showing does not justify shifting those fees to Jane's funds.`

## Best Reminder

- Keep the answer narrow.
- Return to the strongest record anchors.
- Do not overclaim beyond the current documents.
