# Title 18 And Contract Source-Linked Duty Matrix

This matrix is limited to duties supported by the source texts actually reviewed:

- `42 U.S.C. § 1437p`
- `24 C.F.R. § 970.21`
- HUD's Section 18 guidance
- HACC's July 1, 2025 Administrative Plan
- HACC's Blossom lease-up page
- HACC's January 8, 2026 Blossom / TPV notice
- HACC's later email admitting the packet-routing failure

It is designed to distinguish:

- duties imposed directly by federal law;
- duties reflected in HACC's own program documents; and
- undertaking-based duties shown by HACC's notices and emails.

## 1. Statutory Relocation Duty

Duty:
HACC had a federal duty to provide relocation and replacement-housing assistance in connection with the Hillside demolition/disposition process.

Source:
- `42 U.S.C. § 1437p(h)`

Why this is directly source-linked:
- The statute itself authorizes HUD tenant-based assistance funds for `relocation and replacement housing` for units demolished or disposed from the public housing inventory.

## 2. Regulatory Comparable-Housing / Counseling / Moving-Expense Duty

Duty:
HACC had to carry out actual relocation steps, including comparable housing, counseling, and moving-expense support, as part of the displacement process.

Source:
- `24 C.F.R. § 970.21`

Why this is directly source-linked:
- This is the clearest implementing regulation for Section 18 relocation obligations.

## 3. No-Treating-Relocation-As-Complete-Before-Completion Duty

Duty:
HACC could not lawfully treat displacement as complete while the required relocation components remained materially unfinished.

Source:
- `24 C.F.R. § 970.21`
- HUD Section 18 Overview PDF

Why this is directly source-linked:
- The regulation makes relocation performance part of the approved demolition/disposition process, not an optional afterthought.

## 4. Project-Specific PBV/RAD Waiting-List Administration Duty

Duty:
HACC had to administer the Hillside Manor replacement-housing opportunity through the PBV/RAD waiting-list structure it adopted in its Administrative Plan.

Sources:
- HACC Administrative Plan, `18-V.D. Organization of the Waiting List`
- HACC Administrative Plan, Exhibit 18-1

Why this is directly source-linked:
- HACC's own plan says it will establish and manage separate waiting lists for individual RAD PBV projects and identifies `Hillside Manor`.

## 5. No-Rescreening / Right-to-Return Protection For Existing Relocation Households

Duty:
HACC could not treat current relocation households as ordinary new-market applicants divorced from the conversion and right-to-return protections recognized in its own plan.

Sources:
- HACC Administrative Plan, `18-V.B. Prohibited Rescreening of Existing Tenants Upon Conversion`
- HUD Notice PIH 2019-23, as incorporated in that plan text

Why this is directly source-linked:
- HACC's own plan says current households cannot be excluded based on rescreening upon conversion and that right-to-return protections extend to legacy non-RAD PBV units in the same project.

## 6. Owner / Project-Side HAP Compliance Duty

Duty:
The project-side owner or manager participating in the PBV/RAD path had to lease contract units only through the HACC-referred or authorized waiting-list process and in compliance with the HAP structure.

Source:
- HACC Administrative Plan, `18-IV.E. Owner Responsibilities under the HAP Contract [24 CFR 983.210]`

Why this is directly source-linked:
- HACC's plan states the owner certifies that each contract unit receiving HAP is leased to an eligible family referred by HACC or selected from the owner-maintained waiting list and in accordance with HUD requirements.

## 7. Hearing-Before-Eviction Duty In The RAD/PBV Context

Duty:
Before eviction in the RAD/PBV context, the owner had to provide an opportunity for an informal hearing, and HACC had hearing responsibility for additional RAD hearings.

Source:
- HACC Administrative Plan, `18-VI.H. Informal Reviews and Hearings`

Why this is directly source-linked:
- The plan expressly says: `The owner must provide an opportunity for an informal hearing before an eviction.`

## 8. Blossom Replacement-Housing Processing Duty

Duty:
The Blossom replacement-housing path had to be processed through HACC's PBV waitlist rather than left as an untracked side process.

Source:
- [blossom-community-apartments](/home/barberb/HACC/blossom-community-apartments)

Why this is directly source-linked:
- HACC's public page states: `Applications for Blossom & Community Apartments will be processed through HACC's PBV waitlist.`

## 9. Priority-To-Relocation-Households Duty

Duty:
HACC had to administer Blossom lease-up with explicit priority for Hillside relocation households and other HACC households facing relocation due to repositioning.

Source:
- [blossom-community-apartments](/home/barberb/HACC/blossom-community-apartments)

Why this is directly source-linked:
- The page expressly identifies Phase 1 relocated residents, Phase 2 residents facing relocation, and HACC public-housing residents facing relocation due to repositioning as priority groups.

## 10. Undertaken Dual-Track Relocation Processing Duty

Duty:
Once HACC directed the household to choose between Blossom and a Tenant Protection Voucher, HACC had an undertaken duty to actually administer at least one of those tracks coherently.

Source:
- [HACC Jan 2026 blossom.pdf](/home/barberb/HACC/evidence/paper%20documents/HACC%20Jan%202026%20blossom.pdf)

Why this is directly source-linked:
- HACC's January 8, 2026 notice told the household it had to choose between:
  - moving forward with Blossom; or
  - using a Tenant Protection Voucher.

## 11. Undertaken Intake-Routing Duty

Duty:
HACC could not direct the household into the Hillside Manor / Blossom intake route and then treat the resulting routing failure as the household's own default.

Sources:
- [HACC Jan 2026 blossom.pdf](/home/barberb/HACC/evidence/paper%20documents/HACC%20Jan%202026%20blossom.pdf)
- [message.eml](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/manual-imap-download-2026-03-31/20260202-164227_Re-Allegations-of-Fraud---JC-Household/message.eml)

Why this is directly source-linked:
- HACC's records show both:
  - that the household was directed into the Blossom / TPV process; and
  - that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum staff at Hillside Manor but had not been provided to HACC.

## 12. Packet-Preservation / Forwarding / Review Duty

Duty:
Once HACC knew a previously submitted intake packet existed and had been left with Quantum staff, HACC had a process duty to resolve that intake failure rather than convert it into grounds for treating the housing path as abandoned.

Source:
- [message.eml](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/manual-imap-download-2026-03-31/20260202-164227_Re-Allegations-of-Fraud---JC-Household/message.eml)

Why this is directly source-linked:
- In that thread HACC states the household may ask Quantum staff to send the previously submitted packet directly to HACC or complete a new intake packet, which confirms the packet-routing failure was known and concrete.

## 13. Quantum Role As A Documented Project-Side Participant

Duty implication:
Quantum's role is supported as part of the project-side administration and intake chain, even though the actual standalone management agreement is not presently in the repository.

Sources:
- HACC Administrative Plan, Exhibit 18-1 (`Property Management Company: Quantum Residential Property Management`)
- [message.eml](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/manual-imap-download-2026-03-31/20260202-164227_Re-Allegations-of-Fraud---JC-Household/message.eml)

Why this matters:
- This is not yet the same thing as having the signed management contract text.
- But it is enough to ground a strong factual and procedural theory that Quantum was inside the intake-and-replacement-housing chain HACC was using.

## 14. Contract-Record Limitation

Important limitation:
The current record appears to show the existence of:

- a `06/01/2020 HAP contract`,
- a `01/01/2021 RAD HAP contract`,
- and a Quantum management role,

but not the full executed contract texts themselves.

Source:
- HACC Administrative Plan, Exhibit 18-1

Why this matters:
- Where the analysis speaks in contract terms, the strongest current basis is:
  - HACC's own plan-level description of the contracts,
  - HACC's public and direct notices,
  - and HACC's undertaken processing directions.
- The analysis becomes even stronger if the actual HAP, management, ground-lease, or RAD-use documents are later found.

## 15. Deontic Summary

The best-supported deontic formulation from the presently reviewed sources is:

- `O(HACC, perform Section 18 relocation steps required by law)`
- `O(HACC, administer the PBV/RAD replacement-housing path it adopted in its own plan)`
- `O(HACC, administer the Blossom-or-TPV choice it directed the household to make)`
- `O(HACC, provide hearing opportunity before eviction in the RAD/PBV setting)`
- `O(HACC/Quantum process chain, preserve and forward the intake packet through the documented route)`
- `not completed(relocation and undertaken processing duties) -> F(HACC, treat household as ordinary eviction default)`

That last step is not being asserted as a free-floating moral judgment. It is the combined consequence of the federal relocation sources plus HACC's own documented replacement-housing process.
