# Plaintiffs' Motion for Preliminary Injunction Against HACC and Quantum

## Requested Relief

Plaintiffs move for a preliminary injunction against defendants `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` (`HACC`) and `Quantum Residential` (`Quantum`). Plaintiffs seek narrow interim relief tied to clear federal housing duties, Section 18 relocation duties, and relocation-processing commitments that are not matters of discretion. If the housing-duty appendix is attached, the compact duty grid is `Exhibit C-1` and the narrative law-first duty explanation is `Exhibit C-3`, as set out in [129_housing_duty_appendix_exhibit_plan_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/drafts/final_filing_set/129_housing_duty_appendix_exhibit_plan_2026-04-09.md). See also [housing_duty_appendix_table_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/drafts/housing_duty_appendix_table_2026-04-09.md); [law_first_hacc_quantum_duty_memo_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/law_first_hacc_quantum_duty_memo_2026-04-09.md); [housing_duties_fact_law_encoding_2026-04-09.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/housing_duties_fact_law_encoding_2026-04-09.md):

1. HACC and Quantum shall not coerce, intimidate, threaten, retaliate against, or otherwise interfere with plaintiffs' exercise of rights protected by the Fair Housing Act, including complaints about discrimination, disability accommodation, service-animal use, and voucher-backed housing access.
2. HACC and Quantum shall not refuse, discard, ignore, or selectively burden plaintiffs' housing application, transfer path, voucher-backed housing opportunity, disability accommodation request, or service-animal-related housing request on discriminatory or retaliatory grounds.
3. HACC shall not terminate assistance, cause eviction, cause lockout, extinguish tenancy status, or otherwise take materially adverse housing action against plaintiffs while the transfer, voucher, and hearing paths at issue in this case remain unlawfully unresolved.
4. HACC shall provide a written status statement identifying the current status of plaintiffs' voucher, portability request, safety-based transfer path, and any hearing or review path, together with the concrete next steps and deadlines then being applied.
5. HACC shall administer plaintiffs' external transfer or port-out route in a manner consistent with VAWA confidentiality and emergency-transfer requirements, and shall provide clear status, next-step, and review information while that route remains active and unresolved.
6. HACC shall not treat plaintiffs' relocation or displacement as complete unless and until plaintiffs have actually been provided a lawful comparable replacement-housing path consistent with Section 18 relocation requirements and the relocation commitments HACC itself triggered.
7. HACC shall preserve and lawfully carry forward the concrete relocation steps it triggered for plaintiffs, including comparable-housing, voucher, portability, counseling, and relocation-follow-through administration, and shall not allow those steps to collapse or be treated as complete before actual relocation occurs.
8. HACC and Quantum shall preserve and not frustrate the relocation-processing chain they undertook for plaintiffs, including intake, forwarding, review, and status-tracking of plaintiffs' Blossom / Hillside Manor / Parkside Heights application materials and related replacement-housing paperwork.
9. Quantum shall preserve plaintiffs' Blossom / Parkside application materials and, to the extent Quantum received or controlled that intake path, handle those application materials under nondiscriminatory, nonretaliatory, and project-role-consistent standards.

## Legal Standard

A preliminary injunction is warranted where plaintiffs show likely success on the merits, likely irreparable harm absent relief, that the balance of equities tips in their favor, and that an injunction is in the public interest. [Winter v. NRDC, 555 U.S. 7, 20 (2008)](https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/07pdf/07-1239.pdf). In the Ninth Circuit, serious questions going to the merits may support relief where the balance of hardships tips sharply in plaintiffs' favor, so long as irreparable harm and public interest are also shown. [Alliance for the Wild Rockies v. Cottrell, 632 F.3d 1127 (9th Cir. 2011)](https://cdn.ca9.uscourts.gov/datastore/opinions/2011/02/25/09-35756.pdf). Because mandatory interim relief is disfavored, the requested order should be framed narrowly and should track clear legal duties rather than broad programmatic supervision. [Marlyn Nutraceuticals, Inc. v. Mucos Pharma GmbH, 571 F.3d 873, 879 (9th Cir. 2009)](https://cdn.ca9.uscourts.gov/datastore/opinions/2009/07/17/08-55331.pdf).

## Argument

### I. Plaintiffs are likely to succeed, or at minimum raise serious questions, on the joint Fair Housing Act claims against both HACC and Quantum.

The strongest joint merits theory is the Fair Housing Act's non-discretionary prohibition on interference, retaliation, and disability-based obstruction in housing access. Federal law makes it unlawful to coerce, intimidate, threaten, or interfere with a person because that person exercised or aided the exercise of fair-housing rights. [42 U.S.C. section 3617](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?edition=prelim&num=0&req=granuleid%3AUSC-prelim-title42-section3617); [24 C.F.R. section 100.400](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/subtitle-B/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-100/subpart-D/section-100.400). Federal law also requires housing providers to make reasonable accommodations in rules, policies, practices, or services when necessary to afford a person with a disability equal housing opportunity. [42 U.S.C. section 3604(f)(3)(B)](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title42-section3604&num=0&edition=prelim); [24 C.F.R. section 100.204](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/subtitle-B/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-100/subpart-B/section-100.204). And it is unlawful to discriminate in the terms, conditions, privileges, or services connected with a dwelling because of disability. [42 U.S.C. section 3604(f)(2)](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title42-section3604&num=0&edition=prelim).

The present record supports these claims against both defendants together. Plaintiffs repeatedly complained in writing that Blossom had refused to process the application, refused to house a service animal, and was engaging in discrimination on the basis of race while HACC and Quantum continued to delay the processes needed to leave HACC-controlled housing. [improved-complaint-from-temporary-session.cited.md](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/improved-complaint-from-temporary-session.cited.md#L61). On February 2, 2026, Benjamin Jay Barber emailed both Ashley Ferron and `blossom@quantumres.com` stating that he had met a Black couple with one child at Balfour Park who had just been granted Section 8 and were interviewing for Blossom, while plaintiffs' own Blossom application had not been sent onward to HACC. [message.eml](/home/barberb/HACC/evidence/email_imports/starworks5-fraud-reimport-20260404/0047-Re-Allegations-of-Fraud---JC-Household-CAMTdTS-00cFTv4dFf8yZ3NpPztCKwr2LB-on4Xxz-BFEv9_5jA-mail.gmail.com/message.eml). Plaintiffs also produced provider-backed disability and service-animal support by March 2026, yet the record still reflects continued barriers, one-bedroom pressure, and nonprocessing rather than a clean accommodation path. [improved-complaint-from-temporary-session.cited.md](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/improved-complaint-from-temporary-session.cited.md#L72); [improved-complaint-from-temporary-session.cited.md](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/improved-complaint-from-temporary-session.cited.md#L95).

This is enough, at minimum, to raise serious questions that both defendants were participating in an unlawful pattern of interference, discriminatory processing, and accommodation failure. The requested injunction does not ask the Court to decide the final merits of every leasing or voucher dispute immediately. It asks only that both defendants stop conduct that federal housing law unequivocally forbids while the merits are litigated.

### II. Plaintiffs are also likely to succeed, or at minimum raise serious questions, on the Section 18 relocation and contract-preservation theories.

Section 18 supplies another set of clear duties that strengthen interim relief. Federal law requires a public housing agency carrying out an approved demolition or disposition to give the required displacement notice, pay actual and reasonable relocation expenses, and ensure that each displaced resident is offered comparable housing. [42 U.S.C. section 1437p](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title42-section1437p&num=0&edition=prelim). HUD's implementing and explanatory materials likewise treat comparable housing, counseling, moving-expense assistance, and completed resident relocation as mandatory features of an approved Section 18 process, and HUD explains that demolition or disposition cannot be completed until residents are relocated. [24 C.F.R. section 970.21](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/subtitle-B/chapter-IX/part-970/section-970.21); [HUD Section 18 Overview](https://www.hud.gov/sites/dfiles/PIH/documents/Section%2018%20Overview%20.pdf); [HUD SAC Library](https://www.hud.gov/helping-americans/public-indian-housing-sac/library).

The present record supports the conclusion that HACC triggered that relocation framework, directed plaintiffs into a replacement-housing path, and then pursued displacement while relocation was still incomplete. The contract analysis already assembled in the record reflects a high-confidence finding that HACC likely breached the relocation commitment by leaving relocation incomplete and still pursuing eviction, and that Quantum likely breached the intake-processing commitment by receiving but not forwarding the household packet and by not completing the promised intake path. [contract_breach_report.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/outputs/contract_breach_report.md). That same analysis further concludes that HACC likely created at least an implied-in-fact relocation-process agreement through its notices, referral instructions, and conduct, and that Quantum likely created at least an implied-in-fact processing undertaking through its office intake role. [contract_breach_report.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/outputs/contract_breach_report.md).

These Section 18 and contract theories are especially well suited to status-quo relief. Plaintiffs are not asking the Court at this stage to award contract damages or to decide every final merits question. Plaintiffs ask only that HACC not complete displacement before lawful relocation is actually completed, and that HACC and Quantum not destroy or sabotage the very relocation and intake process they themselves put in motion. Where a defendant has undertaken an ongoing relocation process and the plaintiff faces imminent housing loss, preserving that process and preventing pre-completion displacement is narrower and more defensible than attempting to compel a specific final unit award.

### III. Interim relief is also needed to preserve the possibility of final equitable relief, including specific performance.

Plaintiffs do not contend that the words `specific performance` alone entitle them to a preliminary injunction. A preliminary injunction still must satisfy the federal interim-relief standards above. But the availability of final equitable relief matters in two important ways here.

First, Oregon specific-performance law supports the view that at least part of the relief sought at final judgment is equitable in nature. Oregon cases treat specific performance as appropriate where the obligation to be enforced is sufficiently clear and definite and where a legal remedy would not be practical, efficient, and complete. [Kazlauskas v. Emmert](https://law.justia.com/cases/oregon/court-of-appeals/2012/a141575.html); [County of Lincoln v. Fischer](https://law.justia.com/cases/oregon/supreme-court/1959/216-or-421-3.html); [Pittenger Equip. Co. v. Timber Struct., Inc.](https://law.justia.com/cases/oregon/supreme-court/1950/189-or-1-2.html). Here, plaintiffs do not ask the Court at this stage to order every promise fully performed in all respects. But the present record supports specific equitable obligations concrete enough to preserve now: do not complete displacement before relocation is complete, do not destroy the relocation-processing chain, do not discard the application materials, and do not defeat the transfer path through delay or obstruction.

Second, without interim relief, the final equitable remedy may be defeated before the Court can adjudicate the merits. If defendants are permitted to complete displacement, terminate the housing path, lose or disregard the application materials, or let the relocation chain collapse while litigation is pending, then any final order requiring lawful completion of relocation or lawful processing of the undertaken housing path will be substantially undermined. In that sense, the preliminary injunction is not a substitute for final specific performance. It is the means of preserving the subject matter so final equitable relief remains meaningful.

### IV. Plaintiffs are separately likely to succeed on HACC-specific duties that reinforce the need for interim relief.

HACC carries additional non-discretionary duties that strengthen the injunction record. In the voucher context, federal law requires lawful notice and hearing procedures before covered adverse actions. [42 U.S.C. section 1437d(k)](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?edition=prelim&num=0&req=granuleid%3AUSC-prelim-title42-section1437d); [24 C.F.R. section 982.555](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/part-982/section-982.555). HACC also had the clearer VAWA duty to maintain and use an emergency-transfer framework and to handle safety-based transfer requests with confidentiality and real administrative follow-through. [24 C.F.R. section 5.2009](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/title-24/subtitle-A/part-5/subpart-L/section-5.2009); [HUD Form 5380](https://www.hud.gov/sites/default/files/OCHCO/documents/5380.pdf); [HUD HCV Fair Housing Guidebook, Apr. 2025](https://www.hud.gov/sites/dfiles/PIH/documents/HCV_Guidebook-Chapter_Fair-Housing_April-2025.pdf).

The Waterleaf chronology supports the inference that HACC did not perform that safety-transfer function in a lawful and coherent way. By January 12, 2026, HACC was told that Waterleaf wanted the voucher amount and that the application had already been signed and turned in. By January 26, 2026, HACC acknowledged that plaintiffs had chosen to port to Multnomah County. Yet by March 25-26, 2026, Waterleaf still had plaintiffs only on a waiting list pending voucher issuance. [message.eml](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/manual-imap-download-2026-03-31/20260202-164227_Re-Allegations-of-Fraud---JC-Household/message.eml); [message.eml](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/temporary-cli-session-migration/prior-research-results/full-evidence-review-run/chronology/emergency_motion_packet/exhibits/Exhibit%20M%20-%20starworks5-ktilton-orientation-import/0008-Re-HCV-Orientation-CAMTdTS_hM81x2YFGZBGX3tGzBJjMjn5WhiFW-NwOC0k23-63LQ-mail.gmail.com/message.eml). Even if VAWA does not impose a short fixed numeric deadline for out-of-county portability, it does require more than passive drift while housing instability and move pressure continue.

### V. Plaintiffs face irreparable harm absent immediate relief.

Loss of housing, loss of subsidy, forced displacement before relocation is lawfully completed, collapse of a safety-based transfer route, and denial of disability accommodation are classic irreparable harms. The present record reflects continuing move pressure, stalled transfer processing, service-animal and accommodation disputes, and a housing opportunity that plaintiffs contend was never lawfully or contractually processed to completion. Where the injury is ongoing interference with housing rights and the realistic threat of housing loss, money damages are not an adequate substitute.

### VI. The balance of equities and the public interest favor narrow relief.

The requested relief imposes only the burden of obeying existing federal law and preserving the relocation and processing commitments defendants already undertook. It does not require the Court to micromanage every housing decision or to compel an immediate lease of a particular unit. Instead, it preserves the status quo, bars further interference and retaliation, bars displacement before relocation is lawfully complete, requires lawful accommodation handling, requires written status clarity from HACC, and requires both defendants to stop blocking or distorting plaintiffs' housing path on unlawful grounds. The public interest strongly favors interim orders that prevent discriminatory or premature housing loss and require compliance with federal housing protections and federally required relocation obligations.

## Proposed Order

Plaintiffs respectfully request that the Court enter a preliminary injunction providing that:

1. `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` and `Quantum Residential` shall not coerce, intimidate, threaten, retaliate against, or otherwise interfere with plaintiffs' exercise of rights protected by the Fair Housing Act, including rights relating to discrimination complaints, disability accommodation, service-animal use, and voucher-backed housing access.
2. `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` and `Quantum Residential` shall not refuse, discard, ignore, suppress, or selectively burden plaintiffs' housing application, portability path, transfer path, accommodation request, or service-animal-related housing request on discriminatory or retaliatory grounds.
3. `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` shall not terminate assistance, cause eviction, cause lockout, or impose materially adverse housing consequences against plaintiffs on the matters at issue in this action until HACC has lawfully processed the transfer, voucher, and hearing paths at issue.
4. Within a short date certain fixed by the Court, `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` shall provide plaintiffs with a written status statement identifying the current voucher status, portability status, transfer status, and any pending review or hearing path, together with the concrete next steps and deadlines.
5. `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` shall administer plaintiffs' safety-based external transfer or port-out route in a manner consistent with VAWA confidentiality protections and shall provide clear status, next-step, and review information while that route remains active and unresolved.
6. `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` shall not treat plaintiffs' Section 18 relocation as complete, and shall not complete displacement as to plaintiffs, unless and until plaintiffs have actually been provided a lawful comparable replacement-housing path consistent with federal relocation requirements and the relocation commitments HACC triggered.
7. `Housing Authority of Clackamas County` and `Quantum Residential` shall preserve, maintain, and not frustrate the relocation-processing chain undertaken for plaintiffs, including intake, forwarding, review, status tracking, and communication concerning plaintiffs' Blossom application materials and related replacement-housing paperwork.
8. `Quantum Residential` shall preserve plaintiffs' Blossom application materials and, to the extent Quantum received or controlled that intake path, handle those materials under nondiscriminatory, nonretaliatory, and project-role-consistent standards.
