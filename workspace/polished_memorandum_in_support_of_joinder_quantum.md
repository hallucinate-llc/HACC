Defendants submit this memorandum in support of their motion to join Quantum Residential Property Management, L.P. This memorandum is designed to pair with defendants' response to Plaintiff's eviction motion and related cross-motion for narrow interim relief because the same intake, forwarding, review, and relocation chain is already before the Court in that filing set.

## I. Core Point

The present eviction dispute is not limited to a simple failure to vacate. HACC itself has placed Quantum inside the housing-processing chain that defendants say was supposed to move the household out of HACC-controlled housing and into replacement housing. Once HACC did that, Quantum ceased to be a stranger to the dispute. It became a materially involved actor whose presence is needed for complete relief and to avoid inconsistent positions about what happened to the household's intake packet and replacement-housing path.

## II. Record Basis For Joinder

The current record supports the following narrow points:

1. HACC triggered redevelopment-related relocation obligations through the Hillside Park process.
2. HACC's January 8, 2026 notice directed the household into the Blossom / Tenant Protection Voucher path, which defendants understood to include the Hillside Manor office intake route.
3. HACC later wrote that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum Residential staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office but had not been provided to HACC.
4. HACC told the household to ask Quantum staff to send the previously submitted packet directly to HACC.
5. Benjamin Barber contemporaneously complained that Blossom had not processed the application, had refused to house a service animal, and had engaged in race discrimination.
6. Defendants' broader eviction response now seeks to preserve exactly that intake, forwarding, review, and status-tracking chain while the Court determines whether eviction is premature.

That record is already enough to show that Quantum is not merely adjacent to the controversy. Quantum is alleged to be one of the reasons the replacement-housing path stalled.

## III. ORCP 29A Supports Joinder

ORCP 29A allows joinder where complete relief cannot be accorded among existing parties in the person's absence, or where the action may leave existing parties subject to a substantial risk of inconsistent obligations.

That standard is met here for at least two reasons.

First, complete relief is difficult without Quantum. HACC's own written account is that the disputed intake packet was left with Quantum staff but did not reach HACC. If the Court is asked to decide whether the household followed the directed housing path, whether the intake chain failed, whether records must be preserved, or whether additional processing relief is warranted, Quantum's absence leaves a material gap in the adjudication.

Second, there is a substantial risk of inconsistent positions or obligations if Quantum is not joined. HACC has effectively said: the packet was submitted to Quantum staff, and Quantum did not get it to HACC. If HACC remains the only party, the Court could be asked to allocate blame, preserve records, or shape relief around a processing failure that Quantum has not had to admit, deny, or explain. ORCP 29A exists precisely to avoid that kind of fragmented adjudication.

## IV. The Strongest Theory Is Processing-Chain Joinder, Not Maximum-Theory Joinder

The safest joinder theory is the narrow one.

Defendants do not need to prove at this stage that Quantum inherited every possible Section 18, RAD, VAWA, disability, or race-discrimination duty in full before joinder can be justified. The stronger and cleaner point is that:

1. HACC itself routed the household into a housing path that used Quantum's office and staff.
2. HACC itself later said the packet had been given to Quantum staff and had not been transmitted onward.
3. Defendants now seek relief that would preserve or restore that same processing chain.

That alone is enough to make Quantum materially connected to the Court's ability to accord complete relief.

Quantum's property-management role at Hillside Manor still matters, but primarily because it reinforces that Quantum was participating in the same replacement-housing environment and intake route, not because the Court must decide every underlying federal merits issue before joinder.

## V. Quantum Also Fits The Alternative ORCP 22B Theory

If the Court views this issue through third-party practice rather than compulsory joinder, Quantum is still a proper party in substance under ORCP 22B. HACC's own account places Quantum in the packet-routing and intake-processing chain that defendants say contributed to the stalled relocation path and the premature eviction posture. That is enough to support bringing Quantum into the same action so the Court can resolve the connected processing dispute in one place.

## VI. The Same Source-Linked Duty Structure Makes Quantum's Presence More Important

The eviction-response packet now identifies the main duties at issue through specific sources rather than generalized assertion. That matters here too.

The case now turns in part on:

1. Section 18 relocation completion duties under 42 U.S.C. § 1437p(d) and 24 C.F.R. § 970.21.
2. VAWA transfer and related confidentiality duties under 24 C.F.R. §§ 5.2007 and 5.2009.
3. Hearing duties under 42 U.S.C. § 1437d(k) and 24 C.F.R. § 982.555.
4. Fair Housing Act accommodation duties under 42 U.S.C. § 3604(f)(3)(B) and 24 C.F.R. § 100.204.
5. FHA anti-interference duties under 42 U.S.C. § 3617 and 24 C.F.R. § 100.400.
6. Section 1981 contracting-equality duties under 42 U.S.C. § 1981.

The joinder point is not that Quantum necessarily bears all of those duties in the same way as HACC. The joinder point is that Quantum is already inside the factual chain through which the Court will be asked to evaluate and preserve relief on those issues. That makes Quantum's presence materially useful and, on this record, likely necessary.

The source-linked Title 18 and contract-side chain is set out more fully in [title18_statute_and_contract_source_review.md](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/title18_statute_and_contract_source_review.md) and [title18_contract_source_linked_duty_matrix.md](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/title18_contract_source_linked_duty_matrix.md). Those materials matter here because they show that the intake and forwarding failures involving Quantum occurred inside a documented federal-relocation and HACC-administered replacement-housing process, not an isolated private rental conversation.

More specifically, HACC's own documents show:

1. Hillside Manor is a RAD PBV project in HACC's Administrative Plan;
2. Quantum is identified there as the property management company;
3. HACC publicly stated that applications for Blossom and Community Apartments would be processed through HACC's PBV waitlist and that Hillside relocation households were priority groups; and
4. HACC later wrote that the packet had been submitted to Quantum staff at the Hillside Manor office but had not reached HACC.

That combination is why Quantum belongs in the case. The Court is not being asked to drag in a stranger to the relocation process. The Court is being asked to join the project-side participant HACC itself placed inside the federally rooted and locally administered replacement-housing chain.

## VII. Conclusion

The Court should grant the motion to join Quantum Residential Property Management, L.P. The narrower and better-grounded basis is that HACC's own records place Quantum in the exact intake-and-relocation chain the Court is already being asked to evaluate and preserve. At minimum, the Court should permit joinder or equivalent third-party practice so the intake-processing dispute is resolved in one action rather than through partial litigation against only HACC.
