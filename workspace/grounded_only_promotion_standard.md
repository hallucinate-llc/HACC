# Grounded-Only Promotion Standard

Case No. 26FE0586

This standard applies whenever any secondary, older, or support draft is promoted into the active state-court packet.

## Promotion Rule

No draft should be promoted into the active packet unless it satisfies all of the following:

1. every major factual sentence points to a document or declaration;
2. every major duty sentence points to a statute, regulation, plan text, notice, or email;
3. any contract-like point is stated by citing the actual source in the record, such as:
   - HACC's January 8, 2026 notice,
   - HACC's March 26, 2026 options email,
   - HACC's Administrative Plan,
   - HACC's public Blossom page;
4. the filing uses the grounded exhibit order `B, G, V, M, W` as the lead set unless a narrower reason requires a different order;
5. the filing uses the deontic chain in the correct sequence:
   - source-linked duty,
   - source-linked fact,
   - prohibition, permission, or obligation consequence;
6. any mention of the formal model is phrased as support for the legal consequence of source-linked facts, not proof of all historical facts.

## Required Language Rules

Use:

- `the record supports`
- `HACC's own documents state`
- `HACC later acknowledged in writing`
- `on the present record`
- `the formal model confirms the normative consequence of the source-linked facts`

Replace broader shorthand as follows:

- replace `the contracts say` with the specific source name;
- replace `the graph proves` with a formal-model support formulation;
- replace absolute delay claims with `a substantial amount, and likely most, of the documented delay`;
- replace broad liability shorthand with the narrower process-chain formulation.

## Promotion Checklist

Before promotion, confirm:

- the caption and case number are correct;
- the filing matches the current grounded exhibit order;
- the filing does not contain internal drafting language;
- the filing does not rely on missing contract texts when a notice, plan, or email is the real source;
- the opening paragraph matches the current state-court packet theory.

## Effect

This standard resolves the checklist's forward-looking issue by making grounded-only cleanup a standing promotion requirement for future filings.
