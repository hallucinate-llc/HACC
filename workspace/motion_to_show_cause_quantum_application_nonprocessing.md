# Defendants' Motion For Order To Show Cause Re Quantum Residential's Nonprocessing Of The Barber-Cortez Applications

IN THE CIRCUIT COURT OF THE STATE OF OREGON
FOR THE COUNTY OF CLACKAMAS

HOUSING AUTHORITY OF CLACKAMAS COUNTY,
Plaintiff,

v.

BENJAMIN JAY BARBER and JANE KAY CORTEZ,
Defendants.

Case No. __________________

DEFENDANTS' MOTION FOR ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE RE QUANTUM RESIDENTIAL'S NONPROCESSING OF THE BARBER-CORTEZ APPLICATIONS

Defendants Benjamin Jay Barber and Jane Kay Cortez move for an order requiring Quantum Residential Property Management, L.P., upon joinder or other appearance in this case, to show cause why the Blossom / Hillside Manor applications submitted on behalf of Benjamin Barber and Jane Kay Cortez were not processed, transmitted, or brought to a clear approval, denial, or written-deficiency result.

## Basis For The Motion

This request is narrow and record-based.

1. HACC's January 8, 2026 notice directed the household into the Blossom / Tenant Protection Voucher path.
2. Benjamin Barber states that he personally delivered a paper Blossom application in December 2025.
3. HACC later acknowledged in writing that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum Residential staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office but had not been provided to HACC.
4. Benjamin Barber contemporaneously complained in writing that Blossom had refused to process the application, had refused to house a service animal, and had engaged in discrimination.
5. The same intake and processing failure is already central to defendants' eviction response, interim-relief request, and motion to join Quantum.

## Requested Show-Cause Topics

Defendants ask the Court to require Quantum to state, in writing and under appropriate certification:

1. whether Quantum received an application or intake packet from or on behalf of Benjamin Barber and Jane Kay Cortez;
2. if so, when, where, and through whom the packet was received;
3. what happened to the packet after receipt, including scanning, copying, logging, review, forwarding, or rejection;
4. whether Quantum ever sent the packet or any portion of it to HACC, and if so when;
5. whether Quantum issued any written approval, denial, deficiency notice, or request for additional information;
6. whether service-animal concerns, breed restrictions, source-of-income issues, voucher timing, race-related concerns, or any other screening factor played any role in nonprocessing or delay; and
7. what records Quantum presently maintains concerning that application path.

## Why The Order Is Needed

Without a show-cause response, the Court is left with only a partial record: HACC says the packet was left with Quantum staff and did not reach HACC, while defendants say the application was submitted and then not processed. A targeted response from Quantum would help the Court determine whether the application path failed, whether records are missing, and whether additional preservation or interim relief is necessary.

## Relief Requested

Defendants ask the Court to enter an order directing Quantum Residential Property Management, L.P., upon joinder or other authorized appearance in this action, to file a written show-cause response addressing the points above within a time set by the Court.
