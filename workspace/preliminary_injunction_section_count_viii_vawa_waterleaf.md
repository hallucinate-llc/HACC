# Preliminary Injunction Section for Count VIII

## Narrow Injunction Section

Plaintiffs are likely to succeed, or at minimum raise serious questions going to the merits, on their VAWA transfer-delay claim against HACC. The present record shows that plaintiffs were pursuing Waterleaf in Multnomah County by December 23, 2025, that by January 12, 2026 HACC was told Waterleaf wanted the voucher amount and that the application had already been signed and turned in, that by January 26, 2026 HACC acknowledged the household had chosen to port to Multnomah County, and that by March 25-26, 2026 Waterleaf still had the household only on a waiting list pending issuance of the voucher. Even if VAWA does not impose a short fixed numeric deadline for out-of-county portability, it does require a covered housing provider to maintain and use an emergency transfer plan and not nullify a safety-based transfer through delay or incoherent administration. The chronology here supports the inference that HACC did not meaningfully carry out that transfer function.

Plaintiffs also face irreparable harm. The same record reflects continuing move-out pressure, threatened eviction consequences, delayed voucher issuance, and continuing instability in a household that was attempting to leave for safety-related reasons. Loss of housing, loss of subsidy, and forced displacement while the transfer route remains stalled are classic irreparable injuries. The balance of equities and the public interest favor relief requiring HACC to preserve the status quo and promptly process the Waterleaf / Multnomah County route in a lawful manner, because such relief merely requires compliance with federal housing protections and prevents avoidable housing loss while the merits are resolved.

Accordingly, plaintiffs request a preliminary injunction barring eviction, lockout, voucher termination, or other adverse housing action during the pendency of this action, and requiring HACC to: (1) refrain from taking further action inconsistent with plaintiffs' VAWA transfer rights; (2) process the external transfer / port-out route with reasonable promptness; (3) maintain the confidentiality protections required by VAWA; and (4) provide clear written notice of the status of the voucher, transfer, and next required steps.

## Short Relief Paragraph

As interim relief on Count VIII, plaintiffs request an order preserving the tenancy and subsidy status quo and requiring HACC to process the Waterleaf / Multnomah County transfer path promptly and lawfully, rather than allowing the transfer route to continue drifting while eviction pressure remains in place.
