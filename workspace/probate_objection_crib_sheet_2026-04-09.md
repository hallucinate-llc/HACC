# Probate Objection Crib Sheet

## Core Theme

This petition should not be used to obtain emergency or plenary control over Jane Cortez on a one-sided and materially incomplete record.

## Best Narrow Ask

1. deny or sharply narrow emergency relief;
2. deny any further writ-based forced transport or comparable coercive removal relief without a clean predicate order and notice record;
3. deny plenary authority and broad placement authority on the present record;
4. deny attorney fees from Jane's funds;
5. keep HACC / Quantum / eviction merits out of probate except to the extent narrowly necessary;
6. limit any hearing to emergency basis, notice, scope, and record sufficiency.

## Best Three Points

### 1. APS / Police Story Is Incomplete

- Solomon says Benjamin interfered with APS / police.
- Same-day context: Jane was at the courthouse seeking a restraining order against Solomon.
- That means the APS story is not a neutral emergency narrative.

### 2. Housing Crisis Was Not Solely Benjamin-Caused

- The petition overattributes the housing crisis to Benjamin.
- Current record shows overlapping HACC / Quantum delay, relocation failure, and outside document-flow issues.
- Probate should not use a disputed housing crisis as a shortcut to sweeping guardianship relief.

### 3. Petition Seeks Extreme Control On Missing Papers

- writ-based forced transport
- possible facility placement
- plenary authority
- fees from Jane's funds
- trust / estate / authority papers still not fully produced

## Trust / Will Point

- Gerald Miller estate / will / trust packet exists.
- Solomon's own text shows he was already asserting a trust-related role.
- Earlier restraining-order exhibits appear to show Jane describing papers she signed with Sam / Solomon that she did not understand, including one she thought related to her dad's will.
- That is a reason to demand actual papers before granting broad financial control.

## Writ Point

- The writ sought forced entry, pickup, and hospital transport.
- Jane's April 8 declaration supports that police came, she was upset, and Solomon was trying to take her away without approval.
- Court should require a clean predicate order and notice basis before any similar relief is considered.

## Fee Point

- Fees should not come from Jane's funds on this disputed record.
- `ORS 125.098` makes benefit to Jane the greatest-weight factor.
- That benefit is not established strongly enough here.
- Any separate HACC / Quantum or housing-rights fee issues belong in the proper housing / civil-rights forum, not as a probate-side charge against Jane.

## If The Judge Pushes Back

`Are you saying Jane needs no help at all?`

- No. The point is that disputed need for some help does not justify sweeping emergency, transport, placement, and fee relief on this record.

`Why does housing matter here?`

- Only because Solomon uses the housing crisis as part of the emergency story against Benjamin. Probate should not become the forum for trying the full housing merits.

`Why deny fees from Jane's funds?`

- Because the petition seeks disputed coercive control and the benefit to Jane is not established strongly enough to charge her funds for it.

`What is the shortest legal point?`

- The Court can require a clean notice and predicate record, deny overbroad relief, and tailor any further proceedings to genuine guardianship questions.
