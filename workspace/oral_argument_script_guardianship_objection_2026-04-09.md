# Oral Argument Script: Guardianship Petition Objection

## 45-Second Opening

Your Honor, the problem with this petition is not just that it asks for help for Jane Cortez. It asks for sweeping emergency and plenary control on a disputed and materially incomplete record. The APS allegation omits that, on the very day Solomon says he was trying to bring APS to the house, Jane was at the courthouse seeking a restraining order against him. The housing crisis is also not a simple Benjamin-caused event; the record shows overlapping HACC and Quantum delay, voucher and relocation failures, and disputed outside document flows. And the petition goes much further than asking for modest assistance because it seeks writ-based forced transport, broad placement authority, plenary control, and attorney fees from Jane's own funds.

## Core Ask

I am asking the Court to do four things.

1. Deny or sharply narrow any emergency relief that depends on that one-sided factual narrative.
2. Deny any further writ-based forced entry or forced transport relief unless there is a clean predicate order and notice record.
3. Keep collateral Section 18, HACC, Quantum, and eviction merits out of probate except to the extent narrowly necessary to understand a real guardianship issue.
4. If the Court concludes the petition was materially barred or seriously defective, reserve or consider appropriate sanctions-related or claim-limiting relief.
5. Deny fee shifting from Jane's funds because Benjamin completed the relocation-side tasks assigned to him and was left waiting for the housing actors to materialize an actual contract or lease, and leave any HACC / Quantum or Home Forward / Brighthaven fee issues, if they ever arise, to the separate properly framed federal civil-rights / housing track rather than this probate case.

## Best Three Facts

1. The APS / police interference story is incomplete because Jane was at the courthouse seeking a restraining order against Solomon that same day.
2. Solomon's petition falsely or misleadingly portrays Benjamin as someone who had only recently begun living with Jane, when Benjamin has lived with her for more than six years.
3. The petition seeks extreme control, including possible facility placement, plenary authority, and fee shifting from Jane's funds, while trust and authority papers still have not been cleanly produced. The earlier restraining-order exhibits even appear to show Jane describing papers she signed with Sam / Solomon that she did not understand, including one she thought had something to do with her dad's will.
4. If the Court agrees the petition was materially barred or defect-ridden, that also raises a serious question whether it should have been filed or maintained in this form.

## If The Court Asks Why Housing Matters

Housing matters here only because Solomon's petition uses the housing crisis as part of the emergency story against Benjamin. I am not asking this probate court to try the full HACC or Quantum merits. I am asking the Court not to misattribute that housing crisis to Benjamin when the current record shows overlapping outside causes.

## If The Court Asks Whether Jane Needs Some Help

The issue is not whether Jane may benefit from some help. The issue is whether this petition proves the need for emergency, coercive, plenary control on this record. It does not.

## If The Court Asks For The Narrowest Order

The narrowest order would be:

1. deny further coercive writ-based relief;
2. deny plenary and placement authority on the present showing;
3. deny fees from Jane's funds;
4. set any further hearing only on genuine guardianship questions, with a clean notice and predicate record.
5. reserve any sanctions decision unless and until the Court decides whether the petition was materially barred or seriously defective.
