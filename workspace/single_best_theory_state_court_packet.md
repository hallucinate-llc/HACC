# Single Best Theory Of The State-Court Packet

This page is the shortest accurate summary of the combined filing set.

## Core Theory

This is not an ordinary eviction for simple noncompliance.

HACC triggered redevelopment-related relocation duties under Section 18, then directed the household into a specific replacement-housing process, and then moved toward eviction while that process was still materially incomplete.

That means the strongest theory is:

- HACC had direct relocation duties under federal law;
- HACC also undertook a specific local replacement-housing process through its own plan, notices, and routing instructions;
- the Blossom / Hillside Manor path was not processed through to a clear result;
- the alternate Waterleaf / Multnomah port-out path remained incomplete across four calendar months;
- a substantial amount, and likely most, of the documented delay from January 8, 2026 through late March 2026 was caused by HACC's own administration;
- therefore HACC was barred from filing eviction while the relocation and undertaken processing duties remained materially incomplete.

## The Short Deontic Form

- `O(HACC, complete Section 18 relocation duties)`
- `O(HACC, administer the replacement-housing path it adopted and directed)`
- `O(HACC, administer the Blossom-or-TPV choice it required the household to make)`
- `not completed(relocation and undertaken processing duties) -> F(HACC, file eviction)`

## Why Quantum Matters

Quantum is not just adjacent to the dispute.

HACC's own records show that:

- Hillside Manor is a RAD PBV project;
- Quantum is the property manager there;
- HACC publicly said Blossom applications would be processed through HACC's PBV waitlist; and
- HACC later admitted the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum staff at the Hillside Manor office but had not been provided to HACC.

So Quantum belongs in the case because Quantum was inside the same replacement-housing intake chain the Court is being asked to evaluate and preserve.
