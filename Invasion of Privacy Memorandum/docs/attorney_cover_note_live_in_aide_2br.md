# Attorney Cover Note

## Re: Section 504 / Live-In Aide / Two-Bedroom Reasonable Accommodation

This packet is organized around a narrow position: a two-bedroom voucher is the minimum reasonable accommodation for a disabled tenant who needs a live-in aide, and a one-bedroom arrangement that forces the aide into the living room is not an effective accommodation in practice.

The strongest authority in the packet is HUD Notice PIH 2014-25. That notice specifically recognizes that a family may request a reasonable-accommodation exception to subsidy standards and gives, as an example, an additional bedroom for disability-related overnight care so the family can have equal use and enjoyment of the unit. That guidance fits the present facts closely because the dispute is not about a discretionary upgrade, but about the minimum bedroom size needed for the live-in-aide arrangement to function.

The packet also includes the physician-supported reasonable-accommodation record, the March 2026 HACC email thread showing that the two-bedroom issue was being handled as an accommodation question, and the demonstrative privacy exhibit showing why a one-bedroom layout fails in practice. The Ninth Circuit authorities are used more cautiously, as analogical support for the broader proposition that disability accommodations in housing must be evaluated functionally and not merely on paper.

The requested review is whether these facts support a Section 504 reasonable-accommodation claim, and whether related FHA, ADA, state-law, or administrative-hearing claims should be added or prioritized.
