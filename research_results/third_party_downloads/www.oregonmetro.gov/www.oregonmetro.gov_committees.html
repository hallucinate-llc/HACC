Committees

Metro convenes a variety of advisory, technical and community-focused committees to guide regional planning, policy, funding and oversight. Each brings diverse perspectives to help shape decisions for the Portland metropolitan region.

Affordable Housing Bond Oversight Committee

The Affordable Housing Bond Oversight Committee is made up of volunteers who provide independent and transparent oversight of the housing bond.

Metro Investment Advisory Board

The Investment Advisory Board advises Metro on investment strategies, banking relationships, the legality and probity of investment activities and the establishment of written procedures for the investment operations.

Supportive Housing Services Oversight Committee

The Supportive Housing Services Oversight Committee is made up of volunteers who provide independent and transparent oversight of the SHS fund.

Audit committee

The committee assists the Metro Council in reviewing accounting policies and reporting practices as they relate to Metro’s annual comprehensive financial report.

Committee on Disability Inclusion

Committee on Racial Equity

The Committee on Racial Equity brings together a diverse group of advocates to advise Metro Council on how to advance racial equity across the agency’s work.

Joint Policy Advisory Committee on Transportation

The Joint Policy Advisory Committee on Transportation makes recommendations to the Metro Council on transportation needs in the region.

Metro Policy Advisory Committee

Established by the Metro Charter in 1992, MPAC advises the Metro Council on the amendment or adoption of the Regional Framework Plan.

Metro Technical Advisory Committee

MTAC is a 35-member committee of planners, citizens and business representatives that provides detailed technical support to the Metro Policy Advisory Committee.

Metropolitan Exposition Recreation Commission

The Metropolitan Exposition Recreation Commission works to protect the public investment in three of Metro's visitor venues: Oregon Convention Center, Portland Expo Center and Portland’5 Centers for the Arts.
