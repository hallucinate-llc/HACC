         The LFO recommended 2023-25 budget for the Secretary of State is $111,716,131 total
         funds and 240 positions (239.50 FTE), including $17,648,792 General Fund, $89,044,692
         Other Funds, and $5,022,647 Federal Funds, which represents a 2.3% increase over the
         2021-23 legislatively approved budget. The Secretary of State is Oregon’s chief elections
         officer, auditor of public funds, maintainer of the state’s business registrations, and keeper
         of public records.

         The recommended budget includes the following significant adjustments:
             e     $1.1 million General Fund for elections improvements, including adding
                   positions to address election complaints, improve security and monitor election
                   threats. Funding was also added to conduct a pilot of risk limiting audits and
                   provide factual information relating to elections.
             °     $3.1 million Other Funds and five positions are added for Information
                   Technology expenditures related to improving security, modernization of
                   custom-built legacy systems and management of agency IT assets.
   °       $1.2 million Other Funds was added to continue work preserving recordings of
           legislative hearings and complete the conversion to compact shelving at the
           Archives Building.

Package 110 Archivists
Package Description       Adds two permanent Archivist 1 positions (2.00 FTE) to convert fifty years’ worth of legislative recordings that are at risk of being irretrievably
lost. Legislative committee hearings dating from 1957 to 2007 were recorded using propriety technology and inherently unstable media, including Sawyer Rols,
reel-to-reel tapes and audiocassette tapes. These verbatim audio recordings serve as the only complete record of the legislative committee hearings and are accessed
by Oregon’s legal researchers and constituents alike. Long-term access to these records is at risk due to the inevitable degradation of the media and the obsolescence
of hardware needed to play the recordings. Digitized records will be available online for easy access by those interested. These positions were approved as
limited-duration positions for the 2021-23 biennium.
