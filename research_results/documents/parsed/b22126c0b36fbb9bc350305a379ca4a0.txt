Analyst:    Michelle Deister

Request: Increase Federal Funds expenditure limitation by $13,757,960, establish eight permanent
positions (3.50 FTE), and reclassify one existing position to fund administrative needs associated with
the Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment Program.

Analysis: In June 2023, the National Telecommunications and Information Administration (NTIA)
announced state funding awards from the Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment (BEAD) Program,
approved as part of the federal Infrastructure, Investment and Jobs Act of 2021. Oregon’s allocation is
$689 million, of which $5 million in expenditure limitation was requested and approved in November
2022 for planning activities. The plans include a required Five-Year Action Plan, and Initial Proposal,
Volume | and Il. In part, the Initial Proposals identify underserved and unserved areas, describe the BEAD
challenge process, and identify subgrantee selection criteria.

The $13.8 million in expenditure limitation associated with this request will be used as follows: $704,427
will fund a portion of personal services costs associated with eight new positions, and the reclassification
of an existing Program Analyst 3 position to a Business Operations Administrator position; $597,782 will
fund position-related services and supplies; and $106,645 will fund other Services and Supplies including
training, travel, and Attorney General expenses. The balance of the request - $13,053,533 -- will fund
professional services contracts to implement the BEAD Challenge Process as well as program
implementation. Professional technical engineering assistance procured by these contracts will help
OBDD to evaluate costs and technical validity of subrecipient proposals and manage an estimated 200
grant contracts serving some 130,000 locations. Each contract will require oversight to ensure
appropriate financial reimbursement, federal regulations compliance, and ongoing reporting. OBDD is
targeting the end of May to release an RFP for these contracted professional services.

OBDD currently has 13 employees dedicated to the activities of the Oregon Broadband Office (OBO).
OBO manages the Broadband Technical Assistance Program, the American Rescue Plan Act Capital
Projects Fund (ARPA-CPF), the Digital Equity State Capacity Grant program, and the BEAD program, most
of which operate simultaneously. The request would add the following six full-time permanent positions
to assist with impending workload: three Program Analyst 3 positions; an Accountant 3; An Accounting
Technician, and an Administrative Specialist 2. A part-time Accounting Manager 1 and Human Resource
Analyst 2 would also be added to the agency’s Operations division. These positions and the
reclassification will be partially funded by existing ARPA-CPF funds for the remainder of this biennium, as
they will also support the work of the Broadband Deployment Program, which is funded from that
source. Once ARPA-CPF is exhausted, financial support for these positions will transition fully to BEAD
funds, which allow ongoing programmatic administrative costs of 2% of the state allocation (a further
$13.8 million), which are anticipated to be sufficient to support ongoing staffing costs over the life of the
program.

OBDD’s subgrantees will have four years in which to expend any funds granted under the BEAD
program. No matching funds are required for planning activities associated with the BEAD program, but

Discussion: The BEAD Program was authorized by the Infrastructure Investment and
Jobs Act of 2021 (IIJA). The BEAD Program provides $42.45 billion in federal funding
from the U.S. Department of Commerce, National Telecommunications and Information
Administration (NTIA) to grant to all 50 states, the District of Columbia, and territories,
for broadband planning, deployment, mapping, equity, and adoption activities. The BEAD
grant amount for each state has already been determined and is not competitive;
however, the state must apply for the grant pro forma to receive the funding. In
September 2022, the Oregon Business Development Department (OBDD) was granted
the authority by the Emergency Board to apply for an initial amount of $5.0 million out of
its impending allocation amount for the initial broadband planning funds. The planning
funds were used to pay for staff and a consultant to prepare a required five-year action
plan and the Initial Proposal, volumes 1 and 2, for submittal to the NTIA. Oregon
submitted its Five-Year Action Plan on August 25, 2023. In November 2023, after the
Federal Communications Commission issued Broadband DATA Maps, OBDD was
granted the authority by the Interim Joint Committee on Ways and Means to apply for
$683.9 million for the implementation phase of the BEAD program.

OBDD received approval from NTIA in March for the        Initial Proposal Volume 1, which
identifies Oregon's BEAD Challenge Process. This Challenge Process is designed to
identify every location in Oregon eligible to receive BEAD funding. The Oregon
Broadband Office is in the final stages of development of its Initial Proposal Volume 2,
which identifies the sub-grantee selection process Oregon will undertake to fund
projects to provide high-speed, reliable internet access to the locations identified in the
Challenge Process. While OBDD has not received official notice regarding the award of
the $683.9, a final funding announcement is expected from NTIA within the next two
months. However, notice could come in a matter of weeks. For this reason, OBDD is
coming forward now to ask for expenditure limitation and positions necessary to begin
the implementation of the BEAD program as soon as it receives the funding.

OBDD’s request is for an increase of Federal Funds expenditure limitation in the amount
of $13.7 million, which includes the establishment of six-permanent full-time positions,
including three Program Analysts, one Administrative Specialist, one Accountant, and
one Accounting Technician (3.00 FTE); two permanent half-time positions, including an

Nature of the Request
The Oregon Business Development Department (Department) respectfully requests spending
limitation and position authority to support the operations of its Oregon Broadband Office. The
Department is requesting an increase in Federal Funds limitation in the amount of $13,757,960 to
allow the Department to expend awarded grant funds from the National Telecommunications and
Information Administration (NTIA) for the Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment Program
(BEAD) grant. We ate also requesting position authority for the addition of six permanent positions
(3.0 FTE), two permanent half-time positions (0.5 FTE), and the reclassification of one permanent
position, to allow the Department to administer broadband related grant funds allocated to the
state.

Background
The BEAD Program was authorized by the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act of 2021, also
known as the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law. The BEAD Program provides new federal funding for
NTIA to grant to all fifty states, the District of Columbia, and U.S. territories for broadband planning,
deployment, mapping, equity, and adoption activities.

Funding is distributed primarily based on the relative number of “unserved” locations (i.e.,
broadband-serviceable locations that lack access to Reliable Broadband Service at speeds of at least
25 Mbps downstream and 3 Mbps upstream) in each state and territory. Each state is eligible to
receive a minimum of $100 million.

In conjunction with the Federal Communications Commission issuing Broadband DATA Maps,
NTIA issued a notice on June 26, 2023 that contained the estimated amount of program funds that
each state is eligible to receive, and Oregon’s allocation is $689 million. Oregon has previously
requested $5 million of its minimum allocation in Initial Planning Funds. The NTIA allows for 2% of
the grant to be used for the administration of the grant. The NTIA does not require a match to
receive planning or administrative funds but will require a 25% match for the remaining
deployment funds. The required match may come from private, public, or other federal sources; in
some instances, the matchmay be waived.

The American Rescue Plan Act Coronavirus Capital Projects Fund (ARPA CPF) appropriated $10
billion to the U.S. Treasury to provide payments to states, territories, freely associated states, and
tribal governments “to carry out critical capital projects directly enabling work, education, and
health monitoring, including remote options, in response to the public health emergency with
