    What are Shared Services?
    ¢   ODHS and OHA Shared Services include mission critical
        support functions that serve both agencies.

    ¢   Service areas range from budget and finance to
        contracts and procurement to facilities
        management.

    ¢   By leveraging resources across both agencies,
        the shared services model promotes greater
        efficiency and alignment of agency processes.

    OFS staffing needs
    Current Office of Financial Services (OFS) staffing needs stem from:

    ¢   HB 2086 and HB 2910 (2021), which have had workload impacts but did not
        include staffing increases in their enrolled versions;
    ¢   Financial reporting requirements associated with
        the use of American Recovery Plan Act (ARPA)
        and State and Local Fiscal Recovery Funds (SLFRF);
        and
    ¢   The shifting of accounts payable functions from
        third parties to in-house in compliance with
        public funds law.

    The Office of Health, Safety and Emergency Services (OHSE) provides a range of

Office of the ODHS Chief
Administrative Officer
    OCAO’s expanding tasks
    The Office of the Chief Administrative Officer (OQCAO) supports 12 Shared Services
    and four Central Services that include over 600 ODHS employees.

    The office has also led the agencywide Trauma Informed
    Organization and Suicide Prevention initiatives, training
    more than 8,000 ODHS staff in evidence-based suicide
    prevention skills.

    While the various Shared and Central Services have
    grown in size and complexity, the CAO staff is still
    composed of just two administrators, one project
    manager, and one administrative support position.

        were amplified by the pandemic.

        addressed and do not undermine the equity focus within the
        response.
