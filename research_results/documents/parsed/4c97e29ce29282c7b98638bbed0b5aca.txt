Summary of Revenue Changes
The Subcommittee’s recommended budget for the Oregon Business Development Department (OBDD) is comprised of 11.7% General Fund,
10.4% Lottery Funds, 62.2% Other Funds, and 15.7% Federal Funds. Of the General Fund, 52.8% is for Debt Service and of the Lottery Funds
revenues, 35.3% is for Debt Service.

Revenues supporting the Department’s Other Funds programs include loan principal and interest repayments, investment interest earnings,
General Obligation Bond and Oregon Bond Bank revenue bond proceeds, loan and service fees, donations, pass through monies received from
Oregon Health Authority for the Safe Drinking Water Program, and an assessment from state agencies for the Certification Office of Business
Inclusion and Diversity. The Oregon Department of Transportation (ODOT) finances 45% of the COBID assessment because OBDD manages the
certification of the U.S. Department of Transportation’s Disadvantaged Business Enterprise program on behalf of ODOT. The remaining 55% is
assessed to state agencies based on FTE.

Summary of Transportation and Economic Development Subcommittee Action
OBDD is the state’s economic development department. Its mission is to invest in Oregon businesses, communities, and people to promote a
globally competitive, diverse, and inclusive economy. The Department’s vision statement is “Prosperity for all Oregonians.” Core functions of
the agency include rural community development and infrastructure financing; business retention, expansion and recruitment; export
promotion and international trade; industry innovation and research and development; entrepreneurship and small business assistance; and
support for arts and cultural organizations. OBDD is split into four operating divisions: Business, Innovation, and Trade; the Infrastructure
Finance Authority; the Arts Division; and Operations. The Office of Film and Video is a program unit within the Department’s budget for
passthrough purposes only.

The Subcommittee approved a budget of $1,801,284,094 total funds consisting of $210,589,991 General Fund, $187,548,462 Lottery Funds,
$743,624,247 Other Funds expenditure limitation, $283,081,621 Federal Funds expenditure limitation, $376,439,773 Other Funds Nonlimited,
and 200 positions (196.13 FTE). This is a total funds decrease of 20.8% from the 2023-25 legislatively approved budget (LAB), and a 56.5%
increase from the 2025-27 current service level.

Division is funded with General Fund, Lottery Funds, Other Funds assessed against agency programs and Federal Funds.

Business, Innovation, Trade
The Business, Innovation, and Trade (BIT) Division promotes business retention, growth, and job creation through multiple programs and
services. Business retention and recruitment are an area of focus for this division. BIT promotes access to capital through loans, credit
guarantees, and investing in early-stage businesses through the Oregon Growth Board. The Division promotes exports by Oregon businesses
through trade missions, the attraction of foreign direct investment, and the promotion of exports for small and medium sized businesses.
Finally, BIT funds a variety of initiatives aimed at encouraging research and development and entrepreneurship, in some cases through the
university system.

The Division is primarily supported by Lottery Funds. Federal Funds support the Brownfields program, the State Small Business Credit Initiative
grant, and the State Trade Export Promotion (STEP) grant. Other Funds are from loan principal and interest repayments, loan and service fees,
investment interest earnings, and miscellaneous receipts. BIT coordinates with economic development organizations at the federal and local
level, as well as with private enterprises, to drive a unified approach to grow Oregon’s economy.

Infrastructure
The Infrastructure Finance Division (IFD) assists communities in building and maintaining critical infrastructure through low-cost financing,
grants, technical assistance, and capacity building. IFD has dedicated funding sources for municipal infrastructure, water, and wastewater
facilities to ensure compliance with federal law; seismic rehabilitation for schools and emergency facilities; port and airport facilities; levee
improvements; marine navigation improvements; and other facilities supporting economic and community development. IFD programs are a
primary source for the state’s small and rural communities to access low-cost financing for vital public infrastructure projects. As communities
and entities identify projects, IFD staff work directly with applicants to develop preliminary proposals before proceeding to complete program
applications.

IFD programs are largely revolving loan funds (SPWF, Water/Wastewater Fund, Port Revolving Loan Fund, and Brownfields Redevelopment
Fund). Revolving loan funds have dedicated funding from repaid loans and interest earnings by statutorily identified accounts. Federal Funds
support the EPA Safe Drinking Water, EPA Brownfields and HUD Community Development Block Grant programs, and the federal Infrastructure
Investment and Jobs Act, which provided additional funding in the 2021-23 and 2023-25 biennia. State match to federal programs (Safe
Drinking Water, CDBG, and FEMA emergency grants) is provided by the Special Public Works Fund. Periodic special allocations of Lottery funds
or one-time bond sales recapitalize state infrastructure program funds. IFD also receives general obligation bonds (Article XI-M & XI-N)
revenues for the Seismic Rehabilitation grant program.

Film and Video
The Oregon Film and Video Office (OFVO) is a semi-independent agency recruiting and facilitating film and television production in Oregon. The
OFVO carries out this mission by being a first point of entry for both out-of-state and in-state production companies and by using marketing
and sales tools, a website, blog, newsletter and social media presence, a large locations database, and other key recruitment tools to enhance
the industry’s revenues, profile, and reputation within Oregon and among the industry internationally. This is done through research,
responsiveness and the close administration of its programs, which include the Oregon Production Investment Fund, Greenlight Oregon Labor
Rebate Program, the Oregon Film Trail, and the programs and grants of the Creative Opportunity Program.

OFVO is a semi-independent state agency; as such, its staff are not state employees. OFVO staff is under the direction of a five-member board
appointed by the Governor. The Film and Video Office exists in OBDD’s budget as a transfer of Lottery Funds via a Special Payment from the
Department.

The Subcommittee recommended a budget of $1,541,600 Lottery Funds. This program has no positions. The OFVO is funded at the 2025-27
current service level.

Arts
The mission of the Arts Division is to increase access to culture for all Oregonians, to promote the talent of Oregon’s youth and to play a role in
job creation and retention. The Arts Division includes both the Arts Commission and the Oregon Cultural Trust (OCT). The Arts Commission
develops statewide policy and provides services to arts organizations, artists, and communities. The Commission also provides statewide
funding to arts organizations, individual artists, and arts programs through grants and special initiatives. Through Oregon’s Cultural Tax Credit,
the OCT encourages public participation in contributing directly to one or more of 1,600+ approved cultural nonprofit organizations and then
making a matching contribution to the OCT. In recent years the OCT averaged 10,000 contributions annually. Over the OCT’s 21-year history, it
has raised a total of $79 million and has made more than 10,000 grant awards. Contributions to the OCT grew on average by 7% annually
between 2015 and 2023. Total contribution in fiscal year 2024 total was $5.2 million.

The Subcommittee recommended a budget of $26,694,044 total funds and 10 positions (10.00 FTE). This represents a 55.1% increase from the
2025-27 current service level; positions and FTE for the division are maintained at the 2023-25 current service level. The Subcommittee also
