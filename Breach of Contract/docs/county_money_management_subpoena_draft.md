# County Money Management Subpoena Draft

This draft outlines the subpoena targets most likely to produce the missing proof concerning `Jemiliah`, Clackamas County `Money Management`, representative-payee influence, rent-payment instructions, and county-side involvement in Jane Cortez's housing choices.

## 1. Primary Target

### Custodian of Records

Clackamas County Social Services  
Money Management Program / Representative Payee Program  
or other appropriate county records custodian

## 2. Documents to Request

1. The complete case file for Jane Cortez maintained by Clackamas County Social Services, Money Management, representative-payee, or related county programs from January 1, 2025 through April 30, 2026.
2. All notes, communications, call logs, emails, text messages, task entries, or CRM/case-management records concerning Jane Cortez's rent, housing, voucher, portability, or housing-location decisions.
3. All records identifying any county employee or agent named `Jemiliah`, `Jemiliah`, `Jamila`, `Jamilah`, or similar who communicated with Benjamin Barber, Jane Cortez, HACC, or any housing provider concerning Jane Cortez.
4. All records reflecting any statement that Jane Cortez's housing or residence would be endangered if she pursued housing or voucher use outside Clackamas County, including at Waterleaf in Multnomah County.
5. All records reflecting any instruction or recommendation that Benjamin Barber should stop paying Jane Cortez's rent.
6. All records reflecting any representation that a check or payment would be sent to HACC on Jane Cortez's behalf.
7. All payment ledgers, check registers, accounting records, payment authorizations, and related notes concerning Jane Cortez's rent for January through April 2026.
8. All communications between county personnel and HACC concerning Jane Cortez's rent, representative-payee status, portability, relocation, or housing decisions.
9. All communications between county personnel and any landlord or housing provider concerning Jane Cortez, including Waterleaf, Blossom, Parkside Heights, Easton Ridge Apartments, and Quantum Residential.
10. All policies, manuals, desk guides, and training materials governing county communications about a participant's rent, voucher, portability, or housing-location choices.

## 3. Metadata / Audit Trail Requests

If the county uses a case-management or accounting system, also request:

1. audit logs showing who created, edited, or viewed Jane Cortez's file;
2. timestamps for entries concerning rent, Waterleaf, portability, or Money Management communications;
3. user identifiers for staff who entered notes or payment instructions; and
4. document metadata for any payment authorization, check issuance, or representative-payee communication.

## 4. Custodian Topics For Testimony

If testimony is needed, the custodian or Rule 30(b)(6)-style designee should be prepared to explain:

1. what the Money Management Program was doing for Jane Cortez;
2. who communicated with Benjamin Barber;
3. whether any county worker told him to stop paying Jane Cortez's rent;
4. whether any check was actually sent to HACC;
5. whether county staff discouraged use of housing outside Clackamas County; and
6. how county staff communicated with HACC about Jane Cortez's rent and housing decisions.

## 5. Why This Matters

These records could prove:

- the identity of `Jemiliah` or the similarly named county worker;
- whether county-side payment instructions were real and not imagined;
- whether county personnel were steering or discouraging out-of-county housing;
- whether HACC was aware of and relying on county-side housing or payment input; and
- whether Jane Cortez's dependency, disability-related limitations, and need for assistance were already well known across county systems.

## 6. Best Service Order

The best sequence is usually:

1. serve HACC discovery first if you want to pin HACC down on what it knew;
2. serve the county subpoena next if HACC claims the payment or representative-payee issue was outside its control or knowledge; and
3. use the county production to test whether HACC and county Social Services were acting on the same understanding of Jane Cortez's housing and payment situation.
