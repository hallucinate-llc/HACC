# County Money Management Discovery Requests

This focused discovery set targets Clackamas County Social Services / Money Management issues that appear in the preserved HACC correspondence concerning Jane Cortez's rent handling, representative-payee influence, portability choices, and voucher-related housing decisions.

## Core Theory

The preserved record supports the following narrow theory:

- by January 6, 2026, HACC had been told that Jane Cortez's Clackamas County representative-payee-side contact was discouraging use of a tenant protection voucher at Waterleaf in Multnomah County;
- by February 5, 2026, HACC had been told that `Jemiliah at money management` said she would send a check and told Benjamin Barber not to keep paying Jane Cortez's rent; and
- Kati Tilton responded that `The Money Management Program is operated out of Clackamas County Social Services.`

So the next proof target is whether county-side representative-payee or Money Management personnel were influencing Jane Cortez's rent, voucher, portability, or housing decisions in a way HACC knew about, relied on, or failed to correct.

## Requests for Production to Clackamas County Social Services / Money Management

1. Produce all records concerning Jane Cortez held by Clackamas County Social Services, Money Management, representative-payee staff, or any related county unit from January 1, 2025 through April 30, 2026.
2. Produce all records identifying every county employee, contractor, volunteer, case manager, representative payee, or Money Management worker assigned to, communicating about, or handling matters for Jane Cortez during that period.
3. Produce all records showing whether any county employee named `Jemiliah`, `Jemiliah`, `Jamila`, `Jamilah`, or a substantially similar name communicated with Benjamin Barber, Jane Cortez, HACC, or any housing provider concerning Jane Cortez.
4. Produce all records reflecting any communication that Jane Cortez's housing, voucher, portability, or ability to reside somewhere would be endangered if a voucher were used outside Clackamas County, including at Waterleaf or in Multnomah County.
5. Produce all records reflecting any communication that Benjamin Barber should stop paying Jane Cortez's rent.
6. Produce all records reflecting any statement that a check or payment would be sent to HACC on Jane Cortez's behalf.
7. Produce all records showing whether any such check or payment was actually issued, mailed, transmitted, logged, voided, stopped, or returned.
8. Produce all ledgers, payment authorizations, check registers, account notes, accounting records, and internal communications concerning Jane Cortez's rent payments from January 1, 2026 through April 30, 2026.
9. Produce all records reflecting communication between Clackamas County Social Services / Money Management and HACC concerning Jane Cortez's rent, arrears, voucher use, portability, relocation, household composition, or housing decisions.
10. Produce all records reflecting communication between county-side representative-payee or Money Management personnel and Waterleaf Apartments, Blossom, Parkside Heights, Easton Ridge Apartments, Quantum Residential, or any other housing provider concerning Jane Cortez.
11. Produce all policies, manuals, desk guides, training materials, or instructions governing how county representative-payee or Money Management personnel may communicate about a participant's rent, voucher, portability, relocation, or housing decisions.
12. Produce all policies, manuals, or instructions governing when county-side representative-payee or Money Management personnel may direct another person to stop paying a participant's rent.
13. Produce all records reflecting whether Benjamin Barber was identified to county personnel as Jane Cortez's caregiver, live-in aide, guardian ad litem, rent payer, or housing advocate.
14. Produce all records reflecting whether county personnel understood Jane Cortez to have disability-related limitations affecting independent housing decisions, rent management, or need for assistance.
15. Produce all records reflecting any discussion of whether Jane Cortez should remain in Clackamas County rather than pursue housing in Multnomah County.

2. State whether any county employee or agent told Benjamin Barber that Jane Cortez's housing or residence would be endangered if the household used a voucher at Waterleaf or outside Clackamas County, and if so identify the speaker, the date, and the substance of the statement.
3. State whether any county employee or agent told Benjamin Barber not to keep paying Jane Cortez's rent, and if so identify the speaker, the date, and the basis for that instruction.
4. State whether any county employee or agent represented that a check or payment would be sent to HACC on Jane Cortez's behalf, and if so identify the speaker, the date, the amount, and what happened next.
5. State whether any county payment for Jane Cortez's rent was issued, attempted, or expected in February 2026, and if so identify all supporting records.
6. State whether county personnel communicated with HACC about Jane Cortez's voucher portability, Waterleaf, Multnomah County housing, or any concern about Jane Cortez leaving Clackamas County.
7. State whether county personnel understood Benjamin Barber to be acting as Jane Cortez's caregiver, live-in aide, guardian ad litem, or primary housing helper, and if so when that understanding arose.
8. State whether county personnel were aware of Jane Cortez's disability-related limitations affecting rent handling, independent living, or housing decision-making, and identify the basis for that awareness.
9. State whether county personnel communicated with any housing provider or landlord about Jane Cortez during the relevant period, and identify each provider, the date, and the subject of the communication.
10. State the full legal and programmatic basis for any county effort to discourage, influence, or comment upon whether Jane Cortez should use housing outside Clackamas County.

## Best Use

This set is designed to prove one or more of the following:

- county-side personnel were actively influencing Jane Cortez's housing and rent decisions;
- HACC had written notice of that influence;
- county personnel may have discouraged portability or out-of-county housing;
- county personnel may have altered or attempted to alter the expected rent-payment flow; and
- those county-side actions support the broader notice, dependency, caregiver, and process-interference theories in the FED case.
