## 1. Core Theme

The short version is:

- HACC may have been allowed to request some targeted verification,
- but it was not allowed to turn the caregiver and accommodation issue into a broad, stale, delay-producing financial investigation,
- and then use the resulting delay against the household in an eviction posture.

## 2. Very Short Opening

Your Honor, this defense is not that HACC could never ask for documents. The narrower point is that once HACC knew Jane Cortez was disabled, knew Benjamin Barber was acting as her caregiver, and knew county-side Money Management was already involved in her rent handling, HACC had to keep the verification process tied to what was actually necessary. Instead, the record shows repeated and expanding demands for financial and business records, including records tied to companies said not to have existed for about ten years, while the voucher and accommodation process kept slipping.

## 4. Best Bench Narrative

The Court can treat this as a sequence:

1. HACC knew there was a disability-related accommodation and caregiver issue.
2. HACC also knew county-side representative-payee and Money Management personnel were affecting Jane Cortez's rent handling.
3. HACC nevertheless expanded the process into repeated business and financial demands.
4. The household complained in writing, in real time, that those stale demands were delaying the move.
5. HACC then continued to rely on the incomplete transition and delay in the same general period that it was moving toward eviction.

## 5. Best Legal Framing

If the Court asks for the legal point, the answer is:

- a housing authority can ask for information reasonably necessary to evaluate eligibility or an accommodation request,
- but it must seek the least information necessary and cannot keep expanding the inquiry once the disability-related and caregiver issues are already on notice,
- especially where the result is delay in accommodation, voucher, and relocation processing.

## 6. Best One-Sentence Ask

The Court should not grant possession based on a relocation and accommodation process that HACC itself materially prolonged through overbroad and stale verification demands.

## 7. If The Court Pushes Back

If the Court says `but the housing authority can ask for financial documents`, the answer is:

- yes, some targeted verification may be allowed;
- the problem here is the scope, age, and persistence of the demands, and the fact that the record shows those demands were being used in a way that delayed the move rather than resolving a clearly defined current eligibility question.

## 8. Short Closing

This is why the verification issue matters. It is not a side dispute about paperwork. It is part of the reason the relocation and accommodation path remained incomplete, and HACC should not be allowed to benefit from delay that its own process materially created.
