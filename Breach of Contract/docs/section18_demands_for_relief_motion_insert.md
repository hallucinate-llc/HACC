## I. Federal Section 18 Duties Control Displacement Preconditions

Plaintiff/Defendant [party name] alleges the housing authority and related actors pursued demolition/disposition-related displacement without satisfying mandatory preconditions under Section 18.

Section 18 and its implementing regulations require, among other duties:

1. HUD written approval before demolition/disposition action.
- 24 CFR 970.7(a); 24 CFR 970.25(a).

2. 90-day pre-displacement notice to each affected family (except imminent health/safety threat cases).
- 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(A); 24 CFR 970.21(e)(1).

3. Relocation to comparable housing before demolition/disposition completion.
- 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(E); 24 CFR 970.21(b).

4. Comparable housing standards requiring HQS and area not generally less desirable.
- 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(A)(iii); 24 CFR 970.21(a).

5. Relocation expenses and counseling for displaced residents.
- 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(B), (D); 24 CFR 970.21(e)(2), (4).

6. Consultation with affected residents, resident councils/RAB, and local officials as part of valid approval.
- 42 U.S.C. 1437p(b)(2); 24 CFR 970.9(a); 24 CFR 970.7(a)(7), (14).

7. In disposition cases, resident purchase-opportunity timeline protections.
- 42 U.S.C. 1437p(c)(2); 24 CFR 970.9(b); 24 CFR 970.11.

## II. Application to This Record (Alleged)

1. HACC formally notified Defendant's household of Section 18 disposition by delivering the Hillside Park Apartments Phase II General Information Notice on September 19, 2024 by hand and certified mail, confirming that HUD had granted HACC permission to redevelop and triggering the Tenant Protection Voucher relocation process. (Exhibit M — HACC phase2 2024.pdf.)

2. Despite that triggering notice, no complete HUD approval packet, relocation plan, or consultation record has been produced to this household demonstrating compliance with the mandatory application contents required under 24 CFR 970.7(a), including the relocation plan with disability accommodations and timetable, consultation evidence, and board resolution timing.

3. 90-day advance written notice compliance is disputed: Exhibits A, B, and M show notice activity, but the comparability analysis and relocation timetable required by 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(A) and 24 CFR 970.21(e)(1) have not been provided.

4. No comparable replacement housing offer meeting disability and accessibility requirements was provided. The relocation option offered included a third-floor unit that is physically inaccessible for a household member with documented stair and mobility limitations. (Exhibit I — accommodation records; 24 CFR 970.21(a), 24 CFR part 8.)

5. No documented payment or advance of actual and reasonable relocation expenses has been provided to this household, despite the statutory and regulatory obligation to pay those costs. (42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(B); 24 CFR 970.21(e)(2).)

6. The Blossom and Community Apartments placement option — which was one of the relocation tracks identified by HACC — was processed through an HACC PBV waitlist (Exhibit C), but the application was not completed due to a packet-transmission failure at the Quantum Residential / Hillside Manor leasing office interface, as confirmed in HACC's own written communications. (Exhibit L.) HACC's own Administrative Plan additionally identifies Quantum Residential as the property management company for a PHA-owned HAP contract development in Clackamas County, and classifies Blossom & Community Apartments as replacement housing for a demolished HACC project under the same Section 18 relocation framework.

7. The household was not provided with relocation counseling documenting alternative options, comparability assessments, or assistance with the failed Blossom intake process. (24 CFR 970.21(e)(4).)

8. Demolition-related displacement proceeded against this household before all relocation preconditions were met, in that the household remains in displacement/eviction proceedings without having received a compliant comparable housing offer, required relocation expenses, or a lawful alternative placement. (42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(E); 24 CFR 970.21(b).)

## III. Requested Relief

Movant requests the Court enter an order:

1. Staying eviction/displacement-related enforcement until respondents prove Section 18 compliance for this household.
2. Requiring production within [X] days of the full Section 18 application and approval packet, including:
- HUD approval letter and all submitted exhibits;
- relocation plan and comparability analysis;
- consultation records and written comments;
- 90-day notices and proof of service;
- accommodation request logs and determinations;
- disposition appraisal/proceeds documentation (if disposition is at issue).

3. Requiring respondents to offer and document a lawful comparable housing option that includes required disability accommodations.
4. Requiring payment/advance of actual and reasonable relocation expenses and relocation counseling as required by federal law.
5. Awarding any additional equitable or statutory relief the Court finds just and proper.
