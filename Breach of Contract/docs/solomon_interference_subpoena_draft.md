# Subpoena Draft Re Solomon Interference And January 2026 Lease Removal

This draft provides third-party and records-custodian subpoena targets for the Solomon-interference / lease-removal issue. It is meant to complement the HACC-directed requests in [solomon_interference_formal_discovery_draft.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/docs/solomon_interference_formal_discovery_draft.md).

## Purpose

These subpoenas are intended to obtain records that HACC may not voluntarily produce, including:

- phone-system logs showing calls to or from staff who handled the lease change;
- visitor and building-access records showing in-person contact;
- CRM or case-management audit logs showing who entered, changed, or approved household-composition actions;
- and any external-system metadata identifying the source of the January 2026 lease-side action.

## Subpoena 1: Phone-System Custodian

### Target

- HACC or Clackamas County phone-system custodian
- any telecom or hosted-voice vendor maintaining call-detail records, voicemail logs, or extension-level records for HACC housing staff

### Documents to Request

1. Call-detail records for all inbound and outbound calls involving the HACC employees who handled the Barber/Cortez household lease, bifurcation, relocation, or household-composition issues from October 1, 2025 through February 28, 2026.
2. Logs identifying date, time, caller number, called number, extension, duration, disposition, and voicemail status for each such call.
3. Voicemail logs, voicemail metadata, and any preserved voicemail audio or transcript associated with those calls.
4. Call-routing records, hunt-group logs, transfer logs, or extension-routing records showing whether calls from Solomon Samuel Barber or his number were routed to HACC housing staff.
5. Any available caller-ID or ANI records reflecting communications from or associated with Solomon Samuel Barber.
6. Any retention-policy records sufficient to explain what phone records existed for the relevant period and whether any such records were deleted or are no longer available.

### Purpose of Subpoena 1

- prove or disprove whether Solomon called HACC staff about lease or household matters;
- identify which staff members spoke with him;
- establish whether off-record phone contact explains the lease-side removal sequence.

## Subpoena 2: Visitor / Building Access Custodian

### Target

- Clackamas County facilities or security records custodian
- any contractor or building-management entity maintaining visitor sign-in, badge, or access logs for HACC offices

### Documents to Request

1. Visitor logs, sign-in sheets, reception logs, appointment records, badge logs, and access-control logs for HACC offices or related county offices used by HACC housing staff from October 1, 2025 through February 28, 2026.
2. All records identifying visits by Solomon Samuel Barber, Sam Barber, Samuel Barber, or any visitor using those names or closely associated contact information.
3. All records identifying visits relating to the Barber/Cortez household, bifurcation, lease changes, or restraining-order issues.
4. Any notes made by reception staff or security staff concerning visitor purpose, destination, or staff contact for such visits.
5. Any available surveillance retention records or camera-location logs sufficient to identify whether footage once existed for relevant visits, even if the footage is no longer retained.

### Purpose of Subpoena 2

- prove or disprove whether Solomon appeared in person at HACC or a related county office;
- identify which staff he contacted;
- show whether in-person off-record contact may have influenced lease administration.

## Subpoena 3: CRM / Case-Management / Housing-Administration System Custodian

### Target

- any vendor or custodian maintaining the CRM, case-management, housing-management, or household-composition system used by HACC for this household

### Documents to Request

1. Complete audit logs for all entries concerning Benjamin Jay Barber, Jane Kay Cortez, Julio Cortez, the Barber/Cortez household, or the household's lease composition from October 1, 2025 through February 28, 2026.
2. Metadata showing creation date, modification date, user ID, source workstation or terminal, and approval history for the January 1, 2026 lease amendment / household-composition entry.
3. All system notes, comments, linked tasks, event history, and status-change logs relating to the December 4, 2025 lease adjustment, the January 1, 2026 lease amendment, and any January 2026 removal or restoration actions.
4. All records showing the stated reason code, free-text comment, or source note for Benjamin Jay Barber's January 2026 lease removal.
5. All records showing the stated reason code, free-text comment, or source note for restoring any restrained party to the lease.
6. All retention and deletion-policy records governing these data fields and audit logs.

### Purpose of Subpoena 3

- identify the user who entered or approved the lease-side change;
- determine whether the system captured who requested the action;
- preserve metadata that may reveal whether the action was based on a call, in-person contact, or undocumented note.

## Optional Subpoena 4: County Counsel / Records Custodian

### Target

- county records custodian, limited to non-privileged logs and administrative routing records

### Documents to Request

1. Non-privileged routing logs, intake logs, or administrative records showing whether questions were submitted to county counsel or supervisors concerning who could influence lease changes affecting the Barber/Cortez household.
2. Non-privileged records showing whether staff sought guidance regarding restraining-order or bifurcation issues affecting household composition.

### Purpose of Subpoena 4

- show whether HACC internally recognized that the lease-change source or process was problematic;
- identify additional custodians and decision-makers.

## Best Immediate Priority

If only one subpoena can go out first, the highest-value order is:

1. CRM / case-management audit logs
2. phone-system logs
3. visitor / access logs

The CRM audit logs are likely to be the fastest route to learning who entered the January 2026 change and whether the system captured the source of the request.

## Best Current Use

These subpoenas are designed to convert the current issue from an inference-based theory into a records-based chronology showing:

- who contacted HACC,
- who changed the lease,
- what source they relied on,
- and whether a restrained non-household actor was influencing lease administration despite the restraining-order framework.
