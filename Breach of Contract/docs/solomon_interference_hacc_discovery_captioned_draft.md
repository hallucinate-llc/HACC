TO: Plaintiff Housing Authority of Clackamas County, and its attorneys of record, if any:

Pursuant to the Oregon Rules of Civil Procedure and any applicable local rules or court orders, Defendants Benjamin Jay Barber and Jane Kay Cortez request that Plaintiff respond to the following Requests for Production, Interrogatories, and Requests for Admission concerning the January 2026 lease-side removal of Benjamin Jay Barber, the related household-composition changes, and any communications or influence by Solomon Samuel Barber.

## Requests for Production

1. Produce the complete household lease-administration, lease-change, household-composition, and bifurcation file for Benjamin Jay Barber, Jane Kay Cortez, and Julio Cortez from October 1, 2025 through February 28, 2026.
2. Produce all records identifying the person or persons who requested, initiated, authorized, approved, entered, processed, reviewed, or discussed any January 2026 removal of Benjamin Jay Barber from the lease.
3. Produce all records identifying the person or persons who requested, initiated, authorized, approved, entered, processed, reviewed, or discussed any restoration of any restrained party to the lease during the same period.
4. Produce all internal case notes, housing-management notes, desk notes, diary notes, CRM entries, task logs, and timeline entries concerning the household's lease composition or lease changes.
5. Produce all emails, text messages, letters, instant messages, Teams messages, memoranda, and internal communications concerning requests to remove, restore, add, or exclude any member of the household from the lease.
6. Produce all records showing who requested or supported the December 4, 2025 lease adjustment referenced by Benjamin Barber in his December 10, 2025 communication to HACC.
7. Produce all drafts, routing slips, review comments, approval records, version history, and metadata for the January 1, 2026 lease amendment / household-composition document.
8. Produce all records reflecting the factual basis for removing Benjamin Jay Barber from the lease in January 2026.
9. Produce all records reflecting the factual basis for later restoring Benjamin Jay Barber to the lease.
10. Produce all records reflecting who Plaintiff considered to be `the restrained party` referred to in the January 2026 lease sequence.
11. Produce all communications to or from Plaintiff personnel that mention Solomon Samuel Barber, Sam Barber, Samuel Barber, stepfather abuse, bifurcation, protective order, restraining order, lease removal, lease restoration, or household composition for this household.
12. Produce all call logs, voicemail logs, visitor logs, front-desk logs, handwritten notes, appointment records, or contact logs showing direct or indirect contact from Solomon Samuel Barber concerning the household.
13. Produce all records reflecting any communication from a non-household person concerning who should remain on, be removed from, or be restored to the household lease.
14. Produce all records reflecting Plaintiff's understanding of whether restraining-order or protective-order issues limited who Plaintiff could communicate with about lease administration for this household.
15. Produce all communications between Plaintiff staff and county counsel, supervisors, or decision-makers concerning whether a restrained person or a non-household person could influence lease changes affecting this household.

## Interrogatories

1. Identify every person who requested, authorized, approved, entered, processed, reviewed, or discussed Benjamin Jay Barber's January 2026 lease removal.
2. State every fact on which Plaintiff relied in removing Benjamin Jay Barber from the lease in January 2026.
3. Identify every person from whom Plaintiff received information, instructions, pressure, or a request concerning that removal.
4. State whether Plaintiff contends Benjamin Jay Barber requested his own removal from the lease in January 2026; if so, state all facts supporting that contention and identify all supporting records.
5. State whether Plaintiff contends Jane Kay Cortez requested Benjamin Jay Barber's removal from the lease in January 2026; if so, state all facts supporting that contention and identify all supporting records.
6. State whether Plaintiff contends Julio Cortez requested Benjamin Jay Barber's removal from the lease in January 2026; if so, state all facts supporting that contention and identify all supporting records.
7. State whether Plaintiff contends any non-household person requested or influenced Benjamin Jay Barber's January 2026 lease removal; if so, identify that person and state all supporting facts.
8. State whether Plaintiff received any direct or indirect communication from Solomon Samuel Barber concerning the household, lease composition, bifurcation, restraining orders, continued occupancy, or any lease change from October 1, 2025 through March 31, 2026; if so, identify each communication by date, method, participants, and substance.
9. State what Plaintiff meant by the allegation that in January 2026 it `removed Benjamin Barber from the lease and restored the restrained party to the lease.`
10. State the complete factual and procedural basis on which Plaintiff concluded that the January 1, 2026 lease amendment / household-composition change should be effective as of January 1, 2026.
11. Identify every Plaintiff employee who spoke by phone or in person with Solomon Samuel Barber from October 1, 2025 through March 31, 2026.
12. Identify every document, note, email, system entry, or communication in Plaintiff's possession that refers to Solomon Samuel Barber in relation to this household.
