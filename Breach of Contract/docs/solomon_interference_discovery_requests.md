# Discovery Requests Re Solomon Interference And Lease Removal

This discovery set is targeted to one question:

- who caused or requested the January 2026 lease-side removal of Benjamin Barber, and did Solomon Samuel Barber continue influencing HACC despite the restraining-order framework?

## Purpose

These requests are designed to prove or disprove the following:

- whether HACC received any communication from Solomon Samuel Barber about lease or household matters;
- whether HACC relied on any off-record or undocumented contact to remove Benjamin Barber from the lease;
- whether any staff member recorded who requested the lease change;
- whether HACC restored or maintained a restrained party's lease status based on improper contact;
- whether HACC's lease adjustment and household-composition actions were influenced by a restrained non-household actor.

## Requests for Production to HACC

1. Produce the complete lease-change, household-composition, and bifurcation file for the Barber/Cortez household from October 1, 2025 through February 28, 2026.
2. Produce all records identifying who requested, initiated, approved, or entered any lease-side removal, restoration, household-composition change, or lease amendment affecting Benjamin Barber, Jane Cortez, Julio Cortez, or any restrained party during that period.
3. Produce all internal case notes, housing-management notes, call logs, contemporaneous staff notes, diary entries, CRM records, and task logs concerning lease changes for the household.
4. Produce all communications to or from HACC personnel that mention Solomon Samuel Barber, Sam Barber, Samuel Barber, stepfather abuse, bifurcation, protective order, restraining order, or requests to remove or restore any person on the lease.
5. Produce all records showing who requested or supported the December 4, 2025 lease adjustment referenced in the household's December 10, 2025 email.
6. Produce all drafts, notes, routing slips, approval records, and metadata for the January 1, 2026 lease amendment / household-composition document.
7. Produce all call records, voicemail logs, Teams or Zoom records, handwritten notes, visitor logs, and front-desk logs showing contact from or about Solomon Samuel Barber concerning the household.
8. Produce all records reflecting any HACC belief that Solomon Samuel Barber remained authorized to influence lease or household decisions after the household raised restraining-order and bifurcation issues.
9. Produce all records identifying the factual basis for removing Benjamin Barber from the lease in January 2026 and later restoring him.
10. Produce all records reflecting who HACC considered to be `the restrained party` referred to in the January 2026 lease sequence.
11. Produce all records of communications between HACC and county counsel, HACC supervisors, or housing-management staff concerning whether a restrained person or a non-household actor could influence lease changes affecting this household.
12. Produce all records concerning HACC's understanding of whether restraining-order issues limited with whom HACC should communicate regarding lease administration for this household.

## Interrogatories to HACC

1. Identify every person who requested, authorized, approved, entered, processed, reviewed, or discussed Benjamin Barber's January 2026 lease removal.
2. State every fact supporting HACC's decision to remove Benjamin Barber from the lease in January 2026.
3. State the identity of each person from whom HACC received information, direction, or pressure bearing on that removal.
4. State whether HACC received any communication from Solomon Samuel Barber, directly or indirectly, concerning the household, lease composition, bifurcation, restraining orders, or continued occupancy; if so, identify each communication by date, method, participants, and substance.
5. State whether HACC contends Benjamin Barber requested his own removal; if so, identify every supporting document and witness.
6. State whether HACC contends Jane Cortez requested Benjamin Barber's removal; if so, identify every supporting document and witness.
7. State whether HACC contends Julio Cortez requested Benjamin Barber's removal; if so, identify every supporting document and witness.
8. State whether HACC contends any non-household person requested or influenced the January 2026 lease change; if so, identify that person and all supporting facts.
9. State what HACC meant, in the household's January 2026 lease sequence, by `restored the restrained party to the lease`.
10. State the complete basis on which HACC concluded that the January 1, 2026 lease amendment / household-composition change should take effect as of that date.
11. Identify every HACC employee who spoke by phone or in person with Solomon Samuel Barber from October 1, 2025 through March 31, 2026.
12. Identify every document, note, email, or system entry that refers to Solomon Samuel Barber in relation to the Barber/Cortez household.

## Requests for Admission to HACC

1. Admit that Benjamin Barber did not request his own January 2026 lease removal.
2. Admit that HACC possesses no written request from Benjamin Barber asking to be removed from the lease in January 2026.
3. Admit that HACC possesses no written request from Jane Cortez asking that Benjamin Barber be removed from the lease in January 2026.
4. Admit that HACC possesses no written request from Julio Cortez asking that Benjamin Barber be removed from the lease in January 2026.
5. Admit that HACC altered the household's lease or household-composition status before clearly documenting for the household who had requested the change.
6. Admit that HACC was aware of restraining-order or protective-order issues affecting the household before January 1, 2026.
7. Admit that HACC later treated the household's composition inconsistently between the January 1, January 12, and January 27, 2026 records.

3. HACC's communications with Solomon Samuel Barber, if any.

## Best Immediate Targets

If discovery must be prioritized, the highest-value items are:

1. every case note and call log tied to the January 2026 lease removal;
2. the author, approver, and metadata for the January 1, 2026 lease amendment;
3. any record naming who requested Benjamin's removal;
4. any HACC communication with Solomon Samuel Barber;

## Best Current Use

These requests are designed to convert the current `on information and belief` theory into either:

- a directly documented claim that Solomon communicated with HACC and caused the lease change; or
- a directly documented claim that HACC changed the lease without any lawful household request and without documenting a valid source.
