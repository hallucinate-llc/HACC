# Oregon Rule and Citation Addendum (Joinder and Third-Party Complaint)

This addendum is designed to be attached to, or integrated into, your motion packet.

Important: Confirm each rule citation and local supplement in your exact court and case type before filing.

## 1) Oregon Civil Procedure Framework

Use these as your primary state-court anchors (verify current text and applicability):

1. ORCP 22 - Counterclaims and cross-claims.
2. ORCP 29 - Required joinder of persons needed for just adjudication.
3. ORCP 30 - Permissive joinder of parties.
4. ORCP 33 C - Third-party practice (impleader / third-party complaint).
5. ORCP 21 A and related timing provisions - responsive pleading/motion interplay.

## 2) Core Argument Structure Using Oregon Rules

### A. Joinder under ORCP 29 and/or ORCP 30

1. Quantum Residential's alleged conduct is directly tied to the same housing transition facts at issue in the eviction case.
2. Complete relief and full factual adjudication are not practical without Quantum's participation.
3. Permissive joinder is proper because common questions of fact and causation predominate.

### B. Third-Party Practice under ORCP 33 C

1. Third-party claims arise from the same transaction or occurrence set as the underlying eviction dispute.
2. Third-party pleading promotes efficiency and avoids multiple suits over one relocation chronology.
3. Any prejudice from joinder is outweighed by risk of inconsistent findings if tried separately.

### C. Equity and Case-Management Grounds

1. Defendant alleges reliance on promised relocation pathways.
2. Defendant alleges failures in those pathways materially contributed to current eviction posture.
3. Case-management efficiency favors one coordinated record and one set of factual findings.

## 3) Housing-Substance Authorities to Preserve in Background Section

These are not substitutes for procedural joinder rules, but they support materiality of the relocation/accommodation facts:

1. 42 U.S.C. 3604(f) (reasonable accommodation and disability-related housing protections).
2. 42 U.S.C. 3617 (interference/retaliation context).
3. 24 C.F.R. 982.316 and related voucher/aide provisions (as applicable to your fact record).
4. ORS 659A.145, ORS 659A.421, ORS 659A.425, ORS 659A.885 (state housing discrimination framework).

## 4) Suggested Paragraph Block for Motion

Defendant seeks joinder and leave for third-party pleading under ORCP 29, ORCP 30, and ORCP 33 C. The proposed third-party claims against Quantum Residential arise from the same series of housing and relocation events that form the factual basis of the pending eviction and defenses. Defendant alleges that promised relocation pathways, including Parkside Heights processing and voucher portability, were not completed as represented, and that these failures materially contributed to the alleged tenancy default posture. Joinder and third-party practice are necessary to afford complete relief, avoid inconsistent adjudications, and promote judicial economy.
