# Illusory Options / Promissory Estoppel Memo

This memo supports the narrower FED theory that HACC offered relocation pathways that were too incomplete, conditional, or delayed to function as real timely alternatives to displacement by the end of March 2026.

## Core Point

The strongest formulation is not that HACC had already formed a final executed lease contract with Home Forward, Parkside, or Quantum. The stronger and safer formulation is that HACC identified three supposed relocation pathways, induced the household to act on them, and then pressed toward eviction while each path remained materially incomplete. On this record, those options were illusory in operation even if some preliminary contractual or administrative steps had begun.

## Best Current Timeline

1. On March 19-20, 2026, HACC was only then issuing the one-bedroom voucher and told the household that Jane Cortez had to sign and return it so HACC could start the port process. The record therefore supports the point that the Multnomah County portability path was not already complete; it was still just beginning at that stage.
2. On March 25-26, 2026, HACC's own housing-options summary still described the Home Forward path as needing further contact, intake, orientation, and unit search. That means the port to Multnomah County had not yet been finished.
3. On March 29-30, 2026, Defendants submitted the Parkside Heights application and Parkside acknowledged receipt, but Parkside also said the requested two-bedroom units were still under construction and estimated occupancy was not until the end of April 2026.
4. The Blossom / Quantum path had already been delayed for months, and HACC later acknowledged that Quantum had received the intake packet but had not provided it onward to HACC.
5. HACC still pursued eviction on or about March 31, 2026 while these pathways remained incomplete.

## Why This Supports Estoppel

Promissory or equitable estoppel is strongest here as a reliance theory:

- HACC represented that relocation pathways existed and could be pursued.
- Defendants relied on those representations by pursuing porting, submitting applications, and providing packet materials.
- HACC should reasonably have expected that reliance.
- HACC then sought possession while the paths it had directed the household into were still conditional, delayed, or unfinished.

That makes the better argument:

- HACC cannot point to noncompletion of those same pathways as if Defendants simply failed to act;
- HACC is bound at minimum by promissory or equitable estoppel not to treat those unfinished pathways as completed opportunities that justified eviction;
- and the offered options were illusory in practice because they did not function as real, timely, usable alternatives to displacement by the stated move deadline.

## Specific Path Analysis

### 1. Multnomah County / Home Forward portability path

This is not strongest as a final contract theory. It is strongest as an unfinished administrative relocation path. HACC's own March 20 communications show the voucher was only then being issued and the port process was only then going to start. HACC's later March 25-26 options summary still described additional Home Forward intake, orientation, and unit-search steps. So the path was not complete.

### 2. Parkside Heights path

This is strongest as an application-processing and reliance path, not a final lease contract. Defendants submitted the application after HACC identified Parkside as an available route, but Parkside acknowledged receipt while also saying the two-bedroom units were still under construction through the end of April. That made the option too uncertain and delayed to serve as a timely completed relocation alternative before the end-of-March eviction pressure.

### 3. Blossom / Quantum path

This is strongest as an intake-obstruction theory. Defendants submitted the packet, but HACC later said Quantum had not provided it onward. So this path was not merely unfinished; the record supports that it was actively obstructed.

## Safest Pleading Language

The best pleading language is:

- "too conditional, incomplete, or delayed to function as real timely alternatives to displacement"
- "illusory in operation"
- "unfinished relocation pathways"

The least safe formulation is:

- claiming a fully executed final lease contract already existed when the documents do not clearly show that.
