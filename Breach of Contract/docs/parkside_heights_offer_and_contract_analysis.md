# Parkside Heights Offer and Contract Analysis

This memo focuses on a narrower question than the broader Section 18 relocation theories: whether the Parkside Heights path created a contractually binding relationship, and whether the household satisfied its side by submitting the application.

## Short Answer

On the current record, there is strong evidence that HACC presented Parkside Heights as a real relocation option and that you substantially performed the next step HACC identified by submitting a Parkside Heights application. There is also direct evidence that Parkside Heights acknowledged receipt of that application.

What the record does **not** yet show is a completed, contractually binding lease or final housing agreement for a Parkside Heights unit. The available documents look more like:

1. an HACC-presented relocation option,
2. an invitation to apply / conditional housing opportunity,
3. a completed application by the household,
4. and an acknowledgement from Parkside Heights that the application was received, while noting that the requested two-bedroom units were still under construction.

So the strongest current position is:

- **Yes**, there was at least a concrete offered pathway and an application-processing relationship.
- **Yes**, the record strongly supports that you performed the identified next step by turning in the application.
- **No, not yet clearly**, the present record does not prove that Parkside Heights or HACC entered a final binding lease contract obligating them to deliver a specific unit by a specific date.

## Was There a Contractually Binding Relationship?

## A. Strongest current answer: application-stage and reliance relationship

The present record supports at least an **application-stage relationship** and likely a **promissory/reliance relationship**:

1. HACC did not merely say "find housing somewhere." It identified Parkside Heights as a specific option and spelled out next steps.  
   Source: [message.json](/home/barberb/HACC/evidence/email_imports/starworks5-confirmed-case-import/0015-RE-HCV-Orientation-a0136cad0c5f44b984403575346f8d34-clackamas.us/message.json#L14)

2. The Parkside application shows the move was being pursued because of public-housing redevelopment and at HACC Operations Manager Kati Tilton's direction/referral.  
   Source file: [Application 3-29-2026 Parkside Heights.pdf](/home/barberb/HACC/evidence/history/Application%203-29-2026%20Parkside%20Heights.pdf)

3. Parkside then acknowledged the application and continued the conversation about unit availability.  
   Source: [message.json](/home/barberb/HACC/evidence/email_imports/solomon-live-fetch-2026/starworks5/gmail-import/0000000-0000999/msg_IA1PR12MB768640073BF7F45CE3CF649FB152A-IA1PR12MB7686.namprd12.prod.outlook.com_20260330_Re-Application/message.json#L12)

That is enough to argue:

- the Parkside pathway was not imaginary or illusory in the abstract,
- HACC induced reliance on it as part of the relocation process,
- and you performed the next required step by applying.

## B. Weaker current answer: not yet a final lease contract

What is missing for a classic binding lease contract is evidence of:

1. final approval,
2. a designated unit actually committed to you,
3. final rent and subsidy approval,
4. mutual assent to a lease term,
5. signatures on a rental agreement,
6. acceptance of deposit/hold terms, or
7. HACC approval of tenancy commencement.

The application itself cuts against the existence of a completed final contract because it says that **if the application is approved**, there will later be a 72-hour window to execute the rental agreement or hold deposit. That language strongly suggests:

- the application was an offer to be considered or an invitation to process,
- not the completed rental contract itself.

So if the question is "Did Parkside Heights and the household already have a final enforceable lease contract?" the current record does **not** establish that.

## C. But there may still be an enforceable relocation promise / estoppel theory

Even without a final lease, the facts are useful for:

- **promissory estoppel**
- **prevention / hindrance**
- **failure of an agreed relocation path**
- **illusory-promise / frustration / reliance** arguments

The strongest formulation is:

"HACC identified Parkside Heights as an available relocation path, directed the household into that path, the household submitted the required Parkside application, and neither HACC nor Parkside completed the path in time to prevent eviction/displacement."

That is not the same thing as proving a signed lease contract, but it is still powerful.

## B. Not necessarily full performance of every later condition

The other side might still argue that additional conditions remained, such as:

- approval screening,
- unit availability,
- subsidy/HACC approval,
- affordability calculations,
- reasonable-accommodation determination,
- rental-agreement execution,
- deposit or hold payment.

So the safest conclusion is:

- **You satisfied the application-submission step.**
- The record does **not yet** show that every later approval and execution step was completed.

## Litigation Value of the Parkside Evidence

The Parkside evidence is strongest for these arguments:

1. **Substantial compliance by the household**  
   You were not refusing to relocate; you were actively pursuing the path HACC identified.

2. **Prevention / hindrance**  
   If the relocation failed, it was not because you did nothing. The record shows submission and follow-up.

3. **Reliance / estoppel**  
   HACC represented Parkside as an available path; you relied on that representation by applying.

4. **Not a clean tenant default case**  
   The eviction timeline overlapped with a still-developing Parkside option, including acknowledgment from the property manager that two-bedroom units were still under construction with expected occupancy not until the end of April.

## Best Current Conclusion

The best evidence-supported position is:

- HACC offered Parkside Heights as a real relocation option, at minimum as a concrete path to pursue.
- You accepted that path at least to the extent required by submitting the Parkside application.
- Parkside Heights acknowledged receipt of the application.
- The current record does not yet prove a final lease contract or completed rental agreement.
- The record does strongly support a claim that you performed the required application step and that the Parkside path remained conditional, incomplete, or delayed for reasons not attributable solely to you.

## Key Missing Proof to Strengthen the Parkside Theory

1. Any HACC email expressly telling you to apply at Parkside or confirming referral completion.
2. Any Parkside screening result, approval, denial, waitlist notice, or hold notice.
3. Proof that the application fee was actually mailed/received/processed.
4. Any Parkside response after "Would you still be interested?"
5. Any HACC or Quantum record tying the Parkside application to the April 30 relocation deadline.
6. Any RTA, voucher approval, unit-hold record, or lease draft for Parkside.
