# Discovery Plan for Cherry Picking / Lemon Dropping / Race Steering Theories

## Purpose

This plan is designed to prove or disprove the following factual theories:

- whether the household's Blossom application was effectively `lemon dropped`
- whether other households were selectively advanced, i.e. `cherry picked`
- whether race, protected-activity complaints, or equity-targeting practices influenced those outcomes
- whether HACC, Quantum, or related county-linked housing actors certified nondiscrimination while operating differently in practice

## Core Questions

1. Was the household's Blossom application actually received?
2. If received, who handled it and what happened to it?
3. Was it delayed, parked, ignored, or rejected without a lawful written resolution?
4. Which other displaced households were moved forward during the same period?
5. What criteria were used to select, prioritize, interview, or place households?
6. Did race, BIPOC-targeting, equity scoring, or protected complaints affect those decisions?
7. What nondiscrimination promises or certifications were made to Metro, SHS, HUD, or other funders?

Goal:

Ask for:

- paper application receipt logs

Cherry-picking / lemon-dropping value:

### 2. Comparative applicant pipeline records

Goal:

- determine whether other households were advanced while this household stalled

Ask for:

- anonymized or protective-order-governed list of Blossom applicants during the relevant period
- dates of application receipt
- dates of interview, contact, approval, denial, or placement
- reason codes for any denials or holds
- whether applicants were HACC-displaced households
- unit assignment dates
- any waitlist, ranking, or prioritization fields

Cherry-picking / lemon-dropping value:

- this is the most direct way to prove selective advancement or selective freezing-out

### 3. HACC referral and relocation records

Goal:

- prove how the household was routed and whether HACC knew the Blossom path was stalled

Ask for:

- referrals sent to Blossom, Parkside, Easton Ridge, and any other replacement-housing destinations
- internal relocation tracking sheets
- case-management notes
- relocation counseling logs
- communications with Quantum about packet status
- status reports on the household's relocation path
- records showing when HACC knew the packet had not been transmitted

Cherry-picking / lemon-dropping value:

- shows whether HACC kept the household in a broken path or redirected others differently

### 4. Selection, prioritization, and equity criteria

Goal:

- determine whether race-conscious or equity-driven criteria were actually used in housing access decisions

Ask for:

- written criteria for referral, prioritization, interview, and placement
- scoring rubrics
- equity criteria documents
- BIPOC-focused targeting guidance
- race-disaggregated dashboards used operationally
- implementation memos describing how to achieve racial-equity outcomes
- training materials given to HACC, SHS, Metro, or Quantum staff

Cherry-picking / lemon-dropping value:

- connects policy language to actual selection behavior

### 5. Nondiscrimination certifications and contract compliance

## Specific Discovery Requests by Theory

### To prove lemon dropping

Best requests:

- every intake log showing receipt of the application
- every internal note showing inactivity, hold, or non-transmission
- every deficiency or missing-document notice
- every communication showing no final decision was issued

Best fact to prove:

- the file was not fairly processed; it was stalled or effectively buried

### To prove cherry picking

Best requests:

- comparative applicant list
- interview schedules
- approval dates
- move-in dates
- waitlist rankings
- placement records for other displaced households

Best fact to prove:

- others in the same pipeline were affirmatively advanced while this household remained stuck

### To prove race steering / unequal treatment

Best requests:

- criteria or notes referencing race, BIPOC status, equity priorities, or targeted populations
- comparative treatment records
- staff communications discussing which households were preferred

Best fact to prove:

- race or race-coded policy objectives materially influenced who moved forward and who did not

### To prove civil-rights-fraud potential

Best requests:

- contract certifications
- grant assurances
- compliance attestations
- internal concern memos about legality of race-based practices

Best fact to prove:

- the actors certified compliance while knowingly operating differently

## Best Procedural Vehicles

Depending on posture, use:

- requests for production to HACC
- requests for production or subpoena to Quantum
- subpoena to Metro or county SHS administrators
- interrogatories asking who handled the file, what happened, and what criteria were used
- deposition topics for HACC relocation staff, Quantum intake staff, and SHS program administrators

## Model Interrogatory Topics

1. Identify every person who received, reviewed, handled, stored, scanned, transmitted, rejected, or closed the household's Blossom application.
2. State every date on which the application was received, reviewed, transmitted, held, deemed incomplete, denied, or otherwise acted upon.
3. Identify every reason the application was not approved, transmitted, or finalized.
4. Identify all criteria used to prioritize or place applicants at Blossom during the relevant period.
5. Identify every displaced HACC household referred to Blossom during the same period and state whether each was interviewed, approved, denied, or placed.
6. Identify all funding sources, contracts, and nondiscrimination certifications governing the Blossom path and the SHS / RLRA / HACC housing pipeline at issue.

## Model RFP Topics

1. All documents reflecting receipt, intake, review, scanning, transmission, deficiency, denial, hold, closure, or placement relating to plaintiffs' Blossom application.
2. All communications between HACC and Quantum concerning plaintiffs, Blossom, Hillside Manor, Parkside, Easton Ridge, or relocation packet handling.
3. All documents showing the criteria, ranking, scoring, or prioritization used for Blossom or related replacement-housing opportunities.
4. All documents sufficient to show which displaced households were referred to or moved into Blossom during the relevant time period.
5. All contracts, grant agreements, certifications, assurances, policy directives, and compliance records governing SHS, RLRA, H4S, Metro-funded housing, and fair-housing / Title VI compliance.
6. All documents concerning complaints by plaintiffs about race discrimination, housing steering, service-animal issues, accommodation, retaliation, or nonprocessing.

## Best First Wave

If you can only ask for a few things first, prioritize:

1. Quantum intake logs and packet history
2. HACC-to-Quantum communications about the packet
3. comparative Blossom applicant movement records
4. written prioritization / scoring criteria
5. SHS / Metro / HACC nondiscrimination certifications

## Practical Goal

The objective is to move from:

- `we believe they treated us unequally`

to:

- `here is the intake record showing our file stalled`,
- `here are the other files that moved`,
- `here are the criteria they used`,
- and `here is what they promised the government about nondiscrimination`.

That combination is what can turn a strong suspicion of `lemon dropping` and `cherry picking` into a provable race-steering, `1981`, Title VI, or civil-rights-fraud theory.
