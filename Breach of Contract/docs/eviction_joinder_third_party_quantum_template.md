## 3) Short Factual Basis (for Motion)

Defendant alleges the following facts relevant to joinder and third-party practice:

1. Defendant's household resides in public housing for disabled persons and was required to relocate as part of an approved transition/relocation process.
2. Housing authority representative Kati Tilton represented that there were three relocation solutions, including:
   - A two-bedroom application at Parkside Heights.
   - Porting the voucher to Multnomah County.
   - [Insert third option as reflected in documents/communications].
3. Defendant alleges HACC's Blossom notice and related materials placed Blossom inside the same HACC-controlled relocation and voucher-processing sequence.
4. Defendant alleges Blossom-side materials state that applications for the project were processed through the HACC PBV waitlist.
5. Defendant alleges preserved complaints gave notice both to HACC staff and to the Blossom-side participant that the application was not being processed and that housing-access barriers were preventing the move.
6. Defendant relied on these representations while attempting to comply with relocation and tenancy obligations.
7. Defendant also alleges Quantum (or affiliated management acting under Quantum's authority) offered a third-floor unit not accessible for Defendant's disabled mother, who cannot safely use stairs.
8. Defendant alleges Quantum and the housing authority failed to complete and/or process promised relocation pathways, materially contributing to the housing instability underlying the eviction case.
9. Defendant further alleges, on information and belief, that Quantum Residential exercised a controlling landlord-management role over Blossom and related properties involved in these events, and may also be a successor in interest to relevant property interests to the extent discovery confirms that relationship.

## 4) Grounds for Joinder / Third-Party Relief

Defendant seeks joinder and third-party relief because:

1. Quantum's conduct is part of the same transaction/occurrence or a closely related series of occurrences tied to the asserted eviction claims and defenses.
2. Complete relief cannot be afforded among existing parties without adjudicating Quantum's role in the relocation failures and contractual/promissory obligations.
3. The present record supports that the housing authority handled the displacement and voucher side of the same transaction while the owner-manager side handled application intake, review, and response duties.
4. Defendant's defenses and equitable claims (including prevention, estoppel, offset/recoupment, and failure of conditions attributable to relocation failures) materially overlap with Quantum's alleged conduct.
5. Judicial economy favors one proceeding to avoid inconsistent rulings on the same housing facts and obligations.

## 6) Memorandum in Support (Short Form)

### A. The Court Should Join Quantum

Quantum is a central actor in the relocation facts underlying this eviction. The Court cannot fairly evaluate causation, prevention, or equitable defenses without Quantum's participation because the record supports one integrated HACC-linked relocation and application process rather than separate unrelated disputes.

### B. Third-Party Practice is Appropriate

Defendant's claims against Quantum derive from the same housing transition facts now before the Court. Trying these issues together avoids duplicative litigation and inconsistent outcomes.

### C. Equity Favors Full Adjudication

Defendant alleges compliance efforts were frustrated by failures of promised relocation pathways controlled by the housing authority/Quantum process. Equity favors hearing those facts before imposing eviction consequences.
