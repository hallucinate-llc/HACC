# Illusory Options / Promissory Estoppel Hearing Script

Your Honor, HACC is treating these supposed relocation options as if they were completed opportunities that Defendants simply failed to use. The record shows the opposite. By the end of March 2026, the three options HACC pointed to were still incomplete, conditional, or obstructed.

First, the Multnomah County portability path was not finished. HACC's own March 20, 2026 message says the one-bedroom voucher was only being issued then and had to be signed and returned so HACC could start the port process. Then HACC's later housing-options summary still said Home Forward had to contact the household, intake and orientation still had to happen, and a unit still had to be found. That is not a completed relocation placement. See `Exhibit 4` and `Exhibit 5`.

Second, the Parkside Heights path was also not complete. The household applied, and Parkside acknowledged receipt, but Parkside also said the two-bedroom units were still under construction and estimated occupancy was not until the end of April. So that option was still conditional and unavailable within the eviction timeline. See `Exhibit 5`.

Third, the Blossom / Quantum path was not completed either. The household submitted the packet, and HACC later admitted Quantum had received it but had not provided it onward to HACC. So that pathway was not merely unfinished; the record supports that it was obstructed. See `Exhibit 6`.

That matters because HACC was still moving toward eviction on March 31, 2026 while all three of those supposed options remained unfinished. HACC cannot induce the household to rely on those pathways, keep them incomplete, and then turn around and say Defendants failed to relocate.

The legal point is modest but important: Defendants are not claiming a final lease had already been executed for each option. The point is that HACC is bound at minimum by promissory or equitable estoppel and cannot rely on the noncompletion of these same incomplete, illusory-in-operation pathways as a basis for possession.

If the Court asks what relief follows from that, the answer is that possession should be denied, stayed, or at minimum not granted on a theory that Defendants failed to complete relocation when the offered options had not yet matured into real timely alternatives to displacement.

## Short Response If HACC Says "We Offered Options"

Offering options is not enough if those options were still incomplete when the eviction was pushed forward. The question is whether there was a real, timely, usable relocation alternative before displacement. On this record, there was not.
