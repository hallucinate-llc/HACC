# VAWA Breach Analysis for HACC and Quantum Residential

This memo reviews the current additions in the `Breach of Contract` folder and asks a narrower question than the Title 18 work: whether the present record supports a good-faith argument that the Housing Authority of Clackamas County (`HACC`) and/or Quantum Residential breached federal VAWA housing protections.

This is not framed as a criminal-law theory. It is a housing-program compliance and civil-rights theory grounded mainly in HUD's VAWA regulations and guidance.

## 1. Governing VAWA rules

The main current federal authorities are:

- [24 CFR 5.2005](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/part-5/section-5.2005)
- [24 CFR 5.2007](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/part-5/section-5.2007)
- [24 CFR 5.2009](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/part-5/section-5.2009)
- [HUD Notice PIH 2017-08](https://www.hud.gov/sites/documents/17-08PIHN.PDF)

The parts most relevant here are:

- `24 CFR 5.2005(a)`: covered housing providers must give the VAWA notice of occupancy rights and certification form at admission, denial, and with any eviction or termination notice.
- `24 CFR 5.2005(b)-(d)`: a tenant cannot be denied assistance, terminated, or evicted because the tenant is or has been a victim of domestic violence, dating violence, sexual assault, or stalking, and abuse incidents cannot be treated as lease violations by the victim.
- `24 CFR 5.2005(e)`: each covered housing provider identified in the program-specific rules must maintain an emergency transfer plan, keep records of requests and outcomes, and protect transfer confidentiality.
- `24 CFR 5.2007(a)-(c)`: if the provider asks for VAWA documentation, the request must be in writing, the tenant has 14 business days to respond, the tenant may choose among listed forms of documentation, and submitted VAWA information must be kept confidential.
- `24 CFR 5.2009(a)-(b)`: a covered housing provider may bifurcate a lease to remove the perpetrator without penalizing the victim, and if bifurcation affects eligibility the remaining household must receive the required time to establish eligibility or find other housing.

HUD's 2017 implementation guidance matters to party allocation. For public housing, HCV, and PBV, HUD says the `PHA, not the owner`, is the covered housing provider responsible for the VAWA Notice of Occupancy Rights. See PIH 2017-08 at page 18. The same notice explains that PHAs and owners both have confidentiality duties once VAWA information is submitted to them, and owners can still be involved in lease bifurcation and tenancy-side remedies. See PIH 2017-08 at pages 9, 18, and 31-32.

## 3. HACC: strongest VAWA breach theories

### A. Likely strongest theory: HACC penalized the protected side of the household rather than using VAWA the way the rule allows

If the present household narrative is true that HACC removed Benjamin and restored the restrained party, that is the opposite of the lease-bifurcation remedy VAWA authorizes.

Under `24 CFR 5.2009(a)(1)`, a covered housing provider may remove the household member who engaged in abuse-related criminal activity, but may not thereby evict, remove, terminate assistance to, or otherwise penalize the victim or lawful occupant. Under `24 CFR 5.2005(b)-(c)`, abuse cannot be used as the basis to deny or terminate the victim's housing rights, and the incident cannot be treated as a lease violation by the victim.

On the current record, this is the cleanest HACC-side VAWA argument:

- abuse and bifurcation were raised in October 2025;
- HACC knew by late November 2025 that Julio-related legal-status and residency issues were active;
- HACC took lease-side action by early December 2025;
- HACC was still demanding paperwork from the household in January 2026;
- and the practical result, according to the present narrative, was that the household member seeking protection was destabilized while the restrained party was restored.

If proved, that would fit a direct VAWA misuse theory better than a mere administrative delay theory.

### B. Strong supplemental theory: HACC may have failed the mandatory VAWA notice-and-form requirements

For public housing/HCV/PBV, HUD places the `Notice of Occupancy Rights under VAWA` duty on the PHA. `24 CFR 5.2005(a)` and PIH 2017-08 require the notice and certification form to be provided with any eviction or termination notice and at the specified program stages.

The present record includes repeated lease, relocation, and eviction activity, including the February 4, 2026 eviction sequence described in [did-key-hacc-temp-session.json](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/did-key-hacc-temp-session.json) and [eviction_answer_affirmative_defenses_and_counterclaims_title18.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/docs/eviction_answer_affirmative_defenses_and_counterclaims_title18.md), but the working evidence set does not yet show HACC serving the HUD VAWA notice or HUD certification form with those notices.

That is not conclusive proof of nonservice. It is, however, enough to support:

- a targeted discovery demand;
- a request for production of the exact notice packet used with each eviction or termination communication;
- and an argument that HACC should be required to prove VAWA notice compliance if it wants to rely on VAWA-related household-composition or termination consequences.

### C. Plausible theory: HACC imposed a documentation regime stricter than VAWA permits

`24 CFR 5.2007(a)-(b)` allows a written request for documentation, but it limits both the manner and the acceptable forms of proof. The tenant may use a HUD certification, a professional's statement, a court or agency record, or other evidence accepted at the provider's discretion. The tenant also gets 14 business days after a written request.

The present record shows HACC saying a restraining order was required, and later requesting court documentation showing Julio could not reside in the household. See [protective_order_and_hacc_notice_timeline.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/docs/protective_order_and_hacc_notice_timeline.md).

That supports a possible over-demand theory if the facts develop to show:

- HACC treated a restraining order as the only acceptable proof;
- HACC failed to make the request in the written VAWA form contemplated by the rule;
- HACC denied protection before the 14-business-day response period ran;
- or HACC refused other permissible documentation.

At present, this theory is plausible but not yet fully proved, because the current file set does not yet isolate the exact written request packet or show whether HACC gave the required form and deadline.

### D. Plausible theory: HACC failed to operate an emergency-transfer process despite a safety-based request to separate the household

`24 CFR 5.2005(e)` requires an emergency transfer plan, confidentiality measures, records of requests and outcomes, and policies for helping a tenant move quickly, including with tenant-based assistance where applicable.

The present record shows repeated safety-based separation requests, a portability discussion, and statements that Jane did not want to live with Julio. See [protective_order_and_hacc_notice_timeline.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/docs/protective_order_and_hacc_notice_timeline.md).

This gives HACC some exposure under VAWA's transfer provisions if the facts show that the household was functionally seeking a safety transfer and HACC:

- failed to route the request through an emergency transfer plan;
- failed to keep any transfer record;
- delayed the port or transfer path despite a VAWA-qualifying safety basis;
- or treated the request only as ordinary relocation paperwork instead of as a VAWA safety request.

This is a meaningful theory, but it is still secondary to the lease-bifurcation theory because the current file set does not yet contain HACC's actual emergency transfer plan or a formal transfer-request record.

### E. Confidentiality theory against HACC is possible but not yet mature

`24 CFR 5.2007(c)` prohibits disclosure of VAWA-submitted information except in narrow circumstances.

The current record shows a broad multi-party chain involving HACC Public Housing, HACC Relocation, county-side personnel, and later intense discussion about who was and was not in the household. That creates a confidentiality question, but the present file set does not yet establish an improper disclosure outside the rule's exceptions. So this should presently be treated as a discovery theory, not yet as a headline claim.

## 4. Quantum Residential: weaker direct VAWA theory on the current record

Quantum's exposure is narrower.

The strongest reason is allocation of duties. For the notice duty under `24 CFR 5.2005(a)`, HUD's own guidance says that for public housing, HCV, and PBV, the `PHA, not the owner`, is the covered housing provider responsible for serving the VAWA notice. On the current record, that points first to HACC, not Quantum.

The same is largely true for the present emergency-transfer proof. The current record shows HACC handling the Julio documentation and household-composition review. I have not found a Quantum communication in the present record where Quantum:

- received a VAWA request directly;
- denied VAWA protections directly;
- served VAWA-related notices;
- requested VAWA documentation;
- or explained how it was handling a lease bifurcation.

So, on the current file set, a direct VAWA claim against Quantum is weaker than against HACC.

## 5. Quantum: the narrower VAWA theories that could still work

Quantum should not be treated as categorically immune. A narrower VAWA theory could still be developed if discovery shows any of the following:

- Quantum controlled or participated in the lease-side decision that removed the protected household member or preserved the alleged abuser's occupancy.
- Quantum refused to honor a lawful bifurcation or safe-transfer decision after receiving notice from HACC.
- Quantum used VAWA-related victim information in a way that breached confidentiality.
- Quantum denied housing access because the applicant or tenant was a victim of domestic violence, dating violence, sexual assault, or stalking.
- Quantum used the abuse-related household situation as a pretext to obstruct intake, placement, or occupancy.

That said, the present evidence in this folder points much more strongly to Quantum's role in intake obstruction, packet non-transmission, relocation interference, and disability-access problems than to a direct VAWA compliance failure. See [quantum_pattern_and_practice_delay_analysis.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/docs/quantum_pattern_and_practice_delay_analysis.md) and [eviction_answer_affirmative_defenses_and_counterclaims_title18.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/docs/eviction_answer_affirmative_defenses_and_counterclaims_title18.md).

So the better present framing is:

- `HACC`: primary VAWA defendant.
- `Quantum`: possible derivative or participatory VAWA defendant only if discovery ties it to the actual bifurcation, penalization, confidentiality breach, or survivor-specific denial.

## 6. Best current bottom line

### HACC

The current record supports a materially stronger VAWA theory against HACC than against Quantum.

At minimum, the record supports a good-faith argument that HACC may have violated VAWA by:

- mishandling lease bifurcation and household protection after abuse-related requests;
- penalizing the side of the household seeking protection rather than isolating the alleged perpetrator;
- failing to prove service of the mandatory VAWA notice and certification form with eviction/termination activity;
- possibly demanding narrower proof than VAWA permits;
- and possibly failing to process the situation under an emergency transfer framework.

The cleanest present pleading formulation is:

`HACC knew the household had invoked abuse-related safety protections, demanded or solicited court documentation concerning whether Julio could reside in the household, acted on household composition during that same period, and then used the resulting household change in a way that allegedly destabilized the protected side of the household rather than protecting it, contrary to 24 CFR 5.2005, 5.2007, and 5.2009.`

### Quantum

The current record does not yet support as strong a direct VAWA-breach theory against Quantum.

The safest present formulation is:

`On the current record, Quantum's more clearly supported role is relocation obstruction and housing-access interference, not primary VAWA administration. A VAWA claim against Quantum is presently strongest only if discovery shows that Quantum itself participated in lease bifurcation, survivor penalization, confidentiality violations, or denial of occupancy because of the household's abuse-protection status.`

## 7. Best next proof targets

To convert the HACC VAWA theory from strong inference to stronger proof, the best next targets are:

- Any HACC packet that should have included `HUD-5380` and `HUD-5382`, especially with the February 2026 eviction or termination notices.
- HACC's current and 2025-2026 `VAWA Notice of Occupancy Rights`, `Emergency Transfer Plan`, and any local policy or desk guide for bifurcation.
- Any written HACC request for VAWA documentation showing what proof was demanded and what deadline was given.
- Any HACC case notes identifying who decided to remove or restore which household member and why.
- Any record showing whether HACC treated the October 2025 request as a VAWA bifurcation request, an emergency transfer request, both, or neither.

To strengthen any Quantum-side VAWA theory, the best next targets are:

- lease files showing whether Quantum executed, proposed, or influenced any household-composition or occupancy change tied to the abuse dispute;
- any communications between Quantum and HACC mentioning `VAWA`, `bifurcation`, `protective order`, `restraining order`, or `who is and is not currently residing in the household`;
- and any proof that Quantum received confidential victim-status information and used it to obstruct or deny housing.

## 8. Practical pleading conclusion

Compared with the Title 18 analysis, VAWA is a better fit as a direct regulatory and civil-rights theory against HACC than as a broad all-parties theory.

For now, the strongest litigation position appears to be:

- plead VAWA noncompliance directly and primarily against HACC;
- keep Quantum in the VAWA discussion only in the alternative, on information and belief, unless and until discovery ties Quantum to the actual VAWA-handling decisions;
- and use VAWA mainly to show that the household's abuse-related safety request should have triggered protections, not penalties.

## 10. Important limits of the formal outputs

The formal outputs do **not** prove disputed history by themselves.

They do three narrower things:

- they formalize the duties imposed by VAWA and HUD guidance;
- they map the current record to those duties in a way that makes the strongest and weakest theories easier to see;
- and they show which conclusions are `supported`, `supported if household theory proved`, `supported but record gap remains`, or `weak on current record`.

So the cleanest use of these outputs is:

- as a litigation-organization and issue-spotting tool;
- as a way to draft discovery requests targeted to the exact missing VAWA packets, transfer records, and case notes;
- and as a way to keep the complaint or motion language disciplined so that HACC is framed as the primary VAWA actor unless and until discovery brings Quantum more directly into the VAWA decision chain.
