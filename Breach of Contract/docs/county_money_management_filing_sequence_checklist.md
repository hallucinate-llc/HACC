## 1. Fill In Before Service

1. Insert the Clackamas County case number in both captioned drafts.
2. Confirm the exact service name and address for Clackamas County Social Services / Money Management or the proper custodian.
3. Fill in the certificate-of-service date and service method in the discovery draft.
4. Identify the likely subpoena custodians:
   - Money Management / representative-payee records custodian
   - county case-management or accounting-system custodian
   - county communications or records custodian
5. Check whether the court requires a separate subpoena form, notice, witness fee, or advance notice to the opposing party before third-party service.

## 3. What To Demand First

The most important items to watch for are:

1. the identity of the county worker who communicated with Benjamin Barber
2. any note that Benjamin Barber should stop paying Jane Cortez's rent
3. any note that a check or payment would be sent to HACC
4. any note that Jane Cortez should not pursue Waterleaf or housing outside Clackamas County
5. any communication between county personnel and HACC about those issues

## 4. Decision Point After Initial Response

If the county produces:

- direct records of a payment instruction:
  - compare them against HACC's rent-handling and delay sequence
- direct records of portability discouragement:
  - use them to strengthen the steering / interference / notice theory
- no meaningful production:
  - move to the subpoena stage immediately

## 5. Best Subpoena Order

If the initial response is incomplete, issue subpoenas in this order:

1. county Social Services / Money Management custodian
2. county case-management / accounting-system custodian
3. county communications / records custodian

Why this order:

- the primary county file is most likely to identify the staff member and the substantive communication
- system logs are next best for proving whether the note or payment entry was actually made
- general communications records are useful, but usually broader and slower to review
