# Hearing Order of Operations

This memo is a practical order for arguing the FED hearing if time is short or the court narrows the issues quickly.

## Best Overall Order

1. Premature eviction before relocation completion
2. Easton Ridge / inaccessible replacement
3. Overbroad verification / accommodation delay
4. Illusory options / promissory estoppel

## Why This Order Works

The first issue is the umbrella issue. It gives the court the cleanest high-level reason not to treat this as an ordinary eviction: HACC moved toward possession before relocation was actually completed.

The second issue is the strongest human and factual issue. It shows that one of the supposed replacement paths was facially unusable for Jane Cortez because of her stair and mobility limitations.

The third issue explains why delay should not be blamed on Defendants. It shows HACC kept expanding the verification process even while accommodation, caregiver, and county-side money-management issues were already known.

The fourth issue is the fallback if the court says, "But HACC did offer options." That is where the response is: yes, but those options were still incomplete, conditional, or obstructed when HACC pressed toward eviction.

## Fast Fallback Version

If the judge gives only a minute or two, use this sequence:

1. "This is not an ordinary eviction because relocation was not complete."
2. "The Easton Ridge unit was not accessible for Jane Cortez."
3. "The paperwork delay was not ours alone; HACC kept expanding the verification demands."
4. "And the options HACC points to were still incomplete when eviction was pushed forward."

## If The Court Cuts You Off Early

If the court cuts off factual detail, fall back to this short formulation:

- HACC had not finished relocation.
- HACC had not provided a usable accessible replacement.
- HACC was still delaying matters with overbroad verification demands.
- HACC cannot use incomplete relocation pathways as proof that Defendants failed to act.
