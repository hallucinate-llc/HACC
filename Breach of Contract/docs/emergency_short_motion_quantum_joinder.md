EMERGENCY MOTION FOR JOINDER OF THIRD PARTY AND FOR LEAVE TO FILE THIRD-PARTY COMPLAINT

Defendants move for expedited relief joining Quantum Residential and granting leave to file the attached Third-Party Complaint.

## Emergency Basis

1. Defendants face active eviction consequences tied to facts that materially overlap with proposed claims against Quantum.
2. Without joinder, the Court will decide causation and equitable defenses on an incomplete party record.
3. Delay risks inconsistent outcomes and immediate irreparable housing harm.

## Core Allegations (Short Form)

1. Defendants allege they were promised three relocation pathways, including:
   - Parkside Heights two-bedroom application,
   - Voucher portability to Multnomah County,
   - A third relocation option documented in communications.
2. HACC's September 19, 2024 Hillside Park Apartments Phase II General Information Notice (hand-delivered and certified-mailed) formally confirmed HUD Section 18 disposition approval and triggered the Tenant Protection Voucher relocation process at issue in this case. (Exhibit M.)
3. Defendants allege HACC's January 2026 Blossom notice tied Blossom to the same Tenant Protection Voucher process and directed the family to contact Ashley Ferron to start that process.
4. Defendants allege Blossom materials state that applications for the project were processed through the HACC PBV waitlist.
5. HACC's own Administrative Plan identifies Quantum Residential as the property management company for at least three PHA-owned or HACC-contracted mixed-finance developments, including the Hillside Manor site under both the PBV program (Chapter 17) and the RAD PBV program (Chapter 18), where the private successor entity is the **Hillside Manor Limited Partnership** (RAD closing 1/1/2021, PIH 2019-23), with HAP contracts including an "Eminent Domain 5 PBV" component tied to the original Hillside Park land acquisition. Quantum Residential Property Management is named as the LP's manager (HillsideManor@QResInc.com). The same plan classifies Blossom & Community Apartments as Section 18 replacement housing under HACC's PBV program, and HACC staff's in-litigation claim that Quantum is "separate from the county" is directly contradicted by these Administrative Plan entries. This is on-record administrative documentation placing Quantum inside the private successor ownership structure at the same site whose demolition generated this eviction — not merely an allegation.
6. Defendants allege preserved emails gave notice both to HACC staff and to the Blossom-side participant that the application was not being processed. Those emails also document that Quantum Residential "falsely claimed that we did not provide them with an application" — a disputed fact that can only be resolved with Quantum in the case.
7. Defendants allege those pathways were not completed as represented.
8. Defendants allege accessibility-related placement failures for a household member with stair limitations.

## Legal Basis (Confirm Local Rule)

Defendants seek relief under ORCP 29, ORCP 30, and ORCP 33 C because proposed third-party allegations arise from the same transaction or occurrence and are required for complete relief and efficient adjudication.

## Requested Relief

Defendants ask the Court to:

1. Set expedited hearing on this motion.
2. Join Quantum Residential as a party.
3. Grant leave to file and serve the Third-Party Complaint.
4. Enter a coordinated schedule for responsive pleading and hearing.
