### 3. What this likely shows

The current preserved record supports the following chronology:

1. By `January 6, 2026`, HACC had been told that Jane's representative-payee-side county contact was allegedly discouraging the household from using a voucher outside Clackamas County.
2. By `February 5, 2026`, HACC had been told more specifically that `Jemiliah at money management` had instructed Benjamin not to keep paying Jane's rent and had represented that a check would be sent.
3. On the same day, Kati Tilton acknowledged that the Money Management Program was a county-run program housed in `Clackamas County Social Services`, rather than disclaiming any county connection.

### 4. Why this matters

This evidence is useful for several reasons:

- It strengthens the argument that county personnel outside HACC proper were still affecting Jane's housing and payment circumstances during the relocation and voucher dispute.
- It supports a county-knowledge theory rather than a purely private misunderstanding, because Kati expressly acknowledged that the program was operated by Clackamas County Social Services.
- It supports an argument that Benjamin was trying to coordinate rent/payment handling based on instructions coming from county-affiliated personnel, rather than acting unilaterally without agency direction.
- It gives more context for the broader allegation that Jane's representative-payee / money-management arrangement was part of the county knowledge base about Jane's vulnerability, dependency, and need for assistance in handling housing affairs.
