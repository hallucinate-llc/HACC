## Memorandum in Support

### I. Factual Background (Alleged)

1. Defendant's household resides in public housing for disabled persons and was required to relocate through a housing-authority relocation process.
2. Defendant alleges that HACC represented there were three relocation solutions:
   - Submission of a two-bedroom apartment application at Parkside Heights;
   - Portability/port of the housing voucher to Multnomah County; and
   - A third relocation pathway reflected in the parties' communications and records.
3. Defendant alleges HACC's own January 2026 Blossom notice placed Blossom inside the same HACC-controlled relocation and Tenant Protection Voucher process and directed Defendant's household to contact Ashley Ferron to start that process.
4. Defendant further alleges HACC's public Hillside Park redevelopment materials stated that, once Section 18 disposition approval and Tenant Protection Vouchers were in place, residents would begin the relocation process with HACC guidance and could either move into a new unit at Hillside Park or relocate with a Tenant Protection Voucher.
5. Defendant alleges preserved HACC emails directed that, if Defendant's household was pursuing Blossom or Community Apartments, the packet should be returned to Blossom-side office staff, and later acknowledged that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum Residential staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office but had not been provided to HACC.
6. Defendant alleges Blossom-side materials state that applications for the project were processed through the HACC PBV waitlist, showing that the Blossom opportunity was part of an integrated HACC-linked housing pipeline rather than an unrelated private-market option.
7. Defendant alleges preserved complaint emails gave contemporaneous notice both to HACC staff and to a Blossom-side participant that the Blossom application was not being processed, that a service animal was being refused, and that the family was being blocked from leaving the current HACC-controlled housing placement.
8. Defendant alleges owner/agent application materials imposed a duty to review the application and notify the applicant of the results within a reasonable time after receipt of the required materials.
9. Defendant alleges active reliance on these relocation pathways while attempting to comply with all relocation requirements.
10. Defendant further alleges a unit was proposed on a third floor despite known mobility limitations of Defendant's mother, who cannot safely climb stairs.
11. Defendant alleges failures in processing and honoring the HACC-linked relocation pathways materially contributed to the alleged noncompliance now used as the basis for eviction.
12. Defendant further alleges that preserved emails contain a statement that Quantum Residential "falsely claimed that we did not provide them with an application, which was also made to your organization in the first place" — a direct on-record dispute about Quantum's conduct that cannot be fairly resolved without Quantum's participation in this proceeding.
13. Defendant further alleges, supported by review of HACC's own Administrative Plan, that HACC's plan names Quantum Residential as the property management company for at least one PHA-owned mixed-finance development in Clackamas County (Clayton Mohr Commons, 399 Caufield St, Oregon City) operating under a 20-year HUD HAP contract. The same Administrative Plan classifies both Blossom & Community Apartments and Hillside Manor Apartments as excluded units constituting replacement housing for a demolished original HACC project, with a first-occupancy preference for former residents — the same relocation preference structure applicable to this household.

### II. Joinder Is Necessary for Complete Relief

1. Quantum's alleged conduct is intertwined with the same core housing facts now before the Court because the dispute arises from one integrated relocation and housing-access transaction involving both HACC and the Blossom-side processing path.
2. The current record supports allegations that HACC controlled the displacement and voucher side of the process while the owner/manager side controlled intake, packet transmission, review, and response duties on the Blossom application path.
3. HACC's own public Hillside Park redevelopment materials further support that Hillside Park placement and TPV relocation were being administered as parts of one redevelopment-driven relocation program rather than unrelated housing tracks.
4. HACC's own Administrative Plan identifies Quantum Residential as the named property management company for a PHA-owned HAP contract development in Clackamas County, and classifies both Blossom & Community Apartments and Hillside Manor Apartments as Section 18 replacement housing under HACC's PBV program. This documentary evidence establishes that Quantum's connection to HACC's subsidized housing program is not merely alleged; it is an on-record fact in HACC's filed administrative plan, making joinder grounded in the published administrative record rather than in inference alone.
5. Complete and fair adjudication of defenses, causation, prevention, and equitable issues cannot occur without Quantum's participation.
6. Trying these interrelated issues in one action promotes judicial economy and avoids inconsistent rulings about who prevented the relocation pathway from being completed.

### III. Leave for Third-Party Complaint Is Proper

Defendant's proposed third-party claims arise from the same series of transactions and occurrences underlying this eviction case, including relocation obligations, application processing, and accessibility-related placement failures.
