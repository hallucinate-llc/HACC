# One-Page Hearing Outline (Joinder and Third-Party Complaint)

## Relief Requested

1. Join Quantum Residential as a party.
2. Grant leave to file and serve third-party complaint.

## 30-Second Theme

This eviction cannot be fairly decided in isolation because Defendants' alleged tenancy default is tied to one HACC-linked redevelopment and relocation process involving promised pathways, Hillside or TPV relocation options, accessibility needs, Blossom processing through the HACC PBV waitlist, and alleged non-processing by the owner-manager side.

## Three Required Findings for Court to Grant Motion

1. Common facts overlap between eviction and proposed third-party claims.
2. Quantum is materially connected to those same facts.
3. Joinder promotes complete relief and avoids inconsistent outcomes.

## Fast Exhibit Path (Use in This Order)

1. Exhibit M (Hillside Park Phase II General Information Notice, September 19, 2024)
   - Hand-delivered and certified-mailed HACC notice confirming HUD Section 18 disposition approval and triggering the TPV and relocation process. Establishes that HACC formally created the relocation obligations at issue here.
2. Exhibit L (Confirmed HACC / Quantum Intake Thread)
   - Shows HACC directed the packet to Blossom-side office staff and later confirmed it had been submitted to Quantum staff but not provided onward to HACC.
3. Exhibit D (Parkside Application)
   - Shows the promised Parkside path was actually pursued.
4. Exhibit E (Voucher Portability Records)
   - Shows portability/voucher pathway activity and timeline.
5. Exhibit C (Blossom Documents)
   - Supports allegation that Blossom was part of the same HACC-linked processing path and that the application was not completed.
6. Exhibit G (Quantum Application/Pet Amendment)
   - Connects the owner-manager side to application and tenancy process evidence.
7. Exhibit I (Accommodation/Accessibility)
   - Supports disability-access materiality and infeasibility of inaccessible unit options.

## Core Argument Script

1. We allege there were three relocation solutions promised, and we acted on them.
2. Exhibit M: HACC's own September 19, 2024 Phase II notice — hand-delivered and certified-mailed — formally confirmed HUD Section 18 disposition approval and created the TPV relocation obligations that govern this dispute.
3. HACC's own Hillside Park materials described redevelopment relocation as including either a new Hillside unit or a Tenant Protection Voucher.
4. Exhibit L shows HACC told us to return the packet to Blossom-side staff and later confirmed the packet had gone to Quantum staff but not back to HACC.
5. Exhibit C shows Blossom was tied to the same voucher relocation process and HACC PBV waitlist.
6. Critically: HACC's own Administrative Plan names Quantum Residential as property management company for a PHA-owned HAP contract development in Clackamas County and classifies both Blossom and Hillside Manor as Section 18 replacement housing under HACC's PBV program. HACC's own filed administrative record connects Quantum to this housing program. That is not circumstantial.
7. We allege the owner-manager side failed to process or respond to that application path, and preserved emails show we told HACC that Quantum falsely denied receiving our application.
8. Those failures are part of the same housing story now used to justify eviction.
9. The Court should hear all materially related facts in one case.

## If Court Asks "Why Not Separate Lawsuit?"

Because the same fact sequence is being used in this eviction now. Separate litigation risks inconsistent findings about the same HACC-linked redevelopment relocation, application-processing, and causation record.

## If Court Asks "Will This Delay?"

Any short schedule adjustment is outweighed by avoiding duplicative proceedings and conflicting rulings.

## Close

Defendants request joinder and leave to file the third-party complaint, with a prompt coordinated schedule.
