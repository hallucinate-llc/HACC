# Title 18 Breach Pleading Section

This draft is structured for adaptation into a complaint, amended answer/counterclaim, joinder memorandum, or opposition filing.

## Core Theory

HACC triggered Section 18 relocation duties no later than September 19, 2024, when HUD granted Phase II approval for redevelopment of Hillside Park and HACC served the corresponding notice. After those duties were triggered, HACC was required to carry out relocation obligations before displacing the household, including counseling, relocation-expense support, and the provision of comparable replacement housing. Instead, the current record shows that HACC pursued eviction while relocation remained incomplete, offered a non-accessible third-floor unit to a mobility-impaired household, and failed to complete required relocation services. The same record shows that Quantum Residential received the household's intake/application materials through the replacement-housing pathway but did not transmit them to HACC, materially obstructing the relocation process.

## Breach Allegations Against HACC

1. On September 19, 2024, HUD granted HACC permission to proceed with the Phase II redevelopment/relocation process for Hillside Park, and HACC served the corresponding Phase II notice to the affected household.

2. By reason of that approval and notice, HACC's Section 18 relocation duties were triggered, including duties to provide relocation counseling, commit or pay relocation expenses, and furnish comparable replacement housing before displacement was completed.

3. HACC's own relocation materials represented that qualified residents would receive relocation advisory services, including counseling, one-on-one consultations, and assistance locating replacement housing.

4. HACC's own administrative materials further classified Hillside Manor Apartments and Blossom & Community Apartments as replacement housing for a demolished original project, with first-occupancy preference for former residents, thereby confirming that these properties were part of the operative relocation track.

5. Despite those duties, HACC did not complete relocation counseling for the Barber/Cortez household before seeking displacement.

6. Despite those duties, HACC did not commit or pay relocation expenses for the Barber/Cortez household before seeking displacement.

7. Despite those duties, HACC did not furnish comparable replacement housing to the household before seeking displacement.

8. Instead, HACC offered the household a third-floor unit even though the household's mobility limitations and scooter use made that unit inaccessible, and no accessible ground-floor alternative was provided.

9. On or before March 31, 2026, HACC pursued eviction/displacement while relocation remained incomplete.

10. The present record reflects that, as of that eviction deadline, relocation was still incomplete, with no comparable housing, no relocation expenses, and no counseling.

11. By pursuing eviction before completing relocation, HACC breached and/or violated its Section 18 relocation obligations and acted contrary to the relocation protections triggered by the redevelopment approval.

## Breach Allegations Against Quantum Residential

12. HACC's own administrative plan identifies Quantum Residential as property manager for HACC-linked developments within the same replacement-housing and RAD/PBV portfolio used for the Hillside Park relocation track.

13. HACC's own administrative materials and related evidence show that applications for Blossom & Community Apartments were processed through the HACC PBV waitlist.

14. The household submitted Blossom/intake materials to Quantum Residential staff through that relocation pathway.

15. On January 26, 2026, HACC, through Ashley Ferron, acknowledged in writing that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum Residential staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office but had not been provided to HACC.

16. In that same communication, HACC instructed Quantum Residential staff to send the previously submitted intake packet directly to HACC.

17. The application materials also state that the owner/agent must notify the applicant of the results of review within a reasonable time after receipt of the required materials.

18. Quantum's receipt and non-transmission of the packet materially obstructed the household's access to the replacement-housing process and interfered with HACC's performance of the relocation pathway.

19. On information and belief, Quantum also falsely denied receiving the application after it had in fact received the materials, further delaying or frustrating the household's placement processing.

20. By receiving but failing to transmit the packet, and by failing to process the household's materials through the replacement-housing intake path, Quantum breached duties arising from its role in the relocation process and proximately contributed to the household's displacement injury.

## Developing Oversight Allegation Against HUD

21. HUD approved the Phase II redevelopment/relocation action that triggered the operative Section 18 duties.

22. To the extent HUD received notice of subsequent noncompliance by HACC and/or Quantum and failed to take enforcement or corrective action, discovery may further support an oversight-based claim or remedial theory.

23. At present, the strongest claims are against HACC and Quantum; the HUD theory should be pleaded only to the extent consistent with available notice and non-enforcement evidence.

## Damages / Harm Theory

24. As a direct and proximate result of the foregoing breaches, the household was denied the timely and lawful relocation process required after demolition approval.

25. The household was exposed to eviction/displacement while the relocation pathway remained incomplete.

26. The household was deprived of timely packet processing, timely counseling, timely expense support, and access to a comparable and accessible replacement unit.

27. The household suffered housing instability, delay, additional administrative burden, and loss of the protections that should have accompanied the Section 18 relocation process.

## Prayer / Requested Relief Concepts

1. Declaratory relief that HACC's relocation duties were triggered and remained unfulfilled when eviction was pursued.
2. Declaratory relief that Quantum obstructed or failed to carry out required intake-processing duties in the replacement-housing pathway.
3. Injunctive or equitable relief barring displacement or eviction until relocation obligations are completed.
4. An order compelling production of relocation, intake, comparability, counseling, and payment records.
5. Damages, costs, fees, and any other relief authorized by the governing claims ultimately pleaded.
