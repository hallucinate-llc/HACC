2. Produce all records identifying every county employee, contractor, case manager, representative payee, or Money Management worker who handled, discussed, or communicated about Jane Cortez during that period.
3. Produce all records showing whether any county employee named `Jemiliah`, `Jemiliah`, `Jamila`, `Jamilah`, or a substantially similar name communicated with Benjamin Barber, Jane Cortez, HACC, or any housing provider concerning Jane Cortez.
4. Produce all records reflecting any communication that Jane Cortez's housing, residence, voucher use, portability, or ability to live somewhere would be endangered if housing were pursued outside Clackamas County, including at Waterleaf or in Multnomah County.
5. Produce all records reflecting any instruction, recommendation, or statement that Benjamin Barber should stop paying Jane Cortez's rent.
6. Produce all records reflecting any statement that a check or payment would be sent to HACC on Jane Cortez's behalf.
7. Produce all records showing whether any such check or payment was issued, mailed, transmitted, logged, voided, stopped, rejected, or returned.

9. Produce all communications between county-side representative-payee or Money Management personnel and HACC concerning Jane Cortez's rent, arrears, voucher use, portability, relocation, household composition, or housing decisions.
10. Produce all communications between county-side representative-payee or Money Management personnel and any landlord or housing provider concerning Jane Cortez, including Waterleaf Apartments, Blossom, Parkside Heights, Easton Ridge Apartments, and Quantum Residential.
11. Produce all policies, manuals, desk guides, training materials, and instructions governing how county personnel may communicate about a participant's rent, voucher, portability, relocation, or housing-location choices.
12. Produce all policies, manuals, desk guides, training materials, and instructions governing when county personnel may direct another person to stop paying a participant's rent.
13. Produce all records reflecting whether Benjamin Barber was identified to county personnel as Jane Cortez's caregiver, live-in aide, guardian ad litem, rent payer, or housing advocate.
14. Produce all records reflecting whether county personnel understood Jane Cortez to have disability-related limitations affecting rent management, housing decisions, or need for assistance.

## Interrogatories

1. Identify every county employee or agent who communicated with Benjamin Barber about Jane Cortez's rent, voucher, portability, or housing decisions from January 1, 2025 through April 30, 2026.
2. State whether any county employee or agent told Benjamin Barber that Jane Cortez's housing or residence would be endangered if the household used a voucher at Waterleaf or outside Clackamas County; if so, identify the speaker, the date, and the substance of the statement.
3. State whether any county employee or agent told Benjamin Barber not to keep paying Jane Cortez's rent; if so, identify the speaker, the date, and the basis for that instruction.
4. State whether any county employee or agent represented that a check or payment would be sent to HACC on Jane Cortez's behalf; if so, identify the speaker, the date, the amount, and what happened next.
5. State whether any county payment for Jane Cortez's rent was issued, attempted, expected, or discussed in February 2026; if so, identify all supporting records.
6. State whether county personnel communicated with HACC about Jane Cortez's voucher portability, Waterleaf, Multnomah County housing, or any concern about Jane Cortez leaving Clackamas County.
7. State whether county personnel understood Benjamin Barber to be acting as Jane Cortez's caregiver, live-in aide, guardian ad litem, or primary housing helper, and if so when that understanding arose.
8. State whether county personnel were aware of Jane Cortez's disability-related limitations affecting rent handling, independent living, or housing decision-making, and identify the basis for that awareness.
9. State whether county personnel communicated with any housing provider or landlord about Jane Cortez during the relevant period, and identify each provider, the date, and the subject of the communication.
10. State the full legal and programmatic basis for any county effort to discourage, influence, or comment upon whether Jane Cortez should use housing outside Clackamas County.
