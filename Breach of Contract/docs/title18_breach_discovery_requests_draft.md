# Title 18 Breach Discovery Requests Draft

These requests are keyed to the current strongest breach findings: HACC premature eviction and relocation failures, plus Quantum intake obstruction.

1. Produce the complete Hillside Park Section 18 Phase II approval file, including the approval letter, all attachments, all conditions of approval, and all relocation-related submissions to HUD.
2. Produce every notice served on Benjamin Barber, Jane Cortez, or their household concerning demolition, displacement, relocation, vacate deadlines, or eviction from September 1, 2024 to present.
3. Produce proof of service, mailing logs, certified-mail receipts, posting logs, and delivery records for each such notice.
4. Produce the complete household-specific relocation file for the Barber/Cortez household.
5. Produce all relocation counseling logs, case notes, call logs, meeting notes, calendars, and advisory-service records for the Barber/Cortez household, or certify that no such records exist.
6. Produce all records of relocation expense estimates, commitments, approvals, denials, reimbursements, payments, and ledger entries for the Barber/Cortez household.
7. Produce all records identifying each replacement unit considered, screened, proposed, offered, rejected, or withheld for the Barber/Cortez household.
8. Produce all HQS inspections, comparability analyses, accessibility analyses, and approval records for each replacement unit offered or considered for the household.
9. Produce all records concerning the third-floor unit offered to the household, including accessibility review, internal discussion, and any consideration of ground-floor alternatives.
10. Produce all disability accommodation requests submitted by or on behalf of the household, and all responses, determinations, denials, approvals, or interactive-process records.
11. Produce all communications between HACC and Quantum Residential concerning Blossom & Community Apartments, Hillside Manor, or any packet/intake/application submitted by or for the household.
12. Produce all records reflecting HACC's understanding that Quantum Residential received but did not transmit the household's packet.
13. Produce all records stating, suggesting, or asserting that the household's relocation was complete or substantially complete before March 31, 2026.
14. Produce the complete eviction file relating to the March 31, 2026 displacement/eviction action, including the complaint, summons, filing transmittals, internal approvals, and proof of service.
15. Produce all internal communications discussing whether eviction could proceed before relocation counseling, relocation expenses, and comparable housing had been completed.

## Interrogatories to HACC

1. Identify every person who participated in relocation counseling, advisory services, or housing-placement decisions for the Barber/Cortez household.
2. State the date on which HACC contends Section 18 relocation duties became operative for the household and describe every duty HACC contends it fulfilled before eviction was pursued.
3. Identify each replacement unit HACC contends was comparable for the household and state all facts supporting that contention.
4. State whether HACC contends the third-floor unit offered to the household was accessible and comparable, and if so identify all supporting records and persons with knowledge.
5. State every fact supporting HACC's decision to pursue eviction or displacement on or before March 31, 2026 despite the household's contention that relocation remained incomplete.
6. Identify every communication in which Quantum Residential was instructed to transmit, process, or review the household's intake/application packet.
7. State whether HACC contends Quantum Residential was acting independently, as an HACC contractor, as a property manager in an HACC-linked portfolio, or in some other role with respect to the household's relocation path, and describe the factual basis for that contention.
