## Legal answer

VAWA does not appear to impose a single fixed numeric deadline for an out-of-county transfer or portability process. But it does require a covered housing provider to maintain and follow an emergency transfer plan and to take steps to provide internal and external emergency transfers for qualifying survivors. In the HCV context, HUD materials make clear the PHA is the covered housing provider responsible for the emergency transfer plan and procedures. That means HACC could not simply leave a survivor household in a drawn-out, contradictory process while move-out pressure and eviction threats continued.

The strongest legal framing is:

- not `HACC had X days under VAWA to finish porting to Multnomah County`;
- but `HACC had to respond to a qualifying VAWA safety request through an actual emergency-transfer process, assist with internal or external transfer steps, keep the information confidential, and not use delay or contradictory requirements to defeat the move`.

## Official authorities

- `24 C.F.R. § 5.2009` requires covered housing providers to adopt and follow an emergency transfer plan.
- HUD's April 2025 HCV Fair Housing chapter explains that in the HCV program, `PHAs, not owners, are the covered housing provider` responsible for the emergency transfer plan and for receiving and responding to emergency transfer requests.
- Form `HUD-5380` states that the provider must keep emergency-transfer requests confidential and must honor court orders concerning access or control of the property.
- HUD portability guidance explains portability is the mechanism for moving subsidy outside the initial PHA jurisdiction, but it does not supply a short hard deadline. See HUD portability overview and `24 C.F.R. §§ 982.353-355`.

## Practical litigation use

The strongest record-based argument is:

- by January 12, 2026, HACC was already on notice that Waterleaf was a live out-of-county option and that the application had been signed and turned in;
- by January 26, 2026, HACC itself acknowledged the household had chosen to port to Multnomah County;
- by March 17-18, 2026, Benjamin was complaining that he had been trying to move to Waterleaf for nearly three months;
- by March 25-26, 2026, Waterleaf had the household on a waiting list, but the voucher still had not been timely issued and ported.

That does not prove a statutory per se deadline violation by itself. But it does strongly support the claim that HACC's handling of the transfer was not prompt, not coherent, and not consistent with a genuine VAWA safety-transfer response.
