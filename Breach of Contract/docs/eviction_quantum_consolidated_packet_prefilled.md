## Memorandum in Support

### I. Overview

The allegations underlying this eviction are intertwined with a disability-related relocation process and promised housing pathways that Defendants allege were not completed. The present record supports a narrower but stronger showing that HACC and the Blossom-side owner/manager process were part of one integrated relocation and housing-access transaction. Quantum Residential is alleged to have participated in the Blossom-side intake, review, response, and placement failures now central to causation and defenses, and is further alleged on information and belief to have successor or related property interests to the extent discovery confirms that relationship.

### II. Factual Allegations Relevant to Joinder

1. Defendants allege they were in public housing for disabled residents and were required to transition through a relocation process.
2. Defendants allege Kati Tilton represented there were three relocation solutions, including:
   - A two-bedroom Parkside Heights application;
   - Voucher portability to Multnomah County; and
   - A third relocation pathway reflected in agency communications.
3. Defendants allege HACC's January 2026 Blossom notice tied Blossom to the same Tenant Protection Voucher relocation process and directed the family to contact Ashley Ferron to begin that process.
4. Defendants further allege HACC's public Hillside Park redevelopment materials stated that, after Section 18 disposition approval and issuance of Tenant Protection Vouchers, residents would relocate with HACC guidance and could either move into a new unit at Hillside Park or relocate with a Tenant Protection Voucher.
5. Defendants allege preserved HACC emails directed that, if the household was pursuing Blossom or Community Apartments, the packet should be returned to Blossom-side office staff, and later acknowledged that the packet had been submitted to Quantum Residential staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office but had not been provided to HACC.
6. Defendants allege Blossom materials state that applications for the project were processed through the HACC PBV waitlist.
7. Defendants allege they sent complaint emails to both HACC staff and a Blossom-side participant stating that the Blossom application was not being processed and that service-animal, discrimination, and housing-access problems were blocking the move.
8. Defendants allege owner/agent materials imposed a duty to review the application and notify the applicant of the result within a reasonable time.
9. Defendants allege they pursued the promised pathways in good faith.
10. Defendants allege relocation/accessibility failures included non-processing of promised applications and an inaccessible third-floor option for a household member who cannot safely use stairs.
11. Defendants allege those failures materially contributed to the circumstances now cited as eviction grounds.
12. Defendants further allege that preserved emails document that Quantum Residential "falsely claimed that we did not provide them with an application, which was also made to your organization in the first place" — a factual dispute about Quantum's conduct that requires Quantum's participation to resolve.
13. Defendants further allege, supported by HACC's own Administrative Plan, that Quantum Residential is named therein as the property management company for a PHA-owned mixed-finance development (Clayton Mohr Commons, 399 Caufield St, Oregon City, OR 97045) operating under a 20-year HUD HAP contract. The same plan classifies both Blossom & Community Apartments and Hillside Manor Apartments as excluded units constituting Section 18 replacement housing for a demolished original HACC project, with a first-occupancy preference for former residents — establishing that Quantum's role in HACC's housing program is documented in HACC's own filed administrative records, not merely alleged.

### III. Joinder and Third-Party Practice Are Appropriate

1. Common facts predominate across the eviction and third-party allegations.
2. The present record supports that HACC handled the displacement and voucher side of the same transaction while the Blossom-side actors handled application intake, packet transmission, review, and response duties.
3. HACC's own public Hillside Park redevelopment materials further support that Hillside Park placement and TPV relocation were being administered as parts of one redevelopment-driven relocation program.
4. HACC's own Administrative Plan names Quantum Residential as property management company for a PHA-owned HAP contract development in Clackamas County, and simultaneously classifies Blossom & Community Apartments as Section 18 replacement housing under HACC's PBV program. This is on-record administrative evidence — not inference — connecting Quantum's role to HACC's formal housing program structure, making joinder grounded in HACC's own filed documents.
5. Complete relief cannot be fairly adjudicated without Quantum's participation.
6. One proceeding avoids duplicative litigation and inconsistent outcomes.

### IV. Requested Relief

Defendants request joinder of Quantum Residential, leave to file and serve the third-party complaint, and case-management scheduling that permits coordinated adjudication.
