## Timing and Court Rules Compliance

### Pre-Motion Deadlines
- **Service:** At least 5 days before hearing (Oregon default; local rules may vary)
- **Local Rules Check:** Confirm Clackamas County Circuit Court rules for motion practice
- **Ex Parte Motions:** If emergency (eviction imminent), file Ex Parte Motion for Expedited Joinder (see `emergency_short_motion_quantum_joinder.md`)

### Motion Hearing Strategy
- **1st Appearance:** Argue joinder motion ONLY (ORCP 29A)
- **If Joinder Granted:** Proceed to argument on affirmative defenses
- **If Joinder Denied:** Preserve record via exception; continue with defenses against HACC alone
- **Hearing Duration:** Plan for 30–45 minutes (10–15 min per side for joinder + defenses)

### Post-Joinder Timeline
- If joinder granted, Quantum has 20 days to respond to complaint (ORCP 12(a))
- If Quantum files counterclaim or third-party claim, adjust discovery timeline

### Oral Argument Framework (30-40 minutes)
- **Opening (2 min):** Joinder is mandated by ORCP 29A because Quantum's acts prevent relocation, and its RAD successor status creates inconsistent obligations.
- **Quantum as Successor (8 min):** HACC's own Admin Plan names Quantum as property manager of Hillside Manor LP, the RAD successor created 1/1/2021. PIH 2019-23 binds all successor obligations. [Show Admin Plan excerpt; cite PIH 2019-23]
- **Intake Failure (8 min):** Ferron email proves Quantum received the packet but did not transmit it. Household submitted to Quantum's office; Quantum falsely denied receiving it. This intake obstruction prevented timely relocation—a key Section 18 precondition. [Read Ferron email; play household audio or read declaration]
- **Complete Relief Impossible (6 min):** Without Quantum, household cannot recover damages for intake failure; cannot enforce RAD right-to-return; cannot compel relocation cooperation. A judgment against HACC alone leaves Quantum free to obstruct.
- **Inconsistent Obligations (4 min):** HACC may be ordered to provide relocation assistance. But if Quantum is not a party, Quantum (as property manager) can continue to refuse cooperation, creating impossible conflicting duties.
- **Conclusion (2 min):** ORCP 29A mandates joinder. Joinder under 22B is also appropriate. Request order joining Quantum and scheduling further proceedings.
