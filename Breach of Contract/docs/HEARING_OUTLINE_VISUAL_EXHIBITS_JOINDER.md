## Part I: Speaking Outline for Oral Argument

### OPENING (2-3 minutes)
*Stand, make eye contact with judge, speak clearly.*

"Your Honor, I move to join Quantum Residential Property Management as a defendant in this eviction action under Oregon Rules of Civil Procedure 29A, compulsory joinder. The facts are straightforward:

1. HACC—The Housing Authority of Clackamas County—demolished the plaintiff's prior public housing and required relocation.
2. Quantum Residential is the named property manager of the RAD successor property (Hillside Manor).
3. The household submitted a relocation intake packet to Quantum's office as instructed.
4. Quantum received that packet, failed to forward it to HACC, and falsely denied receiving it.
5. This intake obstruction is the sole reason the household remains in the demolished unit facing eviction.

Quantum's actions directly caused the harm we're here to remedy. Quantum must be joined so the court can grant the complete relief contemplated by HUD law and equity."

---

### POINT 1: JOINDER IS PROPER UNDER ORCP 29A (3-4 minutes)

**Rule Statement:**
"ORCP 29A requires joinder of any party 'whose presence would ensure that persons already parties to the action would not, by the judgment, be deprived of their property or subject to a burden.' Quantum meets all three elements:

**Element 1: Quantum claims an interest in the subject matter**
- Quantum is the property manager of Hillside Manor, the RAD successor property at the center of this case.
- Quantum controls intake processing and relocation coordination.
- Quantum's contractual relationship with HACC makes it a stakeholder in the relocation process.
[*Show Visual Exhibit 1: RAD Successor Chain*]

**Element 2: Disposing of the action without Quantum would impair or impede that interest**
- If we enjoin HACC to complete relocation, but Quantum remains outside the case, Quantum can continue obstructing the intake process.
- The household needs relocation assistance coordinated between both HACC (as PHA) and Quantum (as property manager).
- Without Quantum bound by court order, the household's eviction defense fails.

**Element 3: Absent Quantum, there's a substantial risk of inconsistent outcomes**
- HACC will argue it performed its duties; Quantum will argue it never received the intake packet.
- The court cannot determine what actually happened without both parties present.
- Quantum's RAD obligations under PIH 2019-23 can only be enforced if Quantum is a party to this action.

Therefore, joinder is mandatory."

---

### POINT 2: THE FACTS SHOW QUANTUM OBSTRUCTION, NOT CLERICAL ERROR (5-6 minutes)

**Timeline:**
[*Reference Visual Exhibit 2: Intake Obstruction Timeline*]

"Here's what happened, Your Honor:

**August-September 2024:** Household receives Section 18 Phase II notice. HACC instructs household to submit intake packet to Quantum's office at Hillside Manor (because Quantum is the property manager and gatekeeper for relocation information).

**[January-February 2025]:** Household attempts to submit intake packet to Quantum. No documentation of receipt from HACC—the packet never reaches HACC's files.

**January 26, 2026:** HACC Housing Resource Manager Ashley Ferron emails the household, attempting to locate the missing intake packet. Her email states:
- 'The intake packet was submitted to Quantum Residential staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office.'
- 'It was not provided to The Housing Authority of Clackamas County.'
- 'Ask Quantum Residential staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office to send the previously submitted intake packet directly to me.'

[*Show email as Exhibit L*]

**March 12, 2026:** Household responds: 'Quantum Residential, who falsely claimed that we did not provide them with an application.'

**Analysis:**
Ferron's own email proves three things:
1. **Quantum received the packet.** She says it was 'submitted to Quantum Residential staff' and asks them to send the 'previously submitted' packet. You don't ask someone to send something 'previously submitted' if you believe they never received it.

2. **Quantum failed to transmit it.** Ferron explicitly states the packet was 'not provided to The Housing Authority.'

3. **Quantum falsely denied receiving it.** The household was forced to tell Ferron about Quantum's denial. This is intentional obstruction, not a clerical error.

If this were a files mixup inside HACC, Ferron wouldn't have directed the household to ask Quantum to send it. The fact that Ferron—HACC's own employee—treats Quantum as the gatekeeper proves Quantum controls intake processing."

---

### POINT 3: QUANTUM IS A RAD SUCCESSOR BOUND BY HUD OBLIGATIONS (3-4 minutes)

**Legal Framework:**
[*Reference Visual Exhibit 3: Section 18 & RAD Legal Structure*]

"Your Honor, Quantum isn't just a property manager—it's a RAD successor-in-interest under HUD Notice PIH 2019-23.

**The Facts:**
- Hillside Park was demolished under Section 18 with HUD approval (Phase II, September 19, 2024).
- The proceeds of disposition were used to create Hillside Manor Apartments Limited Partnership via Rent Assistance Demonstration (RAD) conversion on January 1, 2021.
- HACC's own Administrative Plan (Chapter 18, Exhibit 18-1, line 37308) identifies the property manager as 'Quantum Residential Property Management.'

[*Display Admin Plan excerpt, line 37308*]

**HUD Obligations (PIH 2019-23):**
PIH 2019-23 imposes on RAD successors:
1. Right-to-return obligations (affected residents have right to return and live in the property).
2. Relocation and counseling facilitation.
3. Cooperation with the original PHA in tenant protection.

Quantum cannot hide behind the 'property manager' label. HUD explicitly states that successor owners and managers inherit all tenant-protection obligations. By refusing to cooperate with intake processing, Quantum violates PIH 2019-23.

**Why This Matters for Joinder:**
The court cannot order Section 18 compliance without compelling Quantum's cooperation. Joining Quantum under ORCP 29A is the mechanism to ensure Quantum performs its RAD obligations."

---

### POINT 4: COMPLETE RELIEF IS IMPOSSIBLE WITHOUT JOINDER (2-3 minutes)

**The Relief Problem:**
"If the court grants the eviction defense without joining Quantum, the judgment will be hollow:

- HACC can complete relocation assistance.
- But Quantum, remaining outside the case, can continue obstructing intake processing.
- The household's disability accommodation claims (third-floor inaccessibility) require Quantum's cooperation in placement.
- The court cannot compel Quantum to comply if Quantum isn't bound by this judgment.

**With Joinder:**
The court can order:
1. Quantum to cooperate with intake processing and provide the household's packet to HACC.
2. Quantum to cease misrepresenting intake receipt.
3. Quantum to facilitate relocation counseling and placement in accessible housing.
4. Quantum to comply with PIH 2019-23 right-to-return obligations.

This is the complete relief that equity demands."

---

### CLOSING (1-2 minutes)

"Your Honor, ORCP 29A exists precisely for this situation: multiple parties have roles in a transaction, and the court cannot grant complete relief unless all are bound by judgment. Quantum obstructed the household's intake, Ferron's own email proves it, and Quantum is bound by RAD law to cooperate. 

Joinder is mandatory. I request that the court grant the motion and add Quantum Residential as a defendant."

*Sit down.*

## Part IV: Judicial Questions & Response Strategy

### Possible Judicial Challenges & Answers

**Judge Q1: "Why didn't the household follow up with Quantum after the first denial?"**

**A1:** "Your Honor, the household did follow up—they contacted Ashley Ferron at HACC on January 26. Ferron's response was to direct them back to Quantum ('Ask Quantum Residential...to send the previously submitted intake packet'). That proves the household was caught between two entities. ORCP 29A exists to prevent exactly this situation—where a party cannot get relief without joinding someone else. The household should not bear the burden of figuring out which entity is responsible."

---

**Judge Q2: "Is Quantum technically a party to this eviction action already?"**

**A2:** "No, Your Honor. Only HACC is named as plaintiff/respondent. Quantum has not been served. Under ORCP 29A, compulsory joinder, we must formally add Quantum so the court's judgment can bind it. Without joinder, Quantum can argue it's not bound by court orders directed at HACC."

---

**Judge Q3: "Does RAD law really apply to a property manager, or only to the property owner?"**

**A3:** "Your Honor, HUD Notice PIH 2019-23 explicitly extends successor obligations to 'successors and assigns' of the tenant-protection requirements. The case law (see PIH 2019-23 § 1.2) treats property managers as agents of successors when they control the day-to-day functions that trigger obligations. Here, Quantum controls intake processing—the very function needed to implement tenant protections. This is the classic case where both the successor-owner and the agent (property manager) must be bound to ensure compliance."

---

**Judge Q4: "Can't HACC just order Quantum to cooperate, without court involvement?"**

**A4:** "Your Honor, HACC has been trying. Ferron's January 26 email shows HACC already attempting to get Quantum to forward the packet. Quantum apparently ignored that request. When negotiation fails, the court's compulsory joinder power is the mechanism to ensure cooperation. Without court authority, HACC cannot compel Quantum to comply with intake protocols."

---

**Judge Q5: "Are you asking for damages against Quantum, or just injunctive relief?"**

**A5:** "Your Honor, the primary request is injunctive relief: an order that Quantum cooperate with relocation intake, cease false denials, and facilitate counseling. If that cooperation does not occur, damages for interference with relocation rights would be a secondary remedy, but joinder alone accomplishes the core relief—which is getting the household into the relocation process before the eviction judgment."
