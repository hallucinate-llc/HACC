# Eviction Counterclaim Elements Checklist

Use this as a filing-prep checklist to decide which sections of the Answer/Counterclaim draft are ready now and which should stay alternative or conditional.

## HACC Counterclaim Theory: Strongest Current Support

### 1. Prior Material Breach / Premature Eviction

- Current support:
  - Section 18 Phase II approval/notice triggered relocation process.
  - March 31, 2026 eviction event states relocation was incomplete.
  - Current record says no comparable housing, no relocation expenses, no counseling.
- Best exhibits:
  - Exhibit M
  - Exhibit I
  - Exhibit L
- What would strengthen it:
  - Certified eviction docket and service records.
  - HACC counseling and relocation-payment files.

### 2. Failure to Provide Comparable / Accessible Replacement Housing

- Current support:
  - HACC administrative materials classify Blossom and Hillside Manor as replacement housing.
  - Third-floor unit offered despite mobility limitations.
  - No accessible alternative yet shown.
- Best exhibits:
  - Exhibit I
  - Admin Plan excerpts
- What would strengthen it:
  - HQS/comparability analyses.
  - Formal denial or lack of accessible alternative records.

### 3. Failure to Provide Relocation Counseling / Expense Support

- Current support:
  - HACC relocation materials promise counseling/advisory services.
  - Eviction event states no counseling and no relocation expenses.
- Best exhibits:
  - Exhibit M
  - relocation materials
  - eviction timeline records
- What would strengthen it:
  - Relocation counselor logs.
  - Payment ledger and reimbursement file.

## Quantum Theory: Strongest Current Support

### 4. Intake Obstruction / Failure to Forward Packet

- Current support:
  - Packet submitted to Quantum-side office.
  - HACC later admits it was not provided to HACC.
  - HACC instructs Quantum to send the packet.
  - Application materials impose owner/agent review duty.
- Best exhibits:
  - Exhibit L
  - Exhibit C
  - Vera application materials
- What would strengthen it:
  - Quantum intake logs.
  - Transmission metadata.
  - Internal communications.

### 5. False Denial of Receipt

- Current support:
  - Household email says Quantum falsely claimed no application was provided.
- Best exhibits:
  - Exhibit L
- Use carefully:
  - Best pleaded on information and belief unless corroborated.
- What would strengthen it:
  - Quantum email, witness, CRM note, or timestamped receipt proof.

## HUD Theory: Weaker, Keep Conditional

### 6. Oversight / Non-Enforcement

- Current support:
  - HUD approved the redevelopment step that triggered relocation duties.
- Current weakness:
  - No equally strong proof yet that HUD received notice of later noncompliance and declined to act.
- Recommendation:
  - Keep as a developing theory unless notice-and-response records are obtained.

## Filing Guidance

- Safe to plead now:
  - affirmative defenses based on prior breach, prevention, failure of condition precedent, unclean hands, accessibility failure, and recoupment
  - counterclaim theories against HACC for relocation failures
  - joinder / third-party theory against Quantum for packet obstruction
- Plead more cautiously:
  - any claim that Quantum expressly lied about receipt unless framed on information and belief
  - any direct HUD liability theory
- Best immediate supporting attachments:
  - Exhibit M
  - Exhibit I
  - Exhibit L
  - Administrative Plan excerpts for Hillside Manor / Blossom / excluded units
