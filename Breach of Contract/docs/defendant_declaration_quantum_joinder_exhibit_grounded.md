# Defendant Declaration In Support Of Joinder And Leave To File Third-Party Complaint

I, [DEFENDANT NAME], declare under penalty of perjury as follows:

1. I am the Defendant in this action and make this declaration from personal knowledge.
2. My household was required to relocate through a Housing Authority of Clackamas County relocation process connected to the loss of our current housing placement. Exhibits A, B, F, and J.
3. HACC represented that there were multiple relocation pathways, including a Parkside Heights application, voucher portability, and the Blossom opportunity. Exhibits C, D, E, and F.
4. On January 8, 2026, HACC sent a Blossom-related notice stating in substance that the Tenant Protection Voucher process had not yet started and directing us to contact Ashley Ferron to begin that process. Exhibit C.
5. The Blossom materials we received stated that applications for the project were processed through the HACC PBV waitlist. Exhibit C.
6. I also reviewed HACC's public Hillside Park redevelopment page, which stated that residents in the redevelopment process would relocate with HACC guidance and could either move into a new Hillside Park unit or relocate with a Tenant Protection Voucher. Based on that public information, I understood Hillside-related placement and voucher relocation to be parts of the same HACC-run redevelopment and relocation process.
7. HACC also directed in writing that, if our household was pursuing Blossom or Community Apartments, the packet should be returned to the Blossom-side office staff. Exhibit L.
8. HACC later confirmed in writing that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum Residential staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office but had not been provided to HACC. Exhibit L.
9. HACC then instructed that Quantum staff send the previously submitted intake packet directly to HACC or that a new packet be completed and returned. Exhibit L.
10. Based on those communications, I understood that HACC and the Blossom or Quantum side were operating in one connected intake, review, and voucher-processing sequence rather than in two unrelated housing processes. Exhibits C and L.
11. My household pursued the relocation pathways in good faith, including the Parkside path and voucher-related steps. Exhibits D and E.
12. I also understood that Quantum or the Blossom-side staff were participating in the application, packet-handling, or placement process because HACC's own written communications referred to returning the packet to that office and later stated that Quantum staff had received the packet. Exhibit L.
13. In a contemporaneous email sent to HACC during this period, I stated in substance that the person causing delays was HACC's office and Quantum Residential, "who falsely claimed that we did not provide them with an application, which was also made to your organization in the first place." Exhibit L. That denial is part of the dispute this proceeding cannot fairly resolve without Quantum's participation.
14. I have also reviewed HACC's own Administrative Plan materials, which, based on my review, identify Quantum Residential as the property management company for a Housing Authority of Clackamas County-owned development operating under a federal housing assistance payment contract. The same plan materials classify both Blossom &amp; Community Apartments and Hillside Manor Apartments as replacement housing for a demolished original HACC project, with a first-occupancy preference for former residents. Based on my review, I understand that the Blossom opportunity was embedded in HACC's formal administrative program for relocation housing, not an independent private-market listing.
15. My household also faced accessibility problems in the proposed relocation process, including the issue that a third-floor unit would not be feasible for a household member with serious stair and mobility limitations. Exhibit I.
16. In my understanding, the failures in intake handling, application processing, voucher progression, and accessibility-related placement all contributed to the circumstances now being used as grounds for eviction. Exhibits C, E, I, J, K, and L.
17. I believe complete relief cannot be granted in this matter unless Quantum Residential is joined, because HACC's own records show that Quantum-side staff were part of the same relocation and intake process at issue here, and HACC's own administrative plan documents identify Quantum Residential's role in HACC-owned housing operations in Clackamas County. Exhibits C and L.
18. I further believe that trying these issues separately would create a serious risk of incomplete factfinding or inconsistent rulings about who handled, delayed, or failed to transmit the paperwork and housing opportunity tied to our relocation.

I declare under penalty of perjury that the foregoing is true and correct.
