# Optional Public-Source Note: HACC Hillside Park Redevelopment Page

Use this note only as supplemental public-source support. It is not a substitute for Exhibit L or the preserved HACC email record.

## Source

- URL: https://www.clackamas.us/housingauthority/hillsidemasterplan.html
- Page Title: Hillside Park Redevelopment
- Publisher: Housing Authority of Clackamas County / Clackamas County

## Key Public Statements

The page states in substance:

1. HACC submitted a Section 18 disposition application to HUD for the remaining public housing units at Hillside Park.
2. Once HACC receives disposition approval and Tenant Protection Vouchers, residents can begin the relocation process with guidance and assistance from HACC and its relocation consultants.
3. Residents will be given the option to move into a new unit at Hillside Park or relocate with a Tenant Protection Voucher to a unit of their choosing.

## Why It Matters To The Joinder Motion

1. It supports the framing that Hillside-related placement and TPV relocation were being presented by HACC as linked parts of the same redevelopment-driven relocation program.
2. It helps rebut any claim that Hillside, Blossom, or voucher relocation should automatically be treated as wholly unrelated housing tracks.
3. It provides public-facing context for why HACC-directed relocation, owner-manager intake, and voucher administration should be viewed together.

## Litigation Use Caution

1. Use this note as public-context support, not as the primary proof of the HACC-to-Quantum handoff.
2. The stronger direct evidence remains Exhibit L, where HACC stated that the packet had been submitted to Quantum staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office and was not provided to HACC.
3. If you attach or cite this page in court, identify it as a public agency webpage or request judicial notice only if appropriate under local practice.

HACC's own Hillside Park redevelopment page publicly described redevelopment relocation as including either a new Hillside unit or relocation with a Tenant Protection Voucher, which is consistent with the record showing one integrated relocation process rather than unrelated housing tracks.
