# Hearing Script: Motion for Joinder and Third-Party Complaint (Quantum Residential)

## Opening

Your Honor, this motion asks for joinder of Quantum Residential and leave to file a third-party complaint so the Court can resolve all materially related housing facts in one proceeding.

This is not just a separate landlord dispute. The present record shows one integrated relocation and housing-access process involving both HACC and the Blossom-side application path.

## Core Points

1. This eviction did not arise in isolation. It arose in the middle of a disability-related relocation process.
2. HACC identified three relocation pathways, including a two-bedroom Parkside Heights application and voucher portability to Multnomah County.
3. HACC's September 19, 2024 Phase II General Information Notice — delivered by hand and certified mail — confirmed that HUD granted HACC Section 18 disposition approval for Hillside Park and formally triggered the Tenant Protection Voucher and relocation process. That is Exhibit M.
4. HACC's January 2026 Blossom notice tied Blossom to the same Tenant Protection Voucher process and directed us to contact Ashley Ferron to start that process.
5. HACC's own Hillside Park redevelopment page also states residents would relocate with HACC guidance and could either move into a new Hillside Park unit or relocate with a Tenant Protection Voucher.
6. HACC also directed that, if we were pursuing Blossom, the packet should be returned to Blossom-side office staff, and later acknowledged that the packet had been submitted to Quantum staff but had not been provided onward to HACC. Exhibit L.
7. Preserved emails also show we told HACC directly that Quantum Residential "falsely claimed that we did not provide them with an application" — a factual dispute that can only be resolved with Quantum in the case.
8. Blossom materials stated that applications for that project were processed through the HACC PBV waitlist.
9. Critically, HACC's own Administrative Plan names Quantum Residential as the property management company for a PHA-owned mixed-finance development in Oregon City, Clackamas County, operating under a 20-year HUD HAP contract. That same plan also classifies Blossom & Community Apartments and Hillside Manor Apartments as excluded units — Section 18 replacement housing for a demolished HACC project — with former-resident relocation preferences. This is not inference. It is in HACC's own filed administrative plan.
10. We then sent complaint emails to both HACC staff and the Blossom-side contact stating that the application was not being processed and that accommodation and discrimination issues were blocking the move.
11. The owner or agent side also had its own duty to review the application and notify us of the result.
12. We allege the promised application path was not completed and that an inaccessible third-floor option was offered for a resident who cannot use stairs.
13. Those alleged failures are directly connected to the claimed default now used for eviction.

## Why Joinder Is Needed

1. Complete relief cannot be granted without Quantum in the case.
2. The facts overlap substantially with defenses and equitable issues already before this Court because HACC handled the displacement and voucher side while the Blossom-side actors handled intake, review, and response.
3. HACC's own public redevelopment materials show Hillside Park placement and TPV relocation were being presented as part of one relocation program, not unrelated tracks.
4. **Most importantly: HACC's own Administrative Plan, a filed administrative record, names Quantum Residential as property management company for a PHA-owned HAP contract development and classifies both Blossom and Hillside Manor as Section 18 replacement housing under HACC's PBV program.** This is not circumstantial. It is HACC's own administrative documentation of the relationship.
5. If Quantum is not in the case, the Court will be asked to decide causation and prevention on an incomplete party record.
6. One case avoids duplicate litigation and inconsistent results.

## Requested Relief

I ask the Court to grant joinder and grant leave to file and serve the third-party complaint.

## Optional Rebuttal Lines

If opposing counsel says this is a separate dispute:

- The same relocation facts are being used as context for eviction liability, and the present record ties Blossom directly to HACC's voucher and waitlist process, so they are not truly separate.

If opposing counsel says this will delay the case:

- Any short scheduling adjustment is outweighed by avoiding duplicative litigation and inconsistent rulings.

If opposing counsel says allegations are unproven:

- That is exactly why joinder is necessary: to litigate those material facts with the responsible entity present.

If opposing counsel says Quantum has no duty to us:

- The record already supports that the owner or agent side had an application-review and notice role within the same HACC-linked housing process.
