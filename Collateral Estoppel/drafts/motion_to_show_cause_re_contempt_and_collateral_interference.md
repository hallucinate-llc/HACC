## MOTION FOR ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE
## RE: WHY SOLOMON BARBER SHOULD NOT BE HELD IN CONTEMPT

Movant requests an Order requiring Solomon Barber to appear and show cause why the Court should not enter contempt-related relief, noninterference directives, and related remedial orders.

### I. Requested relief

1. Issue an Order to Show Cause requiring Solomon Barber to appear on a date certain.
2. Require Solomon Barber to explain why his conduct does not amount to contempt or contempt-like noncompliance with operative court orders, if the Court finds a valid enforceable order and notice.
3. Enter interim noninterference directives preventing further collateral conduct affecting Jane Cortez, her housing path, or any court-authorized authority structure.
4. Award any other relief the Court finds just, including evidentiary, coercive, corrective, or claim-limiting relief authorized by law and supported by the record.

### II. Core grounds

5. Movant alleges that an enforceable order or authority structure existed and that Solomon Barber had notice of it.
6. Movant further alleges that, instead of seeking timely court modification or clarification, Solomon Barber treated enforceability as optional or self-defined.
7. The current staged record supports the following provisional chronology:
   a. a restraining order against `Solomon Samuel Barber` was granted on or about `November 20, 2025`, and the order materials state that the order was in effect and that no further service was necessary because respondent appeared in person before the court;
   b. Solomon's own later messages state, among other things, `I'm just not incentivized to cooperate`, `I'd just have the judge overturn anyway`, and `Once I get served and it goes into effect, I will comply ... But it is not in effect`;
   c. Benjamin and Jane later declared that, during the March 23, 2026 Hillside Park inspection, Charley Skee explained in substance that Benjamin had been taken off the lease because Benjamin's brother and stepfather had been emailing documents to HACC; and
   d. by March 17 and March 25-26, 2026, HACC was being told in writing that restrained-party communications and third-party housing contact were still occurring.
8. If proven, that sequence supports a show-cause inquiry into whether Solomon continued through collateral channels rather than through lawful court process, and whether contempt-related noncompliance occurred.

### III. Contempt theory (proof-gated)

9. This motion does not assume contempt has already been established.
10. Instead, Movant asks the Court to require Solomon Barber to explain why contempt-related relief should not enter if the Court finds:
   a. a valid enforceable order existed;
   b. Solomon Barber had notice of it;
   c. he nevertheless engaged in prohibited interference, noncompliance, or collateral conduct inconsistent with the order's purpose; and
   d. that conduct caused prejudice, delay, or disruption.
11. The present theory is that Solomon Barber sought practical control through collateral channels despite court-created boundaries, including housing-related influence if proven by admissible records or testimony.

### IV. Repeated usurpation / collateral-channel theory

12. On information and belief, the same pattern appears across the related guardianship and housing disputes:
   a. a legal status, protective boundary, or role allocation is granted;
   b. Solomon Barber has notice or acts as if he knows of it;
   c. rather than seek orderly relief from the court, he continues through collateral channels; and
   d. those collateral acts are used to affect control, access, housing, or authority outcomes involving Jane Cortez.
13. This theory is offered as a reason to require explanation now, not as a request that the Court make findings beyond the current proof.
