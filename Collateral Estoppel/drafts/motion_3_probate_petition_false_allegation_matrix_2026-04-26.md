## Legal Sorting Categories

Use these labels when reviewing each allegation:

1. `Direct false statement`: a factual assertion that can be affirmatively disproved.
2. `Material misleading omission`: a statement that may be literally partial but creates a false emergency narrative by omitting known context.
3. `No reasonable basis`: an allegation that Solomon must identify a competent source for, because it was presented as evidence in an emergency petition.
4. `Improper collateral purpose`: an allegation used to bypass, undermine, or evade the `25PO11530` restraining order instead of seeking relief in the issuing court.
5. `Needs proof`: allegation is suspicious or disputed but should not yet be called perjury without more records.

### 7. Court-process / eviction allegation

Benjamin's proffer:

- Benjamin's insistence on court process was not misconduct.
- Federal relocation law required more process before displacement.

Federal authorities:

- 24 CFR 970.21 requires a PHA in a demolition/disposition context to notify families 90 days before displacement and provide comparable housing.
- 24 CFR 970.21 states tenant-based assistance such as a voucher is not comparable housing until the family is actually relocated into such housing.
- If a separate URA-covered relocation framework applies, 49 CFR 24.203 provides that no lawful occupant may be required to move without 90 days advance written notice and that the occupant cannot be required to move unless a comparable replacement dwelling has been made available.

Use in Motion 3:

- Do not overstate as a universal `90 days after voucher issuance` rule.
- Stronger phrasing: `Solomon's petition omitted the federal relocation framework, under which the issue was not simply whether Benjamin would leave voluntarily, but whether HACC had completed the required notice, comparable-housing, voucher, relocation, and displacement steps.`

Proof targets:

- HACC Section 18 / relocation notice.
- Voucher issuance date.
- Comparable replacement-housing offer.
- Actual relocation status.
- [federal_relocation_90_day_and_comparable_housing_authorities_2026-04-26.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/federal_relocation_90_day_and_comparable_housing_authorities_2026-04-26.md).

## Current Strongest Sanctions Theory

The cleanest sanctions theory is not simply that Solomon was wrong. It is:

1. Solomon had actual notice of the `25PO11530` restraining order and record-production obligations.
2. Solomon took the position that the order was not binding unless served on his terms.
3. Solomon then filed a collateral probate petition under penalty of perjury.
4. The petition sought emergency and plenary control over Jane, waiver of notice, fees from Jane's funds, and a writ allowing law enforcement entry and transport.
5. The petition used a housing-and-danger narrative that omitted the restraining order, omitted Solomon's alleged role in the HACC/authority chain, omitted HACC/Quantum delay, and misdescribed Benjamin's household history.
6. If Solomon knew those omissions or statements were false or materially misleading, the filing supports remedial contempt in `25PO11530` as an evasion of the protective order, and supports sanctions or referral in `26PR00641`.
