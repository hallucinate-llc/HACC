## Filing Track 1: Remedial Contempt for Failure to Produce Court-Ordered Records

Related use: cite or attach this motion in probate case `26PR00641` only if Solomon relies on estate, trust, power-of-attorney, HACC, or authority papers while still refusing production.

This is the strongest and cleanest contempt track. The court order required Solomon to return or produce records relating to Jane Cortez, estate/property matters, and records mentioning Jane. Solomon did not produce them by the deadline and continued not producing them after written notice through counsel.

### Elements to plead

4. Solomon had possession, custody, control, or at least responsive knowledge of the records or could identify what happened to them.
5. Solomon failed or refused to produce them.
6. The failure was willful, or at minimum should be treated as willful unless Solomon proves inability to comply.

## Filing Track 2: Remedial Contempt / Protective-Order Violation for Evasion, Service-Avoidance, and Housing Interference

### Proposed title

### Best forum / case

File in `25PO11530`, because ORS 124.012 provides that contempt proceedings for violation of an EPPDAPA restraining order must be conducted by the issuing court or by the circuit court for a county where the violation occurs.

Related use: file a notice or copy in `26PR00641` only if needed to show the probate court that contempt/enforcement issues are pending in the restraining-order case.

### Core theory

Solomon knew about the restraining order, denied that he was bound by it unless served on his terms, said he was not incentivized to cooperate, and then continued acting through collateral channels. The alleged collateral conduct includes interfering with Jane and Benjamin's HACC housing and lease status by communicating with HACC or causing documents to be presented under purported authority, including alleged power-of-attorney authority.

This motion should be factually careful: the direct HACC actor-identification record is still a key proof target. The motion can allege the HACC interference on information and belief, supported by the Google Voice messages, Solomon's own petition allegations, Skee declaration testimony, and the HACC household-composition timeline.

### Elements to plead

1. A valid EPPDAPA restraining order existed.
2. Solomon had notice or actual awareness of it.
3. The order restrained abuse, intimidation, property-control conduct, contact/interference, and required compliance with guardian/conservator instructions.
4. Solomon knowingly took the position that the order was not effective unless served on his terms, rather than seeking proper modification or clarification.
5. Solomon continued or caused collateral housing conduct involving Jane, Benjamin, HACC, lease status, household composition, housing documents, or purported authority.
6. That conduct violated, resisted, obstructed, or evaded the court's order and impaired the protection the court created.

## Filing Track 3: Remedial Contempt / Protective-Order Evasion Based on Collateral Probate Petition Allegations

### Proposed title

Motion for Order to Show Cause Why Solomon Samuel Barber Should Not Be Held in Remedial Contempt for Evading the Restraining Order Through a Collateral Probate Petition Containing False or Materially Misleading Allegations

### Best forum / case

File in `25PO11530`.

Important framing point: because the filing is in `25PO11530`, this should not be styled as a standalone ORCP 17 sanctions motion. ORCP 17 sanctions for a probate filing are procedurally cleaner in probate case `26PR00641`. In `25PO11530`, the theory should be that the probate petition was part of a pattern of evading, resisting, or obstructing the restraining order and the protective framework created by that order.

### Core theory

Solomon filed a probate petition that made materially misleading or false factual allegations, including that Benjamin had been incarcerated until recently, had just begun living with Jane, was dangerous, and caused the housing crisis. The petition omitted critical context: Jane was seeking protective relief against Solomon, Solomon had already been restrained, HACC and Quantum caused or contributed to housing delays, and outside document flows from Benjamin's brother and stepfather were identified as affecting lease handling. In `25PO11530`, the point is not to impose ORCP 17 sanctions for the probate filing, but to show that the probate filing was a collateral method of resisting or evading the protective order.

### Issues to separate inside the motion

1. False or materially misleading recent-arrival/incarceration narrative:
   - Solomon's petition allegedly portrayed Benjamin as recently incarcerated and newly living with Jane.
   - Benjamin's staged declaration evidence says Jane raised him and he had lived with her for years.
2. False or materially misleading danger narrative:
   - The petition used danger and APS/police-interference allegations to support emergency relief.
   - The omitted context is that Jane was seeking restraining-order relief against Solomon and that Solomon was the restrained person.
3. False or materially misleading housing-causation narrative:
   - The petition blamed Benjamin for housing delay or voucher failure.
   - The record shows HACC/Quantum delay, lease-removal confusion, outside document flows, and HACC's own internal-review statements.
4. Omitted Solomon-caused or Solomon-linked housing interference:
   - The petition complained about housing while omitting Solomon's own alleged role in collateral communications, claimed authority, or document flows.
5. Internal trust / estate inconsistency:
   - The petition presents the estate as small while also invoking substantial inheritance / special-needs trust issues.
   - This supports record-completion and credibility relief.

5. Enter evidentiary or issue-limiting relief in `25PO11530`, including an order that Solomon may not rely in the restraining-order case on unproduced documents or materially misleading allegations.
6. Award remedial contempt sanctions, costs, fees if available, and any other effective remedy under ORS 33.105.
7. State that ORCP 17 sanctions for the probate filing itself may be separately sought in `26PR00641`.

3. File Track 3 in `25PO11530` only as a contempt/evasion motion. Keep any ORCP 17 sanctions request for `26PR00641` separate.

## Drafting Cautions

1. Keep contempt against Solomon separate from counsel conduct unless the motion specifically targets counsel under ORCP 17 or another rule.
2. Do not overstate the HACC actor-identification proof. Say "on information and belief" unless a source record directly names Solomon as the person who caused Benjamin's lease removal.
3. For Track 3, use "false or materially misleading" where the record proves contradiction; use "unsupported and requiring proof" where the record is not yet complete.
4. Avoid asking for punitive contempt unless a prosecutor or appointed prosecutor is involved. Remedial contempt is the correct private-party path.
5. For spoliation/evidence suppression, ask for adverse presumptions and issue-limiting relief as alternatives if Solomon claims records are gone, unavailable, or never existed.
6. Do not ask the `25PO11530` court to impose ORCP 17 sanctions for a pleading filed in `26PR00641`; ask for contempt/evasion relief in `25PO11530` and reserve probate sanctions for the probate case.
