## MOTION FOR ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE
## RE: SOLOMON BARBER'S POST-ORDER HOUSING INTERFERENCE AND POSSIBLE VIOLATION OR EVASION OF THE ORIGINAL RESTRAINING ORDER

Movant requests an Order requiring Solomon Barber to appear and show cause why the Court should not find that he violated, evaded, or improperly worked around the original restraining-order framework by continuing to interfere in housing matters involving Jane Kay Cortez, Benjamin Barber, and the Housing Authority of Clackamas County (`HACC`).

### I. Requested relief

1. Issue an Order to Show Cause requiring Solomon Barber to appear on a date certain.
2. Require Solomon Barber to explain why his post-order conduct concerning Jane Cortez's housing, household composition, voucher, relocation, or lease matters did not violate or evade the original restraining-order framework.
3. Require Solomon Barber to explain why his later guardianship filing includes detailed housing allegations, household address information, and occupancy / voucher assertions concerning Jane Cortez and Benjamin Barber.
4. Require Solomon Barber to explain why any collateral housing-related communications or influence efforts should not be treated as improper interference with the original matter and the court-created protective structure.
5. Enter appropriate remedial orders, including noninterference directives, claim-limiting relief, or further record-production orders to the extent authorized by law and supported by admissible evidence.

In context, that March 17 warning was made during an active dispute over voucher issuance, relocation, lease status, and housing administration, and is best read as notice that continued communication with Solomon or other restrained parties was interfering with Benjamin Barber's tenant rights and the household's housing process.

16. The broader housing workspace and prior synthesis materials therefore support a proof-gated but substantial interference theory that, after notice of the restraining order, Solomon may have continued seeking practical control through collateral housing channels.
17. The current record also supports a narrower elimination-based inference:
   a. Benjamin Barber did not request his own removal from the lease;
   b. Jane Cortez did not request Benjamin's removal from the lease;
   c. Julio Regal Florez-Cortez is separately described in the workspace declarations, from Benjamin's personal knowledge, as unable to read or write in any language, which materially weakens any theory that Julio was independently generating written housing demands, packet corrections, or asserted-authority paperwork during the same period;

   e. the Skee declarations attribute the removal, in substance, to outside document flows from Benjamin's brother and stepfather.
18. On that record, the present question is who, other than Benjamin or Jane, was exerting or claiming authority over Jane's housing decisions.
19. On information and belief, Solomon is a plausible outside actor in that chain, including through possible claimed authority or de facto control, especially where Benjamin and Jane did not request Benjamin's removal and Julio was not in a position to generate written asserted-authority materials on his own, even though the repository does not yet contain a clean power-of-attorney instrument or a direct HACC note confirming the exact basis.

   a. a granted order with in-person appearance language and operative restrictions;
   b. parallel granted Julio protective-order packets showing the same residence and household-control conflict had already been formalized in court;
   c. Solomon's later self-help noncompliance posture;
   d. the later guardianship petition's detailed housing assertions;
   e. declaration testimony that outside document flows from Benjamin's brother and stepfather affected lease handling; and
   f. written HACC notice that continued communication with restrained parties was interfering with Benjamin Barber's tenant rights and the household's housing process.

26. The Court can nonetheless require Solomon to show cause now because the existing record already supports a serious question whether he continued using collateral channels to affect housing matters involving Jane Cortez after the order was granted.
