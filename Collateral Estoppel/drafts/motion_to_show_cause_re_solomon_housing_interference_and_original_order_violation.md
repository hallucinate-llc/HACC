## MOTION FOR ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE
## RE: SOLOMON BARBER'S POST-ORDER HOUSING INTERFERENCE AND POSSIBLE VIOLATION OR EVASION OF THE ORIGINAL RESTRAINING ORDER

Movant requests an Order requiring Solomon Barber to appear and show cause why the Court should not find that he violated, evaded, or improperly worked around the original restraining-order framework by continuing to interfere in housing matters involving Jane Kay Cortez, Benjamin Barber, and the Housing Authority of Clackamas County (`HACC`).

### I. Requested relief

1. Issue an Order to Show Cause requiring Solomon Barber to appear on a date certain.
2. Require Solomon Barber to explain why his post-order conduct concerning Jane Cortez's housing, household composition, voucher, relocation, or lease matters did not violate or evade the original restraining-order framework.
3. Require Solomon Barber to explain why his later guardianship filing includes detailed housing allegations, household address information, and occupancy / voucher assertions concerning Jane Cortez and Benjamin Barber.
4. Require Solomon Barber to explain why any collateral housing-related communications or influence efforts should not be treated as improper interference with the original matter and the court-created protective structure.
5. Enter appropriate remedial orders, including noninterference directives, claim-limiting relief, or further record-production orders to the extent authorized by law and supported by admissible evidence.

### II. Grounds

6. The original Solomon restraining-order packet supports that:
   a. the order was granted;
   b. it was in effect;
   c. no further service was necessary because Respondent appeared in person before the court; and
   d. Solomon was restrained from abuse, interference, contact, and certain property-control conduct.
7. The same packet supports that `Benjamin Jay Barber` appeared in the guardian ad litem / guardian petitioner role for `Jane Kay Cortez`, making the order's directive to follow the petitioner's guardian or conservator more specific in this record.
8. The later Solomon text record supports that after notice of the order, Solomon adopted a self-help posture about service and enforceability rather than a straightforward compliance posture.
9. The present guardianship petition filed by Solomon includes detailed allegations about:
   a. Jane's subsidized housing and eviction status;
   b. Benjamin Barber allegedly living in violation of occupancy rules;
   c. supposed failure to provide documentation to `OHA`;
   d. supposed inability to obtain a housing voucher; and
   e. household address and occupancy details.
10. Those petition allegations matter because they suggest continued knowledge of, and possible continued involvement in, the same housing and household matters that the original protective framework was supposed to separate from Solomon's interference.
11. The March 23, 2026 declarations of Benjamin Barber and Jane Cortez each support that Charley Skee explained in substance that Benjamin had been taken off the lease because Benjamin's brother and stepfather had been emailing documents to HACC, and that the situation continued until staff sorted through those materials.
12. The `JC Household` chain also supports an earlier county-notice point on December 2, 2025, when Benjamin emailed Ashley Ferron and copied Charley Skee, HACC Public Housing Management, and HACC Relocation in the same dispute stream, and by March 17, 2026 HACC had again received direct written warning from Benjamin asking whether HACC was still in communication with parties who had restraining orders against them in the courts.
13. In context, that March 17 warning was made during an active dispute over voucher issuance, relocation, lease status, and housing administration, and is best read as notice that continued communication with Solomon or other restrained parties was interfering with Benjamin Barber's tenant rights and the household's housing process.
14. The broader housing workspace and prior synthesis materials support a proof-gated but substantial interference theory that, after notice of the restraining order, Solomon may have continued seeking practical control through collateral housing channels.
15. The current record also supports a narrower elimination-based inference:
   a. Benjamin Barber did not request his own removal from the lease;
   b. Jane Cortez did not request Benjamin's removal from the lease;
   c. the deeper `JC Household` chain shows that on January 12, 2026 HACC first wrote that `Julio is a member of the Household and will need to complete paperwork as well` unless court documents were submitted, then later wrote that `Currently, Julio and Jane are the only household members and approved occupants of the unit,` and then wrote again that, effective January 1, 2026, Benjamin was no longer listed as a member of the household and that the household composition had been determined to consist of Julio and Jane following `internal review` and based on `court documentation currently on file,` while the broader sequence also includes a February 13, 2026 HACC 30-Day Notice of Termination addressed directly to Julio Regal Florez-Cortez; and
   d. the Skee declarations attribute the removal, in substance, to outside document flows from Benjamin's brother and stepfather.
16. On that record, the present question is who, other than Benjamin or Jane, was exerting or claiming authority over Jane's housing decisions.
17. On information and belief, Solomon is a plausible outside actor in that chain, including through possible claimed authority or de facto control, even though the repository does not yet contain a clean power-of-attorney instrument or a direct HACC note confirming the exact basis.

### III. Proof posture

18. This motion is intentionally proof-gated.
19. Movant does not ask the Court to assume that the repository already contains a direct HACC admission that `Solomon asked us to remove Benjamin from the lease.`
20. The stronger current record is instead:
   a. a granted order with in-person appearance language and operative restrictions;
   b. Solomon's later self-help noncompliance posture;
   c. the later guardianship petition's detailed housing assertions;
   d. declaration testimony that outside document flows from Benjamin's brother and stepfather affected lease handling; and
   e. written HACC notice that continued communication with restrained parties was interfering with Benjamin Barber's tenant rights and the household's housing process.
21. The exact actor-identification record inside HACC remains a live proof target and may require subpoena, records request, or other compelled production.
22. The current record also does not yet prove what, if any, authority document Solomon was invoking, whether it was a purported power of attorney, or what he may have signed in Jane's name.
23. The Court can nonetheless require Solomon to show cause now because the existing record already supports a serious question whether he continued using collateral channels to affect housing matters involving Jane Cortez after the order was granted.

### IV. Governing authority posture

24. The show-cause / contempt / corrective-relief track is grounded in:
   a. `ORS 33.055`, `33.075`, and `33.105` for remedial contempt initiation, compelled appearance, and remedial sanctions if contempt elements are later proved;
   b. `ORCP 17 C(2)-(4)` to the extent later filings are shown to have been pursued for improper purpose or without sufficient factual or legal basis;
   c. the existing protective-proceeding and probate authority set already collected in this folder.
25. Movant's present theory is not that all contempt or sanctions predicates are already fully proved.
26. Movant's present theory is that the Court should require Solomon to answer the existing housing-interference record before further collateral proceedings or practical control efforts continue.
