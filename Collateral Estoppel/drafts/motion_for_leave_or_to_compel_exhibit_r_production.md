## II. Why this production matters

The present record has moved beyond speculation on the existence of a lease change, but it has not yet reached the internal authority chain behind that change.

The current record directly supports:

1. HACC removed Benjamin Barber from the household effective January 1, 2026.
2. HACC communicated on January 12, 2026 that, following internal review and based on court documentation currently on file, the household composition had been determined to consist of Julio and Jane.
3. Benjamin and Jane later declared that Charley Skee explained in substance that Benjamin had been taken off the lease because Benjamin's brother and stepfather had been emailing documents to HACC.

That means the missing question is now a narrow one: not whether a lease change occurred, but who triggered it and on what claimed authority.

## III. The request is narrow and proportional

This motion does not seek the entire HACC case universe. It seeks only the records needed to identify the authority chain for the January 1, 2026 household-composition change.

The request is limited by:

1. subject matter: the January 1, 2026 lease-composition change;
2. household: Jane Cortez, Benjamin Barber, and Julio Cortez;
3. date range: October 1, 2025 through March 31, 2026; and
4. targeted issues: source actor, relied-upon court document, claimed authority, and internal approval path.

## IV. Why good cause exists

Good cause exists because the missing records are likely uniquely held by HACC or its custodians, and the repository review has already reached the point where further local searching is not likely to close the gap.

Without the requested records, the parties and the Court are left with:

1. direct proof that HACC made the lease change;
2. direct proof that HACC claimed internal review and court documentation supported it; but
3. no direct record identifying who supplied the decisive material or what authority HACC thought it was honoring.

That missing link matters to the housing-interference theory, the contempt / sanctions posture, and the factual integrity of any later merits presentation.
