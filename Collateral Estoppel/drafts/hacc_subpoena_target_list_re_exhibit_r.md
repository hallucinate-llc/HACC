# HACC Subpoena Target List Re Exhibit R

This draft is a working target list for subpoena or compelled production aimed at the missing HACC actor-identification and authority-chain record behind Benjamin Barber's January 2026 lease removal.

## Core purpose

The subpoena should be framed to obtain the records that answer four questions:

1. who caused or requested Benjamin's lease removal;
2. what exact court document HACC relied on;
3. what authority HACC believed the communicating person had; and
4. who inside HACC reviewed, approved, or implemented the change.

## Custodian targets

- Housing Authority of Clackamas County
- Clackamas County Housing & Community Development Division
- HACC Relocation
- HACC Public Housing / Property Management
- Any custodian for HACC case-management, CRM, Teams, email, or scanned document repositories

## Named personnel likely to have responsive records

- Ashley Ferron
- Charley Skee
- HACC Relocation staff assigned to Jane Cortez household
- HACC PM / Public Housing staff copied on the household dispute threads
- Any supervisor or county-counsel reviewer who approved or advised on the lease-composition change

## Time window

- October 1, 2025 through March 31, 2026

## Priority document categories

1. The complete lease-change, household-composition, and occupancy file for Jane Cortez, Benjamin Barber, and Julio Cortez.
2. The full approval chain for the January 1, 2026 lease amendment or household-composition change.
3. All case notes, CRM notes, desk notes, diary entries, call logs, and task comments concerning:
   - Benjamin's removal from the lease
   - Julio's inclusion or restoration
   - outside family-member communications
   - court documentation on file
4. All emails, Teams messages, chat messages, internal memos, and forwarded attachments concerning who should or should not be on the lease.
5. The exact court document or documents HACC relied on when stating on January 12, 2026 that internal review and court documentation supported a household consisting of Julio and Jane.
6. All uploaded, scanned, or attached documents in HACC systems tied to that review.
7. All records identifying the sender or source of the decisive document or communication.
8. All records reflecting any claimed authority by a non-household person to speak for Jane or to influence Jane's housing decisions.
9. All records mentioning:
   - Solomon Samuel Barber
   - Sam Barber
   - stepfather
   - brother and stepfather emailing documents
   - power of attorney
   - authorized representative
   - guardian
   - conservator
   - restraining order
   - protective order
10. All records reflecting whether HACC considered restraining-order limits when deciding whose communications to accept regarding the household.

## Metadata and system fields to request

- creation date
- modification date
- author
- last editor
- approver
- routing history
- linked attachments
- case or household identifiers
- upload timestamp
- scanned-document source notes

## Best narrowing language

The subpoena request should make clear that the key issue is not every housing dispute in the file. The key issue is the authority chain and decision path for the January 1, 2026 lease-composition change.
