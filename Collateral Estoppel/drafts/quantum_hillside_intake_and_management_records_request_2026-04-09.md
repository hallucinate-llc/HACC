# Quantum Residential Hillside Intake And Management Records Request

This draft is a targeted records request for Quantum Residential's management-role, intake-handling, and application-routing records relating to Hillside Manor, Blossom, and Parkside Heights.

It is designed for adaptation into an ORCP 55 subpoena attachment, informal preservation demand, or motion-to-compel exhibit.

## Request objective

Please produce records sufficient to identify Quantum Residential's management role and all intake, forwarding, handling, and application-processing activity involving Jane Kay Cortez / Benjamin Barber and the Hillside Manor / Blossom / Parkside Heights relocation path.

## Why this request is specific

HACC's own records and public materials already identify Quantum Residential as the property-management company for Hillside Manor and later state that a packet had been submitted to Quantum staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office but had not been provided to HACC.

The current request therefore seeks the specific records that would show:

1. whether Quantum received the packet;
2. what Quantum did with it;
3. what management or intake role Quantum had in the PBV / RAD / relocation chain; and
4. what application or leasing records Quantum maintained for this household.

## Requested records

1. Any executed management agreement, property-management services agreement, scope-of-services statement, or equivalent document identifying Quantum Residential's role at Hillside Manor.
2. Any policy, procedure, training material, workflow document, or instruction governing:
   - leasing-office intake;
   - application routing;
   - PBV / RAD project intake;
   - relocation-household handling;
   - preservation of application materials; and
   - forwarding of applications or relocation packets to HACC.
3. All records sufficient to identify whether Quantum Residential, Quantum staff, or Hillside Manor leasing-office staff received any packet, application, or supporting materials from or for Jane Kay Cortez / Benjamin Barber.
4. All intake logs, receipt logs, visitor logs, routing logs, scanning logs, shared-drive entries, or internal notes reflecting receipt, review, handling, forwarding, non-forwarding, or loss of any such packet or application.
5. All emails, texts, internal messages, notes, or CRM records concerning Jane Kay Cortez / Benjamin Barber and:
   - Hillside Manor;
   - Blossom / Community Apartments;
   - Parkside Heights;
   - relocation status;
   - service-animal issues;
   - accommodation issues; and
   - any claimed application deficiency.
6. All records sufficient to identify whether Quantum staff communicated with HACC, Kati Tilton, Ashley Ferron, HACC Relocation, Blossom staff, or Parkside Heights staff about those applications or packets.
7. All records sufficient to identify whether Quantum staff determined that any application or packet for Jane Kay Cortez / Benjamin Barber was incomplete, stale, deficient, missing documents, or otherwise not processable, including the date and reason for that determination.
8. All application, screening, leasing, waitlist, or pre-leasing records for Jane Kay Cortez / Benjamin Barber concerning:
   - Blossom / Community Apartments;
   - Hillside Manor office intake;
   - Parkside Heights; and
   - any overlapping Hillside relocation implementation path.
9. All records sufficient to identify whether Quantum staff handled or reviewed service-animal, disability-accommodation, or bedroom-size issues concerning this household.
10. All records sufficient to identify Quantum's role, if any, in preserving, transmitting, or tracking relocation-household paperwork for Hillside relocation households generally.

1. Produce electronically stored information in native format where reasonably available, including metadata showing creation date, modification date, author, recipients, attachments, and routing history.

## Best narrowing sentence

This request does not seek every Quantum property-management file. It seeks the specific management-role, intake, routing, application, and communication records needed to determine what Quantum received, what it did with those materials, and whether it lost, failed to forward, or otherwise mishandled the household's relocation-related paperwork.
