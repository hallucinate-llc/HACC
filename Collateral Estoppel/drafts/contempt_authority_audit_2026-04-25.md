# Contempt Authority Audit

Working date: 2026-04-25

This audit checks the authorities used in the three `25PO11530` show-cause drafts.

## Confirmed statutes

### ORS 33.015

Status: solid.

Use: contempt includes willful disobedience of, resistance to, or obstruction of court authority, process, orders, or judgments. It also separately includes refusal to produce a record, document, or other object contrary to a court order.

Source: https://oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_33.015

### ORS 33.055

Status: solid.

Use: remedial contempt may be initiated by an aggrieved party. The initiating party must file supporting documentation or affidavits giving notice of the specific acts alleged. The contempt defendant must receive a hearing. Inability to comply is an affirmative defense. Proof for remedial sanctions other than confinement is clear and convincing evidence.

Source: https://oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_33.055

### ORS 33.105

Status: solid.

Use: remedial sanctions can include compensatory money, confinement while contempt continues, daily coercive fines within statutory limits, compliance orders, attorney fees caused by contempt, and another effective remedy.

Source: https://oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_33.105

### ORS 124.012

Status: solid.

Use: contempt proceedings for violation of an EPPDAPA restraining order must be conducted by the issuing court or by the circuit court for a county where the violation occurs. This supports filing the contempt motions in `25PO11530`.

Source: https://oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_124.012

### ORS 124.030

Status: solid, but use narrowly.

Use: if an EPPDAPA order recites that the respondent appeared in person before the court, necessity for service and proof of service is waived. A restraining order remains in effect until it expires or is terminated by court order. Pending a contempt hearing for an alleged EPPDAPA violation, release provisions apply.

Source: https://oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_124.030

### ORS 40.135 / OEC 311

Status: solid for evidence-suppression relief.

Use: evidence willfully suppressed is presumed adverse to the suppressing party. This supports an adverse presumption if Solomon cannot account for records he was ordered to produce.

Source: https://oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_40.135

### ORS 40.120 / OEC 308

Status: solid for civil-presumption burden effect.

Use: in civil proceedings, a presumption imposes on the opposing party the burden to prove that nonexistence of the presumed fact is more probable than its existence.

Source: https://oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_40.120

## Confirmed cases

### State v. Arnold, 301 Or App 642 (2020)

Status: solid for EPPDAPA contempt and collateral-attack framing.

Use: an EPPDAPA restraining-order violation can support contempt. The case states that, where a defendant had notice that an order applied, the defendant could not violate it and then collaterally attack the order in the contempt proceeding. It also restates that contempt requires a valid order, knowledge, and willful noncompliance.

Source: https://law.justia.com/cases/oregon/court-of-appeals/2020/a168230.html

### St. Sauver and St. Sauver, 196 Or App 175 (2004)

Status: solid for basic contempt elements.

Use: states the essential elements for contempt as a valid court order, knowledge of the order by the contemnor, and voluntary noncompliance. Also supports a theory that misleading conduct can constitute resistance to or obstruction of a judgment when it defeats the judgment's purpose.

Source: https://law.justia.com/cases/oregon/court-of-appeals/2004/a117317.html

### Cowles and Flormoe-Cowles, 322 Or App 741 (2022)

Status: solid for willfulness and findings.

Use: remedial contempt under ORS 33.015 requires willfulness. Willfulness means intentional action with knowledge that the act or omission was forbidden. The judgment should include a specific finding of willfulness.

Source: https://law.justia.com/cases/oregon/court-of-appeals/2022/a175100.html

## Corrected / limited authority

### ORCP 17

Status: real rule, but not the lead vehicle in `25PO11530`.

Use limit: ORCP 17 is a good vehicle for sanctions based on a false or unsupported pleading in the case where that pleading was filed. Because Solomon's guardianship petition was filed in `26PR00641`, a standalone ORCP 17 sanctions motion belongs there, not as the lead request in `25PO11530`.

Use in `25PO11530`: mention only to reserve separate probate sanctions. In `25PO11530`, frame the probate petition as evidence of collateral evasion, resistance, or obstruction of the restraining order.

Source: https://oregon.public.law/rules-of-civil-procedure/orcp-17-signing-of-pleadings-motions-and-other-papers-sanctions/

## Weak spots to preserve honestly

1. HACC actor identification: the current record supports an information-and-belief allegation that Solomon influenced HACC, but the clean source record directly naming Solomon as the person who caused Benjamin's lease removal remains a proof target.
2. Probate-petition contempt: filing a probate petition is not automatically contempt. The `25PO11530` theory must connect the petition to willful resistance, obstruction, or evasion of the restraining order.
3. ORCP 17 sanctions: reserve those for `26PR00641`; do not ask the `25PO11530` court to impose ORCP 17 sanctions for the probate pleading.
4. Punitive contempt: the drafts should seek remedial contempt only. Punitive contempt has different procedure and is not the clean private-party path.
