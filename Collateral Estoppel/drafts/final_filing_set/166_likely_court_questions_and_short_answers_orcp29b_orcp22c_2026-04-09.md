LIKELY COURT QUESTIONS AND SHORT ANSWERS
Threshold Scope Hearing (26PR00641)

1. "Why is this a probate issue at all if housing facts exist?"
Answer:
   a. Probate remains proper for guardianship/protective determinations.
   b. The request is to prevent collateral housing-merits relitigation here, not to avoid probate adjudication.

2. "Why not just join everyone and decide everything here?"
Answer:
   a. If joinder is not feasible, ORCP 29B directs prejudice/adequacy analysis and shaped relief.
   b. Shaped relief is to keep probate narrow and reserve housing merits to the proper forums.

3. "What prejudice exists if I decide housing issues now?"
Answer:
   a. Absent-party prejudice and inconsistent obligations across probate, eviction, and federal tracks.
   b. Risk of fragmented merits findings without all primary housing actors properly aligned.

4. "What is your ORCP 22C position?"
Answer:
   a. ORCP 22C is stricter and requires derivative liability-over.
   b. On this record, HACC/Quantum are framed as primary actors in separate housing disputes, not pleaded liability-over parties.

5. "Can I still grant meaningful relief without housing merits?"
Answer:
   a. Yes. Guardianship/protective findings can be fully adjudicated without deciding full Section 18/eviction/federal liability merits.

6. "What exact order language do you want?"
Answer:
   a. Exclude collateral Section 18/eviction/federal housing merits from probate.
   b. If joinder is not feasible, enter ORCP 29B findings and proceed with narrowed relief.
   c. Decline ORCP 22C expansion absent pleaded derivative liability-over.

7. "If facts overlap, how should I handle them?"
Answer:
   a. Decide only the minimum fact issues necessary for guardianship/protective rulings.
   b. Reserve broader housing merits to separate forums.
