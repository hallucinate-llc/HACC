MEMORANDUM TRANSLATING RULE-ID REASONING TRACE
INTO PLAIN LEGAL LANGUAGE

I. PURPOSE AND READING METHOD

1. This memorandum translates the Rule-ID shorthand used across the motion set into plain legal language for court readability while preserving the same logic structure.
2. Each Rule-ID is expressed as a five-part reasoning chain:
   a. legal duty or legal limitation;
   b. trigger fact;
   c. required proof gate before final relief;
   d. current evidentiary posture;
   e. requested court action.
3. This memorandum is an organizational crosswalk and does not substitute for admissible exhibits, certified records, or sworn testimony.

II. DEONTIC TERMS IN PLAIN LEGAL LANGUAGE

4. "Obligation" means a party must do the identified act once the trigger conditions are met.
5. "Prohibition" means a party must not do the identified act once the trigger conditions are met.
6. "Permission" means the court may grant identified relief if factual and legal predicates are satisfied.
7. "Proof-gated" means the branch cannot support final dispositive relief until the identified certified or otherwise admissible records are filed.
8. "Admissible-now" means the branch is currently supported enough for threshold management, hearing sequencing, or non-dispositive handling.
9. "Context-only" means the point may orient scope and chronology but is not offered as a completed merits finding.

III. RULE-ID TO PLAIN-LANGUAGE LEGAL TRACE

A. R-SC-01 (In-Effect Order Compliance Branch)

10. Legal duty: once an entered protective/restraining order is in effect, the restrained party must comply with operative terms and must not disregard those terms based on unilateral conditions.
11. Trigger: order entry and continuing effect period, including order text indicating effect after appearance.
12. Proof gate: clause-specific violation mapping, admissible notice pathway, and hearing-level opportunity to respond before any contempt finding.
13. Current posture: order text is in record; alleged noncompliance statements are supported by authenticated communications; violation merits remain for hearing proof.
14. Requested action: order to appear and show cause first; remedial sanctions only if clause-level violation is proved.

B. R-SC-02 (No-Further-Service / Appearance Anchor)

15. Legal duty: once appearance and effect predicates are established, a respondent may not nullify the order's practical effect by asserting a new private service condition that conflicts with the entered order framework.
16. Trigger: prior appearance and order language bearing on ongoing enforceability.
17. Proof gate: if contested, resolve through accepted or certified appearance and service records.
18. Current posture: anchor exists in current record; scope of later contradictory statements remains a contested hearing fact.
19. Requested action: threshold finding on notice and effect posture for case sequencing.

C. R-PI-01 (Collateral Estoppel Branch)

20. Legal limitation: relitigation of an already decided issue is barred only if Oregon issue-preclusion elements are each satisfied on admissible proof.
21. Trigger: overlap between present claim issue and a prior proceeding issue.
22. Proof gate: certified, row-by-row element mapping for identity of issue, finality, party/privity, full and fair opportunity, and fairness of preclusion application.
23. Current posture: branch remains proof-gated pending certified element-complete records.
24. Requested action: threshold hearing and limitation or dismissal only to the extent specific element rows are proved.

D. R-JN-01 (ORCP 29B Fallback Branch)

25. Legal duty of analysis: if joinder is not feasible, the court must consider prejudice, adequacy of judgment, and shaping of relief under ORCP 29B.
26. Trigger: nonfeasible joinder posture in a probate case where collateral merits overlap exists.
27. Proof gate: service-attempt and feasibility facts sufficiently developed for factor analysis.
28. Current posture: legal branch is available now for threshold scope management; factual overlap record is mixed and partially provisional.
29. Requested action: shape relief to keep probate focused on guardianship and reserve collateral housing merits for proper forums.
29A. Supplemental status records, including Exhibit PS-1 (Parkside screening approval), reinforce that the housing-status narrative is actively contested and evolving, which supports threshold scope control rather than immediate coercive merits assumptions in probate.

E. R-JN-02 (ORCP 22C Derivative-Liability Limit)

30. Legal pleading limit: third-party practice under ORCP 22C requires a derivative liability-over theory, not merely factual overlap.
31. Trigger: attempt to implead third parties in this posture.
32. Proof gate: pleading facts showing that third-party defendant is liable for all or part of the claim against the defending party.
33. Current posture: no clean derivative liability-over theory currently pleaded in the probate posture.
34. Requested action: decline ORCP 22C expansion absent derivative pleading; proceed under narrower scope-control alternatives.

F. R-GAL-01 (Prior GAL Continuity Branch)

35. Legal permission/continuity principle: if certified prior records show prior GAL status involving the same protected person, the court may align present representation posture accordingly.
36. Trigger: certified prior order or minute record confirming prior GAL continuity facts.
37. Proof gate: certified prior case records lodged in this file.
38. Current posture: continuity branch remains proof-gated pending certified prior order/minute support.
39. Requested action: if continuity is confirmed, align relief accordingly; if not yet confirmed, decide present GAL request on current threshold need.

G. R-SVC-01 (Sheriff Service Branch, April 9, 2026 Event)

40. Legal sequencing duty: service-dependent coercive relief should rest on official return/proof-of-service records, not photo-only support.
41. Trigger: reported and observed in-person sheriff service event dated April 9, 2026.
42. Proof gate: filing of official sheriff return or equivalent formal proof accepted by the court.
43. Current posture: occurrence evidence is supported by personal observation and photo record; final legal service-sufficiency finding remains proof-gated.
44. Requested action: allow timeline and notice context use now; defer final service-sufficiency enforcement rulings until formal return is lodged.

IV. STRUCTURED INFERENCE FORMAT FOR BRIEFING AND HEARING

45. The Rule-ID shorthand can be translated in argument as:
46. "If trigger facts are proved and proof gates are satisfied, then the legal duty or bar applies; if not, relief remains limited to threshold sequencing."
47. For hearing clarity, each requested ruling should identify:
   a. the Rule-ID branch;
   b. the exact evidence row supporting each predicate;
   c. whether the requested ruling is threshold-only or dispositive.
48. This preserves explainability, limits overreach, and avoids conflating context evidence with merits-complete proof.
