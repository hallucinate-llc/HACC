# Oregon Authority Table

This table is a working authority register for the current packet. It is grounded to official Oregon Legislature rule and statute pages and should still receive final cite-format cleanup before filing.

## 1) Motion for GAL Appointment

1. Protective-proceeding use of civil procedure and evidence rules:
   `ORS 125.050`
   Working use: unless chapter 125 provides otherwise, the Oregon Rules of Civil Procedure and the Oregon Evidence Code apply in protective proceedings.
2. Notice to interested persons in protective proceedings:
   `ORS 125.060`
   Working use: identifies who must receive notice of a petition for appointment of a fiduciary or other protective order, including the respondent and specified relatives or other interested persons.
3. Manner and timing of notice:
   `ORS 125.065`
   Working use: requires personal service on a respondent who has attained 14 years of age, permits mailing for other notice recipients, and generally requires service or mailing at least 15 days before the final date for filing objections.
4. Objections and hearing path:
   `ORS 125.075` and `ORS 125.080`
   Working use: interested persons may present objections; objections to a petition may be written or oral; if objections are presented, the court shall schedule a hearing; and the court may require a hearing and must hold one if the respondent or protected person objects and does not withdraw the objection.
5. Counsel / participation at hearing:
   `ORS 125.080`
   Working use: the respondent or protected person may appear at a hearing in person or by counsel.
6. Protective-person special advocate:
   `ORS 125.120`
   Working use: after appointment of a fiduciary and under the conditions stated in the statute, the court may appoint a protected person special advocate; this is not the same thing as a general-purpose GAL authority and should not be conflated with it.

## 2) Motion to Dismiss (Collateral Estoppel)

1. Oregon issue-preclusion elements:
   `Rawls v. Evans, 234 Or App 316 (2010)` quoting `Nelson v. Emerald People's Utility Dist., 318 Or 99, 104, 862 P2d 1293 (1993)`
   Working use: the five issue-preclusion requirements are identity of issue, actual litigation and essentiality, full and fair opportunity to be heard, party or privity, and a prior proceeding of the type given preclusive effect.
2. Identical-issue requirement:
   `Nelson v. Emerald People's Utility Dist., 318 Or 99, 105-06, 862 P2d 1293 (1993)`
   Working use: issue preclusion does not apply when the issues are merely similar but not identical.
3. Prior separate proceeding requirement / same-case limitation:
   `Hayes Oyster Co. v. Dulcich, 199 Or App 43, 50, 54, 110 P3d 615, rev den, 339 Or 544 (2005)`
   `Westwood Construction Co. v. Hallmark Inns, 182 Or App 624, 631-32, 637 n 10, 50 P3d 238, rev den, 335 Or 42 (2002)`
   Working use: issue preclusion applies to a prior separate proceeding, not merely to different claims or phases within the same lawsuit.
4. Current caution:
   Even though the doctrine is now authority-grounded, do not present the collateral-estoppel branch as established in this matter until the actual prior order / judgment / proceeding record is obtained and mapped to the elements above.

## 3) Motion for Order to Show Cause / Sanctions Track

1. Remedial contempt procedure and aggrieved-party initiation:
   `ORS 33.055`
   Working use: an aggrieved party may initiate a remedial-contempt proceeding, request that the contempt defendant be ordered to appear, and must file supporting documentation sufficient to give notice of the specific acts alleged to constitute contempt.
2. Compelling attendance after failure to appear on an order to appear:
   `ORS 33.075`
   Working use: if a person served with an order to appear under `ORS 33.055` fails to appear, the court may issue any order or warrant necessary to compel appearance.
3. Remedial sanctions available in contempt:
   `ORS 33.105(1)`
   Working use: the court may impose one or more remedial sanctions, including compensation for loss, injury, or costs, orders designed to ensure compliance with a prior court order, attorney fees, and other effective remedial sanctions unless otherwise provided by statute.
4. Improper-purpose / unsupported-filing sanctions:
   `ORCP 17 C(2)-(4)`
   Working use: a signer certifies that a filing is not presented for an improper purpose, that legal positions are warranted by existing law or a nonfrivolous argument, and that factual assertions are supported by evidence or specifically identified as expected to be supported after investigation or discovery.
5. Discovery / production sanction support if subpoena-related production becomes litigated through motion practice:
   `ORCP 46 A(4)` and `ORCP 46 B(2)`
   Working use: the court may award reasonable expenses on a motion to compel and may enter just orders when a party fails to obey an order to provide or permit discovery.

## 4) Service

1. Service of orders, motions, notices, and other later-filed papers:
   `ORCP 9 A-B`
   Working use: subsequent pleadings, orders, motions, notices, and similar papers generally must be served on each party, and service on represented parties is generally made on counsel by delivery, mail, e-mail, electronic service, or other authorized means.
2. Time-computation rule:
   `ORCP 10 A`
   Working use: excludes the triggering day, includes the last day unless it falls on a weekend or legal holiday, and extends the deadline to the next business day when appropriate.
3. Additional time after certain service methods:
   `ORCP 10 B`
   Working use: except for summons, three days are added when a party must act within a prescribed period after service by mail, e-mail, facsimile, or electronic service.
3. Subpoena obedience / warning language:
   `ORCP 55 A(1)(a)(vi)(A)-(B)`
   Working use: subpoenas must state that they must be obeyed unless a judge orders otherwise and that disobedience is punishable by a fine or jail time.
