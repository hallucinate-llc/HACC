FALLBACK MOTION TO EXCLUDE OR STRIKE COLLATERAL
SECTION 18 / EVICTION / FEDERAL HOUSING MERITS FROM PROBATE

Movant Benjamin Barber moves for an order excluding, striking, or deferring collateral housing-merits litigation from this probate proceeding where those issues are being litigated or pursued in separate eviction and federal tracks and involve nonparty entities.

I. RELIEF REQUESTED

1. Exclude from this probate case any merits adjudication of Section 18 relocation-duty violations, eviction liability, and federal housing civil-rights claims against HACC or Quantum.
2. Strike or disregard pleadings/arguments to the extent they seek to obtain merits determinations on those collateral housing issues in this protective proceeding.
3. Limit this matter to guardianship-specific issues, welfare findings, and fiduciary-related determinations under ORS chapter 125.
4. Reserve all housing-merits adjudication to the properly postured eviction and federal forums.

II. AUTHORITY

5. ORS 125.025 authorizes the Court to control protective-proceeding scope to protect welfare and proper case management.
6. ORS 125.050 applies ORCP in protective proceedings; the Court may manage joinder/scope to avoid prejudice and inefficiency.
7. ORCP 29B supplies the nonfeasibility fallback factors (prejudice, shaping relief, adequacy of judgment, and adequacy of alternate remedy), which support a narrowed probate path when collateral merits belong elsewhere.
8. ORCP 22C third-party practice is derivative-liability specific and does not fit absent entities alleged as primary actors unless a clean liability-over theory is pleaded.
9. Oregon issue-preclusion law supports threshold management where overlapping issues are pending in separate forums and finality/identity records remain incomplete.

IV. ARGUMENT

14. Allowing collateral housing-merits litigation here would risk inconsistent rulings, expanded scope, and prejudice to orderly guardianship adjudication.
15. Under ORCP 29B's fallback analysis, prejudice is minimized by shaping relief to exclude collateral housing merits and reserving them for the eviction/federal tracks where the primary housing actors are already positioned.
16. A probate judgment limited to guardianship questions remains adequate and administrable, while housing-merits adequacy is preserved in those separate forums.
17. The narrower and safer remedy is to confine this case to guardianship issues while preserving parties' ability to pursue housing merits in the proper actions.
18. If any overlap issue is necessary for a discrete probate determination, the Court should decide only the minimum required question and reserve broader merits for the separate tracks.
18A. This fallback motion does not request any dispositive issue-preclusion dismissal ruling; any dispositive preclusion request remains in the separate threshold preclusion motion and only to the extent supported by admissible certified records.
18B. Relief requested in this motion is limited to Rule ID `R-JN-01` shaped-relief handling and does not request completion of Rule ID `R-PI-01`.
