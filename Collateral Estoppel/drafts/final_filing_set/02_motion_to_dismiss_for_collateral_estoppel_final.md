II. GOVERNING LAW

5. Oregon issue-preclusion law bars relitigation when the identical issue was actually litigated and essential to a final decision, and preclusion is asserted against a party who had a full and fair opportunity to be heard. See Nelson v. Emerald People's Utility Dist., 318 Or 99, 104-06, 862 P2d 1293 (1993); Rawls v. Evans, 234 Or App 316, 321, 227 P3d 1200 (2010).
6. The issues must be identical, and the doctrine requires a prior separate proceeding rather than merely another phase of the same case. See Hayes Oyster Co. v. Dulcich, 199 Or App 43, 50, 54, 110 P3d 615, rev den, 339 Or 544 (2005); Westwood Construction Co. v. Hallmark Inns, 182 Or App 624, 631-32, 637 n 10, 50 P3d 238, rev den, 335 Or 42 (2002).
