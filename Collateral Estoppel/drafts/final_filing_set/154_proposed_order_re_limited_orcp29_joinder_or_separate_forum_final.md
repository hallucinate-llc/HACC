[PROPOSED] ORDER RE LIMITED ORCP 29 JOINDER
OR SEPARATE-FORUM LIMITATION

This matter came before the Court on Movant's Motion for Limited ORCP 29 Joinder, or in the Alternative, for Order Requiring Section 18 / Eviction Merits to Be Litigated in the Proper Forum.

THE COURT FINDS AND ORDERS:

1. The motion is GRANTED in part as follows:
2. Under ORCP 29A, and only after service and an opportunity to be heard, Housing Authority of Clackamas County and Quantum Residential Property Management, L.P. are joined on a limited basis for threshold party-structure and overlap-control determinations only.
3. Any merits adjudication beyond that limited purpose is reserved to the proper forum.
4. If joinder is not feasible, the Court has considered ORCP 29B factors and finds that prejudice is reduced by shaping relief to exclude collateral Section 18/eviction/federal housing merits from this probate case.
5. The Court further finds that a probate judgment limited to guardianship/protective-proceeding issues remains adequate without collateral housing-merits adjudication, and that adequate remedies for those merits exist in separate eviction/federal forums.
6. The Court finds ORCP 22C third-party practice is not presently satisfied because no pleaded derivative liability-over theory has been established that a nonparty is liable for all or part of the claim against the defending party.
7. Therefore, if limited joinder is denied/deferred, collateral Section 18, eviction-liability, and federal housing-merits issues shall not be adjudicated as probate merits in this proceeding and remain for determination, if at all, in properly postured eviction/federal matters.
8. If limited joinder is ordered, Movant must serve this order and the underlying motion packet on any proposed joined entity within: ______________________ days after entry of this order.
9. Any proposed joined entity may file a response limited to ORCP 29A/29B feasibility and scope within: ______________________ days after service.
10. The parties may file any briefing limited to scope/party-structure issues within: ______________________ days after the response deadline in paragraph 9.
11. The Court reserves all merits determinations not expressly ordered above.
12. Any preclusion or finality determination remains limited to the extent supported by admissible certified records and, where applicable, to specifically identified fully-supported `188` row(s).
13. Unless a derivative liability-over theory is specifically pleaded and supported, ORCP 22C third-party practice remains unavailable in this probate posture.
14. Address references or service-channel references, standing alone, are not treated as conclusive proof of completed ORCP service; service findings will be made on filed proof records.
15. If limited joinder is denied or deferred, this order does not adjudicate collateral housing-merits liability and is entered without prejudice to properly postured claims in separate forums.

IT IS SO ORDERED.
