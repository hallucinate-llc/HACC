Objector Benjamin Barber objects to the First Amended Petition for Appointment of Guardian to the extent it seeks emergency or plenary relief on a one-sided and materially disputed record, including waiver-of-notice relief, writ-based forced transport or removal relief, payment of adverse attorney fees from Jane Kay Cortez's funds, and broad authority over Jane Kay Cortez's housing, residence, placement, or possible estate matters without a clean underlying record.

This objection is not a concession that every disputed merits issue must be adjudicated now. To the contrary, Objector requests that the Court separate true guardianship issues from disputed collateral housing, relocation, trust, and cross-forum matters, require a clean predicate record for any coercive relief, and deny any overbroad request for plenary authority on the present showing.

I. RELIEF REQUESTED

1. Deny emergency or waiver-of-notice relief to the extent it rests on a one-sided or materially incomplete factual record.
2. Deny any writ-based, law-enforcement-assisted, forced-entry, forced-transport, or comparable coercive removal relief unless and until a clean predicate order and notice basis are established by admissible records.
3. Deny any request for plenary authority, facility-placement authority, or similarly broad control over Jane Kay Cortez on the present disputed record.
4. Deny any request to charge Jane Kay Cortez's funds with adverse attorney fees or costs under `ORS 125.095` on the present showing.
5. Require that disputed Section 18 relocation-duty, eviction-merits, HACC/Quantum processing, and related housing-liability issues remain limited to the proper eviction or other appropriate forum, except to the extent strictly necessary to understand guardianship-specific facts.
6. Set any threshold hearing, if one is held, on narrowed issues of emergency basis, notice, scope of requested authority, and the sufficiency of the predicate record.

II. FACTUAL RESPONSE POSTURE

7. Solomon Samuel Barber's petition presents an emergency narrative that attributes Jane Kay Cortez's circumstances primarily to Benjamin Barber while materially understating the roles of Housing Authority of Clackamas County, Quantum Residential, disputed outside document flows, and already-existing protective-order conflict.
8. The petition also seeks extremely broad control, including plenary authority, possible institutional or residential placement, and attorney fees from Jane Kay Cortez's funds.
9. On the present repository-backed record, those requests should not be granted without close scrutiny because multiple material allegations are disputed, overstated, incomplete, or depend on records not yet cleanly produced.

III. SPECIFIC OBJECTIONS

A. EMERGENCY / NOTICE / WRIT ISSUES

10. The petition's incapacity and immediate-danger narrative should not be accepted as a one-sided basis for emergency or plenary relief merely because Jane Kay Cortez may need assistance in some areas.
11. The present record includes contrary and qualifying evidence, including Jane Kay Cortez's own declarations, Benjamin Barber's declaration, and other materials showing disputed housing, protective-order, and family-control context.
12. The petition's APS / police interference allegations against Benjamin Barber are materially incomplete because, on the very day Solomon Samuel Barber was planning to have APS come to the house, Jane Kay Cortez was actually at the Clackamas County courthouse seeking a restraining order against Solomon Samuel Barber.
13. That same-day protective-order context matters. It means the alleged APS / police event should not be treated as a neutral welfare narrative divorced from the ongoing protective-order conflict.
14. The petition's waiver-of-notice / emergency-appointment theory is also overstated because the housing emergency described there was not simply caused by Benjamin Barber. The current record reflects substantial HACC / Quantum delay, Section 18 relocation-duty issues, and disputed housing-authority conduct affecting the household's ability to relocate.
15. The writ-of-assistance / forced-transport component is especially troubling. The current staged record reflects a probate-side writ directing law enforcement to accompany Solomon Barber, enter Jane Kay Cortez's residence forcibly if necessary, locate her, and transport her to Providence Milwaukie or another nearby hospital.
16. Jane Kay Cortez's April 8, 2026 declaration is consistent with the understanding that police came to pick her up, that she was not allowed to take her purse and identification, and that Solomon was trying to take her away without her approval.
17. On this record, the Court should require a clean predicate order and notice basis before any comparable coercive removal or transport relief is considered further.

B. CONTROL / SCOPE ISSUES

18. The petition's assertion that no less restrictive alternatives existed is overstated on the present record.
19. Even where proof remains incomplete, the current record is sufficient to show that narrower protective boundaries, existing court-order history, and less sweeping alternatives were not fairly accounted for before Solomon requested plenary control. At minimum, that unresolved alternatives question weighs against immediate plenary relief because Chapter 125 protective relief should be tailored to what is actually shown on the present record.
20. The petition's financial-control / trust / inheritance theory is internally unstable. It alleges a substantial inheritance / Special Needs Trust issue while also describing Jane Kay Cortez's estate as unknown but not believed to exceed `$10,000`.
21. The current record now includes the Gerald Miller probate packet itself, which confirms a Gerald Miller estate, last will, and revocable-trust structure involving Jane Kay Cortez as an heir or devisee. That makes it even more important that any actual trust papers, estate distribution papers, or authority-linked documents be produced rather than vaguely referenced.
22. At the same time, the petition seeks broad authority over Jane Kay Cortez's affairs without producing the trust papers, estate documents, accounting, corpus information, or any complete authority chain that would let the Court evaluate what financial control is actually being requested.
23. Benjamin Barber's position has been that he should have been allowed to review any papers Solomon Samuel Barber caused Jane Kay Cortez to sign, or purported to sign on her behalf, concerning the estate of Gerald Miller, any proposed trust, or any claimed authority over her affairs.
24. Solomon Samuel Barber's own preserved November 17, 2025 text supports that he was already asserting a trust-related role or channel. In that text, he stated in substance that Jane's disability trust was `between her and the estate,` that Benjamin had `no legal claim` to details, and that Solomon was trying to get `her rep payee and the trust in contact.`
25. The earlier restraining-order exhibit sequence adds to that concern because it appears to show Jane Kay Cortez stating that she signed papers with Sam / Solomon that she did not understand, including a paper she thought had something to do with her dad's will and on which she said she had put her social security number.
26. The petition's proposed placement language is also overbroad. It contemplates possible placement in a mental health treatment facility, nursing home, or other residential facility, which goes far beyond a modest request for help with housing or appointments and should not be granted absent a cleaner record showing that such scope is necessary.
27. The petition expressly seeks plenary authority. On a record this disputed, and given the court's obligation to tailor protective relief to what is actually shown, that request should be denied or at minimum sharply narrowed to any genuinely established need.
28. The request to have Solomon's attorney fees and costs paid from Jane Kay Cortez's funds under `ORS 125.095` should likewise be denied. Adverse coercive proceedings should not be financed from Jane Kay Cortez's funds on the basis of a materially disputed emergency narrative.
29. Under `ORS 125.098`, the Court's greatest-weight factor in deciding attorney fees is the benefit to the person subject to the protective proceeding. On the present record, that factor does not support shifting Solomon Barber's fees to Jane Kay Cortez because the petition seeks disputed coercive control, writ-based transport, overbroad placement and plenary relief, and other contested measures whose benefit to Jane Kay Cortez is not presently established.
30. To the extent any separate fee or cost exposure is argued in connection with HACC / Quantum housing-rights disputes, that issue belongs, if anywhere, in the separate properly framed housing or civil-rights track rather than as a probate-side charge against Jane Kay Cortez's funds in this case.

Solomon Samuel Barber's asserted suitability to serve should also be evaluated in light of the restraining-order record, the preserved March 10, 2026 communications regarding compliance posture, the November 17, 2025 trust text posture, the earlier exhibit sequence in which Jane appears to describe signing papers she did not understand, and the writ-based forced-transport sequence.
