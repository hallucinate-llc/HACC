DEFENDANTS' FIRST TARGETED REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION, FIRST TARGETED INTERROGATORIES, AND FIRST TARGETED REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION TO PLAINTIFF RE EXHIBIT R AUTHORITY CHAIN

TO: Plaintiff Housing Authority of Clackamas County, and its attorneys of record, if any:

Pursuant to the Oregon Rules of Civil Procedure and any applicable local rules or court orders, Defendants Benjamin Jay Barber and Jane Kay Cortez request responses to the following targeted discovery concerning the authority chain behind Benjamin Jay Barber's removal from the household effective January 1, 2026.

These requests are intentionally narrow. They seek the records and information needed to identify:

1. who caused or requested the January 1, 2026 lease-composition change;
2. what court document or documents Plaintiff relied on;
3. what authority Plaintiff believed the communicating person had; and
4. who inside Plaintiff's organization reviewed, approved, or implemented that change.

## Requests for Production

1. Produce records sufficient to show the decision path for removing Benjamin Jay Barber from the household effective January 1, 2026.
2. Produce the January 1, 2026 lease amendment, lease rider, household-composition record, and any version history, routing history, review history, or approval metadata for that change.
3. Produce all records identifying every person who requested, initiated, authorized, approved, entered, processed, reviewed, or discussed Benjamin Jay Barber's January 2026 removal from the household or lease.
4. Produce all case notes, CRM notes, task logs, desk notes, diary notes, timeline entries, and internal comments concerning the January 1, 2026 household-composition change.
5. Produce all emails, Teams messages, chat messages, text messages, memoranda, and forwarded attachments concerning whether Benjamin Jay Barber, Julio Cortez, or any other person should remain on, be removed from, or be restored to the household lease during the period October 1, 2025 through March 31, 2026.
6. Produce the court documentation Plaintiff referred to in its January 12, 2026 communication stating that, following internal review and based on court documentation currently on file, the household composition had been determined to consist of Julio and Jane.
7. Produce all records sufficient to identify who submitted, transmitted, uploaded, forwarded, or otherwise caused Plaintiff to receive that court documentation.
8. Produce all records reflecting any claimed authority by a non-household person to communicate with Plaintiff about Jane Kay Cortez's lease composition, household status, or housing decisions.
9. Produce all records reflecting whether Plaintiff received or relied upon any power of attorney, representative authorization, guardian/conservator authority, or similar claimed authority instrument concerning Jane Kay Cortez.
10. Produce all records mentioning Solomon Samuel Barber, Sam Barber, stepfather, brother and stepfather emailing documents, authorized representative, power of attorney, guardian, conservator, restraining order, protective order, or court documentation on file in connection with this household.
11. Produce all records reflecting whether Plaintiff considered restraining-order or protective-order limits when deciding whose communications to accept about the household's lease composition.
12. Produce all records showing who approved or implemented any later restoration of Benjamin Jay Barber to the household or lease, to the extent such records illuminate the basis for the earlier removal.

## Interrogatories

1. Identify every person who requested, initiated, authorized, approved, entered, processed, reviewed, or discussed Benjamin Jay Barber's removal from the household effective January 1, 2026.
2. Identify every document on which Plaintiff relied in making that change.
3. State every fact on which Plaintiff relied in determining that the household should be treated as consisting of Julio and Jane as of January 1, 2026.
4. Identify every person from whom Plaintiff received information, instructions, requests, pressure, or supporting documentation concerning Benjamin Jay Barber's January 2026 removal.
5. State whether Plaintiff contends Benjamin Jay Barber requested his own removal from the household or lease; if so, state all supporting facts and identify all supporting records.
6. State whether Plaintiff contends Jane Kay Cortez requested Benjamin Jay Barber's removal from the household or lease; if so, state all supporting facts and identify all supporting records.
7. State whether Plaintiff contends Julio Cortez requested Benjamin Jay Barber's removal from the household or lease; if so, state all supporting facts and identify all supporting records.
8. State whether Plaintiff contends any non-household person requested or influenced Benjamin Jay Barber's January 2026 removal; if so, identify that person and state all supporting facts.
9. State what authority, if any, Plaintiff believed any non-household person had to communicate with Plaintiff about Jane Kay Cortez's lease composition or housing decisions.
10. State whether Plaintiff received any direct or indirect communication from Solomon Samuel Barber concerning the household, lease composition, court documentation, restraining orders, continued occupancy, or any lease change during the period October 1, 2025 through March 31, 2026; if so, identify each such communication by date, method, participants, and substance.
11. State what Plaintiff meant in its January 12, 2026 communication by `internal review` and `court documentation currently on file`.
12. Identify every Plaintiff employee who had authority to approve or implement the January 1, 2026 household-composition change.

## Requests for Admission

1. Admit that Plaintiff removed Benjamin Jay Barber from the household effective January 1, 2026.
2. Admit that Plaintiff communicated on January 12, 2026 that the change followed internal review and court documentation currently on file.
3. Admit that Plaintiff possesses no written request from Benjamin Jay Barber asking to be removed from the household or lease effective January 1, 2026.
4. Admit that Plaintiff possesses no written request from Jane Kay Cortez asking that Benjamin Jay Barber be removed from the household or lease effective January 1, 2026.
5. Admit that Plaintiff possesses no written request from Julio Cortez asking that Benjamin Jay Barber be removed from the household or lease effective January 1, 2026.
6. Admit that Plaintiff received information or documentation from at least one person other than Benjamin Jay Barber before implementing the January 1, 2026 household-composition change.
7. Admit that Plaintiff can identify the court document or documents it relied on for that change.
8. Admit that Plaintiff can identify the person or persons who supplied or transmitted the court document or documents it relied on for that change.
9. Admit that Plaintiff can identify the employee or employees who approved or implemented the January 1, 2026 household-composition change.
10. Admit that Plaintiff can identify whether any claimed representative authority, power of attorney, guardianship authority, or similar authority was relied upon in connection with that change.
