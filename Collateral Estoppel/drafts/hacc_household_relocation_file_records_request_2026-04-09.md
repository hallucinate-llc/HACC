# HACC Household Relocation File Records Request

This draft is a targeted records request for the household-specific relocation, voucher, transfer, and accommodation file associated with Jane Kay Cortez and Benjamin Barber.

It is designed for adaptation into a public-records request, ORCP 55 subpoena attachment, or motion-to-compel exhibit.

## Request objective

Please produce the complete household-specific relocation, voucher, transfer, accommodation, and application-routing file for `Jane Kay Cortez` and `Benjamin Barber` in connection with the Hillside Manor / Blossom / Parkside Heights / Tenant Protection Voucher / Waterleaf relocation paths.

## Why this request is specific

The current record already includes:

1. HACC redevelopment and displacement notices tied to the Hillside process;
2. the January 8, 2026 notice directing the household to choose between `Blossom / Community Apartments` and a `Tenant Protection Voucher`;
3. a later HACC written acknowledgment that an intake packet had been submitted to Quantum staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office but had not been provided to HACC;
4. March 2026 communications concerning voucher issuance, bedroom size, accommodation review, and unresolved relocation status;
5. HACC communications reflecting Waterleaf / Multnomah portability discussions; and
6. the March 26, 2026 written options email requiring a move by April 30, 2026.

This request therefore seeks the underlying household file and processing history for the same already-identified household and relocation paths.

## Requested records

1. The complete relocation file for Jane Kay Cortez / Benjamin Barber arising from the Hillside redevelopment / Section 18 process.
2. All household-specific case notes, CRM notes, internal comments, task notes, or status logs concerning:
   - relocation counseling;
   - comparable housing / replacement housing;
   - Blossom / Community Apartments;
   - Hillside Manor;
   - Parkside Heights;
   - Tenant Protection Voucher administration;
   - portability or port-out administration;
   - Waterleaf;
   - Home Forward;
   - Brighthaven / Vera; and
   - accommodation or service-animal requests.
3. All notices, letters, emails, and attachments sent to or received from Jane Kay Cortez, Benjamin Barber, Kati Tilton, Ashley Ferron, HACC Relocation, or any project-side intake address concerning those paths.
4. The complete January 8, 2026 Blossom / TPV direction file, including any underlying referral notes, waitlist notes, or internal explanation of the choice presented.
5. The complete March 26, 2026 three-option file, including internal notes, drafts, routing, and any contemporaneous explanation of:
   - why those three options were identified;
   - what status each option then had;
   - what remained to be completed for each option; and
   - why April 30, 2026 was being used as the deadline.
6. All records sufficient to identify what application packets, forms, or supporting materials were received from or for Jane Kay Cortez / Benjamin Barber for:
   - Blossom / Community Apartments;
   - Hillside Manor office intake;
   - Parkside Heights;
   - Waterleaf; and
   - any portability / transfer path.
7. All records sufficient to identify whether any such packet or submission was considered incomplete, deficient, stale, misplaced, not forwarded, or otherwise not processed, including the specific reason for that status and the date it was assigned.
8. All records sufficient to identify who reviewed, routed, held, or failed to forward the Hillside Manor / Quantum-submitted packet later referenced in HACC's acknowledgment.
9. All records concerning voucher issuance, voucher size, bedroom-size determination, reversals, or modifications for this household from January 1, 2026 through April 9, 2026.
10. All records concerning portability, transfer, or external referral communications involving Multnomah County, Home Forward, Waterleaf, Brighthaven, or Vera Apartments.
11. All records concerning accommodation requests, service-animal requests, first-floor requests, mobility-related requests, live-in-aide or caregiver-related status, and all responses or reviews concerning those requests.
12. All records sufficient to identify any hearing, grievance, review, or appeal path made available or not made available to this household in connection with:
   - household composition changes;
   - lease status;
   - voucher size;
   - assistance-related changes;
   - relocation status; or
   - adverse action.
