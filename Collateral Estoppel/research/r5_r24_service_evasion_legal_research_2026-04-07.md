# R5/R24 Legal Research: Service Evasion Path (Oregon)

Date: 2026-04-07

Scope: legal grounding for the `r5` / `r24` branch when the target avoids service or disputes service effectiveness.

## 1) Core contempt-service framework (ORS 33.055)

Under ORS 33.055 (remedial contempt procedure):
- the initiating party must file documentation identifying specific contempt acts;
- the contempt defendant is served under ORCP 7; and
- the court may issue an order to appear.

Critical service-evasion clause:
- if the contempt defendant cannot be personally served, the court may (on motion + supporting affidavit) order another method of service or issue an arrest warrant.

Practice implication for `r5/r24`:
- service-evasion evidence is relevant and usable,
- but the record should still include a formal motion/affidavit showing inability to complete personal service.

Primary source:
- ORS 33.055: https://oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_33.055
  (original source linked there to Oregon Legislature ORS chapter page)

## 2) Compelling appearance after nonappearance (ORS 33.075)

ORS 33.075 authorizes the court to issue orders/warrants to compel appearance when a person served with an ORS 33.055 order to appear fails to appear.

Practice implication for `r24`:
- strongest path is a two-step proof packet:
  1. proof of valid service of order-to-appear,
  2. certified nonappearance record (minute/register/transcript indicator).

Primary source:
- ORS 33.075: https://oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_33.075

## 3) Alternative service standards (ORCP 7 D(6))

ORCP 7 D(6) allows court-ordered alternative service when ordinary methods are not possible.
The motion must be supported by affidavit/declaration and propose methods reasonably calculated to provide notice.

The current ORCP materials also show electronic alternative service pathways if supported by diligence facts.

## 4) Subpoena disobedience and contempt warning (ORCP 55)

Current ORCP 55 language requires subpoena text to warn that subpoenas must be obeyed unless ordered otherwise, and that disobedience is punishable by fine or jail.
Rule 55 also states witness disobedience/refusal can be punished as contempt.

Primary source:
- 2025 ORCP PDF (Rule 55 A(1)(a)(vi), A(6)(d)):
  https://www.oregonlegislature.gov/bills_laws/lawsstatutes/2025OrLawORCP.pdf

## 5) Case-law signal on service-evasion findings in contempt procedure

The ORS 33.055 notes (as published with the statute text) identify Hiber v. Creditors Collection Service, which is relevant to service-evasion procedure: the court cannot skip the predicate finding regarding inability to personally serve when moving away from personal service in this contempt context.

Primary source (statute notes):
- ORS 33.055 notes section: https://oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_33.055
