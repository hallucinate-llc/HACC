# Prior Alternative-Service Motion: Impact on R5/R24 Posture (2026-04-07)

## User-asserted procedural fact
- Movant reports a **previously submitted motion for alternative service**.
- Current status in this workspace: asserted, not yet tied to a filed-stamped copy/order in the staged evidence set.

## What this changes
- The record no longer depends only on informal service-evasion indicators.
- The key proof question shifts to whether the Court:
  - granted alternative service,
  - specified method(s), and
  - had those method(s) completed and documented.

## R5 / R24 linkage
- R5 support strengthens if prior motion + diligence declaration are in the record.
- R24 still requires nonappearance proof after legally sufficient service of the appearance/related order pathway.

## Highest-priority missing artifacts to collect
1. Filed-stamped motion for alternative service (and supporting declaration/affidavit).
2. Signed order ruling on that motion (grant/deny/partial + authorized channels).
3. Proof of execution of authorized service method(s):
   - declaration of service,
   - mailing certificates,
   - e-service logs,
   - message delivery receipts where court-authorized.
4. Docket entry or register extract showing filing date and ruling date.
5. For R24 path: proof of hearing/order-to-appear service + certified failure-to-appear minute/record.

## Litigation use framing (working)
- If order granted and service performed per order:
  - characterize service as court-authorized and completed,
  - then tie nonappearance/contempt predicates to certified nonappearance records.
- If order pending or no ruling located:
  - request status conference or renewed service-direction order,
  - preserve all evasion evidence as diligence support.

  - prior alternative-service motion filed,
  - alternative-service order granted/denied,
  - alternative-service execution complete.
