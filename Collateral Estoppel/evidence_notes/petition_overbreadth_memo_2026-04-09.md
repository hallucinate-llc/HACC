# Petition Overbreadth Memo - 2026-04-09

This memo isolates why Solomon Samuel Barber's first amended guardianship petition is overbroad even apart from the separate issue-preclusion and sanctions questions.

Primary source:

- [solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt)

## Bottom line

The petition does not merely request narrow protective help for Jane Kay Cortez. It seeks a combined package of:

1. waiver-of-notice emergency relief;
2. temporary and indefinite guardianship;
3. plenary authority;
4. possible institutional or residential placement;
5. writ-based law-enforcement-assisted transport;
6. financial-control authority despite incomplete trust / estate disclosure; and
7. attorney-fee shifting from Jane Kay Cortez's funds.

That combination is substantially broader than a limited request to stabilize immediate circumstances, and it supports a narrow response asking the Court to deny or sharply confine relief unless a much cleaner predicate record is shown.

## Main overbreadth points

### 1. Plenary authority request

The petition expressly seeks plenary authority over Jane Kay Cortez.

Source:

- [solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt#L234)

Why it matters:

- On a materially disputed record, plenary authority is the broadest control request available.
- That request is hard to reconcile with the narrower proposition that the Court need only address a short-term emergency.

### 2. Placement authority request

The petition states that the proposed guardian intends to place Jane Kay Cortez in a mental health treatment facility, a nursing home, or another residential facility if appropriate and necessary.

Source:

- [solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt#L228)

Why it matters:

- This is not merely a request to coordinate housing.
- It is a request for potentially sweeping control over Jane Kay Cortez's residence and living situation.

### 3. Writ-based forced-transport request

The petition separately asks for a writ of assistance authorizing law enforcement to obtain custody of Jane Kay Cortez, transport her to a hospital, and enter the home to do so.

Sources:

- [solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt#L279)
- [solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt#L287)

Why it matters:

- This is extraordinary coercive relief.
- It substantially broadens the petition beyond a simple request for appointment of a guardian.

### 4. Financial-control request without clean document production

The petition says the proposed guardian will exercise control over Jane Kay Cortez's estate, while also saying the aggregate estate is presently unknown but not believed to exceed `$10,000`, and while separately invoking a substantial inheritance / Special Needs Trust theory elsewhere in the petition.

Sources:

- [solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt#L224)
- [solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt#L133)

Why it matters:

- The petition requests broad authority over financial affairs without producing the underlying trust / inheritance papers, accounting, or clean authority chain.
- That is both an inconsistency problem and an overbreadth problem.

### 5. Attorney-fee shifting request

The petition asks that Solomon Barber's attorney fees and costs be paid from Jane Kay Cortez's funds under `ORS 125.095`.

Sources:

- [solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt#L266)
- [solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt](/home/barberb/HACC/Collateral%20Estoppel/evidence_notes/solomon_motion_for_guardianship_ocr.txt#L304)

Why it matters:

- It attempts to make Jane Kay Cortez finance adverse coercive proceedings against herself on a disputed record.
- That widens the petition further and supports a narrow court response unless benefit to Jane Kay Cortez is actually established.

### 6. Combined effect

Each of the above requests is individually significant. Taken together, they show that the petition is not a modest or minimal intervention request. It is a broad transfer-of-control package.

That combined breadth is the cleanest reason to argue:

1. deny or sharply narrow relief;
2. require a clean predicate order and notice record for any coercive process;
3. refuse to let collateral housing and trust disputes be used to bootstrap plenary probate control;
4. deny fee shifting on the present record.
