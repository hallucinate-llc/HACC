# Delay Apportionment Timeline: Solomon Vs. Quantum / HACC - 2026-04-07

Purpose:
- isolate the main delay windows in the housing dispute; and
- give a conservative source-backed framework for apportioning delay between:
  - `Solomon-side interference / household-composition influence`, and
  - `Quantum / Blossom / HACC packet-processing and document-processing delay`.

## Bottom line

The current record supports two overlapping delay branches:

1. **Quantum / Blossom / HACC intake and document-processing delay**
   - begins no later than **December 2, 2025**, when Benjamin said he had completed the packet and had business-minutes / amendment / IRS-tax materials ready;
   - is explicitly acknowledged by HACC on **January 26, 2026**, when Ashley Ferron said the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum staff but not forwarded to HACC; and
   - remains live through at least **March 19, 2026**, when HACC finally wrote that a one-bedroom voucher would be issued that day.

2. **Solomon-side interference / household-control delay**
   - begins, on the current record, no later than **November 20, 2025**, when the Solomon restraining order was granted and restrained him from interference while also identifying Benjamin in the guardian ad litem / guardian petitioner role;
   - becomes housing-material no later than **January 12, 2026**, when HACC removed Benjamin effective January 1 based on `internal review` and `court documentation currently on file`;
   - is reinforced by the March 23 Skee declarations attributing Benjamin's removal to outside document flows from Benjamin's brother and stepfather; and
   - remains active through at least **March 31, 2026**, when Solomon's guardianship petition still purported to know and plead detailed housing facts about Jane and Benjamin.

The safest present apportionment is:

- **Quantum / HACC** bears the clearer direct delay record for the stalled packet, repeated documentation loop, and slow voucher processing.
- **Solomon** is the stronger current candidate for delay caused by outside interference with household composition, lease status, and collateral housing influence.
- The two branches overlap in January-March 2026, and the missing HACC authority-chain record is still the key item needed to quantify the Solomon share more precisely.

## 3. Overlap and tentative apportionment

- during that period, HACC was:
  - acting on the changed household composition;
  - applying one-bedroom consequences;
  - acknowledging the broken Quantum / Hillside Manor packet chain;
  - escalating document demands; and
  - still not issuing the voucher until March 19.

The safest current apportionment framework is:

- **Solomon-side share**
  - strongest for the lease-removal / household-composition disruption;
  - strongest for the theory that outside document flows interfered with Benjamin's tenant rights;
  - not yet cleanly quantifiable beyond the January 1 / January 12 to February 4 restoration window without the HACC authority-chain record.

- **Quantum / HACC share**
  - strongest for the lost or non-forwarded intake packet;
  - strongest for the direct processing and documentation delay;
  - strongest for the seven-plus week delay after HACC admitted the packet-routing failure and before the March 19 voucher issuance marker.
