## What this changes

The `ORCP 17` audit no longer stops at:

1. authority identified; and
2. generic proof-state caution.

It now also tracks whether the workspace has actually mapped a specific challenged filing to:

1. improper purpose;
2. unsupported legal position; and
3. unsupported factual assertion.

## Why this matters

This reduces another common drift problem: saying there is a sanctions theory without identifying which filing is actually being challenged and on what exact `ORCP 17` basis.

The logic layer now forces the workspace to distinguish between:

1. a general sanctions concern; and
2. a filing-specific sanctions case.
