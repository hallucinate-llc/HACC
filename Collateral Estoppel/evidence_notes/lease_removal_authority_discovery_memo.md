## 2. Best current theory to test

The present theory should be tested in this order:

1. Did HACC remove Benjamin without any valid household-side request at all?
2. If HACC did act on an outside request or pressure source, who was it?
3. Did HACC treat that outside person as having authority over Jane's housing decisions?
4. If so, what claimed authority was it:
   - power of attorney
   - representative payee or money-management status
   - family-member status only
   - informal staff assumption
   - some other undocumented claim

## 3. Highest-value records to obtain

The most important records are:

1. The complete lease-change and household-composition file for October 2025 through February 2026.
2. Metadata, routing, approvals, and authorship for the `December 4, 2025` lease adjustment and the `January 1, 2026` lease amendment / household-composition record.
3. Any HACC case notes, CRM logs, call logs, visitor logs, Teams logs, handwritten notes, or task comments mentioning:
   - Benjamin removal
   - Solomon
   - stepfather
   - Julio
   - power of attorney
   - representative payee
   - who is and is not currently residing in the household
4. Any document that HACC believed authorized someone other than Benjamin or Jane to affect lease composition.
5. Any document showing whether HACC believed a restraining-order-limited person was still allowed to influence lease or housing decisions.

## 4. Best admissions to seek

The cleanest admissions would be:

1. HACC has no written request from Benjamin asking to be removed from the lease.
2. HACC has no written request from Jane asking to remove Benjamin from the lease.
3. HACC has no written request from Julio asking to remove Benjamin from the lease.
4. HACC changed the lease or household-composition record before clearly documenting who requested the change.
5. HACC received outside communications from Benjamin's brother or stepfather about household or lease matters.
6. HACC has no valid power-of-attorney or comparable authority record authorizing Solomon to direct Jane's housing decisions.

## 5. Best deposition questions for Charley Skee

If Charley Skee is examined, the key questions are:

1. What exactly did you mean when you said Benjamin had been taken off the lease because his brother and stepfather had been emailing documents to HACC?
2. What documents were you referring to?
3. Who sent them?
4. Who at HACC reviewed them?
5. Did HACC understand any sender to be acting under a power of attorney or other authority?
6. Did HACC verify whether that authority actually existed?
7. Did HACC have any written request from Benjamin or Jane authorizing the lease removal?
8. What role, if any, did restraining-order issues play in deciding whose communications HACC would accept?

## 6. Best current litigation posture

The current safest litigation posture is:

- use the Skee declarations as direct support that HACC itself linked Benjamin's lease removal to outside document flows from the brother and stepfather;
- use the Solomon / claimed-authority theory as a targeted discovery theory rather than as an already-proved fact;
- frame the power-of-attorney issue as `possible claimed authority not yet documented in the reviewed repository`.

## 7. Bottom line

The next breakthrough is unlikely to come from more abstraction. It will come from one of three concrete records:

- the lease-change request source record
- the internal HACC note naming the source actor
- the authority document, if any, that HACC thought let an outside person influence Jane's housing decisions
