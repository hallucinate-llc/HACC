The downloaded HDC Hillside Manor page states in substance that:

- HDC worked with `HACC` to convert Hillside Manor's public-housing subsidies to `project-based Section 8 subsidies` through `RAD`;
- the RAD conversion opened the way for `private debt financing` and `LIHTC equity`; and

This is useful because it corroborates, from an outside project participant, the same broader financing and subsidy-conversion structure reflected in HACC's own plan materials.

## Present evidentiary use

These sources are best used as:

1. public corroboration of role and project structure;
2. supplemental support for HACC's developer / administrator role and Quantum's management role;
3. evidence that a formal management-procurement and development-document chain exists; and
4. support for later requests, subpoenas, or motions seeking the executed management agreement, HAP contract, RAD use agreement, and ground-lease / control documents; and
5. supplemental corroboration that Hillside Manor's financing and subsidy structure involved RAD conversion, project-based Section 8, private debt, and LIHTC equity.

## Important limit

These downloaded public sources still do **not** themselves supply:

- the executed management agreement;
- the executed RAD/PBV HAP contract text; or
- the executed ground lease / project agreement text.

They strengthen the role / structure record, but do not eliminate the need to request or subpoena the executed contracts.
