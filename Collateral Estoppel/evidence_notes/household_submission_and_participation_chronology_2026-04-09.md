# Household Submission And Participation Chronology

This note consolidates the current record showing repeated household participation in the HACC-directed relocation and replacement-housing paths. It is offered to support the narrower proposition that defendants did not abandon the process or sit idle, but instead continued submitting materials, following up, and pursuing the options HACC kept identifying while the overall relocation process remained unresolved.

## Key Chronology

1. **December 2, 2025**
   The preserved `JC Household` chain shows Benjamin Barber emailing Ashley Ferron and copying Charley Skee, HACC Public Housing Management, and HACC Relocation, while stating that the packet was complete and that business-minute, amendment, and IRS tax materials were ready. This is the clearest current starting point for the Clackamas-side packet-and-routing branch.

2. **December 23, 2025**
   The Waterleaf application record reflects a `Created Date: 12/23/2025`, showing that the Multnomah / Waterleaf path was already underway by late December 2025. See Exhibit W cover materials and underlying image record.

3. **January 8, 2026**
   HACC issued the Blossom-or-TPV notice directing the household into a replacement-housing choice structure. That matters because it shows HACC was still actively channeling the household into administered relocation paths rather than treating the process as already completed.

4. **January 12, 2026**
   HACC correspondence reflected that Waterleaf had a signed application and wanted voucher information. The same January 12 period also reflects that HACC was still making household-composition and process decisions affecting the household's housing path. On the current record, January 12 confirms both that the Waterleaf path was active and that HACC remained operationally involved in the household's relocation status.

5. **January 26, 2026**
   HACC later acknowledged in writing that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office but had not been provided to HACC. This is one of the strongest current record points because it confirms both household participation and a break in the HACC/Quantum routing chain.

6. **Late January 2026**
   The Waterleaf application record shows the application remained on the waiting list by January 28, 2026. That supports the narrower inference that the port-out path was active but unresolved, not abandoned.

7. **February 2026**
   The record reflects continued follow-up, repeated HACC-side requests for information, and ongoing housing-process communications rather than household disengagement. The present significance of February is not that every requirement had been finally satisfied, but that the household remained in active process while HACC-side administration continued.

8. **March 19, 2026**
   HACC's communications provide the first clear voucher-issuance marker, while also reflecting a bedroom-size reversal issue. This is important because it shows the household was still waiting on HACC-administered voucher action well into March 2026.

9. **March 26, 2026**
   Kati Tilton's written summary put three options in writing and stated that all available options required a move by April 30, 2026. This is strong evidence that HACC itself still treated the relocation process as active and incomplete at that point.

10. **March 29, 2026**
    Benjamin Barber submitted a Parkside Heights application and copied Kati Tilton and HACC Relocation. This is a concrete late-stage participation event showing continued effort even after the March 19 voucher marker.

11. **March 30, 2026**
    Parkside Heights replied, thanked Benjamin for the application, and stated that the two-bedroom units in Building A were still under construction with estimated occupancy at the end of April. Read together with the March 26 options email, this supports the narrower inference that the Parkside path was still being treated as part of the same unresolved April 30 relocation framework.

12. **Early April 2026**
    As reflected in the declaration and related hearing notes, no actual signable lease or completed move had yet materialized through either the Clackamas-side path involving HACC / Quantum / Blossom / Parkside Heights or the Multnomah-side path involving Home Forward / Waterleaf / Vera / Brighthaven.

## Narrow Inference Supported

The present record does **not** yet prove that every submission was accepted as fully complete or that every downstream reviewer had finished processing it. What it **does** show is:

- repeated household submissions and follow-up activity;
- continued participation in both the Clackamas and Multnomah paths;
- HACC-directed and HACC-administered relocation options remaining open into late March 2026;
- a documented packet-routing failure on the HACC / Quantum side; and
- no completed replacement-housing move despite continued household participation.

That is enough to support the narrower litigation position that the housing breakdown should not presently be treated as a simple household-caused default or abandonment.
