## 4. Best current elimination-based inference

The strongest currently supportable inference is narrower than a flat factual assertion that Solomon definitively removed Benjamin from the lease.

- Benjamin and Jane both now have declaration support that Charley Skee attributed Benjamin's lease removal, in substance, to document flows coming from Benjamin's brother and stepfather.
- The known household-side actors do not presently appear in the reviewed record as having made a preserved request to remove Benjamin from the lease.
- Julio's status was itself disputed and subject to restraining-order / bifurcation issues, making him a poor fit as a stable lawful source for a contrary lease-change request.
- Solomon's own March 10 statements support the theory that he treated service and enforceability in a self-help way rather than as immediately binding once the order was granted.

On that record, the safest present formulation is:

- `On information and belief, HACC acted on outside document flows or pressure from Benjamin's brother and stepfather, and Solomon is a plausible outside actor in that chain.`

That is stronger than the broader proposition:

- `The record already proves that Solomon usurped a power of attorney and removed Benjamin from the lease.`

The current repository does not yet prove that broader proposition directly.

## 5. Best current use of the Skee discussion

The March 23 Skee discussion is valuable for three reasons:

- it gives paired declaration testimony from Benjamin and Jane about what an HACC staff member said in substance about the lease-removal cause;
- it supports the theory that HACC itself understood outside document flows to be driving the delay and lease-side confusion; and
- it narrows discovery toward identifying which outside actor, what documents, and what claimed authority HACC was acting on.
