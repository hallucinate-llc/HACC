# Missing Project Agreements Retrieval Note

This note identifies the project-side documents that do not yet appear in the repository in executed form, explains why they matter, and states the narrower factual points already supported by the current record.

## A. What The Current Record Already Shows

The current record already supports the following points without the executed contracts themselves:

1. HACC's July 1, 2025 Administrative Plan identifies `Hillside Manor Apartments` as a RAD/PBV project, identifies `Quantum Residential Property Management` as the property management company, and states that the project has:
   - a `06/01/2020 HAP contract`; and
   - a `01/01/2021 RAD HAP contract`.

2. HACC's Administrative Plan also states that RAD conversion projects are subject to a `RAD Use Agreement` and discusses ownership/control arrangements involving:
   - fee ownership;
   - ground leases; and
   - control arrangements retained by HACC or a related public/nonprofit entity.

3. HACC's public Blossom lease-up materials state that Blossom applications are processed through `HACC's PBV waitlist` and that Hillside relocation households are priority groups.

4. The household-specific record already supports that HACC and Quantum were both in the intake / routing / replacement-housing chain.

## B. What Is Still Missing

The repository does not yet appear to contain the full executed text of the following project documents:

1. The `06/01/2020 HAP contract` referenced in the Administrative Plan.
2. The `01/01/2021 RAD HAP contract` referenced in the Administrative Plan.
3. The initial `RAD Use Agreement`.
4. Any executed `management agreement` or property-management services agreement identifying Quantum's role and duties.
5. Any executed `ground lease`, `control agreement`, or equivalent project-governance instrument for Hillside Manor.
6. Any owner-side or project-side agreement showing who had authority over:
   - leasing;
   - intake routing;
   - relocation processing;
   - tenant communications; and
   - preservation of application materials.

## C. Why These Missing Documents Matter

These documents would materially strengthen the agreement-side and deontic record because they could clarify:

1. Who the actual contracting parties were.
2. Whether HACC acted as:
   - public housing agency only;
   - contract administrator;
   - owner;
   - related entity; or
   - retained controlling party.
3. Whether Quantum's role was limited to ordinary property management or extended to:
   - intake;
   - eligibility processing;
   - leasing coordination;
   - waitlist administration support; or
   - relocation implementation.
4. What duties were mandatory, discretionary, conditional, or prohibited in the project chain.
5. What preservation, routing, notice, and response obligations existed regarding applications and relocation materials.
6. Whether any contract text expressly incorporated:
   - RAD requirements;
   - PBV requirements;
   - Section 18 replacement-housing obligations;
   - grievance / hearing procedures; or
   - ownership-control restrictions.

## D. Filing-Safe Present Position

Until the executed project agreements are produced, the safest present argument is:

1. HACC's duties are strongly grounded by statute, regulation, and HACC's own Administrative Plan and public PBV/RAD materials.
2. Quantum is presently grounded as the documented project-side property-management and intake-path participant in the same HACC-directed chain.
3. The missing executed agreements would materially strengthen, but are not strictly necessary for, the narrower theories that:
   - HACC had direct relocation and program-administration duties; and
   - Quantum participated in the intake / forwarding / processing path that allegedly broke down.

## E. Best Retrieval Targets

The cleanest project-document retrieval request should ask HACC and/or Quantum to produce:

1. The fully executed `06/01/2020 HAP contract` for Hillside Manor.
2. The fully executed `01/01/2021 RAD HAP contract` for Hillside Manor.
3. The initial and any amended `RAD Use Agreement` for Hillside Manor.
4. Any `management agreement` between Hillside Manor ownership, HACC, any HACC-related entity, and/or Quantum.
5. Any `ground lease`, `control agreement`, regulatory agreement, or equivalent ownership-control instrument governing Hillside Manor.
6. Any document identifying who was responsible for:
   - application intake;
   - routing of relocation packets;
   - waitlist or PBV processing;
   - leasing decisions;
   - and communication with Hillside relocation households.
7. Any policy, addendum, rider, or exhibit incorporated into those agreements that addresses:
   - PBV;
   - RAD;
   - Section 18;
   - tenant selection;
   - grievance / hearing procedures;
   - and relocation implementation.
