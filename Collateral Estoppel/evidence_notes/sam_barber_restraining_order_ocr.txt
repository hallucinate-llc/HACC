> The VinePPO paper [1] investigated the standard value estimations used in Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable
Rewards (RLVR) for Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning, and found that they produce poor estimates, barely
outperforming random baselines when ranking alternative steps, and impairing training and final reasoning
performance. We study whether, for the PPO value-network model in the VinePPO paper's baseline, this is a training-
data problem or a fundamental architectural limitation, by using VinePPO’s accurate Monte-Carlo (MC) estimates as
supervision targets for the value network. Ideally, we would like to develop a hybrid training approach for even more
efficient PPO-like RL, where the value network is regressed against the training outcomes most of the time, as usual,
