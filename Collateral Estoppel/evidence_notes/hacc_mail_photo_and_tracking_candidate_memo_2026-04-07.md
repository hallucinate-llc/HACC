## Bottom line

Yes, the repository now appears to contain a meaningful proof trail:

1. a January 26, 2026 HACC email thread includes three attached photos with timestamp-style names:
   `Image_260126_121526.jpeg`
   `Image_260126_121554.jpeg`

3. the file [Delivery Feb 2nd.HEIC](/home/barberb/HACC/evidence/history/Delivery%20Feb%202nd.HEIC) is a direct photograph of a USPS Priority Mail envelope at a mailbox with the shipping label visible;
4. that visible label appears addressed to HACC at `P.O. Box 1510, Oregon City, OR 97045-1510`, or substantially similar address text;
5. a later preserved image in the February 2, 2026 HACC thread shows USPS tracking delivered to a PO Box in Oregon City, Oregon 97045 on January 27, 2026;
6. together, those items are consistent with a pre-drop mailing photo plus delivery tracking.

Current proof limit:

1. the current repo review does not yet prove with full certainty that the mailed envelope shown in `Delivery Feb 2nd.HEIC` contained the restraining-order packet specifically;
2. the current repo review does not yet identify the full contents of the packet shown in the January 26 photos;
3. this should therefore be treated as a strong mailing-proof trail to HACC, but not yet as a completely verified identity match to the restraining orders themselves.
