# Motion 3 Sanctions Menu: Collateral Probate Petition / Contempt

Case: `25PO11530`

Related probate case: `26PR00641`

Purpose:

This note identifies sanctions and corrective remedies to request if the Court finds Solomon Samuel Barber used the probate petition to resist, obstruct, or evade the `25PO11530` restraining order, or if the Court finds that material probate allegations were knowingly false, materially misleading, or made without reasonable inquiry.

## Governing Remedy Tracks

### Track A: `25PO11530` remedial contempt relief

Use this track for remedies tied to the restraining-order court's authority and to Solomon's alleged resistance, obstruction, evasion, nonproduction, or indirect interference.

Authority:

1. ORS 33.105 authorizes remedial contempt sanctions, including compensatory money, coercive confinement, daily coercive fines, compliance orders, attorney fees caused by contempt, and any other effective remedy.
2. ORS 124.012 supports contempt proceedings in the court that issued the EPPDAPA restraining order or in a county where a violation occurs.
3. ORS 40.135 / OEC 311 supports a presumption that willfully suppressed evidence would be adverse to the suppressing party.

### Track B: `26PR00641` probate / ORCP 17 sanctions

Use this track for sanctions based directly on the filing of the probate petition as a pleading in the probate case.

Authority:

1. ORCP 17 C requires factual assertions to be supported by evidence or specifically identified as expected to be supported after further investigation.
2. ORCP 17 D allows sanctions for false certifications after notice and an opportunity to be heard.
3. The `25PO11530` motion should not ask the restraining-order court to impose ORCP 17 sanctions for a probate pleading. It should ask for referral, transmission of findings, or reservation of a separate sanctions motion in `26PR00641`.

## Best Sanctions To Request In `25PO11530`

### 1. Corrective compliance order

Request:

Order Solomon to comply with the existing restraining order and prohibit him from using probate, HACC, APS, medical, estate, trust, housing, payee, or other third-party channels to do indirectly what the order prohibits directly.

Why it fits:

This is squarely remedial. It is designed to insure compliance with the prior order, which ORS 33.105 expressly permits.

### 2. Mandatory production and sworn accounting

Request:

Order Solomon to produce, by a fixed deadline, every document, communication, and factual source he used, referenced, withheld, or omitted concerning:

1. Jane's trust, estate, inheritance, land, timber, or signed-authority documents;
2. any claimed power of attorney, representative authority, or housing authorization;
3. HACC, Home Forward, Quantum, Waterleaf, Parkside, APS, police, medical, payee, trust, or probate communications;
4. the source for each contested probate allegation;
5. all communications with Alex Bluestone or any attorney relating to the probate petition, HACC, records production, service, or the restraining order, except materials validly logged as privileged.

Require a sworn declaration identifying:

1. what was produced;
2. what does not exist;
3. what was once possessed but is no longer available;
4. who has custody of any withheld or missing record;
5. the asserted privilege basis for any withheld record.

Why it fits:

This is remedial because it cures the continuing nonproduction and lets the Court test whether the probate allegations had a factual basis.

### 3. Evidence-suppression / adverse-presumption order

Request:

If Solomon does not produce ordered records, or cannot account for records that he was ordered to produce and that he used or relied on in probate, the Court should find that the missing records are presumed adverse to Solomon under ORS 40.135 / OEC 311.

Suggested wording:

`For purposes of further proceedings in this case, any Jane-related trust, estate, payee, housing, authority, HACC, APS, police, or probate record within Respondent's possession, custody, control, agent access, or attorney-accessible file that Respondent was ordered to produce but failed to produce shall be presumed adverse to Respondent on the issue for which the record was requested, unless Respondent proves nonexistence, lack of control, privilege, or another lawful basis for nonproduction.`

Why it fits:

This is the cleanest way to request the spoliation / nonproduction remedy without overstating it as a conclusive admission. Oregon's evidence rule supports an adverse presumption for willful suppression; ORS 40.120 then gives presumptions practical burden-shifting effect in civil proceedings.

### 4. Issue-limiting / reliance-preclusion order

Request:

Order that Solomon may not rely in `25PO11530` on any unproduced record, undisclosed factual source, or probate allegation that he refuses or fails to substantiate.

Suggested wording:

`Respondent should be precluded from relying on any unproduced document, undisclosed communication, or materially unsupported probate allegation to narrow, evade, collaterally attack, or justify noncompliance with the restraining order.`

Why it fits:

This is remedial and targeted. It prevents Solomon from benefiting from nonproduction while avoiding a punitive sanction label.

### 5. Corrective notice / transmission to probate court

Request:

If the Court finds that the probate petition omitted material restraining-order facts or relied on materially misleading allegations, ask the Court to:

1. transmit findings or authorize filing of the contempt findings in `26PR00641`;
2. state that the probate court should consider whether the petition should be corrected, limited, dismissed, or sanctioned under probate procedure or ORCP 17;
3. order Solomon to serve the probate court and all probate participants with any contempt findings and with the Google Voice comparison exhibit.

Why it fits:

The restraining-order court avoids directly sanctioning the probate pleading under ORCP 17, while still protecting its order from collateral evasion.

### 6. Withdrawal / correction requirement

Request:

Order Solomon to withdraw, correct, or disclaim reliance on any probate allegation that he cannot support after ordered production and hearing.

Suggested wording:

`Respondent should be ordered to file a corrective notice in probate case 26PR00641 identifying the restraining-order proceeding, the restraining-order terms, the order-production dispute, and any probate allegations that Respondent cannot support after this Court's show-cause hearing.`

Why it fits:

This is remedial because it unwinds the ongoing effect of a misleading collateral filing.

### 7. Fees and costs caused by contempt

Request:

Order Solomon to reimburse reasonable costs, filing expenses, service expenses, transcript/OCR/exhibit preparation expenses, and any available attorney fees caused by the contempt, nonproduction, and collateral probate evasion.

Why it fits:

ORS 33.105 allows compensatory money and attorney fees incurred as the result of contempt. For pro se time, ask for out-of-pocket costs and reserve any statutory fee argument unless counsel appears or a fee basis is identified.

### 8. Coercive daily fine for continuing noncompliance

Request:

If Solomon remains noncompliant after a production deadline, impose a daily coercive sanction up to the amount allowed by ORS 33.105 until he complies.

Suggested conservative ask:

`A daily coercive fine in an amount the Court finds sufficient to compel compliance, beginning after a short cure period, and continuing until Respondent produces the ordered records and sworn accounting.`

Why it fits:

This is classic remedial contempt for ongoing noncompliance. It is stronger if framed as conditional and purgeable.

### 9. No recovery from Jane's funds for the challenged probate effort

Request:

Find that Solomon may not seek or collect fees, costs, reimbursements, or expenses from Jane's SSI/SSA, representative-payee funds, trust funds, inheritance, or property for work connected to any probate allegations or relief found false, materially misleading, or part of collateral evasion.

Why it fits:

This protects Jane from paying for an effort that may have violated or undermined the order protecting her.

### 10. Referral or sanctions reservation

Request:

If the Court finds knowing false statements under penalty of perjury, request a referral or transmission of findings to the probate court and any appropriate authority, while preserving that the `25PO11530` court is not itself adjudicating criminal perjury.

Why it fits:

This keeps the language court-safe. The contempt court can find willful evasion and material misleading conduct without needing to make a criminal perjury determination.

## Sanctions To Avoid Or Phrase Carefully

1. Avoid asking `25PO11530` to impose ORCP 17 sanctions directly for the probate pleading. Ask for referral / findings / reservation instead.
2. Avoid asking for punitive contempt unless the procedural requirements for punitive contempt are separately satisfied.
3. Avoid asking for a conclusive admission that Solomon committed every alleged act solely because records are missing. Ask first for an adverse presumption and issue-limiting relief.
4. Avoid asserting the `$100,000`, 150-acre, timber-lease, or lease-removal facts as established unless the underlying records are attached. Frame them as proffered facts and production targets until proven.
5. Avoid asking for confinement as the first-line sanction. Keep confinement as a last-resort purgeable remedy if Solomon continues to defy production/compliance orders.

## Recommended Sanctions Package For Motion 3

The best current request is a tiered order:

1. Show-cause finding: require Solomon to explain why the probate petition was not a collateral effort to evade `25PO11530`.
2. Production order: require all trust, estate, payee, HACC, APS, police, housing, authority, probate, and attorney-accessible nonprivileged records, plus a privilege log.
3. Sworn accounting: require Solomon to identify the source for each contested probate allegation.
4. Adverse presumption: if records are not produced or accounted for, presume the missing evidence would be adverse to Solomon.
5. Reliance preclusion: bar Solomon from relying on unproduced records or unsupported probate allegations in `25PO11530`.
6. Corrective probate notice: require correction or transmission to `26PR00641` if the Court finds material omissions or falsehoods.
7. Protective channel order: bar Solomon from using third-party channels to interfere with Jane's housing, property, trust, payee, medical, APS, or legal status except through counsel, court filings, or expressly permitted service methods.
8. Costs and fees: award compensatory costs/fees caused by the contempt if available.
9. Coercive fine: impose a purgeable daily sanction only if Solomon misses the production/compliance deadline.
10. Referral/reservation: reserve ORCP 17 / perjury / probate sanctions for the proper forum if knowing false statements are found.
