# Law-First Duty Memo Re HACC And Quantum Residential

This memo is designed for filing use. It states the strongest current duty structure in the proper order:

1. duties imposed directly by law;
2. duties adopted in HACC's own program documents;
3. the narrower project-side role currently shown for Quantum Residential; and
4. the present limits of the contract record.

It is intended to keep the packet grounded in source-backed duty language before turning to later emails, promises, or breach inferences.

## I. Core framing

The strongest current source-backed position is not that every disputed housing wrong arose from an informal promise by email. The stronger position is that:

- federal housing law imposed direct relocation, hearing, VAWA, and fair-housing duties on Housing Authority of Clackamas County (`HACC`);
- HACC's own Administrative Plan and public lease-up materials adopted project-specific PBV/RAD and relocation-processing obligations for the Hillside Manor / Blossom path;
- HACC's own records place Quantum Residential Property Management, L.P. (`Quantum`) inside that intake-and-processing chain as the project-side participant or property-management actor; and
- the later email record is best used to show nonperformance, breakdown, delay, or breach of those already-existing duties, rather than as the original source of duty itself.

## II. HACC duties imposed directly by law

### A. Section 18 relocation duties

The strongest direct law-first duties run to HACC under Section 18 and HUD's implementing regulation.

Official sources:

- `42 U.S.C. § 1437p`
- `24 C.F.R. § 970.21`

Those sources support the following obligations in substance:

- HACC had to provide the required displacement notice.
- HACC had to provide or arrange comparable housing.
- HACC had to provide actual and reasonable relocation assistance, including moving-expense support.
- HACC had to provide counseling as part of the displacement process.
- HACC could not treat demolition/disposition displacement as complete before actual relocation occurred.

The filing-safe consequence is:

- HACC had real relocation duties as a matter of federal law.
- Those duties are stronger than a mere background policy preference.
- The safer litigation position is that HACC should not obtain possession or treat displacement as complete while those relocation duties remained materially unresolved.

### B. Hearing and adverse-action duties

Official sources:

- `42 U.S.C. § 1437d(k)`
- `24 C.F.R. § 982.555`

These sources support the proposition that HACC had covered notice and hearing duties in connection with housing-assistance disputes and adverse housing-program actions.

The filing-safe consequence is:

- HACC was not free to rely on unexplained reversals, assistance-related changes, or disputed adverse actions without the hearing and notice structure those sources require.

### C. VAWA emergency-transfer and confidentiality duties

Official sources:

- `24 C.F.R. § 5.2009`
- `24 C.F.R. § 5.2007(c)`
- `34 U.S.C. § 12491(b)(3)(C)(i)` for protection-order compliance context

These sources support the proposition that HACC had to maintain and use an emergency-transfer framework, protect confidentiality, and handle protection-order-linked access and control issues lawfully.

The filing-safe consequence is:

- HACC had administration duties in the safety-transfer setting.
- The safer formulation is that HACC could not simply let the transfer path drift without coherent administration, status, and confidentiality compliance.
- The stronger formulation is not that federal law guaranteed completion of an out-of-county move by a fixed date unless a source expressly says so.

### D. FHA accommodation and anti-interference duties

Official sources:

- `42 U.S.C. § 3604(f)(2)`
- `42 U.S.C. § 3604(f)(3)(B)`
- `24 C.F.R. § 100.204`
- `42 U.S.C. § 3617`
- `24 C.F.R. § 100.400`

These sources support the proposition that HACC had to:

- avoid disability discrimination in housing-related terms and services;
- provide reasonable accommodations where required; and
- avoid coercion, interference, or retaliation against protected housing-rights activity.

The filing-safe consequence is:

- HACC had direct non-discrimination, accommodation, and anti-interference duties independent of the later email sequence.

## III. HACC duties adopted in its own plan and program materials

The strongest second layer of grounding is HACC's own adopted program structure, not informal promises.

### A. HACC Administrative Plan: project-specific PBV/RAD structure

Source:

- [8ee6f7d1-0c36-48e4-9677-336b95fb9858.txt](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/evidence/did-key-legacy-temporary-session/local-import/0001_8ee6f7d1-0c36-48e4-9677-336b95fb9858.txt/8ee6f7d1-0c36-48e4-9677-336b95fb9858.txt)

That plan-level record supports the following:

- HACC adopted a RAD/PBV structure for `Hillside Manor`.
- HACC said it would establish and manage separate waiting lists for individual RAD PBV projects.
- The plan identifies `Quantum Residential Property Management` as the property-management company for Hillside Manor.
- The plan references a `06/01/2020 HAP contract` and a `01/01/2021 RAD HAP contract`.

The filing-safe consequence is:

- HACC did not merely administer an abstract housing program.
- HACC adopted a project-specific PBV/RAD structure that it itself described in formal program materials.

### B. HACC Administrative Plan: owner responsibilities and hearing before eviction

Same source.

The Administrative Plan states in substance that:

- the owner leases contract units to eligible families referred by HACC or selected from the owner-maintained waiting list;
- the project-side leasing path is governed by the HAP/PBV structure; and
- `The owner must provide an opportunity for an informal hearing before an eviction.`

The filing-safe consequence is:

- HACC's own program materials support a structured lease-up and hearing process in the RAD/PBV setting.
- That is stronger than saying the parties merely hoped for orderly administration.

## IV. Quantum's narrower project-side role

The present record supports a narrower, but still meaningful, duty structure as to Quantum.

### A. What the current record solidly shows

The current record shows:

- HACC's Administrative Plan identifies Quantum as the property-management company for Hillside Manor.
- HACC's own later message states that the intake packet had been submitted to Quantum staff at the Hillside Manor leasing office and had not been provided to HACC.
- HACC's public Blossom materials and January 8 notice place the household inside an HACC-directed PBV / replacement-housing path in the same project environment.

### B. Filing-safe duty formulation for Quantum

The strongest present formulation is:

- if Quantum received, controlled, or participated in the intake / forwarding / processing chain HACC used for the replacement-housing path, Quantum had project-side duties not to lose, block, ignore, discriminatorily burden, or otherwise mishandle that path.

That theory is strongest when tied to:

- intake / forwarding / preservation;
- PBV / project-side lease-up participation;
- FHA / Section 1981 nondiscrimination and anti-interference;
- and the management role HACC itself identifies.

### C. What should not yet be overstated

The current file does **not** yet justify the strongest possible formulation that:

- Section 18, by itself, directly imposed the full relocation-duty chain on Quantum in the same way it did on HACC.

The safer position is:

- Section 18 duties run directly to HACC as the public housing authority.
- Quantum becomes materially relevant because HACC's own records place Quantum inside the very intake and processing chain through which HACC was carrying out that relocation path.

## V. Present contract-record limitation

The current repository appears to show the existence of:

- a HAP contract;
- a RAD HAP contract; and
- a Quantum management role;

but the repository does not yet appear to contain the full executed texts of:

- the signed management agreement;
- the signed HAP contract;
- the ground lease; or
- the full project-side operating agreement.

That matters because:

- HACC's law-based and plan-based duties are already strongly grounded without those missing contracts.
- Quantum's role is already grounded enough for joinder, processing-chain, and nondiscrimination theories.
- But the actual signed agreements would materially strengthen any argument that Quantum separately owed specific forwarding, intake, lease-up, or performance duties by contract.

## VI. Filing-safe deontic summary

The strongest current law-first deontic formulation is:

- `O(HACC, perform the Section 18 relocation steps required by statute and regulation).`
- `O(HACC, provide hearing / review process where the federal housing sources require it).`
- `O(HACC, administer VAWA emergency-transfer and confidentiality obligations lawfully).`
- `O(HACC, comply with accommodation and anti-interference duties under the FHA).`
- `O(HACC, administer the PBV/RAD and Blossom replacement-housing path it adopted in its own plan and public materials).`
- `O(HACC, administer the relocation options it itself directed the household to use).`
- `O(Quantum, if it received or controlled the intake path, preserve and handle that path lawfully and nondiscriminatorily as the project-side participant identified by HACC's own records).`

The key caution is:

- use later emails and communications primarily to prove nonperformance, nonforwarding, delay, inconsistent administration, or breach of these already-grounded duties;
- do not make the later emails the primary source of duty where the stronger source is already statute, regulation, Administrative Plan text, or HACC's public PBV/Blossom materials.

## VII. Practical filing use

This memo supports the following litigation posture:

- lead with statute and regulation for HACC's direct duties;
- use HACC's Administrative Plan, Blossom lease-up page, and formal notices as the second grounding layer;
- describe Quantum as the documented project-side participant in the intake-and-processing chain;
- use email and timeline materials afterward to show how those duties were not carried out in practice.
