## 1. Emergency relief and indefinite plenary relief are blended together

The petition asks for appointment of a guardian for both a `temporary` and `indefinite` period of time, then largely reuses the same factual allegations for both tracks.

Best use:

- argue that the petition turns emergency allegations into a shortcut for final or plenary control;
- argue that a court should require narrowing rather than let a temporary-danger narrative do the work of an indefinite guardianship record.

## 2. Medical-certainty narrative without basic medical grounding

The petition gives a highly specific incapacity and daily-function narrative, but also states that the treating physician is unknown and that petitioner is unaware of what medications Jane is taking.

Best use:

- argue that the petition is more confident than its factual foundation;
- argue that this weakens the reliability of the stronger medical and incapacity characterizations.

## 3. Control request without the underlying papers

The petition asks for broad authority over Jane's estate, inheritance, and trust-related matters without producing:

- trust instruments;
- will-related authority papers;
- accounting or corpus information;
- claimed authority instruments;
- or the papers Jane appears to describe in the earlier restraining-order exhibits as papers she signed but did not understand.

Best use:

- argue that this is a request for control first and document production later;
- argue that the court should invert that order and demand the documents before granting broad authority.

## 4. `No less restrictive alternatives` is pleaded too absolutely

The petition says no less restrictive alternative existed, but the broader record shows ongoing discussion of:

- payee management;
- family assistance;
- move planning;
- possible housing placement;
- and other narrower channels short of plenary coercive control.

Best use:

- argue not that a complete alternative has already been proved;
- but that the petition overstates the point and fails to show fair consideration of narrower measures.

## 5. Housing urgency is asserted without a clean operational plan

The petition uses eviction urgency as a core emergency basis, but it does not provide a comparably concrete housing-execution record showing how Solomon had actually solved the relocation problem.

Best use:

- contrast the petition's rhetoric with the more detailed Clackamas / Multnomah housing-path records already preserved in the packet;
- argue that the petition uses housing emergency as a control lever while omitting the real relocation-process record.

## 6. Fee request reveals adversity to Jane's funds

The petition not only seeks protective authority, but also asks for attorney fees and costs from Jane's funds.

Best use:

- argue that the filing is not purely protective in effect;
- argue that a disputed coercive petition should not be financed from Jane's assets without a much cleaner benefit showing.

## 7. Overly categorical `no other authority / no other court` posture

The petition's categorical position that no other court has acquired jurisdiction and no prior authority existed is too absolute given the local record of overlapping protective-order, eviction, and authority-chain disputes.

Best use:

- treat this as a credibility and overstatement problem;
- keep it proof-gated where necessary, but use it to argue against shortcuts to plenary relief.

## 8. Suitability-to-serve is undermined by the packet's own surrounding context

Even apart from ultimate merits, the surrounding local record undercuts any attempt to present Solomon as a neutral, purely stabilizing fiduciary candidate. The packet instead reflects:

- prior protective-order conflict;
- trust / will / estate-control assertions;
- demands for control of records and communications;
- guardian-ad-litem filing threat in the same sequence;
- and later writ-based forced-transport conduct.

Best use:

- argue that suitability cannot be assessed in isolation from this surrounding pattern;
- argue that these circumstances support caution, narrowing, and record completion before any broad appointment.

## Bottom line

The additional weak spots are:

1. emergency relief used as a bridge to indefinite plenary control;
2. strong incapacity claims without basic medical grounding;
3. trust / estate control requested without the underlying papers;
4. overstatement that no less restrictive alternatives existed;
5. housing urgency pleaded without a clean housing-execution record;
6. fee shifting that is adverse to Jane's funds;
7. overcategorical claims about prior authority and court overlap;
8. a broader control pattern that weakens Solomon's suitability claim.
