# ORS 163.472 Constitutional-Issue Proffer Note (2026-04-09)

This note preserves a narrow point for the probate packet: if Solomon Samuel Barber attempts to use an old conviction involving `ORS 163.472` as a merits shortcut against Benjamin Barber in this probate matter, that old conviction does not by itself foreclose a purely prospective constitutional challenge to the statute.

## Current use point

The filing-safe proposition is limited:

- a prior conviction may be mentioned by the opposing side;
- but if a court later needs to consider whether `ORS 163.472` may constitutionally be applied in the future or whether a prospective challenge is available, the existence of the old conviction does not itself bar that forward-looking constitutional question.

## Supreme Court holding now available

On March 20, 2026, the Supreme Court held in `Olivier v. City of Brandon, Mississippi`, No. `24-993`, that a plaintiff's prior conviction does not bar a `42 U.S.C. § 1983` suit seeking only prospective relief against future enforcement of the challenged law.

Safest statement of the holding:

- `Heck does not bar a suit seeking wholly prospective relief against future enforcement of a challenged law, even when the plaintiff was previously convicted under that law.`

Official source:

- https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/25pdf/24-993_10n2.pdf

Key passages:

- the Court held that Olivier's suit seeking `purely prospective relief` could proceed notwithstanding his prior conviction;
- the Court explained that the suit sought `only to be free from prosecutions for future violations`;
- the Court stated that `Heck, properly understood, does not preclude suits that only attempt to prevent future prosecutions.`

## Probate-packet use note

The current probate packet should use this point conservatively:

- not to ask the probate court to decide the full constitutionality of `ORS 163.472` on the present record unless that issue truly becomes necessary;
- but to prevent the old conviction from being treated as if it automatically forecloses all forward-looking constitutional argument.

## Recommended filing language

Safe current language:

- `To the extent the petition or related filings attempt to rely on an old ORS 163.472 conviction as if it forecloses any future constitutional challenge, Movant notes that a prior conviction does not itself bar a purely prospective constitutional challenge to future enforcement. See Olivier v. City of Brandon, Mississippi, No. 24-993, 607 U.S. ___ (Mar. 20, 2026). If the Court concludes that a merits determination concerning ORS 163.472 becomes necessary, Movant is prepared to provide a separate memorandum of law addressing the statute's constitutionality for the Court's consideration.`

## Scope caution
