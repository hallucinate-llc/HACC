## What "certified" means here

In this workspace, `certified` means a copy or docket record obtained from the court clerk with official certification or other clerk-origin indicia showing that it is the court's official record.

Typical examples:

1. certified copy of a signed order or judgment;
2. certified register of actions / docket printout;
3. certified minute order or appearance record.

## Why certification still matters

Certification mainly matters when trying to prove:

1. exact finality;
2. exact case metadata;
3. the precise prior separate proceeding needed for issue preclusion;
4. a cleaner hearing / appearance / service record.

## Practical consequence for the motions

The motions should not say or imply that no prior source record exists.

The better distinction is:

1. **original stored source scans already exist and support threshold or overlap arguments now**;
2. **clerk-certified copies would strengthen finality, metadata, and preclusion-grade proof later**.

## Current best application

1. Use the original stored scans now for show-cause, threshold control, chronology, and overlap arguments.
2. Treat clerk-certified copies as a later upgrade for final issue-preclusion and docket-specific proof.
