# Section 18 Specific-Performance Duties Memo - 2026-04-09

This memo isolates the clearest Section 18 relocation duties that can support narrow injunctive or specific-performance-style relief against Housing Authority of Clackamas County (`HACC`), and the narrower derivative processing duties that may support relief against Quantum Residential (`Quantum`).

## Primary federal sources

- `42 U.S.C. § 1437p(a)(4)`: https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=(title:42%20section:1437p%20edition:prelim)
- `24 C.F.R. § 970.21`: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/subtitle-B/chapter-IX/part-970/section-970.21

## HACC's clearest Section 18 duties

The strongest current duties are:

1. give the required displacement notice;
2. offer each displaced resident comparable housing;
3. pay actual and reasonable relocation expenses;
4. provide necessary counseling for displaced residents;
5. not commence demolition or complete disposition until residents are relocated.

The strongest statutory point for the current record is that, where comparable housing is being provided through a tenant-based voucher, the comparable-housing requirement is not fulfilled until the family is actually relocated into that housing.

## Clean failure theory on the present record

If the current factual record is credited, HACC failed to perform Section 18 duties by:

1. treating displacement as functionally complete before actual relocation was completed;
2. failing to carry the household into comparable replacement housing;
3. failing to complete the voucher / portability / replacement-housing path;
4. failing to provide the counseling and relocation follow-through necessary to finish the move;
5. leaving the household in a status where no actual lease or contract had been materialized for signature even though HACC had already triggered and described the relocation path.

## Quantum's role

Section 18 directly binds the public housing agency, HACC.

Quantum is strongest as:

1. HACC's intake, forwarding, lease-up, or processing arm for the Blossom / Hillside Manor / Parkside Heights path; and/or
2. a separate actor that undertook a processing obligation by receiving and handling the application path.

So the clean current theory is not that Quantum independently owes Section 18 duties as the public housing agency. It is that Quantum can be ordered not to obstruct, discard, or fail to process the very intake and lease-up path through which HACC was carrying out its Section 18 relocation obligations.

## Best injunction / specific-performance formulation

The narrowest effective relief is:

1. HACC shall not treat relocation or displacement as complete unless and until plaintiffs have actually been relocated into lawful comparable replacement housing.
2. HACC shall complete the voucher, portability, counseling, and comparable-housing steps necessary to finish the relocation it triggered.
3. HACC and Quantum shall preserve and not frustrate the application, intake, forwarding, review, and lease-up chain for the replacement-housing path already undertaken.
4. Quantum shall process, preserve, and not obstruct the application path or materially equivalent successor path under its control.
5. Neither defendant shall let the relocation path fail merely by delay while no signable lease or contract is being produced.

## Why specific performance / mandatory injunction is supportable

The present theory fits specific-performance or mandatory-injunction logic because:

1. the duties are concrete rather than merely aspirational;
2. the harm is ongoing and future-facing;
3. money damages are not an adequate substitute for completed relocation, preserved housing opportunity, or a still-live transfer path.
