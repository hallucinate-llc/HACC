## Quick rule of use

- If a source clearly creates a duty, ask for compliance, preservation, narrowing, or status reporting.
- If a source clearly creates a prohibition, ask the court to stop the prohibited act.
- If the packet shows only overlap, inconsistency risk, or unfairness, ask for narrowing or reservation, not final merits adjudication.
- If the proof is incomplete, use `proof-gated`, `premature`, `unresolved`, `narrowing`, `status`, or `reservation` language instead of `barred`, `must`, `entitled`, or `required` unless the source text truly supports that stronger formulation.
