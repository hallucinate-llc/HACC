# Master Bridge Theory: Probate And Eviction - 2026-04-09

This note states the shortest common theory connecting the probate packet and the eviction / injunction packet.

## Shared theory

The two branches are different forums, but the same practical distortion runs through both:

1. Solomon Samuel Barber has tried to obtain control over Jane Kay Cortez and the household through a record that overstates Benjamin Barber's role and understates outside causes.
2. The housing crisis was not simply caused by Benjamin Barber. It was materially shaped by HACC relocation noncompletion, Quantum intake / processing failure, unresolved portability and lease-up administration, and disputed outside interference.
3. Probate should not be used to turn those unresolved housing and authority disputes into a one-sided emergency narrative justifying plenary or coercive control.
4. Eviction should not be used to convert HACC's own unfinished Section 18 relocation and replacement-housing process into an ordinary tenant default.

## Probate-side translation

In probate, the strongest use of that theory is:

- object to waiver-of-notice, writ-based transport, plenary authority, placement authority, and fee shifting from Jane Kay Cortez's funds;
- fence off collateral housing merits except as narrowly necessary;
- reserve sanctions-related or claim-limiting relief if the petition is found materially barred or seriously defective.

## Eviction / injunction-side translation

In eviction or injunction practice, the strongest use of that theory is:

- HACC triggered Section 18 duties;
- HACC and its chosen intake / lease-up chain did not complete the relocation path;
- the household completed the household-side tasks but still was not given an actual signable lease or completed move;
- therefore HACC was barred from treating the unfinished relocation path as ordinary default.
