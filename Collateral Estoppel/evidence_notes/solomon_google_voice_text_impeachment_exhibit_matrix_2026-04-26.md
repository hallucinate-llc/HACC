## H. Material Facts Solomon Knew But The Probate Petition Omitted

The Google Voice / Google Takeout record is strongest as knowledge-and-omission proof. It does not prove that every accusation in the texts was true. It does show that Solomon knew these subjects existed before he presented an emergency probate narrative.

1. Restraining-order posture: Solomon knew by November 17, 2025 that a restraining-order dispute existed, was told on November 19 that the restraining order had been filed, was told on November 20 that it had been granted, and later received images of the order.
2. Service and order-effect dispute: Solomon knew Benjamin was accusing him of service avoidance by November 21, 2025, and by March 10, 2026 Solomon expressly took the position that he understood the order but was not incentivized to cooperate and would not comply until he accepted that service had occurred.
3. Court-process alternative: Solomon knew Benjamin was insisting that the family, APS, housing, and property disputes be handled through court rather than through informal pickup, third-party pressure, or claimed authority.
4. Trust, estate, and representative-payee facts: Solomon knew of Jane's representative payee, trust/estate communications, and Benjamin's demands for the underlying records before filing or pursuing probate relief.
5. Records preservation and production demands: Solomon was told to preserve records in his possession, his agents' possession, and third parties' possession in anticipation of litigation.
6. APS pickup context: Solomon knew Benjamin disputed the APS pickup plan, asserted Jane was not aware of the appointment, raised Jason's DHS conflict, and said APS could come to the home or proceed through court.
7. Housing and funds context: Solomon knew Benjamin claimed immediate rent resources, knew Marcella/payee issues were involved, and knew the housing dispute was not simply a one-fact narrative that Benjamin alone caused a voucher failure.
8. Relocation or control motive: Solomon stated that he would like to move Jane to Tillamook near Juaneta/Rebekah and that Rebekah and Solomon could help Jane after things were lined up.

Suggested court-safe framing:

`These texts are offered to show Solomon's knowledge, notice, state of mind, and material omissions. They show that the probate petition omitted the restraining-order posture, the order-production dispute, the trust/estate/payee issues, the APS pickup objections, the housing-resource context, and Solomon's own expressed relocation/control plan. The Court need not accept every statement in the texts for its truth to find that Solomon knew these disputes existed and nevertheless presented a one-sided emergency probate narrative.`
