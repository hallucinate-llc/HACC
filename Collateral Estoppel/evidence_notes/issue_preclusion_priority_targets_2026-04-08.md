## Highest-value targets first

### 1. The prior signed order or judgment packet

Priority question answered:

1. What exact prior court ruling is being invoked as the preclusion anchor?

Why it matters:

1. it identifies the actual prior proceeding in a form stronger than summary notes or later references;
2. it is the shortest path to showing what was decided and whether the prior ruling is separate enough to matter.

### 2. The prior docket / register of actions

Priority question answered:

1. What was the prior proceeding's exact case identity, lifecycle, and final procedural posture?

Why it matters:

1. it is the cleanest source for finality, hearing posture, and the sequence of filings and orders;
2. it helps distinguish a separate prior proceeding from later phases of the present case.

### 3. The prior hearing / appearance record

Priority question answered:

1. Did the party against whom preclusion is asserted have a full and fair opportunity to be heard?

Why it matters:

1. hearing minutes, appearance notations, or transcript excerpts can directly support the full-and-fair-opportunity element;
2. they also help anchor what issue was actually presented and contested.

### 4. A completed element mapping from the actual prior materials

Priority question answered:

1. Do the prior materials support identical issue, finality, party/privity, and full-and-fair opportunity?

Why it matters:

1. this is the shortest bridge from “materials present” to a filing-grade threshold comparison;
2. it keeps the dismissal motion disciplined by tying each element to an actual source.
