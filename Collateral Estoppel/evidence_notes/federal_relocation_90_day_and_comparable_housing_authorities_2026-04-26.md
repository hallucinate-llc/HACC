# Federal Relocation / Voucher / 90-Day Authorities

Purpose:

This note marshals the federal housing authorities relevant to Solomon's probate-petition allegation that Benjamin Barber caused Jane Cortez's eviction or voucher failure by saying he would force the courts to evict him.

## 1. Section 18 demolition / disposition relocation rule

Primary source:

- 24 CFR 970.21, `Relocation of residents`
- Source reviewed: [24 CFR § 970.21 - Relocation of residents](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/24/970.21)

Key points for this record:

1. A public housing authority must offer each family displaced by demolition or disposition comparable housing that meets housing quality standards and is located in an area generally not less desirable than the displaced unit.
2. Comparable housing may include tenant-based assistance such as a Housing Choice Voucher.
3. But tenant-based assistance is not considered comparable housing until the family is actually relocated into such housing.
4. The PHA must notify each family residing in the development of the proposed demolition or disposition 90 days before the displacement date, except in cases of imminent threat to health and safety.
5. The PHA must ensure displaced residents are offered comparable replacement housing and must provide actual and reasonable relocation expenses and necessary counseling.

Best use:

- This does not support a loose claim that HACC could evict immediately once a voucher was merely discussed or eventually issued.
- It supports the narrower and stronger point that the probate petition omitted federal relocation protections and oversimplified the housing timeline.
- If the household was being displaced through a Section 18 demolition/disposition path, a voucher alone was not necessarily the end of HACC's obligation, because tenant-based assistance was not comparable housing until actual relocation.

## 2. Uniform Relocation Act / 49 CFR Part 24 fallback

Primary source:

- 49 CFR 24.203, `Relocation notices`
- Source reviewed: [49 CFR § 24.203 - Relocation notices](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/49/24.203)

Key points:

1. A displaced person must be informed that they will not be required to move without at least 90 days advance written notice.
2. A person to be displaced from a dwelling cannot be required to move unless at least one comparable replacement dwelling has been made available.
3. If the 90-day notice is issued before a comparable replacement dwelling is made available, the notice must clearly state that the occupant will not have to move earlier than 90 days after such a dwelling is made available.
4. Urgent shorter notice requires unusual circumstances, such as substantial danger to health or safety, with the agency's determination included in the case file.

Use caution:

- 24 CFR 970.21(g) states that the Uniform Relocation Act does not apply to demolitions and dispositions under Part 970.
- Therefore, use 49 CFR 24.203 as an alternative or fallback authority only if a separate federally assisted relocation regime applies, or as persuasive comparison for why the probate petition's immediate-eviction narrative was incomplete.

## 3. Proposed exhibit use

Use these authorities to rebut the petition's claim that Benjamin caused the housing crisis merely by asserting legal rights or insisting on court process.

Suggested exhibit label:

- Exhibit: Federal Relocation and Comparable-Housing Authorities

Suggested motion language:

`The petition's assertion that Benjamin caused Jane's voucher failure by insisting on court process omitted the federal relocation framework. Under 24 CFR 970.21, families displaced by public-housing demolition or disposition must receive 90-day notice before displacement and comparable housing. Tenant-based assistance such as a voucher is not treated as comparable housing until the family is actually relocated into such housing. To the extent another federally assisted relocation framework applies, 49 CFR 24.203 further provides that a lawful occupant may not be required to move without at least 90 days advance written notice and cannot be required to move unless a comparable replacement dwelling has been made available.`
