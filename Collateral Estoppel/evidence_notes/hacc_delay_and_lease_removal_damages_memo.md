# HACC Delay And Lease-Removal Damages Memo

This memo summarizes the strongest current repository support for:

- damage caused by Benjamin Barber being taken off the lease; and
- the amount and character of delay that can presently be attributed to HACC.

It is written conservatively and separates supported harm from figures or categories that still need declaration support or calculation.

## 1. Lease-removal-specific harm

The current record supports that Benjamin's removal from the lease was not a harmless paperwork event. The strongest presently supportable harms are:

- household-composition confusion during the same period HACC was making relocation and voucher decisions;
- increased risk of loss of housing rights and instability in the lease / voucher path;
- one-bedroom voucher consequences tied to changed household composition;
- forced response to an altered housing path while the household still maintained that the composition change was wrong;
- added delay while HACC sorted through outside document flows and contradictory household-status positions.

## 2. HACC-attributable delay overall

The current record supports that HACC-caused or HACC-associated delay was substantial and extended across multiple months.

### Waterleaf / portability delay

The strongest documented HACC-attributable delay chronology is:

- `2025-12-23`: Waterleaf application created. See [waterleaf_application.png](/home/barberb/HACC/evidence/history/waterleaf_application.png) and [waterleaf_vawa_port_timeline.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/docs/waterleaf_vawa_port_timeline.md).
- `2026-01-12`: HACC was told Waterleaf had the signed application and wanted the voucher amount. See [waterleaf_vawa_port_timeline.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/docs/waterleaf_vawa_port_timeline.md).
- `2026-01-26`: HACC acknowledged the household had chosen to port to Multnomah County. See [waterleaf_vawa_port_timeline.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/docs/waterleaf_vawa_port_timeline.md).
- `2026-03-17` and `2026-03-18`: Benjamin wrote that he had been trying to move to Waterleaf for nearly three months. See [waterleaf_vawa_port_timeline.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/docs/waterleaf_vawa_port_timeline.md).
- `2026-03-25` and `2026-03-26`: Waterleaf still had the household on a waiting list pending voucher issuance by HACC. See [waterleaf_vawa_port_timeline.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/docs/waterleaf_vawa_port_timeline.md).

Best present measurement:

- at least from `January 12, 2026` to `March 25-26, 2026`, HACC was on direct notice that Waterleaf needed voucher-side action yet the move remained incomplete, which is roughly `10 to 11 weeks`;
- the broader path likely ran from `December 23, 2025` to `March 25-26, 2026`, roughly `13 weeks`, but the earliest portion is better framed as the start of the move effort rather than delay conclusively chargeable only to HACC.

### Voucher / relocation-support delay

- The damages memo already notes support that by `March 17, 2026`, no relocation-assistance check had been provided to contract movers and no voucher had yet issued despite an imminent move timeline. See [damages-evidence-memo.md](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/damages-evidence-memo.md).
- HACC then told the household on `March 19, 2026` that the two-bedroom voucher had been created and sent in error and that a one-bedroom voucher would instead issue. That supports additional HACC-caused instability and delay in the usable housing path. See [title18_contract_deontic_bar_on_eviction_memo.md](/home/barberb/HACC/workspace/title18_contract_deontic_bar_on_eviction_memo.md) and the Exhibit M orientation thread materials cited in [waterleaf_vawa_port_timeline.md](/home/barberb/HACC/Breach%20of%20Contract/docs/waterleaf_vawa_port_timeline.md).
- The current record also supports that the delay did not end on March 19. On `March 29, 2026`, Benjamin sent a Parkside Heights application to `parksideheights@quantumres.com` and copied Kati Tilton and HACC Relocation, stating that a copy and application fee would be mailed the next day. On `March 30, 2026`, Parkside Heights replied, thanked him for submitting the application, and said the two-bedroom units in Building A were still under construction with estimated occupancy at the end of April. When read together with HACC's March 26 options email requiring a move by `April 30`, that Parkside exchange supports that the household was still being pushed through the same April 30 move-out framework rather than reaching a completed relocation.
