# Subpoena Target Memo: HACC Lease-Authority Record

Date: 2026-04-07

This memo records the current working conclusion that the repository does not contain the source-authority record needed to identify who caused Benjamin Barber's lease removal or what authority HACC believed it was acting on.

## Current conclusion

The best current read is:

- the repository contains direct proof that HACC removed Benjamin from the household effective January 1, 2026;
- the repository contains direct proof that HACC claimed this followed internal review and court documentation on file; but
- the repository does not contain the internal HACC record that identifies the source actor, the specific court document relied on, or any claimed authority instrument.

Accordingly, the source-authority record should now be treated as a subpoena or compelled-production target rather than as an ordinary local-search task.

## What the existing record already proves

- HACC told Benjamin on January 12, 2026 that he was no longer listed as a household member effective January 1, 2026.
- HACC said the household composition decision followed internal review and court documentation currently on file.
- Benjamin and Jane later declared that Charley Skee explained in substance that Benjamin had been taken off the lease because Benjamin's brother and stepfather had been emailing documents to HACC.

These points materially narrow the likely location and character of the missing record.

## Highest-value compelled records

- the internal HACC case note or CRM entry recording why Benjamin was removed from the lease
- the lease-change request or approval chain for the January 1, 2026 household-composition change
- any email thread forwarding or attaching the court document HACC said it had on file
- any scanned or uploaded court document actually relied upon for the household-composition change
- any note, email, or intake record identifying the person who submitted the decisive material
- any representative-authorization, power-of-attorney-like document, or claimed-authority note tied to Jane's housing decisions

## Best current filing formulation

The safest current formulation is:

`The repository establishes the lease-removal decision and HACC's stated basis for that decision, but the actor-identification and authority-chain record is absent from the local materials and must be obtained from HACC or another third-party custodian through subpoena, records request, or other compelled production.`

## Relationship to the motion packet

- This memo narrows `Exhibit R`.
- `Exhibit R` should now be described as a compelled-production target.
- The packet should stop implying that further local searching is likely to close this gap.
