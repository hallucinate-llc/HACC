# Section 18 Demolition/Disposition Source Manifest

Compiled: 2026-04-05
Purpose: Archive primary legal authorities for Section 18 demolition/disposition and ground demands for relief.

## Primary Authorities Archived

1. 42 U.S.C. 1437p (Demolition and disposition of public housing)
- URL: https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title42-section1437p&num=0&edition=prelim
- Why it matters: Core statutory requirements for approval, resident notice/relocation, proceeds use, resident opportunity to purchase, and disapproval grounds.

2. 24 CFR 970.7 (General requirements for HUD approval)
- URL: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/section-970.7
- Why it matters: Enumerates application contents, consultation proof, board resolution timing, and environmental/civil-rights certifications.

3. 24 CFR 970.9 (Resident participation and purchase opportunity)
- URL: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/section-970.9
- Why it matters: Consultation obligations and resident organization purchase process with 30/60 day windows.

4. 24 CFR 970.13 (Environmental review)
- URL: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/section-970.13
- Why it matters: Requires Part 58 (or Part 50 if applicable), scope for known reuse, and disaster context handling.

5. 24 CFR 970.19 (Disposition proceeds)
- URL: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/section-970.19
- Why it matters: Fair market value/appraisal requirements and restricted use of net proceeds.

6. 24 CFR 970.21 (Relocation of residents)
- URL: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/section-970.21
- Why it matters: Comparable housing standards, 90-day notices, moving expenses, counseling, disability accommodation, and funding interactions.

7. 24 CFR 970.25 (Actions prohibited before HUD approval)
- URL: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/section-970.25
- Why it matters: Bars demolition/disposition before approval and clarifies pre-approval limits.

8. 24 CFR 970.29 (Disapproval criteria)
- URL: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/section-970.29
- Why it matters: HUD must disapprove when certifications conflict with data or required consultations are missing.
