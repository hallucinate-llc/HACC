# Section 18 Demands-for-Relief Grounding Matrix

Compiled: 2026-04-05
Use: Tie each requested remedy to a specific statutory/regulatory duty.

## 1) Request: Stay eviction and preserve tenancy until lawful relocation offered

- Grounding rule: No demolition/disposition completion before relocation of all residents in affected building.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(E); 24 CFR 970.21(b), 970.21(e)(1)(ii).
- Evidence to cite: Notice date, relocation status records, unit occupancy records.

## 2) Request: Order production of HUD approval packet and relocation plan

- Grounding rule: HUD approval and detailed application components are mandatory.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.7(a), 970.25(a).
- Evidence to cite: Missing or incomplete application disclosures.

## 3) Request: Enforce 90-day written notice requirement

- Grounding rule: 90-day advance notice to each family, except imminent threat cases.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(A); 24 CFR 970.21(e)(1).
- Evidence to cite: Notice letters, mail logs, tenant declarations.

## 4) Request: Require comparable replacement housing offer before displacement

- Grounding rule: Must offer HQS-compliant comparable housing in area not less desirable.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(A)(iii); 24 CFR 970.21(a).
- Evidence to cite: Unit comparison sheets, rent sheets, neighborhood comparability.

## 5) Request: Require disability accommodation in relocation option

- Grounding rule: Comparable housing for displaced residents with disabilities should include similar accommodations; reasonable accommodation requirements apply.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.21(a), 970.21(e)(1)(iii), 24 CFR part 8.
- Evidence to cite: Medical/accessibility records, prior accommodation approvals, offered-unit accessibility defects.

## 6) Request: Relocation expense reimbursement and counseling services

- Grounding rule: PHA must provide actual and reasonable relocation expenses and counseling.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(B), (D); 24 CFR 970.21(e)(2), (4).
- Evidence to cite: Out-of-pocket receipts, counselor assignment records.

## 7) Request: Challenge validity of disposition where resident purchase process ignored

- Grounding rule: Resident groups have protected 30/60-day purchase-opportunity timelines in disposition context.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(c); 24 CFR 970.9 and 970.11.
- Evidence to cite: Sale notices, timestamps, resident organization correspondence.

## 8) Request: Challenge unlawful pre-approval actions

- Grounding rule: PHA cannot demolish/dispose before HUD approval; must continue ACC duties.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.25(a); 24 CFR 970.7(a).
- Evidence to cite: Work orders, contractor records, vacancy handling timeline, service reductions.

## 9) Request: Challenge disapproval-level defects in application process

- Grounding rule: HUD must disapprove where certifications conflict with record, or consultation duties not met.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(b); 24 CFR 970.29.
- Evidence to cite: Inconsistency table between application certifications and documentary record.

## 10) Request: Financial accountability for proceeds in disposition case

- Grounding rule: Proceeds have restricted uses and debt-retirement priority unless waived.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(5); 24 CFR 970.19(e).
- Evidence to cite: Appraisal, closing statement, proceeds ledger, HUD waiver documents.

## Drafting Note

When pleading, frame factual points as alleged noncompliance with specific requirements above, then request targeted relief tied to the same requirement (injunctive hold, production order, accommodation order, reimbursement order, or compliance declaration).
