# Section 18 Operational Requirements (Tenant-Enforcement View)

Compiled: 2026-04-05

## A. Threshold Requirement: HUD Approval Before Action

1. PHA must obtain written HUD approval before demolition/disposition transactions.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.7(a); 24 CFR 970.25(a).
- Tenant-facing implication: Any demolition/disposition action taken before approval can be challenged as noncompliant.

2. PHA must continue ACC obligations and full housing services while application is pending.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.25(a).
- Tenant-facing implication: Service degradation before approval may support injunctive or defensive relief.

## B. Required Application Content and Process

1. PHA Annual Plan alignment and identity between plan description and application.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.7(a)(1).

2. Relocation plan including disability accommodations and timetable.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.7(a)(6); 24 CFR 970.21.

3. Evidence of resident and RAB consultation and comments/evaluation.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.7(a)(7); 24 CFR 970.9(a).

4. Board resolution must be signed after resident and local-government consultation is complete.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.7(a)(13).

5. Environmental review approval required.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.7(a)(15); 24 CFR 970.13.

## C. Consultation and Resident Purchase Rights (Disposition)

1. Consultation with affected residents, resident orgs, and RAB is mandatory.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.9(a); 42 U.S.C. 1437p(b)(2).

2. Resident purchase timeline:
- 30 days to express interest after notice of proposed sale.
- 60 days after expression to obtain firm financing commitment.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(c)(2)(A)-(B); 24 CFR 970.9(b)(1)-(2).

## D. Relocation, Notice, and Comparable Housing Duties

1. 90-day notice prior to displacement (except imminent health/safety threat).
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(A); 24 CFR 970.21(e)(1).

2. Demolition/disposition cannot proceed until all residents in the building are relocated.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(E); 24 CFR 970.21(b), 970.21(e)(1)(ii).

3. Comparable housing standard includes:
- HQS-compliant housing;
- area generally not less desirable;
- nondiscriminatory offering;
- disability accommodation parity where required.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(A)(iii); 24 CFR 970.21(a), 970.21(e)(1)(iii).

4. PHA must pay actual and reasonable relocation expenses and provide counseling.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(4)(B), (D); 24 CFR 970.21(e)(2), (4).

## E. Disposition Proceeds and Appraisal Rules

1. Fair market value baseline; less-than-FMV requires commensurate public benefit and HUD acceptance.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.19(a).

2. Independent appraisal generally required.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.19(c).

3. Net proceeds are restricted:
- debt retirement unless waived;
- remaining proceeds for low-income housing/resident benefit or specific onsite leverage uses.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(a)(5); 24 CFR 970.19(e).

## F. HUD Disapproval Triggers

1. Certification conflicts with PHA Plan or available data.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.29(a); 42 U.S.C. 1437p(b)(1).

2. Inadequate consultation with affected residents/resident councils/RAB/local officials.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.29(b); 42 U.S.C. 1437p(b)(2).

## G. URA and Funding Interaction

1. URA does not apply to Section 18 demolitions/dispositions.
- Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437p(g); 24 CFR 970.21(g).

2. If CDBG/HOME/UDAG-related funding is involved, section 104(d) anti-displacement provisions can attach.
- Authority: 24 CFR 970.21(c)(2).

## H. Evidence Checklist to Ground Relief Requests

1. HUD approval letter and full application exhibits.
2. PHA Annual Plan language and board resolution dates.
3. Consultation logs, comments, and responses.
4. 90-day notices and proof of service.
5. Individual relocation offers and comparable-housing analyses.
6. Disability accommodation requests and determinations.
7. Appraisal and proceeds-use documentation.
8. Any evidence of pre-approval demolition/disposition steps.
